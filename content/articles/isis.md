## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2004 | Origins in Iraq under Abu Musab al-Zarqawi, commonly traced to **Jama'at al-Tawhid wal-Jihad**, later **Al-Qaeda in Iraq (AQI)**. |
| 2006 | Rebranded as the **Islamic State of Iraq (ISI)** following AQI's announcement of an umbrella organization. |
| 2013 | Expanded into Syria; the name **Islamic State of Iraq and al-Sham (ISIS/ISIL)** became widely used in media and policy. |
| 2014 | Captured Mosul and declared a “caliphate” led by **Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi**; held significant territory in Iraq and Syria. |
| 2015–2017 | Territorial losses accelerated under multi-party military campaigns; propaganda and external attacks became a major concern. |
| 2019 | Loss of remaining major territorial holdings in Syria (Baghuz); group shifted toward insurgency and dispersed networks. |
| 2019 | Baghdadi killed in a U.S. raid; subsequent leaders announced by the organization. |

## Notes on Naming and Scope

- **ISIS / ISIL / IS** generally refer to the same organization at different points in time and naming conventions.
- This page exists as a short identifier entry. For the primary biographical profile, the original document points to **Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi**.

## Overview (High-Level)

- **Type:** Sunni Islamist militant and terrorist organization.
- **Core claim (2014–2019 peak):** Establishment of a “caliphate” and governance over captured territory.
- **Operations:** Insurgency, territorial control (during peak years), armed attacks, and extensive media/projection operations.
- **International status:** Widely designated as a terrorist organization by numerous governments and international bodies.

## Ideology and Objectives (Summary)

- Commonly described as adhering to an **extreme, violent interpretation of Sunni Islam**.
- Objectives have included:
  - Overthrowing existing state structures in parts of the Middle East.
  - Establishing governance aligned with its ideological program.
  - Recruiting supporters globally through propaganda and social media distribution.

## Current Status (General)

- After major territorial defeat in 2019, ISIS has been reported as continuing as an **insurgent movement** with **affiliated or inspired networks** in multiple regions.
- The scale, organization, and operational capacity have varied significantly by time and geography.
