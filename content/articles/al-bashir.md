## Background

- **Omar Hassan Ahmad al-Bashir** (Arabic: عمر حسن أحمد البشير) is a Sudanese former military officer and political leader who ruled Sudan for three decades (1989–2019).[^1]
- He came to power via a **military coup** in June 1989 and later led the **National Congress Party (NCP)** political system that dominated Sudanese governance during his tenure.[^1]

## Key themes during his rule (high-level)

- **Civil conflict and insurgencies:** His presidency overlapped with major internal conflicts, including the **Second Sudanese Civil War** (ending in the 2005 Comprehensive Peace Agreement) and the **Darfur conflict** that began in 2003.[^1]
- **International legal proceedings:** The International Criminal Court (ICC) issued arrest warrants for al-Bashir (from 2009 onward) in connection with alleged crimes in Darfur; Sudan was not an ICC member state, and enforcement depended on states’ cooperation.[^1][^2]
- **State transitions:** Long-running political and economic pressures, including protests in 2018–2019, culminated in his removal by the military in April 2019.[^1]

- **2011** — South Sudan becomes independent following the 2011 referendum framework established by the CPA.[^1]
