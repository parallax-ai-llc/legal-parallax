## Summary
Umar Farouk Abdulmutallab, the 'Underwear Bomber,' attempted to detonate explosives hidden in his underwear on a flight to Detroit on Christmas Day 2009. The device failed. Sentenced to life.

## Background
- Born in Kaduna, Nigeria; grew up in an affluent family and later studied abroad.
- Attended University College London (UCL), where he was associated with Islamic student groups during his time in the UK.
- Traveled to Yemen in 2009, where U.S. authorities say he was trained and equipped by al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP).

## 2009 Flight 253 Attack Attempt
- On **December 25, 2009**, Abdulmutallab attempted to detonate an improvised explosive device (IED) concealed in his clothing on **Northwest Airlines Flight 253** from Amsterdam to Detroit.
- The device malfunctioned and did not explode as intended; passengers and crew subdued him, and he was arrested after the plane landed.
- The incident became known widely as the "Underwear Bomber" plot.

## Investigation and Prosecution
- U.S. federal authorities charged him with multiple terrorism-related offenses, including attempted use of a weapon of mass destruction and attempted murder.
- In **2011**, he pleaded guilty in federal court.
- He was sentenced to **life imprisonment** without parole in the United States.

## Incarceration and Later Developments
- He has been held in the U.S. federal prison system; public reporting has periodically noted his status and conditions of confinement.
- The case is often cited in discussions of interrogation policy, intelligence sharing, and aviation security procedures.

## Significance
- The attempt accelerated changes to airport screening, including increased use of advanced imaging and tighter scrutiny of concealed explosives.
- It also intensified focus on AQAP's external-operations capability and the role of Yemen-based networks in transnational terrorism planning.
