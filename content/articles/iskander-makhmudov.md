## Summary

Uzbek-born Russian oligarch linked to organized crime and accused of commissioning the murder of journalist Anna Politkovskaya.

## Background

- Born in the Uzbek SSR (Soviet Uzbekistan) and later became a Russian citizen, according to widely-cited biographical summaries.[^1]
- Public biographies commonly describe education in Oriental Studies in Tashkent and early work connected to Soviet foreign trade postings in the Middle East.[^1]

## Business Profile (high-level)

- Often described as a major figure in Russian non-ferrous metals, associated with Ural Mining and Metallurgical Company (UMMC/UGMK) and related industrial assets.[^1]
- Reported to have worked with Trans-World Group (TWG) / associated trading businesses in the 1990s before consolidating interests in copper and coal assets.[^1]
- Frequently reported as involved in transport/engineering holdings (e.g., board-level association with Transmashholding in public profiles).[^1]
