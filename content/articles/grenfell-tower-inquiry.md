## Summary

The Grenfell Tower fire inquiry found that the cladding manufacturer, installer, and local council all bore responsibility for the 72 deaths. Flammable cladding was installed to save money.

## Additional Context

### What the inquiry examined

The Grenfell Tower Inquiry was established to investigate the causes of the Grenfell Tower fire and the circumstances that allowed the fire to spread. It examined building design and refurbishment decisions, product testing and certification, regulatory oversight, and aspects of the emergency response.

### Structure and reporting

The inquiry took evidence in phases, including a first phase focused on the night of the fire and the response, and later work addressing broader systemic issues such as construction practices, governance, and product safety.

### Themes highlighted in findings

High-level themes repeatedly discussed in public reporting include combustible external cladding and insulation, failures in fire safety features intended to prevent fire spread, deficiencies in compliance and enforcement, and decision-making that prioritized cost and schedule over safety.

### Wider impact

The disaster and inquiry contributed to major changes in UK building-safety policy and a broader review of high-rise residential safety, including work to identify and remediate dangerous cladding systems on other buildings.

## Key Takeaways

- A public inquiry can map responsibility across government, contractors, and product supply chains rather than treating disasters as single-point failures.
- High-rise fire safety depends on both materials and the integrity of "defence in depth" measures such as compartmentation and doors.
- Post-incident recommendations often drive regulatory change and large-scale remediation programs.

## Overview
The Grenfell Tower Inquiry is a public inquiry in the United Kingdom established to examine the circumstances surrounding the Grenfell Tower fire in London in June 2017. It has taken evidence from survivors, bereaved families, residents, firefighters, government officials, regulators, and companies involved in the building’s refurbishment and management.

## What the inquiry examines
At a high level, the inquiry has focused on:
- Decisions and actions by the landlord and managing agents before the fire
- The 2010s refurbishment of Grenfell Tower, including design choices and materials
- Building regulations, guidance, and enforcement in relation to high‑rise fire safety
- The London Fire Brigade’s response, including operational decision-making and communications

## Why it matters
The Grenfell Tower fire became a watershed moment for UK building safety policy. The inquiry has been closely linked to wider reforms in fire safety and building regulation, including scrutiny of cladding and insulation products, the testing and certification ecosystem, and responsibilities across the construction supply chain.
