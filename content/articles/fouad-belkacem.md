## Background

- Fouad Belkacem is a Belgian Islamist activist who became publicly known as a leading figure associated with the group **Sharia4Belgium**.
- Sharia4Belgium was widely described in reporting and court proceedings as a Salafist-jihadist network that promoted extremist ideology and encouraged travel to conflict zones.

## Sharia4Belgium (high-level)

- The group used public demonstrations, online messaging, and confrontational activism to gain attention.
- Belgian authorities and courts linked the network to **recruitment and facilitation** efforts connected to foreign fighter flows to Syria during the early-to-mid 2010s.
- Sharia4Belgium was later designated and prosecuted as a terrorist organization under Belgian law.

## Legal proceedings and convictions

- Belkacem was prosecuted in Belgium for leadership roles connected to the organization.
- In 2015, he was convicted of **leading a terrorist organization** in a major trial involving members of Sharia4Belgium.
- The conviction centered on organizational leadership and the network’s role in mobilization/recruitment, rather than battlefield participation.

## Significance and context

- The Sharia4Belgium case became one of Belgium’s most prominent domestic prosecutions tied to the Syria foreign-fighter phenomenon.
- The trial and conviction are often cited in discussions of how European states used criminal law to address recruitment networks and extremist propaganda.
