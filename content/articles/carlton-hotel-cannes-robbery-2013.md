## Summary

Single armed robber stole €103 million in jewels from a Leviev diamonds exhibition at the Carlton Hotel in Cannes. One of the largest jewel heists ever committed by one person.

## Incident Details (High-Level)

- **Date/Place:** 28 July 2013, Carlton InterContinental Hotel in Cannes, France.
- **Target:** A **Leviev** diamonds exhibition hosted at the hotel.
- **Method:** Contemporary reporting describes a **single armed offender** entering the exhibition area, threatening staff, and collecting jewelry into a bag before departing.
- **Immediate impact:** No injuries were widely reported in initial summaries; the main loss was the jewelry itself.

## Why It Mattered

- The estimated value (reported at **about €103 million**) made it one of the most notable hotel-based jewelry thefts of its era.
- The event became a frequently cited example of how **high-value portable assets** can be vulnerable even in high-end, highly trafficked venues.

## Investigation & Aftermath

- Public summaries emphasize the unusual aspect of a **single-person** execution for such a large theft.
- Long-running media attention focused on how the offender was able to leave the premises with the jewelry despite the hotel setting.

## Security Takeaways (General)

- **Event security planning:** High-value exhibitions require dedicated access control beyond normal hotel security.
- **Layered controls:** Screening, controlled entry/exit points, and rapid-response protocols reduce the feasibility of a quick “in-and-out” grab.
- **Asset handling:** Procedures for transport, display, and end-of-day storage can materially change risk exposure.
