## Summary

Indian organized crime group that split from D-Company and engaged in contract killings and extortion.

## Background and Context

- Wikipedia describes the group as an Indian organized crime network associated with Chhota Rajan (Rajendra Sadashiv Nikalje).[^1]
- It is commonly discussed in relation to Mumbai’s underworld networks and rivalries, particularly its split from D-Company.[^1]

## Reported Activities

High-level activity types frequently attributed to the group in public reporting include:

- Extortion and protection rackets
- Contract killings
- Smuggling and other illicit finance activities

## Relationships and Rivalries

- The group is primarily notable for having separated from D-Company and becoming a rival faction in accounts of organized crime in India.[^1]
- Public sources typically frame this as part of broader factional splits, shifting alliances, and retaliation cycles in Mumbai’s organized crime landscape.[^1]

## Geographic Footprint (High-Level)

- Most descriptions place the group’s origins and early activity in Mumbai and western India, with later coverage involving operations and connections across multiple regions.[^1]

## Why It Appears in Historical/Political Contexts

- The group is referenced in discussions about organized crime’s interaction with politics, business, and law enforcement capacity.
- It is also cited as an example of how large criminal networks can fracture into smaller factions that compete over territory and revenue streams.[^1]
