## Summary

See Idriss Déby. Chadian military dictator.

## Key Facts

- **Primary identity:** The article title “Deby” commonly refers to **Idriss Déby Itno** (often written “Idriss Déby”), a long-serving Chadian military ruler and president.
- **Tenure:** He ruled Chad from **1990** until his death in **2021**, combining electoral politics with strong military control.
- **System of power:** His governance relied heavily on security institutions, patronage networks, and management of factional competition in a conflict-prone region.

## Governance and Security

- Déby’s rule is frequently described as **authoritarian**, with elections and formal institutions existing alongside tight control over opposition and the security sector.
- Chad’s strategic geography (bordering Libya, Sudan, the Central African Republic, Nigeria, Niger, and Cameroon) made internal stability closely tied to regional wars and insurgencies.
- In the 2010s, Chad became a prominent participant in **regional counter-insurgency operations**, including efforts against militant groups in the Lake Chad Basin.

## Death and Aftermath

- Déby’s death in April 2021 occurred during renewed fighting with rebels in northern Chad.
- Following his death, a **military transition** was announced, reflecting the centrality of the armed forces to the state he led.

## Naming Note

This page uses the short name **“Deby”**; many sources index him under **Idriss Déby** or **Idriss Déby Itno**.
