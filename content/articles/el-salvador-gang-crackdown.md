## Summary

President Bukele's state of exception has imprisoned 75,000+ suspected gang members. While homicides dropped dramatically, thousands of innocent people have been arbitrarily detained.

## Background

- El Salvador experienced long-running violence associated with MS-13 and Barrio 18, with periods of very high homicide rates in the 2010s.
- In late March 2022, a surge of killings preceded the government’s declaration of a **state of exception** (régimen de excepción).

## Key Measures (State of Exception)

- Emergency powers enabled rapid, large-scale arrests and extended detention periods.
- Security forces conducted neighborhood sweeps, road checkpoints, and targeted operations in areas associated with gang activity.
- A large new prison complex, the **Terrorism Confinement Center (CECOT)**, was built/expanded to house detainees.

## Reported Outcomes

- Government and media reporting emphasized sharp declines in killings after the crackdown.
- Public opinion polling and electoral politics frequently cited improved perceptions of safety.
- Critics note that correlating the crackdown to crime reduction is complicated by prior trends and limited transparency in some statistics.

## Human Rights & Due Process Concerns

- Human rights organizations and journalists have reported patterns of:
  - Arbitrary detention based on appearance, residence, or anonymous accusations
  - Limited access to legal counsel and delayed court review
  - Overcrowded detention conditions and allegations of abuse
- Families and advocates have reported cases of mistaken identity and prolonged detention without clear evidence.

## Domestic and International Reactions

- Supporters argue the policy restored public security and dismantled gang territorial control.
- Opponents argue the measures normalized emergency rule and weakened constitutional safeguards.
- International observers have issued a mix of praise for improved security and criticism regarding civil liberties and due process.

## Why It Matters (Historical Lens)

- The crackdown became a widely cited contemporary example of a modern, high-intensity anti-gang security strategy.
- It raised a recurring policy dilemma: **rapid public-safety gains vs. rights protections and institutional checks**.
- It also influenced debates in other countries considering exceptional security measures.
