## Overview

- **Affiliation (reported):** The attackers were widely reported as Al-Shabaab militants.
- **Target:** Garissa University College (Kenya).
- **Date:** 2 April 2015.
- **Outcome:** The attack resulted in **148 deaths** and many injuries, and triggered a large security response and national mourning.

## Context

Garissa County lies in northeastern Kenya near the Somalia border, an area that has experienced cross-border security incidents and militant activity. In the years leading up to 2015, Kenya faced a number of attacks attributed to Al-Shabaab, which contributed to heightened security concerns around public venues and institutions.

## Attack (high-level)

- Gunmen entered the campus early in the morning and attacked students and staff.
- A prolonged siege followed as Kenyan security forces moved to regain control of the site.
- Survivors described chaotic evacuation attempts and extended periods of hiding while the assault continued.

## Aftermath and response

- Kenyan authorities launched investigations and manhunts for suspected accomplices and support networks.
- The incident prompted renewed debate about campus security, emergency preparedness, and counter-terrorism policy.
- Universities and public institutions in Kenya reviewed safety protocols, including access control and crisis communications.

## Impact

- The deaths of a large number of students made the attack one of the deadliest militant incidents in Kenya in recent decades.
- The event had lasting effects on perceptions of safety in higher education and public life, and is frequently cited in discussions about regional security.

## Notes on identification

Public reporting often refers to the perpetrators collectively ("the Garissa University attackers") rather than as a stable, individually verified roster. Where names or numbers are provided in sources, details can vary by report.
