## Summary

Abdulkadir Masharipov, Uzbek ISIS member who attacked the Reina nightclub on New Year's Eve killing 39.

Masharipov is widely identified as the assailant in the **Istanbul nightclub shooting** (often referred to as the **Reina nightclub attack**) that took place in Istanbul, Turkey on **1 January 2017**.

## Background and Identification

- **Name**: Abdulkadir Masharipov
- **Origin**: Uzbekistan-born (commonly described as Uzbek/Uzbekistani in reporting)
- **Affiliation**: Islamic State (IS/ISIS) claimed responsibility for the attack.

## Attack (Reina nightclub, 1 January 2017)

High-level facts commonly reported in summaries of the incident:

- **Location**: Reina nightclub, Ortaköy neighborhood, Istanbul, Turkey
- **Date/time**: 1 January 2017 (early morning local time)
- **Casualties**: 39 killed; dozens injured
- **Attack type**: Mass shooting

## Arrest and Legal Proceedings

- Masharipov was **arrested in Istanbul in January 2017** following a multi-day manhunt.
- Court proceedings for Masharipov and alleged accomplices were reported as beginning in **late 2017**, with subsequent hearings continuing afterward.

## Why This Attack Was Notable

- It occurred during New Year's celebrations at a crowded venue.
- It became one of the most internationally covered terrorist incidents in Turkey in 2017.
- The incident is frequently discussed in the context of Turkey's broader conflict with ISIL/ISIS.
