## Summary

Former Stutthof camp secretary convicted at age 97 of being an accessory to over 10,000 murders.

## Background and Role

- Furchner worked as a **secretary and stenographer** at the Stutthof concentration camp.
- According to public reporting, she served in the commandant’s office under **Paul-Werner Hoppe**.
- Prosecutors argued that clerical work in the camp command structure helped the camp function and thus contributed to the crimes carried out there.[^1]

## Charges and Trial

- German prosecutors charged her in 2021 with **accessory to murder (11,412 counts)** and **accessory to attempted murder (18 counts)**, tied to the operation of Stutthof during her period of employment.[^1]
- The case was handled by a **juvenile court** because she was a teenager at the time of the alleged offenses.[^1]
- On the day her trial was scheduled to begin in 2021, she **briefly absconded** and was later located by authorities; proceedings were postponed and later resumed.[^1]

## Verdict, Sentence, and Appeal

- In December 2022 she was found guilty and received a **two-year suspended sentence**.[^1]
- Her conviction was later **upheld by Germany’s Federal Court of Justice** (reported in August 2024).[^1]

## Why the Case Matters

- The trial is frequently cited as part of Germany’s late-stage effort to bring remaining Holocaust-era cases to court, even when defendants are very elderly.
- It also reflects the shift in German jurisprudence toward prosecuting individuals for **participation in the functioning of a killing system**, not only for directly committing violence.
