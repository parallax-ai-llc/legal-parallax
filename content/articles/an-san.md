## Summary

An San won three gold medals in archery at the 2021 Tokyo Olympics — individual, team, and mixed team.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Highlights

- **Historic Olympic triple**: At the Tokyo 2020 Games (held in 2021), An San became the **first archer to win three gold medals at a single Olympic Games**.[^1]
- **Olympic records**: She set an **Olympic record in the women’s ranking round** in Tokyo, underlining her consistency before match play.[^1]
- **Key finals**: Her medal sweep included the women’s **individual** event, the **women’s team** event, and the **mixed team** event.[^1]

## Olympic Performance (Tokyo 2020)

In Tokyo, An San’s run was notable for both volume and difficulty: archery’s elimination brackets require repeated head-to-head wins, where single ends can swing outcomes. She secured gold across three separate disciplines, demonstrating adaptability across different match formats and teammate pairings.[^1]

- **Women’s team**: Part of South Korea’s team that continued the country’s long-running dominance in Olympic women’s team archery.[^1]
- **Mixed team**: Won gold in the mixed team event (a newer Olympic discipline), showcasing fast adjustment to format and partner dynamics.[^1]
- **Individual**: Won the women’s individual title, completing a rare three-gold Games for an archer.[^1]

## Public Attention and Aftermath

Following the Olympics, An San received significant public attention in South Korea. Discussion around her appearance (including her short haircut) and online commentary sparked broader debate about **misogyny, gender norms, and feminism** in Korean society.[^1]

## Legacy

An San is frequently referenced as a modern symbol of South Korean archery excellence because her Tokyo performance combined:

- **Peak results** (three Olympic golds in one Games)
- **Record-level qualification scoring** (ranking round record)
- **High-pressure match consistency** across multiple events
