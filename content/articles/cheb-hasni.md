## Summary

Algerian raï singer assassinated by Islamist extremists during Algeria's civil war.

## Early Life

- Born **Hasni Chakroun** in **Oran, Algeria** (often associated with the Gambetta neighborhood).[^1]
- Grew up in a **working-class** family; accounts note his father worked as a **welder** and he was one of **seven** children.[^1]
- Before music, he was involved in **youth football**; an injury as a teenager reportedly ended his path as a player.[^1]

## Music and Public Image

- Performed and recorded within the **raï** tradition, and is frequently described as a leading figure in a more **sentimental/romantic** strand of the genre.[^1]
- Rose to broad popularity across the **Maghreb** during the **late 1980s and early 1990s**.[^1]
- Some early releases attracted controversy in Algeria; the raï scene in this era often faced criticism from conservative and fundamentalist currents.[^1]

## Performances and Reach

- Like many Algeria-based raï artists during the period of restrictions and curfews, he is reported to have **performed abroad** extensively (including cities such as Paris/Marseille, as well as venues in North America and elsewhere).[^1]
- His **last concert in Algeria** is commonly reported as taking place in **1993** in Algiers, drawing a very large crowd.[^1]

## Death

- Died in **Oran** on **29 September 1994**, at age **26**.[^1]
- His assassination is often discussed in the context of the wider climate of violence during the **Algerian Civil War**, where public cultural figures could become targets.[^1]

## Legacy (High Level)

- Frequently remembered as an emblematic voice of 1990s Algerian popular music, particularly for songs centered on **love, longing, and everyday emotional life**.
- Common epithets in coverage describe him as a major or defining artist of "sentimental" raï.[^1]
