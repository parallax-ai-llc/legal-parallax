## Background

- Enrique Tarrio (born 1984) is a U.S. political activist who became nationally known as a leader of the Proud Boys.
- He has been associated in public reporting with organizing activity around far-right street protests in the late 2010s and early 2020s.

## Role and Public Profile

- Served as chairman of the Proud Boys in the period leading up to the January 6, 2021 attack on the U.S. Capitol (per widely cited reporting and court records).
- Often appeared in media coverage related to the group’s rallies, messaging, and internal organization.

## Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- In connection with January 6, Tarrio was charged in federal court and later convicted on counts including seditious conspiracy (as reported in major outlets and summarized by Wikipedia).
- Although he was not physically at the Capitol on January 6, prosecutors argued he played a coordinating role in plans and communications relevant to the group’s actions.
- Sentencing and custody details have been reported publicly; exact terms can vary by source and update over time.

## Why This Matters

- The seditious conspiracy convictions involving Proud Boys leadership were treated as landmark outcomes in the broader federal January 6 prosecutions.
- The case is frequently cited in discussions about accountability for political violence and organized extremist groups.
