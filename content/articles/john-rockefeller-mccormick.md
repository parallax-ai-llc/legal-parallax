## Disambiguation / identity note

The name **"John Rockefeller McCormick"** is easy to misread as a 19th‑century Rockefeller or a direct extension of **Cyrus Hall McCormick**'s biography. However, the Wikipedia page linked above is associated with **Edith Rockefeller McCormick** (a daughter of Standard Oil co‑founder **John D. Rockefeller**) and describes **John Rockefeller McCormick (1897–1901)** as her child with **Harold Fowler McCormick** (son of Cyrus H. McCormick). In that account, the child died young of **scarlet fever**.

## High-level context

- **Rockefeller side:** The middle name "Rockefeller" in the child’s name reflects the Rockefeller family line through Edith Rockefeller.
- **McCormick side:** The McCormick family prominence comes through Harold Fowler McCormick, whose father was Cyrus H. McCormick (mechanical reaper; McCormick Harvesting Machine Company).
- **Chicago / Gilded Age elite networks:** The Rockefeller and McCormick families were both major American industrial fortunes; the marriage of Edith Rockefeller and Harold F. McCormick connected two well-known dynastic families.
