## Summary

Militant warlord responsible for murders and terrorizing the Weather Coast of Guadalcanal during ethnic tensions.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1998 | Reportedly involved in a raid on a police armory amid rising ethnic tensions on Guadalcanal (per secondary reporting). |
| 1999–2000 | Became associated with militant activity linked to the Guadalcanal Revolutionary Army (GRA) during the broader Solomon Islands "Tensions" period. |
| Late 2000 | Refused to sign a peace deal that some other militias accepted; withdrew into remote areas of the Weather Coast to avoid capture. |
| 2003 | Surrendered to an Australian-led multinational peacekeeping intervention; later prosecuted. |
| 2005 | Convicted of murder (including the killing of Augustine Geve) and sentenced to life imprisonment. |

## Background and Context

- **Conflict setting:** Keke is most commonly discussed in relation to the Solomon Islands civil unrest known as the "Tensions" (late 1990s–early 2000s), a period of violent conflict involving Guadalcanal- and Malaita-linked militants.
- **Geography:** The **Weather Coast of Guadalcanal** is frequently cited as the area where his group operated and where state authority was weakest.

## Role and Allegations

High-level allegations associated with Keke in reporting include:

- Leading or being a prominent figure in armed militia activity connected to the **Guadalcanal Revolutionary Army (GRA)**.
- Involvement in serious crimes during the conflict period, with accounts alleging **kidnapping, arson, assassination, and murder**.
- Attribution (in some sources) of responsibility for a large number of killings; specific counts vary by source and should be treated as claims unless tied to court findings.

## Surrender, Trial, and Sentence

- **Surrender (2003):** Sources describe Keke surrendering after the arrival of an Australian-led intervention force (often discussed under RAMSI-related coverage).
- **Conviction (2005):** He was convicted for murder and received a **life sentence**, according to major reporting and reference summaries.
