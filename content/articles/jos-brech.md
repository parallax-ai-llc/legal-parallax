## Context and Background

- Jos Brech is a Dutch national whose name became widely known in the Netherlands due to the long-running, high-profile investigation into the 1998 killing of Nicky Verstappen.
- The case remained unsolved for many years and was periodically revisited by investigators, with renewed attention driven by advances in forensic methods and case review work.

## Crime and Investigation (High-Level)

- The victim, Nicky Verstappen, was an 11-year-old boy who disappeared during a summer camp in 1998; his body was later found.
- Dutch police treated the incident as a homicide and pursued leads over an extended period.
- Over time, the investigation involved collecting and re-evaluating physical evidence and comparing it to suspects through forensic databases.

## Identification and Arrest

- Brech was identified as a suspect years after the crime, in connection with forensic evidence reported in public case summaries.
- He was arrested roughly two decades after the killing, reflecting the cold-case nature of the investigation.

## Trial, Conviction, and Sentence

- In 2020, a Dutch court convicted Brech for the 1998 murder of Nicky Verstappen.
- Public reporting around the case centers on the unusually long gap between the crime and conviction, and the role of forensic evidence in establishing responsibility.
