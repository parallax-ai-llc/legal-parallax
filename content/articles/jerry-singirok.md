## Expanded Context

### Background

- Jerry Singirok is best known for his role as a senior officer in the Papua New Guinea Defence Force (PNGDF).
- He rose to national prominence during a period of political and security pressure linked to the Bougainville conflict.

### The Sandline Affair (1997)

- The “Sandline affair” refers to the PNG government’s contract with Sandline International, a private military company, to support operations connected to Bougainville.
- Singirok opposed the deployment/contract and took steps that triggered a major civil-military crisis.
- The episode is widely treated as a turning point in PNG civil-military relations, and it drew international media attention because it involved mercenaries and high-level political decisions.

### Significance and Aftermath

- The crisis contributed to the cancellation or unraveling of the Sandline plan and led to leadership and accountability disputes within the PNGDF and the political system.
- In historical summaries, Singirok is often framed as a central figure in the confrontation between the military leadership and elected government authority during 1997.
