## Summary

Duplicate consolidated.

Joaquín Archivaldo Guzmán Loera (often known by the nickname “El Chapo”) is a Mexican drug trafficker who became one of the most prominent leaders associated with the Sinaloa Cartel. He is widely reported to have been involved in large-scale drug trafficking operations and became a high-profile target of Mexican and U.S. law enforcement.

At a high level, accounts commonly emphasize:
- Leadership within the Sinaloa Cartel and influence over transnational trafficking routes
- Multiple arrests and high-security incarcerations
- Notable prison escapes that drew international attention
- Extradition to the United States and subsequent prosecution

## Career Timeline

## Overview (High-Level)

Guzmán Loera’s public profile is largely tied to allegations and prosecutions related to:
- Drug trafficking and coordination of logistics across borders
- Corruption and violence attributed to cartel activity (commonly discussed in reporting)
- Ongoing law-enforcement efforts focused on dismantling large trafficking networks

## Legal Proceedings and Incarceration

Public reporting commonly summarizes his later legal history as:
- A period of detention in Mexico following recapture
- Extradition to the United States as part of bilateral law-enforcement cooperation
- A U.S. federal trial that resulted in conviction and a life sentence

## Historical Significance

In many summaries, his significance is framed in terms of:
- The scale and reach of cartel-linked trafficking networks during his period of activity
- The symbolic impact of repeated prison escapes on public perceptions of state capacity
- The internationalization of anti-trafficking enforcement and prosecutions
