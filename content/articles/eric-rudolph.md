## Summary
Eric Robert Rudolph carried out the 1996 Centennial Olympic Park bombing in Atlanta and bombings of abortion clinics. He evaded capture for five years in Appalachian wilderness. Sentenced to life.

## Background
- **Early life and work**: Public reporting describes Rudolph as having held a variety of jobs (including work connected to outdoor or manual labor) and as having spent extended periods in rural areas, which later contributed to his ability to evade capture.[^2]
- **Ideological framing**: In writings attributed to him (notably a manifesto issued while a fugitive), Rudolph framed the bombings as opposition to abortion and to what he described as government and cultural “legitimization” of abortion and homosexuality.[^3]

## Attacks (high-level)
- **1996 Olympic Park bombing**: A nail bomb exploded in a crowded public area during the Olympics, causing deaths and injuries and prompting major changes in event security practices.
- **Clinic and nightclub bombings (1997–1998)**: The later attacks targeted abortion providers and an LGBTQ venue, escalating the campaign from mass-casualty intimidation to explicitly ideologically targeted violence.

## Manhunt and Capture
- Rudolph became one of the most wanted fugitives in the United States, with intensive searches over multiple years.
- He survived largely off the land, using remote terrain and local knowledge to avoid detection until his arrest in North Carolina in 2003.[^2]

## Prosecution and Sentence
- Rudolph ultimately entered guilty pleas that resolved federal cases without a full trial.
- He was sentenced to life imprisonment without the possibility of parole (multiple life terms) and has remained incarcerated.[^4]

## Impact and Legacy
- **Security and emergency response**: The Olympic Park bombing is frequently cited in discussions of major-event security, perimeter control, and rapid incident response.
- **Domestic terrorism context**: The case is commonly referenced in analyses of ideologically motivated violence in the United States, especially violence connected to anti-abortion extremism.
