Nazi doctor known as 'Dr. Death' who experimented on concentration camp prisoners and fled to Egypt.

## Background

- Heim was an Austrian physician who served in the Waffen-SS during World War II.[^1]
- He is most commonly associated with the Mauthausen concentration camp, where survivors alleged he conducted lethal medical procedures and abuse.[^1]

## Alleged Crimes (High-Level)

- Testimonies and investigations described killings by injection and other forms of medical torture attributed to Heim at Mauthausen.[^1]
- Because many details rely on witness accounts and postwar investigative files, specifics are often reported at a summary level in public sources.[^1]

## Postwar Evasion

- After the war, Heim lived for years in West Germany before disappearing as investigations intensified.[^1]
- Reporting and later investigations linked his later life to Egypt, where he reportedly lived under an alias.[^1]

## Investigation and Death Claims

- Heim became one of Europe's most-wanted Nazi fugitives; multiple agencies and journalists pursued leads over decades.[^1]
- Public reporting later stated he died in Cairo in 1992; the claim was debated and investigated due to the lack of early definitive proof.[^1]

## Why He Matters

- The Heim case is frequently cited as an example of the long tail of Nazi-era accountability and the difficulties of postwar prosecution across borders.[^1]
- It also illustrates how rumors, incomplete records, and delayed documentation can shape fugitive narratives for decades.[^1]
