## Key Contributions (High-level)

- **CP violation framework in particle physics:** With Toshihide Maskawa, extended the quark-mixing (CKM) picture to accommodate CP violation by requiring **three generations** of quarks.
- **KobayashiMaskawa (KM) mechanism:** The idea that a complex phase in the quark-mixing matrix can generate CP-violating effects observed in weak interactions.
- **Impact on experiments:** The KM mechanism underpins the interpretation of measurements in kaon and B-meson systems that test CP violation within the Standard Model.

## Context and Significance

CP violation is one of the essential ingredients discussed in explaining why the observable universe contains more matter than antimatter, though the amount of CP violation in the Standard Model is generally considered insufficient to explain the full baryon asymmetry on its own.
