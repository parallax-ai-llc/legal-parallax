## Summary

Swedish Foreign Minister Anna Lindh was stabbed while shopping at a Stockholm department store, dying the next day. Her attacker, Mijailo Mijailovic, was convicted of murder.

## Background

- **Anna Lindh** was Sweden’s Foreign Minister and a prominent figure in Swedish politics.
- The attack occurred during a period when Sweden was debating major European policy questions, making the crime a national shock.

## The Attack (2003)

- Lindh was **stabbed in public** while shopping at a **Stockholm department store**.
- She was taken to hospital but **died the following day** from her injuries.
- The incident prompted immediate security reviews around high-profile Swedish officials.

## Investigation and Arrest

- Swedish police launched a large-scale investigation, using witness accounts and forensic evidence.
- **Mijailo Mijailovic** was identified as the attacker and arrested shortly after the killing.

## Trial and Legal Outcome

- Mijailovic was **convicted of murder**.
- Subsequent proceedings addressed questions of responsibility and custody, reflecting Sweden’s legal handling of severe violent crime.

## Aftermath and Significance

- Lindh’s death led to **public mourning** and a major national debate about public safety.
- The assassination is often cited in discussions of:
  - security for elected officials,
  - political violence in modern Scandinavia,
  - the vulnerability of public figures in everyday settings.
