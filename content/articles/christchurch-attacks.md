## Summary

Australian white supremacist killed 51 people at two Christchurch mosques while livestreaming. Led to New Zealand banning most semi-automatic weapons within weeks.

## Key Facts

- **Date**: 15 March 2019
- **Location**: Christchurch, New Zealand (Al Noor Mosque and Linwood Islamic Centre)
- **Fatalities**: 51
- **Injured**: 40+
- **Perpetrator**: Single gunman; attack included a livestream and a published manifesto.

## What Happened

- The attacker targeted congregants during Friday prayers at two mosques.
- The first attack occurred at Al Noor Mosque, followed by an attack at Linwood Islamic Centre.
- A livestream circulated online rapidly, raising urgent questions about platform responsibility.

## Immediate Response

- New Zealand authorities launched a major investigation and rapidly detained the suspect.
- Prime Minister Jacinda Ardern’s public response emphasized solidarity with Muslim communities.
- Vigils and memorial events were held nationwide, including at Hagley Park.

## Policy and Legal Aftermath

- New Zealand moved quickly to restrict access to most semi-automatic firearms and high-capacity magazines.
- A national firearms buyback and amnesty program was implemented.
- In 2020, the perpetrator was sentenced to **life imprisonment without parole** in New Zealand.

## Broader Impact

- The attacks intensified global debate over online extremism, live-streaming, and rapid content takedowns.
- New Zealand and France launched the **Christchurch Call** initiative to reduce terrorist and extremist content online.
- The event is frequently cited in discussions of far-right radicalization and transnational extremist networks.
