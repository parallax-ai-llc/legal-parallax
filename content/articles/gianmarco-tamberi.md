## Summary

Gianmarco Tamberi shared 2021 Olympic gold with Barshim in a famous sportsmanship moment.

## Highlights

- Italian high jumper with national records of **2.39 m (outdoor)** and **2.38 m (indoor)**.
- Olympic champion in the men’s high jump at **Tokyo 2020 (held in 2021)**.
- World champion at **Budapest 2023** and European champion at **Rome 2024**.
- Multiple-time **Diamond League** high jump champion (notably 2021, 2022, 2024).

## Background

- Born in **Civitanova Marche, Italy** (1 June 1992).
- Coached for many years by his father **Marco Tamberi** (until 2022), then worked with **Giulio Ciotti**.

## Signature competition ritual

At major championships, Tamberi is known for a distinctive ritual: he keeps a full beard during qualification and shaves **half** for the final—turning it into a personal marker of “making the final” and a recognizable part of his public image.

## Notes on the 2021 shared-gold moment

The Tokyo final ended with Tamberi and Mutaz Essa Barshim tied after both cleared **2.37 m** with identical records. Rather than deciding the title via a jump-off, they agreed to **share the gold**, creating one of the most widely cited modern examples of sportsmanship in Olympic track and field.

## Selected personal bests

- Outdoor high jump: **2.39 m** (Italian national record)
- Indoor high jump: **2.38 m** (Italian national record)
