## Summary

Iranian government executed an estimated 5,000-30,000 political prisoners in a secret mass execution ordered by Ayatollah Khomeini. Primarily targeted MEK members and leftists.

| Year | Event |

## Background

- After the 1979 Iranian Revolution, the Islamic Republic imprisoned large numbers of political opponents.
- Key prisoner groups included members and supporters of the People\'s Mujahedin Organization of Iran (MEK/PMOI) and various leftist organizations.
- The Iran\-Iraq War (1980\-1988) and internal security concerns shaped the government\'s approach to dissent.

## What Happened (High\-Level)

- In mid\-1988, a secret process inside the prison system led to large\-scale executions of political prisoners.
- Decisions were reportedly based on prisoners\' perceived political affiliations, beliefs, and willingness to recant.
- The executions were carried out quickly and with limited or no formal due\-process safeguards.

## Decision\-Making and Institutions

- Accounts commonly describe a directive attributed to Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini that initiated the process.
- Investigations and testimony refer to ad\-hoc committees that reviewed prisoners\' cases; these bodies are often called \"death commissions\" in secondary sources.
- The judiciary, prison authorities, and security institutions were implicated in coordination and implementation.

## Scale and Uncertainty

- Estimates vary widely; commonly cited ranges include several thousand to tens of thousands.
- Variation reflects secrecy, incomplete records, and politicized reporting by different actors.
- Many victims\' identities were compiled later by families, NGOs, and human rights researchers.

## Secrecy, Burials, and Documentation

- Families often reported receiving delayed notice or no official explanation.
- Numerous reports describe burials in unmarked or mass graves and restrictions on mourning.
- Documentation has relied on survivor testimonies, leaked materials, and later public statements by officials.

## Domestic and International Response

- Human rights organizations have repeatedly called for independent investigations and accountability.
- The events remain a sensitive topic in Iranian politics and diaspora memory.
- International attention has increased in waves, including through reporting, advocacy campaigns, and legal proceedings in some jurisdictions.

## Why It Matters

- The 1988 executions are frequently cited as one of the largest episodes of political mass killing in the Islamic Republic\'s history.
- They continue to influence debates about transitional justice, record\-keeping, and the treatment of political prisoners.
- The episode is also referenced in discussions of command responsibility and state secrecy.

## Related Terms and Entities

- **MEK/PMOI**: An Iranian opposition organization that fought the Islamic Republic and became a primary target in many accounts.
- **Political prisoners**: Individuals imprisoned for opposition activity, affiliation, or speech; definitions vary by source.
- **Mass grave**: Burial sites alleged to hold victims; locations and verification differ across reports.
