## Background and Origins

- **Context:** The GIA emerged during Algeria’s civil conflict in the 1990s (often called the **Black Decade**), following the cancellation of elections and a broader confrontation between the state and Islamist insurgents.
- **Formation:** It developed as an armed Islamist faction distinct from, and sometimes in competition with, other insurgent groups (notably the **Islamic Salvation Army (AIS)**, the armed wing associated with the FIS).

## Ideology and Stated Objectives

- The organization promoted a **hardline Islamist insurgency** and framed its campaign as armed struggle against the Algerian state.
- Over time, it became widely associated with **mass-casualty violence** and attacks that included **civilian targets**, which contributed to its notoriety and isolation.

## Notable Violence (High Level)

- The GIA is strongly linked in historical reporting to multiple **massacres** in the mid-to-late 1990s, including events around **1997**.
- It is frequently cited in connection with severe violence during the civil war period, including **village massacres** and urban attacks.

## Legacy and Historical Notes

- The GIA is often discussed as a symbol of the **extreme brutality** associated with parts of the Algerian civil war.
- Its trajectory is also used to illustrate how insurgent movements can **fracture** and lose support when tactics shift toward broad civilian victimization.
