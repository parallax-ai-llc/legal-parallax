## Background

- Eduard Douwes Dekker was a Dutch writer and former colonial civil servant.
- He is best known by his pen name **Multatuli**.
- His experience working in the Dutch East Indies (notably Java) shaped his later critique of colonial administration and abuses.[^1]

## Key Work: *Max Havelaar*

- Full title is commonly rendered as *Max Havelaar, or the Coffee Auctions of the Dutch Trading Company*.[^1]
- Published in **1860**, the novel is framed as a critique of how colonial extraction and local governance failures harmed Javanese people.
- The book became one of the most influential Dutch-language works associated with debates on colonial policy and ethics.[^1]

## Themes (High-Level)

- Bureaucratic incentive failures and the gap between official policy and on-the-ground outcomes
- Moral responsibility of administrators and citizens in distant systems of power
- Economic extraction (e.g., plantation/coffee systems) and its human costs
- The use of literature as a vehicle for public accountability and political pressure

## Legacy

- Often cited as an early example of a public, literary challenge to colonial governance by an insider-turned-critic.
- His work has remained a reference point in Dutch discussions about colonial history, administrative ethics, and the social consequences of imperial policy.[^1]
