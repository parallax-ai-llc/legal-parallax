## Summary

Brazilian rubber tapper and environmental activist assassinated by ranchers opposing his campaign to preserve the Amazon rainforest. His death made him a global symbol of environmental activism.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1988 | Mendes assassinated |

## Context

- **Person**: Francisco Alves Mendes Filho ("Chico Mendes"), a Brazilian rubber tapper, union leader, and environmental activist.
- **Cause**: He opposed the expansion of cattle ranching and deforestation in Acre, advocating for forest-based livelihoods and the creation of extractive reserves.
- **Setting**: Conflict in the Brazilian Amazon (especially Acre) between traditional forest communities and landowners/ranchers during the late 20th century.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- Mendes was **shot and killed in December 1988** outside/near his home in Xapuri, Acre.
- The killing is widely described as an **assassination tied to land and environmental conflict**, with opponents angered by his organizing and conservation efforts.

## Why It Mattered

- His death drew **international attention** to:
  - Violence against environmental and labor activists.
  - Deforestation pressures in the Amazon.
  - The politics of land tenure and rural power in Brazil.
- Mendes became a **global symbol** of environmental defense and forest-community rights.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The case helped accelerate broader discussion and policy focus on **extractive reserves** and conservation strategies that incorporate local livelihoods.
- Mendes’s name is frequently invoked by environmental organizations and human-rights advocates as an emblematic example of the risks faced by activists.

## Key Terms

- **Rubber tapper (seringueiro)**: Worker who harvests latex from rubber trees, traditionally living in and depending on forest ecosystems.
- **Extractive reserve**: A protected area model designed to allow sustainable resource use by local communities while conserving the forest.
