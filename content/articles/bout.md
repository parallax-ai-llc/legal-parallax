## Summary

Duplicate consolidated.

This article refers to **Viktor Bout**, a Russian national widely described as an arms dealer who became internationally known due to his alleged role in trafficking weapons in the post–Cold War era and his later prosecution in the United States.

High-level notes (per publicly available reporting and Wikipedia):
- Bout operated companies involved in air cargo and logistics, which prosecutors alleged were used to move weapons.
- He was arrested in a U.S. sting operation abroad, tried in U.S. federal court, and convicted on conspiracy charges.
- In 2022, he was released from U.S. custody in a high-profile prisoner exchange.

## Why He Became Notable

Bout became widely known in international media and policy circles for a combination of factors:
- The scale of alleged arms transactions attributed to his network.
- The geographic breadth of alleged activity across multiple regions.
- The intersection of aviation logistics, end-user ambiguity, and conflict-zone demand.
- A U.S. prosecution that drew attention to undercover operations and jurisdictional reach.

## Legal Case (Overview)

This section is a non-technical summary intended for general readers.

- **Arrest / extradition:** Bout was arrested in Thailand in 2008 and later extradited to the United States.
- **Charges (broadly):** Conspiracy-related charges tied to allegations of selling weapons intended for use against U.S. personnel.
- **Outcome:** Convicted in U.S. federal court and sentenced to a long prison term.
- **Contestation:** Bout has denied wrongdoing in various public accounts; narratives differ depending on the source.

## 2022 Release

- In 2022, Bout returned to Russia as part of a prisoner exchange between the United States and Russia.
- The exchange drew significant international attention and commentary about diplomatic tradeoffs and prisoner-swap precedent.
