## Summary

Richard Matt and David Sweat escaped from Clinton Correctional Facility using power tools smuggled in by a prison employee they had seduced. Triggered a massive 3-week manhunt.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2015 | Two inmates escaped using smuggled power tools |

## Key People

- **Richard Matt** — one of the two escapees.
- **David Sweat** — the second escapee.
- **Joyce "Tilly" Mitchell** — prison employee implicated in helping the escape (reported to have smuggled tools and assisted planning).

## Background (High Level)

- The escape occurred at **Clinton Correctional Facility** in **Dannemora, New York**.
- Reporting widely emphasized a mix of **inside assistance**, **contraband tools**, and **institutional security gaps**.

## How the Escape Was Reported to Work

- Tools (including power tools) were reportedly **smuggled to the inmates**.
- The inmates are commonly reported to have used tools to **cut through barriers** and navigate **service spaces** to exit.
- The case became a reference point for how **small, repeated security failures** can compound into a major breach.

## Manhunt and Outcome

- The escape triggered a **large-scale multi-agency search** lasting roughly **three weeks**.
- Both men were eventually located; the incident ended with **one escapee killed** and **the other captured alive** (as commonly summarized in news and encyclopedia accounts).

## Aftermath / Impact

- Raised scrutiny on:
  - **Contraband prevention** and tool control inside facilities
  - **Staff vetting and supervision** (including vulnerabilities to manipulation)
  - **Maintenance / infrastructure access paths** that can be exploited
- The case is frequently cited in discussions of **corrections security**, especially around **insider-risk**.

## Notes

- Popular coverage sometimes frames the episode as both a security failure and a cautionary example of **human factors** driving operational risk.
