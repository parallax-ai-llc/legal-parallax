## Summary

Match-fixing scandal in Italian football that saw Juventus stripped of two Serie A titles and relegated. Involved wiretaps revealing systematic referee manipulation by multiple clubs.

## Background

- "Calciopoli" refers to a set of investigations into the governance of Italian football (primarily Serie A) that came to a head in 2006.
- The case emerged from wiretap evidence collected by Italian prosecutors during wider criminal investigations, which was later used in sporting proceedings.
- The core allegation was that club officials sought to influence referee assignments and match oversight rather than bribing players on the pitch.

## How the scheme worked (high-level)

- Phone calls and messages described preferred referees, "unwanted" referees, and lobbying over appointments.
- Influence was alleged to operate through relationships with referee designators and football administrators.
- Because referee appointments are centralized, control over assignments can systematically skew competitive outcomes across a season.

## Major clubs and figures (commonly cited)

- Juventus were the central focus of the sporting sanctions, with executives such as Luciano Moggi and Antonio Giraudo frequently referenced in reporting and proceedings.
- Other top-flight clubs (including AC Milan, Fiorentina, Lazio, and Reggina) were also implicated and received sporting penalties of varying severity.

## Sporting and legal outcomes

- Juventus were stripped of two Serie A titles (2004–05 and 2005–06) and relegated to Serie B with a points deduction for the following season.
- Several other clubs started subsequent seasons with points deductions or other sporting sanctions.
- Individual officials faced bans from football-related activities.

## Impact on Italian football

- The scandal reshaped perceptions of competitive integrity in Serie A and prompted reforms around referee governance and oversight.
- Clubs affected by relegation and points deductions faced immediate financial and sporting consequences (broadcast revenue, sponsorship, and player retention).
- Calciopoli remains a frequent reference point in later debates about transparency, conflicts of interest, and institutional accountability in sport.

## Related concepts

- Match-fixing: direct manipulation of match outcomes (often via players or referees) for competitive or betting-related gain.
- Governance corruption: institutional capture or influence over appointments, oversight, or disciplinary processes.
