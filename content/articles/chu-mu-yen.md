## Summary

Chen Wei-ling (Chu Mu-yen) represented Taiwan in taekwondo. In 2004, Chen Shih-hsin won Taiwan's first Olympic gold in women's taekwondo.

## Expanded Overview

### Olympic Highlights

- **Athens 2004**: Won **gold** in men’s taekwondo **under 58 kg** (flyweight/finweight class naming varies by era and source).
- **Beijing 2008**: Won **bronze** in men’s taekwondo **under 58 kg**.
- Widely described as **Taiwan/Chinese Taipei’s first male Olympic gold medalist**, and among the earliest Olympic gold medalists for the delegation in the modern era.

### Major International Results (high level)

- **World Taekwondo Championships**: Reported gold medalist (notably **2003**).
- Competed across multiple major events (World Championships, Asian Games, Universiade), reflecting a sustained international career rather than a single breakthrough year.

### Clarification / Naming Notes

- The Summary section above contains an unrelated sentence about **Chen Shih-hsin** (who is a different Taiwanese taekwondo athlete). This article’s primary subject is **Chu Mu-yen (朱木炎)**.
- English-language sources typically render the name as **Chu Mu-yen**; Chinese romanization may also appear as **Zhu Mu-yan** (pinyin).
