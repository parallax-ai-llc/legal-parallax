## Summary
Salvador Ramos killed 19 children and 2 teachers at Robb Elementary School in Uvalde, Texas on May 24, 2022. Police response was widely criticized for delayed intervention. He was killed by a tactical team.

## Background (High-Level)
- Ramos lived in Uvalde, Texas and attended Uvalde Consolidated Independent School District schools.
- Public reporting described a history of social isolation and online activity that drew attention after the attack.
- In the period leading up to the shooting, he legally purchased firearms shortly after turning 18, consistent with Texas law at the time.

## Robb Elementary School Shooting (2022-05-24)
- Location: Robb Elementary School, Uvalde, Texas.
- Victims: 19 children and 2 teachers were killed; additional students and adults were injured.
- The attack ended when law enforcement officers entered the classroom area and shot and killed Ramos.

## Law Enforcement Response and Criticism
- The response became a major national story due to the length of time between initial police arrival and the final entry into the classroom.
- Officials faced scrutiny over on-scene command decisions, communication, and adherence to active-shooter training principles.
- Multiple agencies were present; later reviews examined coordination challenges across local, school-district, state, and federal responders.

## Investigations, Reports, and Oversight
- The incident prompted reviews by Texas state authorities, the U.S. Department of Justice, and legislative committees.
- Investigations focused on timelines, dispatch and radio traffic, incident command structure, and resource deployment.
- Findings were used to recommend changes to training, communications, and emergency response protocols.

## Community and Policy Aftermath
- The Uvalde community and victims' families pursued accountability through public advocacy and legal action.
- The shooting intensified public debate in the United States over firearm regulation, school security, and policing standards.
- Public memorials and vigils were held; the event remains a focal point in discussions about preventing school shootings.
