## Background

- Dennis Allen was an Australian figure associated with Melbourne's illegal drug trade.
- He was part of the wider "Allen" criminal milieu that became prominent in Melbourne in the late 20th century.[^1]
- Media and police commentary commonly referred to him by the nickname "Mr Death", reflecting the extent of suspicion around his alleged involvement in violent crime.[^1]

## Notability (High-Level)

- Frequently described as a "drug lord" in accounts of Melbourne's underworld history.[^1]
- Often cited in summaries of Melbourne "gangland" activity because he was suspected in multiple killings, though definitive attribution varies by source.[^1]
- His death ended active investigations and speculation about several unsolved cases linked to the Allen circle.[^1]

## Allegations and Suspected Crimes

- Public sources state that Allen was suspected of involvement in numerous murders; the exact number is disputed and should be treated as an allegation rather than a proven count.[^1]
- He has been linked in secondary reporting to a broader period of violent disputes among criminals in Melbourne.
