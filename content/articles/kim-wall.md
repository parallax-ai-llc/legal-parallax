## Summary

Swedish journalist murdered and dismembered by inventor Peter Madsen aboard his submarine.

## Background

- Kim Isabel Fredrika Wall (1987–2017) was a Swedish freelance journalist.
- She reported internationally and was known for long-form features and profiles.

## Work and Reporting

- Worked as a freelancer with bylines in multiple international outlets (see references).
- Covered topics that often required on-the-ground reporting across countries.

## Murder and Investigation (High-level)

- In August 2017, she boarded inventor Peter Madsen's privately built submarine **UC3 Nautilus** in Denmark for a planned interview.
- She was reported missing after the submarine failed to return as expected.
- Danish authorities conducted a large search and investigation that became widely publicised.

## Trial and Sentencing

- Madsen was prosecuted in Denmark and convicted in 2018.
- He received a **life sentence** under Danish law.

## Public Impact and Legacy

- The case prompted extensive media coverage and public discussion about safety, violence, and accountability.
- It has been the subject of books, articles, and screen adaptations.
- A Danish TV dramatisation, **The Investigation** (2020), depicted the police investigation.
