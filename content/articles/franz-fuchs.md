## Additional Context

### Overview

Franz Fuchs (1949–2000) was an Austrian domestic terrorist associated with a series of letter- and pipe-bomb attacks in the early-to-mid 1990s. His targets included people and institutions connected to immigration and minority communities. The attacks were widely understood as motivated by far-right, xenophobic ideology.

### Notable Attacks (high level)

- **Oberwart (1995):** A pipe-bomb attack connected to an anti-Roma crime that killed several people and injured others. This incident became one of the most notorious attacks attributed to Fuchs and is often cited as a turning point in public awareness of right-wing violence in Austria.
- **Letter-bomb campaign:** Over multiple incidents, explosive devices were mailed or planted with the intent to harm recipients; collectively, the campaign resulted in **4 deaths** and **15 injuries**.

### Investigation and Attribution

- Investigators linked multiple devices and communications to the same perpetrator through forensic similarities (construction methods, materials, and patterns in target selection).
- The attacks were frequently discussed in the context of broader European debates in the 1990s around immigration, xenophobia, and far-right extremism.

### Arrest and Trial (summary)

- Fuchs was arrested in the late 1990s after investigative leads converged on him as the primary suspect.
- He was prosecuted and convicted in Austria for terrorist bombings connected to the campaign.

### Prison and Death

- Fuchs died in prison in **2000**.
- Contemporary reporting and later summaries note that his death occurred while serving his sentence; details are commonly covered in the Wikipedia account and related sources.
