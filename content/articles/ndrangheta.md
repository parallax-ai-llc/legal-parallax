## Summary
The 'Ndrangheta is a powerful Italian mafia from Calabria. It has surpassed the Sicilian Mafia as Europe's most powerful criminal organization, controlling 80% of Europe's cocaine trade. It is organized in autonomous family clans called 'ndrine.

## Structure and Organization
- **Core unit**: the **'ndrina** (clan/family group), typically rooted in a specific town or territory in Calabria.
- **Local grouping**: multiple clans can form a **locale**, coordinating control over an area while keeping substantial autonomy.
- **Regional coordination**: sources describe coordinating bodies (often summarized as **"Crimine"** or **"Provincia"**) that arbitrate disputes and manage relationships between locales.
- **Rituals and codes**: like other Italian mafias, membership is associated with initiation rituals, secrecy norms, and internal dispute-resolution mechanisms.

## Activities and Revenue Streams
- **Drug trafficking**: widely reported involvement in wholesale cocaine trafficking, leveraging ports and logistics networks.
- **Extortion and protection rackets**: control of local businesses through intimidation and "protection" payments.
- **Public procurement and construction**: infiltration of contracts via front companies, bid-rigging, and corrupt intermediaries.
- **Money laundering**: use of cash-intensive businesses, real estate, and cross-border financial channels to obscure illicit proceeds.

## Geographic Reach
- **Italy**: strongholds in Calabria with influence reported in northern Italian regions through migration, business ties, and investment.
- **International**: law enforcement and academic reporting commonly cites presence or partnerships in **Germany**, **Switzerland**, **Canada**, **Australia**, and parts of **Latin America** tied to trafficking supply chains.

## Why It’s Hard to Disrupt
- **Decentralization**: autonomous clans reduce single-point failure; removing one group does not necessarily collapse the network.
- **Family-based recruitment**: kinship ties can increase cohesion and reduce informant risk compared to purely transactional criminal groups.
- **Dual strategy**: combining violence/intimidation with long-term financial integration into legitimate markets.
