## Expanded Context

### Why he is notable

- Jignesh Shah is widely associated with the National Spot Exchange Limited (NSEL) payment crisis in India.
- The episode is commonly described in public reporting as a large-scale settlement default that affected many investors/participants.

### NSEL payment crisis (high-level)

- NSEL operated a spot exchange marketplace structure under India’s commodity/spot-trading ecosystem.
- In 2013, the exchange halted trading and later faced a major payment/settlement shortfall reported in Indian media.
- Regulators and investigating agencies examined whether contracts, warehouse stocks, and collateral representations matched underlying commodities.

### Legal and regulatory proceedings (overview)

- Shah was arrested in connection with investigations tied to the NSEL matter (as reflected in widely cited public sources).
- Multiple Indian agencies and courts have been involved over time, including actions related to asset attachment and recovery efforts.
- Public timelines often include repeated court hearings, bail-related decisions, and ongoing proceedings rather than a single conclusive judgment.

### Entities commonly mentioned alongside the case

- **NSEL** (National Spot Exchange Limited)
- **FTIL** (Financial Technologies (India) Ltd., historically associated with Shah)
- Indian regulators and law enforcement bodies referenced in reporting (e.g., economic offenses investigations)

### Impact

- The crisis became a prominent example in India of market-structure and oversight risk in exchange-like platforms.
- It triggered extended recovery efforts and policy debate around the regulation of spot exchanges and similar trading venues.
