## Historical context: what “highwayman” means

- A **highwayman** was a robber who stole from travelers, typically **on horseback** (as opposed to a "footpad" who robbed on foot).
- In some periods, mounted highwaymen were considered (rightly or wrongly) **socially "above"** footpads in popular imagination, which contributed to later romanticized portrayals.
- The term has related regional usage:
  - In the **19th-century American West**, similar criminals were sometimes called **"road agents"**.
  - In **Australia**, the comparable term is **"bushranger"**.

## Era and decline (general)

- In Britain, highwaymen were especially prevalent in the period from the **Restoration (1660)** through the early **18th century**.
- Over time, a mix of improved transportation systems, more professional policing, and changing economic conditions contributed to the **decline of highway robbery** as a distinct, organized practice.
