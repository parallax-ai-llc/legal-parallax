## Summary

Eritrean soldiers massacred up to 800 civilians in the ancient holy city of Axum during the Tigray War. Victims included people who had sought sanctuary in a church.

## Background

- The Axum Massacre occurred during the Tigray War, a conflict that began in November 2020 in Ethiopia's Tigray region.
- Axum (also spelled Aksum) is a historic city and a major religious site in Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity, which contributed to heightened local sensitivity around attacks on civilians and religious spaces.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- Reports describe Eritrean forces entering Axum and carrying out killings of civilians over a period of days around late November 2020.
- Some victims were reported to have sought refuge in or near religious sites, including churches.
- Accounts in public reporting describe door-to-door killings and shootings of people in streets and homes, though details vary by witness testimony and investigation.

## Casualty Estimates and Reporting

- Publicly cited death toll estimates vary; some reporting and advocacy documentation has described fatalities in the hundreds.
- The "up to 800" figure is commonly repeated in secondary sources, but precise verification is difficult in active-conflict settings.
- Independent confirmation is complicated by access constraints, security risks for witnesses, and disruption of communications during the conflict.

## Alleged Perpetrators

- The incident is widely attributed in public sources to Eritrean troops operating in Ethiopia during the early phase of the war.
- Attribution in conflict incidents typically relies on a mix of witness accounts, patterns of control in the area, and investigative reporting.

## Aftermath and Significance

- The massacre became one of the most cited early atrocities of the Tigray War and contributed to international concern about civilian protection.
- Allegations of mass killings and abuses in Tigray were later referenced in broader discussions about accountability, humanitarian access, and potential war crimes investigations.

## Notes

- Naming conventions vary: "Axum" and "Aksum" refer to the same city in different transliteration standards.
- This article summarizes commonly reported high-level facts; detailed casualty accounting and legal findings may evolve as investigations and archival work progress.
