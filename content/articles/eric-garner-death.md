## Summary

NYPD officer Daniel Pantaleo used a prohibited chokehold on Eric Garner during arrest for selling loose cigarettes. Garner's dying words 'I can't breathe' became a protest rallying cry.

## Career Timeline

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Context:** Garner was approached by officers during an encounter tied to alleged sale of untaxed/“loose” cigarettes.
- **Use of force:** Video recorded Pantaleo’s arm around Garner’s neck area as officers took him to the ground.
- **Final moments:** Garner repeatedly said **“I can’t breathe”**, words that later became central in public discourse about policing.

## Immediate Aftermath

- **Public attention:** The incident received intense media coverage after the bystander video circulated widely.
- **Medical examiner finding:** The death was classified as a homicide by NYC’s medical examiner (per widely reported accounts), shaping subsequent legal and administrative processes.

## Investigations and Proceedings (Overview)

- **Local criminal process:** A grand jury did not return an indictment in 2014.
- **Federal review:** DOJ reviewed the case for potential civil-rights charges and later closed the matter without charges.
- **Administrative discipline:** Separately from criminal court, NYPD conducted an internal disciplinary process that resulted in termination.

## Public Impact and Significance

- The case became a prominent reference point in debates over **chokeholds/neck restraints**, **police accountability**, and **video evidence** in use-of-force incidents.
- Garner’s words were echoed in later protests and campaigns, contributing to broader scrutiny of policing practices in the United States.
