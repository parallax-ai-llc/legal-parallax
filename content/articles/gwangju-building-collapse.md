## Summary

A five-story building under demolition collapsed onto a city bus in Gwangju, killing 9 passengers. The demolition company had ignored safety regulations and proceeded recklessly.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The incident occurred during the demolition of a five-story structure in Gwangju, South Korea.
- The building collapsed outward into the adjacent roadway and struck a city bus.
- The collapse caused multiple fatalities and injuries among bus passengers.

## Contributing Factors (As Reported)

- **Demolition method and sequencing:** Demolition work was conducted in a way that left the structure unstable.
- **Safety controls:** Basic site safety controls and exclusion zones were reportedly inadequate.
- **Oversight and compliance:** The case was widely described as involving serious violations of demolition safety regulations.

## Response and Investigation

- Emergency responders conducted rescue and recovery operations at the scene.
- Authorities investigated demolition planning, supervision, and permit compliance.
- The demolition contractor and related parties were scrutinized for negligence and regulatory violations.

## Aftermath

- The collapse intensified public attention on demolition-site safety and enforcement.
- The incident became a prominent example cited in discussions about construction safety culture.
