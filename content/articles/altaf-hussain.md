## Background

- Altaf Hussain is a Pakistani political figure best known for founding and leading the Muttahida Qaumi Movement (MQM), a party that historically drew significant support from Urdu-speaking Muhajir communities, particularly in Karachi.
- He was born in Karachi (then in Pakistan) and became politically active through student organizing before entering formal party politics.

## Political Role (MQM)

- Hussain is widely described as the central, long-running leader of MQM and an influential powerbroker in Karachi’s municipal and provincial politics during periods when the party was electorally strong.
- Accounts commonly note MQM’s mixture of electoral politics and street-level organizational strength; critics and authorities have also alleged involvement of party elements in intimidation and violence, while supporters argue the party represented marginalized urban constituencies.

## Exile and Remote Leadership

- He has lived in the United Kingdom for decades and led the party for significant stretches from London, communicating via speeches and telephonic addresses that were rebroadcast to supporters in Pakistan.
- This “remote leadership” dynamic is frequently cited as a distinctive feature of MQM’s political organization.

## Legal and Controversy Overview

- Hussain has faced multiple legal allegations and political crackdowns over time in Pakistan, often connected to claims of incitement, violence, or coercion associated with Karachi’s political conflicts.
- Public reporting also notes legal scrutiny in the UK related to speeches and political activity; outcomes have varied by case and jurisdiction.
