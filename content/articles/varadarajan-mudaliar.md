## Summary
Varadarajan Mudaliar, known as 'Vardha Bhai,' was a Bombay underworld don who dominated the city's criminal activities in the 1960-80s. He was known as 'Robinhood of Dharavi' for his philanthropic activities.

## Activities and Influence
- **Base of influence**: Frequently associated with **Dharavi**, one of Mumbai's largest informal settlements, where local protection networks and political connections could be decisive.
- **Underworld role**: Commonly described as a **don/organized crime boss** operating in Bombay’s underworld ecosystem, which included extortion, protection rackets, and control over illicit markets.
- **Community standing**: His reputation includes a dual image—feared as a criminal leader while also remembered by some residents for providing informal help and dispute resolution.

## "Robin Hood of Dharavi" Narrative
Mudaliar was sometimes called the **"Robin Hood of Dharavi"** in popular accounts.

High-level reasons this label appears in reporting and local memory:
- He was reported to have provided **financial assistance** to some residents.
- He was said to have funded or supported **community needs** (e.g., emergency help, small donations).
- The label reflects how organized-crime figures can cultivate **local legitimacy** through patronage.

> Note: Descriptions of philanthropy vary across sources and are often intertwined with myth-making around underworld figures.

## Historical Context
Bombay’s underworld grew alongside:
- **Rapid urbanization** and informal housing expansion.
- **Labor and port-city economies**, where informal “enforcement” and protection sometimes emerged.
- **Political patronage networks**, which could blur lines between criminal groups and local power brokers.

Within this environment, leaders like Mudaliar were often able to persist for years by combining:
- Local social ties and patronage
- Intimidation and violence
- Strategic alliances and rivalries

## Legacy
- **Local memory**: Remembered in parts of Mumbai as a controversial figure—both a symbol of intimidation and a provider of informal welfare.
- **Media portrayal**: Indian popular media has long depicted Bombay underworld characters, and Mudaliar is among the real-life figures referenced in discussions of that era.
- **Public record limits**: Precise operational details are frequently uncertain in secondary summaries; many accounts rely on journalistic reporting and retrospective narratives.
