## Summary

German robber knight with an iron prosthetic hand who conducted feuds and highway robberies across Franconia.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1512 | Conducted robber knight raids across Franconia |

## Historical Context

- In the Holy Roman Empire, private feuding (Fehde) persisted into the 16th century as nobles and knights pursued disputes through raids, sieges, and ransom.
- Berlichingen is commonly described as a **"robber knight"** (Raubritter): a minor noble who used force to extract payments and settle conflicts outside centralized state control.[^1]

## Notable Facts

- **Iron hand prosthesis:** He is famous for an iron prosthetic hand, an early and iconic example of functional prosthetics associated with a well-known historical figure.[^1]
- **Imperial conflict:** His career illustrates the growing tension between local knightly power and imperial/legal efforts to restrict private warfare.[^1]

## Expanded Career Notes

- Feuds attributed to him are typically described as a mix of personal disputes, opportunistic raids, and participation in broader regional conflicts.[^1]
- He was repeatedly drawn into legal and political pressure from imperial authorities and neighboring powers seeking to suppress raiding and enforce public order.[^1]

## Legacy

- Götz von Berlichingen became a literary and cultural symbol in German tradition, most notably through **Johann Wolfgang von Goethe's** drama *Götz von Berlichingen* (1773), which popularized his image as a defiant knight.[^1]
- The phrase commonly attributed to him (often quoted in modern discussions of the play) is part of the enduring popular memory around his persona.[^1]
