Nigerian serial killer who murdered and cannibalized victims under a bridge in Lagos.

## Additional Context

### Background

- Clifford Orji was a Nigerian man who became widely reported in local and international media after his 1999 arrest in Lagos.
- Reports generally place the alleged crimes around the Oworonshoki area, under or near a bridge, where human remains were discovered.

### Allegations and Investigation (as reported)

- Coverage and secondary sources commonly describe:
  - Multiple alleged victims, with some accounts focusing on young women or girls.
  - Evidence of dismemberment and alleged cannibalism.
  - The discovery of human body parts at the location where Orji was found or was said to be staying.
- Specific victim counts and detailed timelines vary across reporting; many summaries rely on a small number of secondary sources.

### Arrest and Detention

- Orji was arrested in 1999 in Lagos in connection with the discovery of human remains.
- He was held in custody for an extended period. Several accounts note that he spent years detained without a publicly well-documented trial outcome.
- He died in 2012 while in custody.

### Why the Case Is Notable

- The case is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - Urban crime and public safety concerns in late-1990s Lagos.
  - Sensational crime reporting and the difficulty of separating verified facts from rumor in high-profile cases.
  - The challenges of criminal justice processes and pre-trial detention in some contexts.

### Key Facts (high-level)

- **Location (reported):** Lagos, Nigeria (often linked to Oworonshoki)
- **Arrest:** 1999
- **Died:** 2012 (in custody)
