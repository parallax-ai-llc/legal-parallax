## Summary

David Gray killed 13 people in the small seaside town of Aramoana, New Zealand. The mass shooting led to tighter gun laws and was New Zealand's worst shooting until Christchurch.

## Background

- **Location:** Aramoana is a small seaside settlement near Dunedin, in New Zealand’s South Island.[^1]
- **Context:** The attack took place during the afternoon and evening of **13 November 1990**.[^1]

## Perpetrator

- **David Gray** was the gunman identified in the Aramoana Massacre.[^1]
- Accounts describe him as a local resident who carried out the shootings alone.[^1]

## Attack overview

- The shootings unfolded across the settlement over several hours, with victims targeted in and around residential buildings.[^1]
- The total death toll was **13 people killed**, with additional people injured.[^1]

## Police response

- New Zealand Police conducted a large manhunt and tactical response operation at Aramoana.[^1]
- The incident ended when David Gray was confronted and killed by police.[^1]

## Aftermath and impact

- The Aramoana Massacre was widely regarded as **New Zealand’s worst mass shooting** until the 2019 Christchurch mosque shootings.[^1]
- The event is commonly cited in discussions of **firearms regulation** and subsequent tightening of gun laws in New Zealand.[^1]

## Public memory

- The massacre remains a major reference point in New Zealand crime history and public safety debates.[^1]
