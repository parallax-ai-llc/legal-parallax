## Summary

Duplicate consolidated.

DB Cooper is the alias used by an unidentified man who hijacked Northwest Orient Airlines Flight 305 on November 24, 1971. After receiving $200,000 in cash and four parachutes in exchange for the passengers, he parachuted from a Boeing 727 over the Pacific Northwest and disappeared. Despite an extensive FBI investigation, Cooper’s identity and fate remain unknown.

## The 1971 Hijacking (Flight 305)

- **Date**: November 24, 1971 (the day before U.S. Thanksgiving)
- **Airline / flight**: Northwest Orient Airlines Flight 305
- **Aircraft**: Boeing 727 (notable for its rear airstair)
- **Route**: Portland (PDX) → Seattle (SEA)
- **Demand**: $200,000 and parachutes
- **Outcome**: Passengers released; suspect escaped by parachute during the flight

## Investigation and Evidence

- The FBI opened a major case (later known as **NORJAK**).
- Physical evidence associated with the hijacking has been limited; most of what is known comes from witness statements, recovered items, and investigative records.
- In 1980, a portion of the ransom money (some of the $20 bills) was found buried near the Columbia River by a child, providing one of the few concrete post-hijacking discoveries.

## Theories and Legacy

- Numerous suspects were proposed over decades, but none were conclusively linked to the hijacking.
- The case became a cultural touchstone in U.S. true-crime history and is often cited as the only unsolved U.S. commercial airline hijacking.
