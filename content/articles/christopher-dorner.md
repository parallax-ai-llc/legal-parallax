## Summary
Christopher Dorner was a former LAPD officer who went on a killing spree in 2013 targeting police officers and their families, killing four. He published a manifesto alleging corruption. He died in a cabin standoff.

## Background
- Dorner was born in New York and later moved to Southern California.
- He served in the U.S. Navy Reserve and deployed to Bahrain (2006–2007).
- He joined the Los Angeles Police Department (LAPD) in the mid-2000s.

## Manifesto (2013)
- Dorner posted a lengthy online manifesto shortly before the attacks.
- The document included grievances against the LAPD and named individuals he blamed.
- He alleged retaliation and misconduct within the department and framed the violence as “revenge.”
- Media coverage widely circulated excerpts; authorities treated the manifesto as a threat indicator.

## 2013 Shootings and Manhunt
- The first known attacks occurred in early February 2013 in Southern California.
- Targets included law-enforcement personnel and relatives of police officers.
- A multi-agency manhunt followed, involving local police departments, county sheriffs, and federal support.
- The search produced numerous public sightings and false alarms and led to heightened community fear.
- Several incidents during the search raised scrutiny of law-enforcement tactics (e.g., mistaken identity stops).

## Death
- Dorner was located in a remote cabin near Big Bear Lake, California.
- A standoff with law enforcement ended with the cabin burning down.
- Dorner was later found dead; authorities reported he died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound.

## Public and Institutional Aftermath
- The case prompted renewed debate over officer discipline, whistleblowing channels, and accountability.
- It also exposed how quickly manifestos and threats can propagate online and shape operational responses.
- LAPD leadership publicly rejected Dorner’s allegations and emphasized internal review mechanisms.

## Key Facts (High-Level)
- Known for: 2013 shootings and ensuing manhunt.
- Motive as stated: grievance-driven retaliation against perceived institutional wrongdoing.
- Impact: multiple deaths, significant policing resources deployed, and intense media attention.
