Led the 1983 military coup in Grenada that murdered PM Maurice Bishop and triggered the US invasion.

## Background

- Hudson Austin was a senior military figure within the People’s Revolutionary Government (PRG) period in Grenada (1979–1983).
- He is primarily known for his role in the October 1983 internal power struggle that culminated in the killing of Prime Minister Maurice Bishop and other officials.

## Role in the 1983 Crisis

- In October 1983, a split within the ruling New Jewel Movement escalated into a confrontation over Bishop’s leadership and political control.
- Bishop was detained and later freed by supporters, after which he and several others were taken to Fort Rupert (now Fort George).
- Bishop and multiple associates were executed during the violence surrounding the coup.
- Austin emerged as the head of a new military authority (often described as a Revolutionary Military Council) immediately after Bishop’s death.

## Aftermath

- The coup and killings were a central trigger for the United States-led invasion of Grenada in late October 1983.
- After the invasion and collapse of the junta, Austin was arrested and later convicted in connection with the 1983 killings in Grenada.
- He received a prison sentence and remained incarcerated for many years before his release.

## Notes on Legacy

- In most historical accounts, Austin is remembered less for a long public career and more for the decisive, short-lived seizure of power in 1983.
- The events of October 1983 remain one of the most consequential episodes in modern Grenadian political history, reshaping the island’s governance and international relationships.
