## Overview

Jean-Claude Duvalier—known as "Baby Doc"—was the second ruler of the Duvalier dynasty in Haiti. He inherited an authoritarian state built by his father and governed during years marked by political repression, corruption, and worsening socioeconomic conditions. His fall in 1986 ended nearly three decades of Duvalier family rule.

## Background

- Born in Haiti in 1951, Duvalier rose to power as a young man after the death of his father, François Duvalier ("Papa Doc").
- The Duvalier regime cultivated a personality cult and relied on fear, patronage, and coercive institutions to maintain control.

## Rule and Governance

- **Authoritarian continuity:** Although his early rule was framed as a generational transition, the state retained heavy-handed tools of political control.
- **Patronage and corruption:** The government was widely associated with corruption and elite enrichment, contributing to public anger.
- **Economic stress:** Haiti faced chronic poverty and economic vulnerability; discontent grew as living conditions deteriorated.

## Human Rights and Political Repression

- The Duvalier era is commonly linked with abuses against political opponents, including intimidation, arbitrary detention, and violence.
- Civil liberties were restricted, and opposition movements were often suppressed.

## Deposition and Exile

- Large-scale protests and instability in the mid-1980s culminated in Duvalier’s removal in 1986.
- He left Haiti for France, where he lived for many years following his flight from power.

## Return to Haiti (2011)

- Duvalier returned to Haiti in 2011, an event that drew significant international and domestic attention.
- His return re-opened debates about accountability, historical memory, and transitional justice in Haiti.

## Legacy

- Duvalier remains a polarizing figure: associated with dictatorship and repression, while also representing a pivotal period in modern Haitian political history.
- The end of his rule is often treated as a major turning point, but Haiti continued to face political instability and governance challenges in the decades that followed.
