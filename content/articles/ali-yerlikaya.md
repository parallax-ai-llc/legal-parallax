## Background

- Ali Yerlikaya (born 1968) is a Turkish civil servant and politician who served in senior provincial administration roles before entering the cabinet.
- He is widely covered in Turkish and international media primarily through his roles as a governor and later as Minister of the Interior.

## Notable Roles (selected)

- **Provincial Governor (Vali):** Served as a governor in multiple provinces (a key appointed role responsible for local administration and security coordination).
- **Governor of Istanbul:** Held the governorship of Istanbul prior to joining the cabinet, a high-profile post due to the city’s political and economic significance.
- **Minister of the Interior (2023– ):** Leads the Interior Ministry, which oversees domestic security, policing, migration-related administration, and coordination with provincial authorities.

## As Interior Minister (high-level)

- **Public security operations:** Frequently announces policing and security operations and associated statistics via official briefings and social channels.
- **Migration and border-related administration:** The ministry plays a central role in migration enforcement and the administration of residency/registration processes.
- **Disaster and emergency coordination:** Works with provincial authorities and relevant agencies during major incidents and emergencies.

## Public Criticism and Controversy (overview)

- Yerlikaya’s tenure has attracted criticism from opposition figures and rights advocates who argue that enforcement actions and investigations have had political effects, including pressure on opposition-linked municipalities, activists, or media.
- Supporters characterize the ministry’s actions as routine law-enforcement and public-order measures.
- Specific allegations and evaluations vary by source; readers should consult multiple outlets and primary statements for context.
