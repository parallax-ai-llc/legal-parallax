## Summary

Police fired tear gas into a football stadium in Malang, East Java, triggering a stampede that killed 135 people. One of the deadliest stadium disasters in football history.

## Context

- **Location**: Kanjuruhan Stadium, Malang (East Java), Indonesia.
- **Event type**: Post-match crowd crush following violence and policing actions.
- **Why it mattered globally**: The death toll placed it among the deadliest football-related stadium disasters.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- After the match, a large number of spectators moved onto the pitch.
- Police responded with crowd-control measures, including the use of **tear gas** inside or toward the stadium.
- Tear gas exposure, panic, and compressed exits contributed to a **crowd surge and crush** in corridors and gate areas.

## Casualties and Immediate Impact

- Reported fatalities: **135**.
- Many victims died from **asphyxiation and traumatic injuries** consistent with crowd-crush dynamics.
- A high number of injuries were reported in addition to deaths.

## Institutional and Public Response

- The incident triggered nationwide and international scrutiny of:
  - policing tactics at sporting events,
  - stadium safety and exit capacity,
  - match-day crowd management.
- Football authorities and government bodies announced reviews and corrective actions in the aftermath.

## Investigations and Accountability (Overview)

- Authorities investigated the sequence of events, including:
  - the decision to deploy tear gas,
  - stewarding and security readiness,
  - gate operations and evacuation routes.
- Safety compliance and operational planning at the venue became part of the public discussion.

## Safety Lessons Commonly Cited

- **Avoid escalation in enclosed venues**: Riot-control agents can amplify panic when used in dense, constrained spaces.
- **Egress design matters**: Bottlenecks at gates and corridors can turn routine movement into a lethal crush.
- **Clear command structure**: Conflicting instructions during emergencies worsens crowd behavior.
- **Medical readiness**: Rapid triage and transport capacity is critical in mass-casualty crowd incidents.
