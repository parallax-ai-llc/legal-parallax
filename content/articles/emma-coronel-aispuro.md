## Background

- Coronel Aispuro is a Mexican national who became widely known through her marriage to Joaquín "El Chapo" Guzmán, the former leader of the Sinaloa Cartel.
- Media reports often describe her as a former beauty pageant contestant; her public profile increased significantly after Guzmán's captures, escape, and extradition-related coverage.

## Allegations and U.S. Legal Case (High Level)

- U.S. prosecutors alleged that Coronel participated in or facilitated:
  - relaying messages for Guzmán while he was incarcerated,
  - assisting with logistics tied to Guzmán's communications and movements,
  - involvement connected to the period surrounding his escape and subsequent recapture.
- In 2021, she pleaded guilty in U.S. federal court to charges tied to knowingly and intentionally helping the cartel's activities (as summarized in public reporting and official announcements).

## Significance

- The case drew attention because it linked a high-profile spouse of a cartel leader to alleged operational support, illustrating how family members and close associates can be implicated in organized-crime investigations.
- It also became a widely reported example of U.S. prosecution strategy targeting support networks around transnational criminal organizations.
