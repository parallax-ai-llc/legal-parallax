## Summary

Spanish colonizer who massacred 800 people at Acoma Pueblo and cut off the feet of survivors.

## Background

- Born into a prominent family in New Spain (colonial Mexico), with ties to Spanish colonial administration.
- Became involved in northern frontier expansion efforts and secured royal authorization to lead a colonizing expedition.

## New Mexico Expedition (High-Level)

- Led Spanish settlers north to establish a colony in what is now New Mexico (late 1590s).
- Served as the colony’s first governor and oversaw early Spanish settlement and mission efforts.

## Acoma Massacre (1599)

- After conflict at Acoma Pueblo, Oñate’s forces assaulted the community.
- Survivors were subjected to severe punishments; accounts describe amputations and long-term enslavement.
- The episode is widely cited as a major atrocity in the early colonial history of the region.

## Later Career and Accountability

- Faced investigation by Spanish colonial authorities for excessive cruelty and misrule.
- Was tried and convicted in colonial proceedings and received penalties that included exile and removal from office (later modified in some accounts).

## Legacy

- Remembered primarily for brutal violence against Indigenous communities.
- Has been the subject of modern controversy, including disputes over public monuments and commemorations.
