## Summary
Veronica Guerin was an Irish journalist murdered by drug lord John Gilligan's gang in 1996. Her death led to sweeping changes in Irish law and the creation of the Criminal Assets Bureau. A film was made about her life.

## Background
- Guerin was an Irish investigative journalist whose work focused on the rise of drug trafficking and associated violence in Ireland during the 1990s.
- She was widely associated with reporting that named or described key figures in Dublin’s criminal milieu, bringing sustained public attention to organized crime.

## Reporting Focus
- **Organized crime and narcotics**: profiles of individuals and groups involved in drug distribution and related racketeering.
- **Impact on communities**: coverage that emphasized the social costs of addiction, intimidation, and corruption.
- **Accountability**: reporting that increased pressure on state institutions to pursue financial disruption of criminal enterprises.

## Murder (1996)
- Guerin was murdered on 26 June 1996.
- The killing shocked Ireland and was treated as a major attack on press freedom and public order.
- Subsequent investigations and prosecutions connected the crime to criminal networks involved in drug trafficking.

## Aftermath and Institutional Response
- Her death is often cited as a catalyst for a more aggressive state strategy against organized crime, especially **targeting criminal assets**.
- **Criminal Assets Bureau (CAB)**: created to use civil and criminal powers to identify and seize proceeds of crime.
- **Proceeds-of-crime framework**: strengthened legal tools aimed at confiscating assets linked to serious criminal activity.

## Legacy
- Guerin remains a prominent symbol of investigative journalism in Ireland.
- Her case is frequently referenced in discussions of:
  - journalist safety and intimidation,
  - the public interest role of investigative reporting,
  - asset-based strategies for dismantling organized crime.
- Her life and work have been portrayed in film and other media.
