## Summary

Kyrgyz crime lord and parliamentarian who controlled organized crime in southern Kyrgyzstan before his assassination.

## Context and Background

- According to Wikipedia, Bayaman Erkinbayev (Баяман Эркинбаев) was a prominent Kyrgyzstani businessman and lawmaker associated with southern Kyrgyzstan, especially the Osh region.
- He is frequently described as operating at the intersection of politics, business, and sport, and was also widely rumored to have connections to the criminal world (allegations rather than court findings).

## Early Life and Sports

- Wikipedia reports that he started out working at a tobacco factory in his hometown of Osh.
- He became well-known as a wrestler and was associated with Alysh (a Kyrgyz style of wrestling).
- He was reported to have led Kyrgyzstan’s National Olympic Committee.

## Political and Business Activity

- Wikipedia describes him as one of the richest businessmen in Kyrgyzstan and as a parliamentary deputy for roughly a decade.
- He reportedly owned “Kara Suu,” described as Central Asia’s largest bazaar.
- He was reported to have funded Kyrgyzstan’s Socialist Party and at one point announced a presidential candidacy for the July 2005 elections, before his party supported Felix Kulov.

## Role in the 2005 Upheaval (Reported)

- Wikipedia describes Erkinbayev as a key figure behind the protests and unrest in southern Kyrgyzstan that contributed to the March 24, 2005 overthrow of President Askar Akayev.
- The article reports that groups connected to him (including martial arts trainees) moved across Jalal-Abad, Osh, and Batken, seizing government sites and blocking highways, with protests later reaching Bishkek.
- Accounts of these events vary across sources; the summary here follows the high-level description in the linked reference.

## Violence, Legal Exposure, and Assassination

- Wikipedia reports an assassination attempt on April 28, 2005, in which he was shot at from a car and survived.
- The same source reports that prosecutors sought to strip him of parliamentary immunity, but parliament refused those attempts.
- He was fatally shot in September 2005 after arriving home by car; Wikipedia states he died in hospital at age 38. (Note: the frontmatter date may differ by one day across sources.)

## Why He Matters (High-Level)

- Erkinbayev is often cited in discussions of:
  - how wealth and patronage networks can shape politics in post-Soviet states,
  - the blurred boundary between legitimate business influence and coercive/illegal power,
  - the role of sports organizations and martial-arts networks in social mobilization.
