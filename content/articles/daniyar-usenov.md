## Expanded Notes

### Overview

- Kyrgyz banker and politician who served as **Prime Minister of Kyrgyzstan** from **October 2009 to April 2010**.[^1]
- Previously held senior roles including **Mayor of Bishkek**.[^1]
- His premiership ended during the **2010 Kyrgyzstan uprising**, when opposition forces seized control in the capital and his resignation was publicly announced.[^1]

### Early life and education (high-level)

- Born in **Frunze** (now Bishkek) in the Kyrgyz SSR (Soviet Union).[^1]
- Graduated from the **Frunze Polytechnic Institute** (engineering background).[^1]
- Worked as a **mining engineer** before entering politics.[^1]

### Selected roles and affiliations (as reported)

- Deputy Prime Minister for Economic Affairs (appointed under President Kurmanbek Bakiyev).[^1]
- Chairman of **Ineximbank** (Kyrgyzstan) in the late 2000s, associated with major construction/real-estate projects.[^1]
- Mayor of Bishkek (appointed in 2007).[^1]

### 2010 uprising and departure from office

- During the April 2010 upheaval, opposition leaders stated that Prime Minister Usenov had resigned after negotiations, following reports that President Bakiyev left the capital.[^1]
- Subsequent reporting describes him as having **fled** Kyrgyzstan around the events of 2010; later legal actions against him were reported as conducted **in absentia** in Kyrgyz media coverage.[^1]

### Later public sightings and reported identity changes

- In **August 2020**, photographs circulating publicly showed Belarusian President **Alexander Lukashenko** with Usenov in **Minsk**, prompting a diplomatic protest by Kyrgyzstan’s Foreign Ministry to the Belarusian embassy in Bishkek.[^1]
- Some reporting around the same episode claimed he had used the name **Daniil Timuravich Uritskiy**.[^1]
