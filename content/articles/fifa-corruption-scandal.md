## Summary

FBI investigation revealed systemic corruption at FIFA involving $200 million in bribes for World Cup hosting rights and media deals. Multiple senior officials were arrested.

## Background

- FIFA is the global governing body for association football, responsible for organizing major international tournaments (including the men’s World Cup).
- By the early 2010s, longstanding allegations about improper payments and conflicts of interest around commercial rights and tournament decisions had accumulated across multiple confederations.

## What the scandal centered on

High-level reporting and charging documents broadly described patterns such as:

- **Bribery and kickbacks** tied to marketing and media rights for regional competitions.
- **Improper influence** and alleged payments connected to decision-making processes.
- **Racketeering-style coordination** across networks of sports officials and intermediaries.

## Investigation and legal actions (high-level)

- The U.S. Department of Justice and the FBI pursued a multi-year investigation that culminated in high-profile arrests in 2015.
- Cases involved alleged violations including wire fraud, money laundering, and racketeering-related offenses.
- The investigation prompted resignations, suspensions, and internal disciplinary actions within football governance bodies.

## Institutional response and impact

- The scandal accelerated public and sponsor pressure for governance reform at FIFA.
- FIFA pursued changes to ethics and compliance programs, including greater scrutiny of commercial agreements and decision-making procedures.
- The events became a reference point in broader debates about transparency, accountability, and oversight in international sports administration.
