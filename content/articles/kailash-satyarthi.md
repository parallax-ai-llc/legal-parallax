## Key Focus Areas

- **Child labour and trafficking**: Advocacy and campaigns aimed at ending the worst forms of child labour and improving enforcement.
- **Right to education**: Public arguments and coalition-building around universal access to schooling for children.
- **Children’s rights framing**: Treating child labour as a human-rights issue rather than only an economic or social-welfare issue.

## Organizations and Initiatives (selected)

- **Bachpan Bachao Andolan (BBA)**: A movement focused on the elimination of child labour and protection of children’s rights in India.[^1]
- **Global March Against Child Labour**: An international network and campaign that helped popularize cross-border advocacy for legal reforms and enforcement.[^1]
- **Global Campaign for Education**: A civil-society coalition associated with the push for education as a universal right.[^1]
- **Kailash Satyarthi Children’s Foundation**: A platform associated with awareness, advocacy, and mobilization around child protection.[^1]

## Nobel Peace Prize (2014)

Satyarthi received the **2014 Nobel Peace Prize**, jointly with Malala Yousafzai, cited for their struggle against the suppression of children and young people and for the right of all children to education.[^2]

## Legacy (high-level)

- Helped keep **child exploitation** on the global human-rights agenda through sustained activism and coalition-building.
- Contributed to **international public pressure** for stronger child-protection norms and enforcement.
