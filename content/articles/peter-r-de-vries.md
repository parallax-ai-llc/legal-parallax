## Summary
Peter R. de Vries was a Dutch investigative journalist murdered in Amsterdam in 2021. He was shot days after serving as a legal adviser in the trial of drug lord Ridouan Taghi. His death shocked the Netherlands.

## Background
- Peter Rudolf de Vries (often styled “Peter R. de Vries”) was known in the Netherlands for long-running television and investigative work focused on violent crime, missing persons, and organized crime.
- He became a prominent public figure by combining reporting with advocacy for victims’ families and witnesses.

## Notable Work (high level)
- **Investigative journalism & TV**: De Vries worked across print and broadcast, gaining wide recognition through crime-focused programs.
- **Victim and witness support**: He was associated with high-profile cases where public attention, media pressure, and protection of witnesses were central issues.
- **Organized crime reporting**: His coverage often intersected with major Dutch efforts against drug trafficking networks.

## 2021 Attack and Death
- De Vries was shot in Amsterdam in July 2021 and died days later from his injuries.
- The killing was widely treated as an attack on press freedom and the rule of law, given his public role and the broader climate around organized crime cases.

## Public and Institutional Response
- Widespread public mourning and condemnation followed in the Netherlands and internationally.
- The case contributed to renewed attention on:
  - safety for journalists,
  - protection for witnesses and legal teams,
  - the reach and intimidation tactics of organized crime networks.
