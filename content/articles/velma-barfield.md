## Summary
Velma Barfield was the first woman executed in the US since 1962. She poisoned six people including her mother and fiancé with rat poison. Executed by lethal injection in North Carolina.

## Background
- Barfield grew up in the southeastern United States and later lived in North Carolina.
- Later reporting described a pattern of unstable relationships and financial stressors.
- In court filings and media accounts, she was described as having problems with prescription drug use.

## Crimes and Victims (reported)
- Barfield was convicted of murdering her boyfriend, Stuart Taylor, by poisoning.
- After her conviction, she confessed to additional poisonings, including family members and people she worked for as a caregiver.
- Most accounts describe her using arsenic-based poison (commonly marketed as rat poison) administered in food or drinks.

## Investigation and Trial
- Suspicion increased after multiple deaths around Barfield were followed by insurance or financial benefits.
- Investigators exhumed at least one earlier victim and reported evidence consistent with poisoning.
- Her case became a widely covered example of domestic and caregiver-related homicide by poisoning.

## Execution and Legacy
- Barfield was executed by lethal injection in North Carolina in 1984.
- She was noted in U.S. historical records as the first woman executed since 1962.
- The case is frequently cited in discussions of serial poisoning, forensic toxicology, and the challenges of detecting arsenic-related deaths.

## Key Takeaways
- Primary method: covert poisoning over time, rather than direct physical attacks.
- Detection: depended heavily on toxicology testing and exhumation in reopened investigations.
- Historical significance: execution marked a major milestone in the modern era of capital punishment for women in the U.S.
