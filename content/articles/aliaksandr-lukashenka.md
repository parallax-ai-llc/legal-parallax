## Additional Context

### Background and Rise

- Aliaksandr Lukashenka has been the dominant political figure in Belarus since the mid-1990s.
- He won the presidency in 1994 and has remained in power through repeated elections widely criticized by international observers.
- Over time, constitutional and legal changes consolidated executive control and weakened checks and balances.

### Governance and Domestic Politics

- Lukashenka’s rule is commonly described as authoritarian, with strong control over state institutions, security services, and national media.
- Independent political opposition and civil society groups have faced restrictions, including arrests, harassment, and limits on organizing.
- Large-scale protests after disputed elections (notably in 2020) were met with an extensive security crackdown.

### Foreign Policy and Regional Role

- Belarus under Lukashenka has maintained a close strategic relationship with Russia, including participation in integration frameworks and security coordination.
- Relations with the European Union and the United States have frequently deteriorated due to human-rights concerns and election-related disputes.
- During the Russia–Ukraine war period, Belarus’s territory has been used for Russian military staging and logistics, increasing Belarus’s international isolation.

### Major Controversies

- 2020 election crisis: the presidential election result was rejected by much of the opposition and drew widespread protests.
- 2021 Ryanair incident: Belarusian authorities forced the diversion of Ryanair Flight 4978 to Minsk, leading to the arrest of journalist Raman Pratasevich; the incident triggered broad condemnation and additional sanctions.
- 2021 border pressure: Belarus was accused by EU governments of facilitating irregular migration toward EU borders as a form of political pressure.

### Sanctions and International Response

- Multiple governments and international bodies have imposed sanctions on Belarusian officials and entities linked to repression and high-profile incidents.
- These measures have targeted travel, assets, and sectors of the Belarusian economy, contributing to deeper dependence on Russia.
