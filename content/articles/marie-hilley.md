## Summary
Audrey Marie Hilley was an Alabama woman who poisoned her husband with arsenic and attempted to poison her daughter. She lived under a false identity for years before recapture.

## Background
- **Full name**: Audrey Marie Hilley (often reported as "Marie" in case coverage).
- **Location**: Associated primarily with Alabama (including Anniston), United States.
- **Notability**: Known for a high-profile **arsenic poisoning** case involving close family members and for later living under an alias.

## Known Crimes (High-Level)
- **Spousal poisoning**: Her husband, Frank Hilley, became ill and later died; the death was later connected to arsenic poisoning in subsequent investigations.
- **Attempted poisoning**: Authorities reported an attempt to poison her daughter, with symptoms consistent with arsenic exposure.

## Modus Operandi
- **Method**: Poison administered over time, leading to symptoms that can resemble natural illness.
- **Concealment**: Exploited the difficulty of diagnosing poisoning without targeted testing and the trust inherent in family relationships.

## Investigation and Capture
- **Re-examination of death**: Frank Hilley’s case was revisited after later events raised suspicion.
- **Alias period**: She evaded authorities for years while using a false identity, a key element that kept the case in the public eye.
- **Recapture**: Eventually identified and taken into custody, bringing the long-running investigation to a close.

## Additional Timeline Notes
- **1980s**: The poisoning allegations and investigation are commonly situated in this decade in public reporting.
- **Post-arrest**: The case became a frequently cited example of domestic poisoning and the investigative challenges involved.

## Legacy and Significance
- Often referenced in discussions of:
  - **Forensic toxicology** and why arsenic cases can be missed without specific screening.
  - **Identity fraud/alias living** in the context of fugitives.
  - The risks and investigative complexity of **intrafamilial crime**.
