## Summary

Duplicate — see Howard Hughes.

This entry exists because **Howard Hughes Medical Institute (HHMI)** is a separate legal organization created by Howard Hughes and frequently referenced in biographies and institutional histories. The institute is best known as a major **philanthropic funder of biomedical research** and science education.

## Overview

- **Founded** by Howard Hughes (the entrepreneur and aviator) as a medical research organization.
- Operates as a **nonprofit research institute and grant-making organization**, supporting investigators and research campuses.
- Commonly described as one of the **largest private biomedical research organizations** in the United States.[^1]

## What HHMI Does (high-level)

- Funds and employs **HHMI Investigators** working at partner universities and research institutions.
- Runs internal research efforts and campuses (including dedicated research facilities).
- Supports science education and training programs designed to strengthen research ecosystems.

## Relationship to Howard Hughes (why it shows up in biographies)

- Hughes established HHMI and initially used it within his broader corporate structure.
- After Hughes’s death, the institute’s assets and governance became central to major legal and financial developments tied to the Hughes estate and related companies.
