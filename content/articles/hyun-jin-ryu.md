## Career Highlights (High-Level)

- Long-time left-handed starting pitcher known for command, sequencing, and changing speeds.
- One of the most internationally recognized Korean pitchers of his era, with major-league success and awards consideration.
- Represented South Korea in international competition (e.g., World Baseball Classic and Olympic play) during his prime years.

## Pitching Profile

- **Handedness**: Left-handed pitcher.
- **Approach**: Works edges of the strike zone, prioritizing weak contact and strike-throwing over pure velocity.
- **Common pitch mix (varies by season)**: Fastball, changeup, breaking ball(s) such as a cutter/slider and curveball.

## MLB & KBO Context

- **KBO impact**: Entered the league with immediate dominance as a teenager, becoming a marquee domestic star with the Hanwha Eagles.
- **MLB transition**: Joined the Dodgers during a period when successful KBO-to-MLB transitions were still relatively uncommon, helping normalize the pathway for later Korean pitchers.
- **Toronto years**: Signed as a rotation anchor; subsequent seasons were shaped heavily by health and workload management.
- **Return to Korea**: Late-career move back to the KBO is consistent with a broader pattern of Korean stars finishing at home.
