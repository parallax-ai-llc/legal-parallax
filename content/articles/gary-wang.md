## Summary

Co-founder of FTX who pleaded guilty to fraud and cooperated against Sam Bankman-Fried.

## Additional Context

### Background

- Born in China and moved to the United States as a child.
- Studied mathematics and computer science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).
- Worked at Google (Google Flights) on systems related to aggregating airline ticket prices.

### Role in Alameda Research and FTX

- Joined Alameda Research after leaving Google.
- Co-founded the cryptocurrency exchange FTX in 2019 with Sam Bankman-Fried.
- Served as FTX's chief technology officer (CTO) and was described in reporting as a relatively low-profile engineering figure.

### Criminal Case and Cooperation

- After FTX's collapse and bankruptcy, pleaded guilty in the U.S. to wire fraud and multiple conspiracy counts related to wire, securities, and commodities fraud.
- Entered into a cooperation agreement with prosecutors and testified in the criminal case against Sam Bankman-Fried.
