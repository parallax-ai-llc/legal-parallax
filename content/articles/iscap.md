## Overview

- **Common name**: Islamic State Central Africa Province (ISCAP)
- **Type**: Islamic State (IS) affiliate network label used for multiple insurgent theaters in Central Africa.
- **Primary theaters commonly referenced**:
  - **Eastern DR Congo** (often linked in reporting to the Allied Democratic Forces/ADF milieu).
  - **Northern Mozambique (Cabo Delgado)** (often linked in reporting to Ansar al-Sunna / Ahlu Sunnah wa Jama’a).

## Context and Structure (High-Level)

- ISCAP is typically described as a **regional branch designation** used by Islamic State media to claim operations in Central Africa.
- Reporting often treats “ISCAP” as an **umbrella brand** rather than a single, centrally commanded organization with uniform membership across countries.
- Affiliations and command relationships are discussed in open sources, but **details vary by source** and can change over time.

## Areas of Operation

- **DR Congo**: activity frequently reported in North Kivu and Ituri provinces.
- **Mozambique**: activity reported primarily in Cabo Delgado province, including attacks around coastal and inland districts.

## Tactics (General)

- Attacks on security forces and local authorities.
- Raids on towns/villages, ambushes on roads, and attacks on infrastructure.
- Use of propaganda and media claims consistent with Islamic State branding.

## Notes on Designations

- Some governments and analysts treat components of ISCAP as **terrorist entities**; naming can be inconsistent across jurisdictions.
- Open-source descriptions may refer to constituent groups by other names (e.g., ADF in DR Congo; insurgent factions in Cabo Delgado).
