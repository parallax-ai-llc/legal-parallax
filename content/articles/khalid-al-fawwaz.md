## Summary

Al-Qaeda operative convicted in US of conspiracy to kill Americans in the 1998 embassy bombings.

## Background and Activities

- Identified in U.S. filings and press reporting as an al-Qaeda operative connected to the conspiracy surrounding the 1998 U.S. embassy bombings in East Africa. [^1][^2]
- According to a U.S. Treasury designation notice, he relocated to London in the mid-1990s and was linked to al-Qaeda-associated activity and facilitation networks. [^3]

## Names and Listings

- Name variants/aliases cited in public sources include **Khalid Abd al-Rahman Hamd al-Fawwaz** and the kunya **Abu Omar al-Sebai**. [^1]
- Reported as listed by the UN (1267/1989/2253) sanctions regime and designated by the U.S. Treasury (OFAC) as a terrorism-related listing. [^1]

## Legal Proceedings (United States)

- **Indictment (1998):** Charged in a wide-ranging case concerning al-Qaeda and the 1998 embassy bombings. [^1]
- **Extradition/arraignment (2012):** Extradited from the United Kingdom to the United States and arraigned in federal court (Oct. 2012). [^1]
- **Trial/conviction (2014–2015):** Tried in the Southern District of New York; sentenced to **life imprisonment** in May 2015. [^2]
