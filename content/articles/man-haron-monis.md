## Summary
Man Haron Monis was an Iranian-born gunman who took 18 people hostage at the Lindt Chocolate Café in Sydney on December 15-16, 2014. Two hostages were killed. Monis was killed by police.

## Background
- Monis was born in Iran and later migrated to Australia, where he lived in Sydney.
- In the years prior to the 2014 hostage-taking, he was known to Australian authorities and had multiple legal matters in progress.

## Notable legal issues (pre-2014)
- Australian media and court reporting described ongoing cases involving serious allegations prior to the hostage crisis.
- Public discussion after the incident focused on bail decisions and how high-risk individuals are assessed and monitored.

## 2014 Sydney hostage crisis (high-level)
- Date: December 15–16, 2014
- Location: Lindt Chocolate Café, Martin Place, Sydney, Australia
- Hostages: 18 people were held during the siege.
- Outcome: Two hostages died; Monis was killed during the police response.

## Immediate aftermath and public impact
- The incident prompted national mourning and extensive media coverage in Australia.
- It triggered reviews of counterterrorism, threat assessment, and public-safety procedures, including how agencies share information.
- The event is often cited in discussions about lone-actor violence and the challenges of preventing rapidly unfolding attacks.

## Investigations and reviews
- Australia conducted formal inquiries and public reporting examining the sequence of events, police response, and systemic issues.
- Key themes included communications during the siege, emergency response coordination, and the adequacy of pre-incident risk controls.
