## Historical Context

- Critias was an Athenian aristocrat active during the late stages of the Peloponnesian War.
- After Athens' defeat by Sparta, Sparta supported an oligarchic regime in Athens.
- The oligarchy commonly called the "Thirty Tyrants" governed briefly but used extreme violence and property seizures.

## Role in the Thirty Tyrants

- Ancient and modern accounts often describe Critias as one of the most hardline figures among the Thirty.
- The regime used executions, exile, and confiscations to eliminate opponents and enrich supporters.
- The commonly cited figure of roughly 1,500 deaths refers to citizens killed during the oligarchy's short rule.

## High-Level Notes (What He Is Remembered For)

- Association with political terror: summary executions and intimidation as tools of governance.
- Factional leadership: pushing the oligarchic project further than some other members were willing to go.
- Polarizing legacy: treated in later Athenian memory as an emblem of oligarchic brutality.

## Related People and Institutions

- **Sparta**: the external power that backed the post-war oligarchic settlement in Athens.
- **The Thirty (Thirty Tyrants)**: oligarchic council that ruled Athens after the war.
- **Athenian democratic resistance**: opponents who ultimately displaced the oligarchic regime.
