## Additional Context (High-Level)

- Ahmad Motevaselian is widely described as an **Iranian Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) commander** whose fate became a long-running Iranian political and media issue.
- Many summaries characterize him as **missing/disappeared** rather than confirmed deceased; accounts differ and definitive public evidence is limited.

## Background

- Born in Tehran, he became involved in **revolutionary activism** prior to the 1979 Iranian Revolution (as reported in common biographical summaries).
- After the revolution, he joined the **IRGC**, which was rapidly expanding during the early 1980s.

## Iran–Iraq War (1980–1988)

- During the early years of the Iran–Iraq War, he is commonly reported to have served in **frontline and command roles** within IRGC units.
- Some sources associate him with the establishment or leadership of formations that later became part of Iran’s wartime order of battle.

## 1982 Lebanon Deployment and Disappearance

- In 1982, following Israel’s invasion of Lebanon, Motevaselian was reportedly sent to Lebanon in an **Iranian advisory/liaison capacity**.
- He is most often reported to have been **abducted at a checkpoint near Beirut** along with three other Iranian personnel.
- Attribution for the kidnapping is frequently made in public accounts to **Lebanese militias** active during the Lebanese Civil War period; narratives vary by source and political perspective.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- In Iran, the case became a recurring topic in **state media, commemorations, and diplomatic messaging**, often framed as an unresolved abduction.
- The uncertainty around his fate has contributed to his status as a symbolic figure in discussions about:
  - Iran’s early involvement in Lebanon
  - hostage-taking and prisoner exchanges during the Lebanese Civil War era
  - missing-in-action narratives and national memory
