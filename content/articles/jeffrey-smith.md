## Context

Jeffrey L. Smith was a Washington, D.C. Metropolitan Police Department (MPD) officer who assisted U.S. Capitol Police during the January 6, 2021 attack on the United States Capitol.[^2]

Because multiple people share the name "Jeffrey/Jeff Smith" on Wikipedia, some sources summarize his story on a dedicated page titled *Death of Jeffrey L. Smith*.[^2]

## Service

- **Department:** Metropolitan Police Department of the District of Columbia (MPD).[^2]
- **Service years:** Reported as **2009–2021** in public summaries.[^2]
- **Role during January 6:** Assisted Capitol Police as part of the broader law-enforcement response to the attack.[^2]

## Death and Aftermath

Public reporting summarized on Wikipedia states that Smith died by suicide on **January 15, 2021**, nine days after January 6.[^2]

Subsequent public discussions and administrative actions focused on whether the events of January 6 and any injuries sustained in the line of duty were causally related to his death, including petitions and determinations regarding line-of-duty benefits for surviving family members.[^2]

## Legal / Policy Notes (High-level)

Wikipedia's summary describes:

- A review process and later official recognition of the death as **line-of-duty** (as reported on the page).[^2]
- Related legislative and public-ceremony acknowledgements of January 6 responders that included Smith in broader commemorations.[^2]

## Related Cases
