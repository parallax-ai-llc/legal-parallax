## Background

- **FTX** was a major cryptocurrency exchange that entered bankruptcy in **November 2022**.
- The criminal case centered on allegations that customer and investor funds were misused through related entities, including **Alameda Research**.
- The proceedings became one of the most high-profile U.S. trials tied to the 2022 crypto market downturn.

## Key Participants

- **Defendant:** Sam Bankman-Fried (founder/ex-CEO of FTX).
- **Prosecution:** U.S. federal prosecutors (Southern District of New York).
- **Court:** U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York (Manhattan).
- **Witnesses (not exhaustive):** former FTX/Alameda executives and employees, customers/investors, and others involved in FTX’s operations.

## Core Issues in the Case (High Level)

- Whether customer deposits were diverted for trading, venture investments, political donations, or other expenditures.
- What internal controls and accounting practices existed (or failed) during FTX’s rapid growth.
- How representations to customers, lenders, and investors compared with internal realities.

## Trial Notes

- The trial took place in **October–November 2023**.
- The case involved extensive documentary evidence (internal communications, spreadsheets, and financial records) and testimony about FTX’s treasury and risk practices.
- Public attention focused on how a fast-growing financial platform could operate with limited governance and unclear separation of funds.

## Outcome and Aftermath

- In **2023**, a jury found Bankman-Fried **guilty** on multiple fraud-related counts.
- Related civil/regulatory actions and bankruptcy proceedings continued in parallel, including efforts to identify assets and pursue recoveries for customers.
- The FTX collapse and trial contributed to renewed debate around crypto exchange oversight, custody, and disclosure standards.
