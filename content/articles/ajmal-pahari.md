## Background and Context

- Ajmal Pahari (also reported as **Shahnawaz**, alias Ajmal Pahari) is described in sources as a *"target killer"* associated with Karachi's political violence in the 2000s.[^1]
- Reporting commonly links him to the **Muttahida Qaumi Movement (MQM)**, a major political party in urban Sindh, amid long-running conflict among political, sectarian, and criminal actors in Karachi.[^1]

## Reported Activities and Legal Status

- Wikipedia summarizes him as belonging to MQM and notes an **on-camera confession** in which he claimed involvement in a very large number of killings; these claims are widely cited but inherently contentious and should be treated as *allegations/claims*, not verified court findings.[^1]
- Wikipedia further notes that he was wanted in multiple murder cases tied to different police jurisdictions in Karachi (Korangi, Liaquatabad, and Bilal Colony) during **2004–2008**.[^1]
- Pakistani press reporting described him as being re-arrested in **January 2013** and characterized him as a "killer of 111 people" in the framing of that coverage.[^3]
- Additional reporting discusses his **transfer to Sukkur** and related administrative/legal notices (Express Tribune, 2014).[^2]

## Death

- This article currently notes that he was **killed by rivals in prison (2013)**. Public accounts vary in detail; consult contemporaneous reporting for specifics and attribution.
