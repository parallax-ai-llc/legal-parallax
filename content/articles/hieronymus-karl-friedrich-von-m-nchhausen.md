## Historical Context (German East Africa)

German East Africa (roughly corresponding to present-day mainland Tanzania, plus parts of neighboring regions under German rule) was administered through a colonial bureaucracy backed by military force. Policies included forced labor, coercive taxation, and harsh punitive expeditions, which contributed to widespread violence and social disruption.[^2]

## Maji Maji Rebellion (1905–1907)

The Maji Maji Rebellion was a large-scale anti-colonial uprising against German rule. German counterinsurgency operations are widely described as extremely brutal and are associated with mass civilian suffering, including famine conditions exacerbated by scorched-earth tactics and disruption of agriculture.[^3]

Within this context, colonial officials and military personnel who participated in the suppression are often discussed as part of the administrative apparatus responsible for atrocities (as summarized by Wikipedia in this biography).[^1]
