## Summary

Kyrgyz suicide bomber who killed 15 people on the St. Petersburg Metro.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2017 | Committed St. Petersburg Metro bombing |

## Background

- **Origin**: Identified by Russian investigators as a native of **Kyrgyzstan** who later lived and worked in **Russia**.
- **Aliases / identity**: Early reports used varying spellings of his name; Russian authorities later stated the attacker was **Akbarzhon Jalilov**.
- **Context**: The attack occurred amid heightened concern in Russia about militant activity and the risk of attacks on mass transit.

## St. Petersburg Metro Bombing (2017)

- **Date**: 3 April 2017.
- **Location**: St. Petersburg Metro, Russia.
- **Method**: A suicide bombing on a metro train traveling between stations in central St. Petersburg.
- **Casualties**: Widely reported as **15 fatalities** (excluding the perpetrator) and dozens injured.
- **Additional device**: Authorities reported a **second explosive device** found and defused at another metro station.

## Investigation and Aftermath

- **Identification**: Jalilov was publicly named by Russian investigative authorities as the bomber.
- **Arrests**: Following the attack, Russian officials announced multiple **detentions/arrests** of suspects alleged to have helped plan or support the bombing.
- **Security response**: Russian cities increased security measures on public transport and at major public venues in the days after the incident.

## Why It Matters

- The bombing is frequently cited as one of the deadliest attacks on the St. Petersburg Metro system in the post-Soviet period.
- It prompted renewed debate in Russia about counter-terrorism strategy, migration, and the protection of critical public infrastructure.
