## Summary

Nigerian militant leader known as 'Tompolo' who commanded a large militia in the Niger Delta.

## Background

- **Common name**: Tompolo (a widely used nickname in Nigerian media and public discourse).
- **Region**: Niger Delta (oil-producing area of southern Nigeria).
- **Associated groups**: Often discussed in connection with militant networks active in the Delta, including the broader milieu around the **Movement for the Emancipation of the Niger Delta (MEND)**.

## Niger Delta Context

Militancy in the Niger Delta has been tied to longstanding disputes over:

- Environmental damage linked to oil extraction.
- Revenue sharing and local development.
- Security and criminal economies around pipelines (oil theft, sabotage, illegal refining).

Tompolo is one of the best-known figures to emerge from this period of armed mobilization and negotiation between the state, oil companies, and local armed groups.

## Public Role and Influence

High-level themes that frequently appear in accounts of Ekpemupolo’s influence include:

- Command of armed loyalists during peaks of Delta militancy.
- Negotiation leverage during ceasefires and demobilization talks.
- Ongoing visibility in regional politics and security debates.

## Legacy and Assessment

- **Supporters** often frame him as a defender of local interests and a product of state–community conflict in an extractive economy.
- **Critics** emphasize allegations of criminality, violence, and the entrenchment of armed patronage networks.
- **Policy relevance**: His prominence is frequently used as a case study for the trade-offs in amnesty, demobilization, and outsourcing security functions.
