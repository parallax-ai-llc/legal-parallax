## Overview

Warren Edward Buffett (born 1930) is an American investor and business executive best known for leading Berkshire Hathaway, a conglomerate holding company based in Omaha, Nebraska. He is frequently described as a long-term, value-oriented investor and has been among the world’s wealthiest individuals for many years.

## Investing Approach (High Level)

- Emphasis on buying understandable businesses with durable competitive advantages (“moats”) at reasonable prices.
- Preference for strong management teams, consistent cash generation, and shareholder-friendly capital allocation.
- Long time horizon and a willingness to hold concentrated positions when conviction is high.
- Publicly advocates for temperament and discipline over short-term forecasting.

## Berkshire Hathaway (Context)

Berkshire Hathaway evolved from a textile company into a diversified conglomerate under Buffett’s leadership, with major operations and investments across:

- Insurance (used as a source of investment “float”)
- Regulated utilities and energy infrastructure
- Railroads
- Consumer brands and industrial businesses
- Public equity investments

## Notable, Widely Reported Investments

Examples often cited in public reporting and Berkshire’s disclosures include long-term holdings or major positions in companies such as Coca-Cola, American Express, and Apple, as well as earlier influential insurance-related investments like GEICO.

## Philanthropy

Buffett has pledged to donate a substantial portion of his wealth to philanthropic causes and has been associated with large-scale giving commitments, including donations routed through major foundations.
