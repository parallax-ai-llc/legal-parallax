## Summary

American serial killer known as 'The Gorilla Man' who strangled and raped over 20 landladies.

## Background

- Nelson was born in San Francisco, California, and is generally described in sources as having an unstable upbringing.
- He was sometimes reported to have suffered a serious head injury earlier in life; accounts vary and are not consistent across summaries.
- Prior to the 1920s crimes for which he is best known, he had repeated contact with the criminal justice system.

## Modus Operandi

- He targeted women who rented rooms or operated boarding houses/rooming houses, often contacting them as a prospective tenant.
- The nickname "The Gorilla Man" was used in contemporary reporting and later summaries to describe his physicality and the nature of the attacks.
- Murders were primarily committed by strangulation; multiple sources also describe sexual violence associated with the crimes.

## Investigation and Capture

- The pattern of attacks across multiple jurisdictions contributed to significant public attention.
- After a series of assaults and killings in the western United States and Canada, he was ultimately arrested in Winnipeg, Manitoba.
- He was extradited to the United States to face prosecution.

## Trial and Execution

- Nelson was convicted of murder in California.
- He was executed at San Quentin State Prison in 1928.
