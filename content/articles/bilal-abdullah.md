## Background

- Bilal Abdullah is an Iraqi-born medical doctor who later became involved in extremist activity in the United Kingdom.
- Public reporting most commonly associates him with the attempted attacks in London and Glasgow in June 2007.

## 2007 London & Glasgow Incidents (High-Level)

- In late June 2007, two car bombs were discovered in central London.
- The following day, an attempted attack occurred at Glasgow Airport involving a vehicle driven into the terminal area.
- Abdullah was identified as one of the participants and later prosecuted in the UK.

## Prosecution and Conviction

- UK authorities charged Abdullah in connection with the 2007 incidents.
- In 2008, he was convicted of terrorism-related offenses for his role in the Glasgow Airport attack.
- Court outcomes are typically reported as a life sentence (or equivalent long custodial term) under UK terrorism laws.
