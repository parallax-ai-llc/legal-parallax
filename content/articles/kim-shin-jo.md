## Summary

North Korean commando who led the failed 1968 raid on the South Korean presidential Blue House.

## Background

- Kim Shin-jo (김신조) was a member of a North Korean special operations unit tasked with infiltration into South Korea.
- His name is primarily associated with the January 1968 "Blue House raid" attempt, aimed at assassinating South Korean President Park Chung-hee.

## 1968 Blue House Raid (Overview)

- A team of North Korean commandos infiltrated across the Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) and moved toward Seoul.
- The operation failed before reaching the presidential compound (the Blue House).
- The incident triggered a major security response in South Korea and became one of the most widely cited Cold War-era infiltration attempts on the peninsula.

## Capture and Aftermath

- Kim Shin-jo was captured alive during the manhunt that followed the failed raid.
- According to widely reported accounts, most of the infiltrators were killed during subsequent engagements, while a small number were not captured.
- His capture was used in South Korea as a high-profile example of North Korean commando operations and as part of broader counter-infiltration efforts.

## Later Life (South Korea)

- After his capture, Kim Shin-jo later became known in South Korea as a public figure connected to security education and anti-infiltration awareness.
- He is often described in secondary sources as having lived in South Korea for decades following the incident.
- Public reporting on his exact later-life details varies by source, and biographies commonly rely on press and interview material rather than primary documents.

## Historical Significance

- The Blue House raid is frequently discussed alongside other late-1960s incidents that increased tensions on the Korean Peninsula.
- It contributed to heightened domestic security measures in South Korea and reinforced the perception of ongoing unconventional threats.
- The event remains a reference point in discussions of North Korean special forces doctrine and inter-Korean confrontation during the Cold War.
