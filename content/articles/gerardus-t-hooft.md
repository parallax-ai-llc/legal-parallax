## Overview

Gerardus (Gerard) 't Hooft is a Dutch theoretical physicist known for foundational contributions to quantum field theory, especially the consistent renormalization of gauge theories. His work helped place the electroweak theory on a mathematically solid footing, enabling precision calculations central to particle physics.

## Key Contributions (High Level)

- **Renormalization of gauge theories**: Demonstrated how non-abelian gauge theories can be renormalized, a critical step for making such theories predictive at the quantum level.
- **Electroweak theory foundations**: Along with collaborators and the broader community, contributed to the framework that underlies the Standard Model’s description of electromagnetic and weak interactions.
- **Non-perturbative phenomena**: Influential work related to instantons and other structures that go beyond simple perturbation expansions.
- **Large-N / planar ideas and dualities (contextual influence)**: Widely cited in discussions of how gauge theories behave in certain limits, connecting to later developments in field theory and quantum gravity research.

## Awards and Recognition

- **Nobel Prize in Physics (1999)**: Shared with Martinus J. G. Veltman for theoretical work clarifying the quantum structure of electroweak interactions.[^2]
