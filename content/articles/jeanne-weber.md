## Summary

French serial killer known as 'The Ogress' who strangled children including her own nephews.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1908 | Committed to asylum after multiple child murders |

## Background

- Weber was a French woman from Paris whose killings were primarily associated with children in her extended family.
- The moniker "The Ogress" is commonly used in later reporting to describe the case.

## Modus Operandi

- Described in sources as strangulation/smothering of children, often in domestic settings.
- Several deaths occurred in circumstances initially interpreted as illness or accident, complicating early detection.

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings

- Multiple child deaths in the same family drew suspicion over time.
- Contemporary investigative limits and the absence of modern forensic methods contributed to uncertainty and delays.
- She faced trials and periods of confinement; historical accounts emphasize that outcomes varied across incidents.

## Later Life

- After repeated allegations and scrutiny, Weber was institutionalized in an asylum.
- She died while confined.

## Legacy and Notes

- The case is frequently cited in discussions of early 20th-century criminal investigation and the challenges of attributing child deaths.
- Later summaries often differ on exact counts and attribution; high-level accounts agree that several children close to her died under suspicious circumstances.
