## Background

- Ibragim Todashev was a Chechen-born man who lived in the United States and drew attention in the aftermath of the 2013 Boston Marathon bombing investigation.[^1]
- Public reporting connected him socially to Tamerlan Tsarnaev (one of the Boston Marathon bombing suspects).[^1]

## Reported Connection to the Boston Investigation

- After the bombing, investigators pursued leads and interviews involving people who had known the Tsarnaev brothers.[^1]
- Todashev was questioned by federal agents during this period as part of that broader inquiry.[^1]

## May 2013 Interrogation and Death

- Todashev died on May 22, 2013, after being shot by an FBI agent during an interrogation in Florida.[^1]
- Accounts summarized in secondary sources describe the shooting as occurring while agents were questioning him, and the circumstances were disputed in public discussion afterward.[^1]

## Aftermath and Public Discussion

- The case became a subject of media coverage and debate, including scrutiny of the use of lethal force during questioning and the clarity of the official narrative.[^1]
- Commentary around the incident often focused on transparency, investigative procedure, and how the event related (or did not relate) to the Boston case itself.[^1]

## Key Points (High-Level)

- He is primarily notable in public records for the circumstances of his death during an FBI interview tied to a high-profile terrorism investigation.[^1]
