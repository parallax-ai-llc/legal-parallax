## Summary

Union Carbide's pesticide plant leaked methyl isocyanate gas, immediately killing 3,787 people. Up to 16,000 died from exposure. The worst industrial disaster in history.

## Background

- **Location**: Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh, India; the incident centered on Union Carbide India Limited (UCIL), a subsidiary associated with Union Carbide Corporation (UCC).
- **Facility purpose**: production of pesticides, including carbaryl (often marketed as Sevin).
- **Chemical released**: **methyl isocyanate (MIC)**, a highly toxic intermediate used in pesticide manufacture.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- In the night of **2–3 December 1984**, water entered a storage tank containing MIC, triggering an **exothermic reaction** that raised pressure and temperature.
- The resulting overpressure led to a large-scale release of toxic gases into surrounding neighborhoods.
- The dense plume moved through nearby residential areas, where many people were asleep and had little warning.

## Immediate Impact

- **Acute health effects** widely reported include choking, coughing, eye irritation, pulmonary edema, and loss of consciousness.
- **Mass displacement** occurred as residents fled on foot, often toward hospitals that were quickly overwhelmed.
- Reported fatality counts vary by source; many summaries distinguish **immediate deaths** from **longer-term deaths** attributable to exposure.

## Longer-Term Public Health Effects

- Many survivors reported chronic respiratory problems, eye injuries, neurological symptoms, and reproductive/child health impacts.
- The disaster is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - disaster medicine and surge capacity
  - industrial toxicology and long-latency harm
  - risk communication for chemical releases

## Legal and Corporate Aftermath (Overview)

- The event led to prolonged legal proceedings and settlement discussions involving Indian authorities and corporate entities.
- Key themes in later analysis include:
  - responsibility across parent/subsidiary structures
  - adequacy and fairness of compensation mechanisms
  - evidentiary challenges in attributing long-term health impacts

## Environmental Legacy

- The site and surrounding areas have been associated in some reporting with concerns about residual contamination and cleanup complexity.
- The broader policy debate often highlights:
  - long-term remediation obligations
  - transparency around industrial hazards
  - community right-to-know and ongoing monitoring

## Why It Matters

- Often described as a defining case in **industrial safety**, **corporate accountability**, and **regulatory governance**.
- Regularly referenced alongside major process-safety frameworks and reforms emphasizing:
  - hazard identification and layered safeguards
  - maintenance and safety culture
  - emergency planning and community preparedness
