Female head of the Tijuana Cartel, one of the few women to lead a major Mexican drug cartel.

## Background

- Enedina Arellano Félix (sometimes written as Enedina Arellano Félix de Toledo) is associated with the Arellano Félix family, a group widely described as central to the founding and leadership of the Tijuana Cartel.
- She was born in Mazatlán, Sinaloa, Mexico, and is frequently described as having an education in accounting.
- Multiple sources characterize her early role in the organization as being less publicly visible than some of her male relatives, while still being operationally important.

## Role in the Tijuana Cartel

- Reporting commonly describes her as a key figure in financial administration and money laundering for the organization.
- After arrests and deaths among senior figures, she is often portrayed as taking on greater leadership responsibilities.
- Accounts emphasize her function as an organizer focused on logistics and finance rather than a high-profile public boss.

## Leadership and operations (high-level)

- Descriptions of her leadership frequently highlight:
  - management of finances and accounting,
  - oversight of alliances and internal coordination,
  - continuity of operations as other leaders were removed.
- She has been portrayed as part of a broader shift toward lower-visibility leadership structures within the organization.

## Legal status and public reporting

- Public profiles typically describe her as a fugitive and link her to allegations involving drug trafficking and money laundering.
- Details about current whereabouts are often uncertain or disputed in open sources, and reporting tends to rely on law-enforcement and investigative journalism accounts.

## Names and nicknames

- Nicknames attributed to her in press and reference sources include "La Jefa" and "La Madrina" (usage and attribution vary by source).
