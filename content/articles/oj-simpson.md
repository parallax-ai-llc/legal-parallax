## Summary
O.J. Simpson was acquitted of the 1994 murders of his ex-wife Nicole Brown Simpson and Ron Goldman in the 'Trial of the Century.' He was later found liable in a civil trial. He died of cancer in 2024.

## Background
- **Full name**: Orenthal James Simpson.
- Became one of the most famous American athletes of the late 20th century, later known globally for highly publicized criminal and civil court proceedings.

## Football Career (high level)
- Played college football at **USC**, where he became a national star and won the **Heisman Trophy (1968)**.
- In the NFL, he played primarily for the **Buffalo Bills**, later briefly for the **San Francisco 49ers**.
- Set multiple rushing records and remains closely associated with the milestone of **2,000+ rushing yards in a single season**.

## Media and Entertainment Work
- Transitioned into public-facing roles beyond sports, including:
  - Sports commentary and broadcasting.
  - Film and television appearances, notably comedic roles that expanded his mainstream fame.

## Legal History (overview)
### 1994–1995 criminal case
- Charged with the murders of **Nicole Brown Simpson** and **Ron Goldman**.
- The case became a major U.S. media event, often described as the **"Trial of the Century"**.
- The jury returned a **not guilty** verdict in 1995.

### 1997 civil case
- In a separate civil proceeding, he was found **liable** for wrongful death.
- Civil findings use a different standard of proof than criminal trials.

### 2007–2017 Las Vegas case
- In 2007, Simpson was involved in a confrontation in a Las Vegas hotel room related to sports memorabilia.
- He was convicted in 2008 of multiple felony counts and served nearly nine years in a Nevada prison.
- Released on parole in 2017.

## Public Impact and Legacy
- Simpson’s story is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - The relationship between celebrity, media coverage, and the justice system.
  - Criminal vs. civil standards of proof.
  - U.S. cultural debates of the 1990s around race, policing, and televised trials.
