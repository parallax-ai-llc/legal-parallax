## Background

- Dimitris Koufodinas is a Greek national identified by Greek authorities as a leading member of the far-left militant organization **Revolutionary Organization 17 November** (commonly "17N").[^1]
- 17N was active primarily in Greece for decades and became known for targeted attacks and assassinations, often describing its actions in political terms in communiqués.[^1]

## Role in 17N (as reported)

- Koufodinas has been described in reporting and court records summarized by secondary sources as an operational figure within 17N, including participation in attacks attributed to the group.[^1]
- He was frequently characterized as a key perpetrator in a series of assassinations for which 17N later faced prosecution.[^1]

## Arrest, Trial, and Convictions

- Following a 2002 breakthrough investigation into 17N, multiple members were arrested and prosecuted in Greece.[^1]
- Koufodinas surrendered to authorities in 2002, shortly after other arrests linked to the investigation.[^1]
- In 2003, a Greek court convicted him in connection with multiple killings and other offenses associated with 17N operations.[^1]

## Imprisonment and Public Attention

- His imprisonment has periodically drawn renewed public attention in Greece, including debates around detention conditions, prison transfers, and the political symbolism attached to 17N’s history.[^1]
- He has undertaken hunger strikes at various points, which became a focal point for public discussion and demonstrations by supporters and opponents.[^1]
