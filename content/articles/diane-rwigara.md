## Summary

Rwandan presidential candidate arrested after challenging Kagame, later acquitted.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2015 | Her father, Assinapol Rwigara, died in a traffic collision; the family later publicly disputed the circumstances. |
| 2017-05 | Announced an intention to run as an independent candidate in the 2017 Rwandan presidential election. |
| 2017-07 | Disqualified by Rwanda's National Electoral Commission on technical grounds related to supporting signatures (disputed by Rwigara). |
| 2017-08 | Police raided her home amid investigations (including alleged forgery and tax issues reported by authorities). |
| 2017-09 | Charged (with her mother among others) including an allegation of “inciting insurrection,” according to reporting and human-rights groups. |
| 2018-12 | Acquitted by the High Court (including an acquittal for her mother), according to major international reporting. |
| 2024-06 | Attempted to run again; her 2024 candidacy was rejected by the electoral commission on signature-related grounds (reported). |

## Context & Significance

- **Public profile:** A Rwandan businesswoman/accountant who gained international attention through an attempted presidential candidacy and subsequent prosecution.
- **Political context:** Her case has been cited in discussions about political competition, civil liberties, and the space for dissent in Rwanda under President Paul Kagame.

## Key Points

- Ran (or attempted to run) as an **independent candidate** in 2017.
- Was **disqualified** from the 2017 ballot on administrative/technical grounds related to supporting signatures (she disputed the decision).
- Was later **arrested and prosecuted** on charges that included an allegation of “inciting insurrection,” and was **acquitted** in 2018.
- Attempted another bid for national office in 2024; the reported outcome was another **rejection of her candidacy**.
