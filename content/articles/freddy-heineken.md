## Background

- Freddy Heineken (Alfred Henry "Freddy" Heineken) was a Dutch business executive best known for leading the global expansion and brand-building of Heineken N.V.
- He was the grandson of Gerard Adriaan Heineken, who acquired the brewery that later became Heineken.

## Role at Heineken

- Heineken is widely credited with turning Heineken into a globally recognized premium lager brand through marketing discipline and consistent brand identity.
- Under his leadership, the company expanded internationally and invested heavily in advertising, sponsorships, and distribution.
- He served as a top executive at Heineken for decades and became one of the most recognizable Dutch business figures of the late 20th century.

## 1983 Kidnapping (high-level)

- In 1983, Heineken and his driver, Ab Doderer, were abducted in Amsterdam and held captive for roughly three weeks.
- The kidnappers demanded a very large ransom; contemporary reporting commonly cites about 35 million Dutch guilders (often reported in English-language sources as about US$12 million at the time).
- The case became one of the most famous kidnappings in the Netherlands, leading to a major police investigation and later prosecutions.

## Aftermath and cultural impact

- The kidnapping and subsequent pursuit of the perpetrators became a lasting part of Dutch popular history and has been revisited in books, documentaries, and dramatizations.
- The event is frequently referenced as an example of the risks faced by highly visible business leaders and the societal impact of organized criminal schemes.

## Notes

- This article’s short summary emphasizes the kidnapping because it is the most widely known single incident in English-language coverage; however, Heineken’s broader historical significance is tied to his long business career and brand strategy at Heineken.
