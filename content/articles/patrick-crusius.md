## Summary
Patrick Crusius killed 23 people at a Walmart in El Paso, Texas on August 3, 2019, targeting Hispanic shoppers. He posted a white supremacist manifesto. Sentenced to 90 consecutive life terms.

## Background
- Crusius grew up in the Dallas–Fort Worth area and later lived in Allen, Texas.
- Public reporting has described a history of social isolation and online radicalization in the period leading up to the attack.

## El Paso Walmart Attack (2019)
- Date/Location: August 3, 2019 — Walmart near the Cielo Vista Mall in El Paso, Texas.
- Targeting: Prosecutors said the attack was aimed at Hispanic/Latino shoppers and designed to terrorize immigrants.
- Casualties: 23 victims were killed; additional people were wounded.
- Arrest: He was taken into custody shortly after the shooting.

## Ideology and Manifesto
- A document attributed to Crusius was posted online shortly before the attack.
- The text echoed white-supremacist and anti-immigrant themes and referenced the concept of a “Hispanic invasion.”
- The case is frequently cited in discussions of extremist violence and the role of online forums in accelerating radicalization.

## Prosecution and Sentencing
- Federal case: He faced hate-crime and firearms charges; the federal court imposed 90 consecutive life sentences.
- State case (Texas): He was prosecuted separately in state court; the state resolution resulted in a life sentence.
- The multiple cases reflected different legal theories (civil-rights/hate-crime statutes at the federal level vs. homicide/assault statutes at the state level).

## Significance
- The attack became one of the deadliest mass shootings in modern U.S. history.
- It prompted renewed scrutiny of domestic violent extremism, particularly white-supremacist violence, and debates over how to prevent copycat attacks.
