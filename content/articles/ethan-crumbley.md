## Summary

Michigan teenager who shot and killed 4 students at Oxford High School; parents also convicted.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2021 | Committed Oxford High School shooting |

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Incident**: Oxford High School shooting (Oxford Township, Michigan, U.S.).
- **Victims**: 4 students killed; additional students and staff injured.
- **Date**: November 30, 2021.
- **Perpetrator**: Student at the school (Ethan Crumbley).

## Background (Publicly Reported)

- Crumbley was a student at Oxford High School at the time of the shooting.
- Reporting around the case emphasized warning signs and school/parent interactions on the day of the attack.

## Oxford High School Shooting (Overview)

- The shooting occurred on campus during the school day.
- Law enforcement responded and Crumbley was taken into custody the same day.

## Criminal Case and Sentencing (Overview)

- Crumbley pleaded guilty to multiple charges related to the deaths and injuries.
- In 2023, he was sentenced in Michigan state court to **life in prison without the possibility of parole**.

## Parents' Convictions (Context)

- Crumbley's parents, **James and Jennifer Crumbley**, were separately prosecuted.
- In 2024, both were convicted of **involuntary manslaughter** in connection with the shooting, a widely noted development in U.S. criminal law discussions.

## Legacy / Aftermath (High-Level)

- The case became a focal point for debates on school safety, threat assessment, firearm access, and parental responsibility.
- It also prompted scrutiny of institutional responses to warning signs and crisis protocols.
