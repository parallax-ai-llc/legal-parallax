## Summary

Series of 10 rapes and murders near Hwaseong that remained unsolved for 33 years until DNA identified Lee Chun-jae.

## Context and Location

- The Hwaseong serial murders occurred in the late 1980s in and around Hwaseong (then a largely rural area) in Gyeonggi Province, South Korea.
- The case became one of South Korea’s most high-profile unsolved series, heavily shaping public perceptions of violent crime and investigative capacity.

## Investigations (High-Level)

- Large-scale police mobilization and repeated re-investigation efforts were conducted over decades, including renewed reviews as forensic methods improved.
- DNA testing and evidence re-analysis were central to later breakthroughs, reflecting broader advances in forensic science.

## Resolution and Aftermath

- In 2019, DNA evidence identified Lee Chun-jae as responsible for the series described in contemporary reporting and later investigations.
- Because the crimes occurred decades earlier, statutes of limitations and procedural constraints affected prosecution pathways at the time of identification.

## Cultural and Societal Impact

- The case is frequently cited in discussions of South Korea’s criminal justice reforms, forensic modernization, and the handling of cold cases.
- It has been referenced in popular culture and media, contributing to long-running public interest in unresolved and later-resolved crimes.
