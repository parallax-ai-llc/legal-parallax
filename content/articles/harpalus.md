## Background

Harpalus was a Macedonian (Greek) court figure connected to Alexander the Great's administration. While not a battlefield commander, he became notable for the financial and political scandal surrounding his custodianship of royal funds during the later years of Alexander's empire-building.

## Role in Alexander's Administration

- **Financial custody**: Ancient sources describe Harpalus as holding responsibility for significant sums belonging to the royal treasury.
- **Logistics and pay**: Managing funds in a campaigning empire included paying troops, contractors, garrisons, and provisioning long supply chains.
- **Perception at court**: Later narratives emphasize extravagance and personal enrichment, which amplified the sense of betrayal when he absconded.

## Flight to Athens and Political Crisis

Harpalus' arrival in Athens mattered because Athens was a major Greek city with its own political factions and a complicated relationship with Macedonian hegemony.

Key high-level outcomes often highlighted in classical histories:

- **Diplomatic pressure**: Macedonian authorities pressed Athens to surrender Harpalus and return the money.
- **Factional conflict**: Athenian leaders argued over policy: resistance, accommodation, or legal containment.
- **Bribery accusations**: The "Harpalus affair" became tied to allegations that influential Athenians accepted funds, which later fueled prosecutions and rhetorical attacks.

## Death and Aftermath

- **Death**: Harpalus was killed in 323 BCE; the exact circumstances vary across ancient accounts, but the consistent theme is violent removal amid disputes over the remaining money.
- **Aftermath**: The affair contributed to political instability in Athens on the eve of the Wars of the Diadochi, when Alexander's death triggered succession conflicts.

## Historical Significance

- **Administrative vulnerability**: Harpalus illustrates how imperial finance could be undermined by personal control over mobile wealth.
- **Greek politics under Macedon**: The episode shows how a single refugee with cash could destabilize civic politics through patronage and accusations.
- **Rhetorical legacy**: Later writers used Harpalus as a cautionary example of corruption, bribery, and the tension between law and expediency.
