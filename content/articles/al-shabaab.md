## Overview

Al-Shabaab (Arabic: "The Youth") is a Somalia-based Islamist militant organization and a designated terrorist group in multiple jurisdictions. It is best known for waging an insurgency against the Somali federal government and international forces, and for carrying out high-casualty attacks in Somalia and neighboring countries, particularly Kenya.

## Ideology and Goals

- **Militant Islamism**: Generally described as a Salafi-jihadist movement seeking governance based on a strict interpretation of Islamic law.
- **State-building through coercion**: Uses violence and intimidation to control territory, populations, and trade corridors.
- **Regional focus with transnational ties**: While its core objectives are centered on Somalia, its affiliation with al-Qaeda has connected it to broader jihadist narratives and networks.

## Organization and Leadership (High-Level)

- Operates with an **emir** (overall leader) and layers of political, military, and intelligence/security structures.
- Has used **local administrations** in areas of influence to enforce rules, collect “taxes,” and manage courts.
- Leadership history overlaps with prominent figures such as **Ahmed Abdi Godane** (often cited as a key leader during the 2010s).

## Areas of Operation

- **Somalia**: Strongest in parts of south-central Somalia; frequently targets Mogadishu and other urban centers.
- **Kenya and the region**: Known for cross-border raids and attacks intended to pressure regional governments and foreign military contributors.

## Tactics and Targeting Patterns

- **Asymmetric warfare**: Ambushes, IEDs, complex assaults, and targeted assassinations.
- **Mass-casualty terrorism**: Attacks on civilian venues and transport hubs have been used to gain attention and apply political pressure.
- **Intimidation and governance**: Coerces communities through threats, punishments, and control of local dispute resolution mechanisms.

## Financing and Logistics (Commonly Reported)

- **Taxation/extortion** in areas under influence (including checkpoints and business levies).
- **Informal economic control** of trade routes and local markets.
- Additional revenue is often discussed in reporting as stemming from **smuggling networks** and illicit commerce, though sources and scale vary by time and location.

## International Status

Al-Shabaab is designated as a terrorist organization by several governments and international bodies. It has been a focus of counterterrorism operations involving Somali forces and international partners.
