## Canonical Subject (High-Level Overview)

Bernard Arnault (born 1949) is a French business executive best known for building **LVMH Moët Hennessy Louis Vuitton** into the world’s largest luxury group.

### Education

- Studied engineering at **École Polytechnique** (France).

### Business Roles

- Chairman and CEO of **LVMH**.
- Principal shareholder of the group through holding structures commonly referenced in public reporting.

### How Control of LVMH Was Built (Context)

Public biographies commonly summarize Arnault’s rise in luxury as a sequence of corporate acquisitions and strategic consolidation:

- 1980s: Repositioning from construction/real estate toward luxury assets.
- Late 1980s: Becoming a leading shareholder in the entity that became modern LVMH, then taking effective control.
- 1990s–2010s: Expanding the portfolio across fashion & leather goods, wines & spirits, perfumes & cosmetics, watches & jewelry, and selective retail.

### LVMH at a Glance

LVMH is typically described as a multi-division luxury conglomerate whose best-known houses include (non-exhaustive):

- **Louis Vuitton** (fashion & leather goods)
- **Dior** (fashion/beauty; corporate structures vary by period)
- **Moët & Chandon / Hennessy** (wines & spirits)
- **Sephora** (selective retail)
- **Tiffany & Co.** (watches & jewelry; acquired by LVMH in 2021)

## Public Profile

- Frequently listed among the world’s wealthiest individuals in major business rankings.
- Often cited as an emblematic figure of modern European luxury brand management and global retail expansion.
