## Summary
Derrick Bird killed 12 people and injured 11 in a shooting spree across Cumbria, England on June 2, 2010, before killing himself. It was the deadliest mass shooting in England since Hungerford.

## Background
- Bird was a self-employed taxi driver in Cumbria, a largely rural county in northwest England.
- Contemporary reporting described him as a long-term local resident who was known in the community through his work.

## 2010 Cumbria shootings (overview)
- Date: 2010-06-02.
- Location: Multiple sites across western Cumbria, including Whitehaven and surrounding areas.
- Impact: 12 people were killed and 11 others were injured before Bird died by suicide.
- This incident is commonly referred to as the **Cumbria shootings**.

## Sequence of events (high level)
- The attack began in the Whitehaven area and then moved across a number of towns and roads as Bird drove between locations.
- Victims included members of the public encountered during the spree as well as individuals believed to have been personally known to him.
- Police responded with large-scale incident procedures, including road closures and coordinated searches across the county.

## Weapon and method
- Bird used firearms during the spree; sources describe a shotgun and a rifle.
- The attack involved both shootings from close range and shootings carried out after traveling to additional locations.

## Law-enforcement response
- The response involved Cumbria Constabulary with support from neighboring forces.
- Armed-response units were deployed, and the incident prompted rapid public safety messaging to stay indoors and avoid affected routes.

## Motive and investigation (reported)
- A definitive single motive has not been universally agreed upon in public summaries.
- Investigators examined Bird’s personal circumstances, finances, and relationships for potential triggers.
- Post-incident reporting also discussed possible grievances and interpersonal conflicts, though details vary by source.

## Public impact and legacy
- The event prompted renewed discussion in the UK about firearms licensing, public safety, and the rarity yet severity of mass shootings.
- It remains one of the deadliest shooting incidents in modern English history.
