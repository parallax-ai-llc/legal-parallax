## Summary

Choi Min-ho won Olympic judo gold at the 2008 Beijing Olympics in the 60kg category.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2008 | Won Olympic gold at Beijing |
| 2004 | Competed at the Athens Olympics and won a bronze medal (men's –60 kg) [^2] |
| 2003 | Won a World Championships title (men's –60 kg) [^2] |
| 2001 | Won an Asian Championships title (men's –60 kg) [^2] |
| 2012 | Won an Asian Championships title after moving to –66 kg [^2] |

## Notes & Disambiguation

- The name **Choi Min-ho (최민호)** is used by multiple notable South Koreans. The athlete referenced in this article is the **judoka** who won Olympic gold in 2008.
- Some basic biographical fields in the frontmatter above may be incomplete or inconsistent across sources; the widely cited judo profile lists his birth as **18 August 1980** and his sport as judo in the **–60 kg** and **–66 kg** classes. [^2]

## Discipline & Competitive Profile

- **Sport:** Judo (International Judo Federation competition format).
- **Weight classes:** Primarily **–60 kg**, later also competed at **–66 kg**. [^2]
- **Competition pattern:** A typical Olympic-cycle career combining national-team selection with repeated appearances at continental championships, world championships, and multi-sport games.

## Selected Achievements (high level)

- **Olympic Games:** Gold (2008, Beijing; men's –60 kg) and bronze (2004, Athens; men's –60 kg). [^2]
- **World Championships:** At least one world title (2003; men's –60 kg), with additional podium-level results listed in major-event summaries. [^2]
- **Asian competitions:** Titles at the Asian Judo Championships (including 2001 and 2012) and medal results at the Asian Games are commonly included in his major-event record. [^2]

## Legacy

- Frequently cited as one of South Korea's standout lighter-weight judoka of his era, with major-event medals spanning multiple Olympic cycles.
- His 2008 Olympic title is a defining highlight in Korean judo history for the men's –60 kg category.
