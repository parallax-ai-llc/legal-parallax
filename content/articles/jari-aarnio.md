## Summary

Former head of Helsinki drug police convicted of drug trafficking and corruption.

## Key Allegations & Convictions (High Level)

- **Position and access:** Aarnio led Helsinki’s anti-drug police unit and spent decades in anti-narcotics work, which prosecutors said gave him unique access and credibility.
- **Drug crimes:** Finnish courts reported convictions related to drug offences, including allegations of facilitating large-scale hashish smuggling and distribution during 2011–2012.
- **Corruption / procurement:** Separate corruption findings were reported in connection with procurement and undisclosed financial interests involving a security company (“Trevoc” is cited in Wikipedia’s summary).
- **Appeals and later status:** The Wikipedia summary describes a multi-year appeal process and later supervised parole/release.

## Why This Matters

- **Trust shock:** The case is frequently cited in Finnish public discussion as an example of how corruption risk can exist even inside law enforcement units tasked with fighting organized crime.
- **Operational lesson:** Concentrated authority, long tenure, and informational asymmetry can create conditions where oversight must be unusually strong.
- **Institutional impact:** High-profile convictions tend to drive internal audits, procurement scrutiny, and reforms around conflicts of interest (discussed broadly in coverage of similar cases).
