## Background

- Commonly identified as **Daniel Rendón Herrera**, a Colombian criminal figure also known by the alias **“Don Mario.”**[^1]
- Emerged from the armed-conflict/criminal milieu of the **Urabá** region, where paramilitary and trafficking networks overlapped.[^1]

## Role in Colombian Illegal Armed and Criminal Networks

High-level points (non-exhaustive):

- Linked to **right-wing paramilitary structures** in Colombia before becoming a prominent trafficking and organized-crime figure.[^1]
- Credited (in open sources) as one of the founders or early leaders of the group later widely known as the **Urabeños** (also referred to in later reporting as the **Clan del Golfo**).[^1]

## Capture and Aftermath

- Captured by Colombian security forces in **2009**, an arrest that was widely publicized as a major blow to emergent post-paramilitary criminal organizations.[^1]
- After capture, open-source accounts describe his case as part of broader Colombian efforts to dismantle leadership nodes of trafficking organizations and successor groups.[^1]
