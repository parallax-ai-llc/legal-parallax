## Summary

Known as Arkan, Serbian paramilitary leader whose Tigers committed ethnic cleansing during the Yugoslav Wars.

## Background

- Željko Ražnatović (often known by the nickname “Arkan”) was a Serbian paramilitary commander and organized crime figure.
- He became prominent during the Yugoslav Wars, where his unit, commonly called **Arkan’s Tigers**, was linked to grave abuses against civilians.

## Role in the Yugoslav Wars (high level)

- The Tigers were a paramilitary formation associated with Serbian nationalist projects during the conflicts in Croatia and Bosnia.
- Multiple investigations and reporting have connected the unit to **ethnic cleansing**, intimidation, looting, and killings of civilians.
- In mainstream historical accounts, Ražnatović is treated as a symbol of the entanglement of paramilitaries, state security networks, and criminal groups in the wars of the 1990s.

## Legal status and international proceedings

- The ICTY indictment (1999) alleged responsibility for crimes against humanity and violations of the laws or customs of war.
- Ražnatović died before any trial could take place, leaving many allegations unresolved in a court judgment.

## Public profile after the wars

- In the late 1990s, he cultivated a high-profile public persona inside Serbia.
- He was associated with football club FK Obilić, which became nationally prominent amid persistent controversy.

## Death

- Ražnatović was shot and killed in January 2000 at a hotel in Belgrade.
- The killing was widely interpreted in media as connected to underworld conflicts and post-war power struggles.
