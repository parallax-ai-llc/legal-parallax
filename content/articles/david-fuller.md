## Summary

British hospital electrician who murdered two women and sexually abused at least 100 corpses in mortuaries.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2021 | Convicted of murder and necrophilia |

## Background

- Fuller was employed as a hospital electrician in Kent, England, which gave him routine access to hospital facilities.
- Public reporting identifies him as a British serial murderer whose offending spanned both homicide and post-mortem sexual abuse.

## Crimes

- **Homicides (1987):** He murdered two women, Wendy Knell and Caroline Pierce, in separate attacks.
- **Necrophilic abuse (2008–2020):** He sexually abused bodies stored in hospital mortuaries over many years; investigators later attributed the abuse to at least 100 victims.
- The scale of the mortuary abuse led to wide scrutiny of mortuary security, access controls, and oversight procedures in the UK.

## Investigation & Identification

- The 1987 murders remained unsolved for decades.
- In 2020, DNA evidence associated with one of the homicide investigations was used to identify and arrest Fuller.
- Searches and subsequent investigative work connected him to both the historical murders and the mortuary offences.

## Trial & Sentencing

- In 2021, Fuller was convicted for murder and for offences relating to necrophilia.
- Courts imposed a whole-life order (a form of life sentence without parole) for the murders, alongside additional sentences for the mortuary-related crimes.

## Aftermath & Institutional Response

- The case prompted reviews of mortuary practices, including controls around keys, staff vetting, CCTV coverage, and audit trails for access.
- Families of victims and affected hospitals sought accountability and reforms to prevent similar exploitation of institutional vulnerabilities.

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Known for:** Dual pattern of violent homicide and long-term abuse of corpses in clinical settings.
- **Location:** Kent, England (United Kingdom).
- **Notable aspect:** One of the largest known necrophilia cases in modern UK criminal history, based on victim count reported by authorities.
