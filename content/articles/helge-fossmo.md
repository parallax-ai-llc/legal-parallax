## Summary

Swedish Pentecostal pastor convicted of manipulating a follower into murdering his wife in the Knutby case.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

## Context (Knutby)

The Knutby case refers to a series of events around the Pentecostal congregation in Knutby, Sweden, which received significant national attention after a 2004 killing and attempted killing.

## What Fossmo Was Convicted Of

According to contemporary reporting and later court summaries, Fossmo was convicted not as the shooter, but for **solicitation** (instigating) the murder of his wife Alexandra Fossmo and the attempted murder of Daniel Linde.[^1]

Key elements described in public accounts include:

- A female follower (Sara Svensson) received anonymous text messages she interpreted as “prophetic” and also received direct instructions.[^1]
- Alexandra Fossmo was killed at home in January 2004, while Daniel Linde survived his gunshot wounds and raised the alarm.[^1]

## Sentence and Release

- Fossmo was sentenced to **life imprisonment** in 2004.[^1]
- Swedish courts later converted (“time-determined”) the life sentence to a fixed term, and he was **paroled in 2022**.[^1]
