## Identity note

The name **Akira Nishiguchi** is associated with multiple notable individuals. The Wikipedia article currently linked in the frontmatter describes a **Japanese serial killer and fraudster (1925–1970)**. The short summary and timeline above describe a different profile; the sections below add high-level context from the Wikipedia source without removing existing text.

## Overview (per Wikipedia)

- Japanese serial killer and fraudster, born in **Osaka** and executed by hanging in **1970**.
- The documented crime spree occurred in **late 1963**, with **five murder victims**.
- Apprehended in **January 1964** after a nationwide manhunt.

## Early life and background (high level)

- Raised in a Catholic family and attended a Catholic mission school in Fukuoka Prefecture.
- Dropped out as a teenager and began a pattern of theft and fraud.
- Repeated arrests and imprisonment in the 1940s–1950s for robbery/fraud-related offenses.

## 1963–1964 crimes and capture (high level)

- The Wikipedia account describes a series of murders in 1963 involving robbery and deception.
- Police conducted a national manhunt; Nishiguchi attempted to evade capture by moving across regions.
- Identified by a child connected to a potential victim, leading to his arrest in early 1964.

## Trial, sentence, and death (high level)

- Convicted of multiple counts of murder and sentenced to death.
- Executed by hanging in **December 1970**.

## Cultural impact and legacy

- The case is noted as a catalyst for discussion of investigative/case-designation procedures in Japan.
- Inspired true-crime coverage and later adaptations; it is commonly cited in connection with the book that led to the film **"Vengeance Is Mine" (1979)**.
