## Summary

Leader of the Nduma Defence of Congo-Rénové militia sanctioned by the UN and US for human rights violations. He controlled illegal mining operations in North Kivu.

## Additional Context

### Operating environment

The Nduma Defence of Congo (and later Nduma Defence of Congo-Rénové) operated in North Kivu, an area affected by longstanding armed conflict involving state forces, local militias, and foreign-linked armed groups. Competition over territory and resources, including mining sites and trade routes, has been a persistent driver of violence.

### Allegations of abuses and exploitation

Sanctions designations and reporting have linked the militia to serious human rights abuses, including attacks on civilians and forced labor. Armed groups in the region have also been associated with extracting revenue from artisanal mining and taxing movement of people and goods.

### International measures

The United Nations Security Council sanctions regime for the Democratic Republic of the Congo and U.S. sanctions programs have been used to target individuals accused of destabilizing activities and abuses. Such measures typically include asset freezes and travel restrictions and are intended to disrupt financing and international support networks.

### Broader significance

Cases like Shimiray Mwissa's are often cited in discussions about how localized armed actors, weak governance, and illicit resource economies intersect, complicating peacebuilding and civilian protection in eastern Congo.
