## Summary

Chicago police officer convicted of murdering Laquan McDonald by shooting him 16 times.

## Background (high level)

- Van Dyke was an officer with the Chicago Police Department.
- He became nationally known in connection with the 2014 shooting of Laquan McDonald.

## Laquan McDonald shooting (2014)

- Date/time: October 20, 2014 (Chicago, Illinois).
- McDonald, a 17-year-old, was shot 16 times.
- Initial police accounts described the shooting as justified.
- Dashcam video later showed McDonald moving away when shots were fired, which intensified public scrutiny.

## Prosecution and trial

- Charges (reported): first-degree murder, multiple counts of aggravated battery with a firearm, and official misconduct.
- Trial period: September–October 2018.
- Verdict (October 2018): guilty of second-degree murder and guilty on 16 counts of aggravated battery with a firearm; not guilty of official misconduct.

## Sentence and custody

- Sentenced (January 2019): 6 years and 9 months (6¾ years) in prison.
- Reported release: February 2022 (early release for good behavior).

## Public impact and civic response

- The case became a focal point for debates about police accountability and transparency in Chicago.
- Public protests and political pressure increased after the video’s release.
- The City of Chicago settled a civil lawsuit with McDonald’s family for $5 million (reported in 2015).
