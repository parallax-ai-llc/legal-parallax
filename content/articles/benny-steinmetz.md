## Summary

Israeli billionaire convicted of corruption for bribing Guinean officials for mining rights.

## Background

- Benny Steinmetz is an Israeli businessman associated with the mining and natural-resources sector.
- He has been linked to the Beny Steinmetz Group Resources (BSGR), a company that has been reported in connection with major mining projects.

## Guinea / Simandou context (high-level)

- One of the best-known controversies connected to Steinmetz involves rights linked to iron ore assets in Guinea (often discussed in relation to the Simandou deposit).
- The core allegation described in public reporting is that intermediaries connected to BSGR offered bribes or other improper benefits to Guinean officials in order to obtain or retain valuable mining concessions.
- Guinea later reviewed and, in multiple accounts, moved to revoke or reassign certain concessions, triggering prolonged disputes and legal proceedings in several jurisdictions.

## Legal proceedings (overview)

- Switzerland has been a central venue for criminal proceedings related to the Guinea mining allegations.
- In 2021, a criminal court in Geneva convicted Steinmetz in a case connected to alleged corruption around Guinean mining rights. (Subsequent appeal activity has been widely reported; outcomes can change over time.)
- Steinmetz has denied wrongdoing in public statements reported by the press.

## Why this matters

- The case is frequently cited as an example of how high-stakes natural-resource licensing can be entangled with political influence, intermediaries, and cross-border enforcement.
- It also illustrates how mining-rights disputes can generate parallel tracks: corporate disputes, state reviews, arbitration claims, and criminal investigations.

## Key entities and terms

- **BSGR**: Common shorthand in reporting for Beny Steinmetz Group Resources, associated with Steinmetz in multiple mining ventures.
- **Simandou**: A large iron ore deposit in Guinea that has been the subject of long-running licensing and governance disputes.
