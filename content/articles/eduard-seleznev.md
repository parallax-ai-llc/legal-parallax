## Source Note

The short bio above describes a "serial killer who murdered and robbed elderly women across Moscow". However, the English Wikipedia article titled **"Eduard Seleznev"** currently describes **Eduard Valerievich Seleznev (born 1969)**, a Russian serial killer in **Arkhangelsk** who was convicted for multiple murders and associated with **cannibalism** allegations.[^2] This article preserves the original stub text and adds high-level, sourced context below.

## Background (per Wikipedia)

- Born in **Arkhangelsk** (then RSFSR), and spent part of his childhood in a boarding school.[^2]
- Reported to have limited formal education (up to 8th grade) and later trained for manual work (e.g., bricklaying/vocational training).[^2]
- Had prior convictions for property-related crimes, and later experienced homelessness after release from prison.[^2]

## Crimes and Convictions (high-level)

According to Wikipedia's summary:

- **2002**: convicted for a **double murder**, serving a term of imprisonment and released in **September 2015**.[^2]
- **2016–2018**: accused of killing additional victims in Arkhangelsk; the article states the bodies were dismembered and that some remains were consumed after being processed.[^2]

## Investigation, Arrest, and Trial

- Wikipedia reports that relatives of a missing man became suspicious after visiting an apartment, noticing unusual meat, and contacting police.[^2]
- After arrest, he reportedly confessed during interrogations; later accounts say he **recanted** and sought a jury trial.[^2]
- The trial is reported to have started in **January 2020**, ending with a **life sentence** in a special-regime colony and a monetary restitution order to victims' families.[^2]
