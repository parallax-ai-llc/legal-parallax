## Summary

ISIS-K gunmen attacked the Crocus City Hall concert venue near Moscow, killing 145 people in Russia's deadliest terrorist attack in 20 years. Gunmen opened fire and set the building ablaze.

## Background

Crocus City Hall is a large entertainment venue and concert complex in Krasnogorsk (Moscow Oblast), on the northwestern edge of Moscow. The attack occurred during a live event with a large audience present.

## Attack Overview

- **Date**: 22 March 2024
- **Location**: Crocus City Hall, near Moscow, Russia
- **Nature of attack**: Armed assault followed by arson (the venue was set on fire).
- **Casualties**: Widely reported as **145 people killed**, with many more injured.

## Claimed Responsibility

The Islamic State's Khorasan Province (**ISIS-K**) claimed responsibility for the attack in public statements. Reporting on attribution and responsibility relied on official statements and subsequent investigation.

## Immediate Response

- Emergency services responded to evacuate attendees, extinguish the fire, and provide medical treatment.
- Russian authorities launched a large-scale security and investigative response after the incident.

## Investigation and Arrests (High-Level)

In the aftermath, Russian authorities reported detentions and arrests of suspected participants and alleged facilitators. The case drew extensive international attention due to the scale of the casualties and the prominence of the target.

## Impact and Significance

- The incident was described as **Russia’s deadliest terrorist attack in roughly two decades**.
- It intensified domestic security measures and renewed focus on counterterrorism policy and intelligence.
- The attack also had broader geopolitical resonance, given the involvement of ISIS-K and the location near the Russian capital.

## Key Facts (Quick Reference)

- **Target type**: Civilian concert venue
- **Method**: Firearms attack and arson
- **Reported death toll**: 145
- **Claim**: ISIS-K
