## Summary

Former President Compaoré was convicted in absentia for the assassination of Thomas Sankara. Sentenced to life imprisonment while living in exile in Ivory Coast.

## Background

- **Thomas Sankara assassination (1987):** Sankara, a revolutionary leader and President of Burkina Faso, was killed during a coup on 15 October 1987. Blaise Compaoré subsequently led the country for decades.
- **Long-running allegations:** For many years, Compaoré and senior associates were accused by opponents and investigators of involvement in the events surrounding Sankara’s killing.

## Trial and Verdict (2022)

- **Venue:** A military tribunal in Burkina Faso tried the case.
- **Proceedings in absentia:** Compaoré was tried while outside Burkina Faso (commonly described as living in exile in Côte d’Ivoire).
- **Outcome:** He was **convicted in absentia** and **sentenced to life imprisonment** for his role in the assassination.

## Key Points

- **Political significance:** The verdict was widely viewed as a landmark for accountability in Burkina Faso’s modern political history.
- **Practical enforcement limits:** Because the conviction was issued in absentia and the defendant was outside the country, enforcement depended on cross-border legal and political decisions.

## Related People and Institutions
