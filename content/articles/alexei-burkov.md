## Summary

Russian cybercriminal who ran a darknet forum selling stolen credit card data.

## Background and Notability

Alexei Burkov is known in cybersecurity reporting primarily in connection with **CarderPlanet**, a darknet forum associated with the sale of stolen payment-card data and related fraud services. His case is often cited as an example of how online carding ecosystems combine:

- marketplaces for compromised card data
- escrow and dispute-resolution mechanisms
- reputation systems that enable repeat fraud at scale

## Case Overview (high level)

Public reporting describes a multi-year legal process involving arrest outside the United States and subsequent extradition to face U.S. charges.

Key themes frequently mentioned in coverage:

- **extradition and jurisdiction** for cybercrime cases with cross-border victims
- **marketplace facilitation** (running or enabling a forum) as distinct from conducting individual fraud transactions
- **sentencing and restitution** in large-scale financial fraud cases

## Allegations / Charges (as commonly summarized)

While specific counts and legal language vary by source, summaries of the case typically describe allegations related to:

- trafficking in stolen credit-card information
- identity-theft and fraud facilitation
- operating or administering an online venue used for carding activity

## Why This Matters

From a historical perspective, the case is useful when documenting:

- the evolution of **carding forums** into organized service ecosystems
- how **law enforcement** targets infrastructure and administrators, not only end-users
- the role of **international cooperation** (and friction) in cybercrime prosecutions

## Related Concepts

- **Carding**: fraud involving stolen payment-card data.
- **Darknet markets / forums**: hidden-service communities used for illicit trade and coordination.
- **KYC/identity laundering**: techniques used to mask beneficial ownership or user identity in online crime.
