## Summary

Swedish career criminal central to the 1973 Norrmalmstorg robbery that gave rise to the term 'Stockholm syndrome.'

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- Olofsson was born in Trollhättan, Sweden (1947).[^1]
- He became widely known in Sweden as a high-profile criminal figure and was later described as a "celebrity gangster" in popular accounts.[^1]
- Wikipedia reports he died on 24 June 2025 in Arvika, Sweden (some biographical summaries may not yet be updated everywhere).[^1]

## Why He Is Historically Notable

The 1973 Norrmalmstorg bank robbery/hostage crisis in Stockholm became internationally famous and is commonly cited as the origin story for the term "Stockholm syndrome"—a label used to describe a hostage forming a bond with a captor. Olofsson's role in that event is the main reason his name remains frequently referenced decades later.[^1]

## Norrmalmstorg Robbery (1973) — Context

- The incident took place in Stockholm at Norrmalmstorg, involving a bank robbery that turned into a multi-day hostage crisis.[^1]
- Olofsson was brought into the situation from prison at the request of the robber, becoming a central figure in the standoff narrative.[^1]
- The event received intense media coverage and later became a touchstone in discussions of crisis negotiation and public fascination with criminals.[^1]

## Later Life (Very Brief)

- His adult life, as summarized in public biographies, involved repeated convictions and long periods of incarceration.[^1]
- Later accounts also describe periods living outside Sweden and further legal trouble connected to drug-related crimes.[^1]

## Cultural Depictions

- Olofsson's life story has been adapted into documentaries and dramatizations; a Netflix drama series titled *Clark* (2022) is one prominent example.[^1]
