## Summary

Nordine Amrani threw grenades and opened fire at a Christmas market in Liège, Belgium, killing 6 and injuring 125 before killing himself.

The incident is commonly referred to as the 2011 Liège attack and is cited as one of Belgium’s deadliest violent public attacks in recent decades.

## Context

- **Location**: central Liège, Wallonia, Belgium (a busy public square used for holiday events).
- **Timing**: during a Christmas market, when pedestrian density is high and families are present.

## Attack overview

High-level sequence (as summarized in public reporting):

1. The attacker arrived near the market area.
2. **Explosives** (grenades) were thrown into the crowd.
3. **Firearms** were used to shoot at civilians and police.
4. The attacker died by **suicide** at the scene.

## Perpetrator

- **Name**: Nordine Amrani.
- Public sources describe prior interactions with law enforcement and weapons-related concerns, which became part of the post-incident scrutiny.

## Casualties and immediate response

- **Fatalities**: 6 (including the perpetrator).
- **Injured**: 125.
- Emergency response included rapid triage, transport to hospitals in the region, and a security sweep of the surrounding city center.

## Aftermath and significance

- The attack prompted public debate in Belgium about:
  - risk assessment for violent offenders,
  - weapons access and monitoring,
  - public-space security during mass gatherings.
- Annual remembrance reporting and historical summaries often reference the event in discussions of public safety and extremist/violent lone-actor attacks (classification varies by source).
