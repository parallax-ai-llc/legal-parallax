## Summary

Brazilian militia leader linked to the Bolsonaro family, killed in a police operation.

## Background

- **Full name:** Adriano Magalhães da Nóbrega.
- **Country/region:** Brazil, with public reporting focused on **Rio de Janeiro** and later **Bahia**.
- He became widely known through investigations and journalism about **militias**—armed criminal groups that have operated in parts of Rio de Janeiro.

## Allegations and public reporting

Public sources commonly describe him as:

- A **former** member of state security forces who later became a **suspected militia figure**.
- A person cited by investigators as connected to (or a leader within) the militia group often referred to as **"Escritório do Crime"** (allegations).
- A figure whose case gained national attention due to **reported political and family-adjacent links** involving individuals connected to **Flávio Bolsonaro** (reporting and ongoing investigations at the time).

> Note: Many details in this case are covered through investigative reporting and legal proceedings; claims should be treated as **allegations** unless confirmed by court findings.

## Death

- He was killed on **9 February 2020** during a police operation in the state of **Bahia**.
- Brazilian authorities and major outlets reported that he was **a fugitive** at the time.

## Why the case mattered

High-level reasons his name appears frequently in modern Brazilian political/crime coverage:

- It sits at the intersection of **organized crime**, **militia structures**, and **political controversy**.
- It became a reference point in broader debates about:
  - accountability for militia violence,
  - corruption and patronage networks,
  - the relationship between local security actors and criminal control of territory.
