## Summary

Italian guerrilla leader and bandit who fought against the French occupation of Naples.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1806 | Captured and hanged by French forces |

## Background and Identity

- **Common name**: Fra Diavolo ("Brother Devil")
- **Birth name**: Michele Pezza
- He is most often described as a **guerrilla leader** active in southern Italy during the **Napoleonic period**, operating at the boundary between insurgency and banditry.

## Historical Context

- The Kingdom of Naples was repeatedly drawn into conflict during the **French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars**.
- French military and administrative pressure in the region helped fuel **local resistance**, which ranged from organized royalist forces to irregular bands.

## Activities (High Level)

- Led small, mobile groups that relied on **local knowledge**, **ambush tactics**, and **hit-and-run raids**.
- Became associated with **royalist / anti-French** resistance in the Neapolitan countryside.
- His reputation was shaped both by **military actions** and by later retellings that framed him as either a criminal bandit or a patriotic fighter.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- After capture and execution, Fra Diavolo persisted in popular memory as a **folk figure** linked to resistance and outlaw mythology.
- His story influenced later cultural works; the best-known is Daniel-François-Esprit **Auber's opera _Fra Diavolo_** (1830), which adapts the legend rather than serving as a strict historical account.

## Notes on Sources

- Many concise biographies rely heavily on secondary summaries; for careful historical work, prefer **archival-based studies** of southern Italian insurgency and Napoleonic occupation.
- When comparing accounts, separate (1) **documented events** (capture, execution, official reports) from (2) **legendary embellishment** in later literature and opera.
