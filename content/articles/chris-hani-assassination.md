## Summary

SACP leader Chris Hani was assassinated by a Polish immigrant and a Conservative Party MP, nearly derailing South Africa's transition to democracy. Mandela's appeal prevented civil war.

## Background and Context

- **Chris Hani** was a prominent anti-apartheid leader and the General Secretary of the **South African Communist Party (SACP)**, widely viewed as influential among younger and militant activists.
- The assassination occurred during a fragile period of **multi-party negotiations** aimed at ending apartheid and establishing majority rule.
- Political violence and distrust between factions meant that a high-profile killing carried an elevated risk of **retaliation and large-scale unrest**.

## Perpetrators and Motive (High-Level)

- The killing is widely reported to have been carried out by **Janusz Waluś**, with the involvement of **Clive Derby-Lewis**.
- Contemporary reporting and later court findings framed the act as politically motivated, intended to destabilize negotiations and provoke broader conflict.

## Immediate Aftermath

- Public anger and fear of escalation rose quickly following the shooting.
- **Nelson Mandela** delivered a nationally televised appeal urging restraint, an intervention frequently credited with helping prevent a spiral into wider violence.
- The event increased pressure on negotiating parties to produce a credible timeline and pathway toward elections.

## Political Impact

- The assassination is commonly described as a major **stress test** of South Africa’s transition, revealing how easily the process could be derailed.
- It also underscored the need for strong, publicly legitimate leadership capable of calming tensions at moments of crisis.
- In historical accounts, it is often linked to an acceleration of commitments toward South Africa’s first democratic national election (held in 1994).

## Investigation, Trial, and Sentencing (Overview)

- The perpetrators were arrested, prosecuted, and convicted.
- They were initially sentenced to death; following constitutional and legal changes, the sentences were **commuted to life imprisonment**.
- Over the years, the case has remained a public touchstone in debates about accountability, political violence, and parole in post-apartheid South Africa.

## Legacy

- The assassination is remembered both as a near-catastrophic moment and as an example of crisis leadership shaping the course of a national transition.
- It remains frequently cited in discussions of political reconciliation, the costs of extremism, and the vulnerability of negotiated settlements.
