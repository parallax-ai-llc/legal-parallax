## Summary

Brazilian money changer who served as the financial operator for numerous corrupt politicians. His cooperation provided crucial evidence in multiple corruption investigations.

## Background

- Funaro is described as a *doleiro* (money changer), a role associated in Brazil with arranging currency exchange, off-the-books transfers, and financial intermediation.
- Public reporting portrays him as a financial operator who moved funds and structured payments for political and business figures, rather than as an elected official.

## Role in major corruption investigations

- Funaro became widely known in the context of Brazil’s large-scale anti-corruption investigations in the 2010s (often referred to in English as the “Car Wash” investigations).
- In that reporting, he is characterized as an intermediary who helped route bribe payments and other illicit funds through corporate and financial channels.
- His alleged activities were linked to networks involving contractors, political parties, and government-linked entities, with the operational details varying across cases.

## Cooperation and testimony

- In 2017, Funaro agreed to cooperate with investigators (a form of plea/cooperation agreement in Brazil).
- Cooperation agreements in this context typically include:
  - providing documentary evidence (e.g., bank records, transaction trails, and communications);
  - describing payment mechanisms and intermediaries;
  - identifying participants and decision-makers;
  - corroborating other witness statements.
- Multiple outlets have described his cooperation as significant because it helped investigators connect financial flows to specific political and business actors.

## Why he is notable (high-level)

- Illustrates the importance of “financial operators” in complex corruption schemes, where the movement of money can be separated from the public-facing decision makers.
- Frequently cited as an example of how currency exchange networks and shell structures can be used to obscure origin, destination, and purpose of funds.

## Related terms and entities

- *Doleiro*: informal money changer; in corruption coverage, often a specialist in moving money outside normal compliance controls.
- Plea/cooperation agreement (*delação premiada*): negotiated testimony and evidence in exchange for legal benefits, used extensively in Brazilian corruption cases.

## Additional timeline notes

- 2010s: Named in major anti-corruption investigations and detained as part of broader enforcement actions.
- 2017: Cooperation agreement reported; testimony and evidence cited across related proceedings.
