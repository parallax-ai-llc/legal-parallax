## Background

- **Full name**: Nazih Abdul-Hamed al-Ruqai (also spelled al-Rugai).
- **Known as**: Abu Anas al-Libi.
- **Origin**: Libyan national reported to have lived for extended periods outside Libya, including time in the United Kingdom.

## Alleged Role in Militant Networks

- Widely described in public reporting as an **al-Qaeda operative**.
- U.S. authorities accused him of involvement connected to **the 1998 U.S. embassy bombings** in East Africa (Nairobi, Kenya and Dar es Salaam, Tanzania).
- He was **indicted in U.S. federal court** as part of cases related to the 1998 bombings.

## Capture and U.S. Prosecution

- **2013 capture**: Detained by U.S. forces during an operation in Tripoli, Libya.
- After capture, he was transferred to U.S. custody and later brought to the United States to face charges.
- His case proceeded in the U.S. federal court system, with reporting noting disputes about health and legal process.

## Death

- Died in early **2015** while in U.S. custody.
- Public accounts commonly cite **health complications** as a major factor around the end of his legal proceedings.

## Why This Figure Appears in Historical Timelines

- The 2013 operation was frequently discussed as an example of **extraterritorial counterterrorism action**.
- His prosecution was part of long-running U.S. efforts to pursue suspects connected to the **1998 embassy attacks**, which were a major precursor event in the broader U.S.–al-Qaeda conflict.
