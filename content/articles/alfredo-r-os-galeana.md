## Context: why prison escapes matter

Repeat escapes typically become a major part of criminal notoriety because they imply:

- **Operational planning:** Escapes often require coordination, tools, inside help, or exploiting procedural weaknesses.
- **Institutional embarrassment:** Each escape draws attention to prison security failures.
- **Myth-building:** Public fascination can turn escapees into “legend” figures, especially when stories are retold without clear sourcing.
