## Summary

KKK members bombed the 16th Street Baptist Church in Birmingham, Alabama, killing four young girls. The attack galvanized support for the civil rights movement and the Civil Rights Act.

## Background

- The 16th Street Baptist Church in Birmingham, Alabama was a central meeting place for Black community organizing and the civil rights movement.
- Birmingham had a long history of segregationist violence, earning the nickname “Bombingham” due to repeated bombings targeting Black residents and institutions.
- The bombing occurred amid heightened tension following major civil rights protests earlier in 1963, including the Birmingham campaign.

## What Happened

- Date: 1963-09-15 (a Sunday morning).
- A bomb was placed near the church and detonated as congregants were preparing for services.
- The explosion killed four girls and injured many others, shocking the nation.

## Victims

The four girls killed in the bombing were:

- Addie Mae Collins
- Denise McNair
- Carole Robertson
- Cynthia Wesley

## Investigation and Prosecutions

- The attack was linked to members of the Ku Klux Klan; legal accountability took decades.
- Robert Edward Chambliss was convicted for the murders in 1977.
- Thomas Edwin Blanton Jr. was convicted in 2001.
- Bobby Frank Cherry was convicted in 2002.
- A fourth suspected participant, Herman Frank Cash, died in 1994 without being prosecuted.

## Impact and Legacy

- The bombing intensified national outrage and helped build momentum for federal civil rights legislation.
- It became a defining example of white supremacist terror used to resist desegregation.
- The church and the victims remain central to public memory of the civil rights struggle in the United States.

## Key Takeaways

- The event illustrates how violence against civilians was used to intimidate civil rights advocates.
- The long gap between the crime and convictions is often cited as an example of delayed justice.
- The victims’ names are frequently invoked in education and memorialization efforts about the era.
