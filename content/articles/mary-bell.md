## Summary
Mary Flora Bell strangled two young boys in Newcastle in 1968 when she was only 10-11 years old. She was convicted of manslaughter and detained for 12 years. She was released with a new identity.

## Background
- **Full name**: Mary Flora Bell.
- **Place associated with the crimes**: Newcastle upon Tyne, England.
- **Age at the time of the killings (1968)**: 10 years old at the first killing; 11 by the time of trial.

## The 1968 Killings (High-Level)
- **Victims**: Two boys, aged **4** and **3**, were killed in separate incidents in Newcastle in 1968.
- **Method**: Both killings involved **strangulation**.
- **Public impact**: The case drew significant attention because of Bell’s age and the rarity of child-on-child homicide at that time.

## Investigation, Trial, and Conviction
- Bell was charged and tried in 1968.
- She was convicted of **manslaughter** (not murder) and ordered to be detained “at Her Majesty’s pleasure,” an indeterminate sentence used for juvenile offenders in the UK.

## Detention and Release
- **Detention length**: Approximately **12 years**.
- **Release**: Bell was released in 1980.
- **Anonymity protections**: UK courts later granted Bell (and her child) **lifelong anonymity** due to security concerns.

## Later Life and Public Debate
- The case is frequently discussed in the context of:
  - **Juvenile justice** and how legal systems treat very young offenders.
  - **Media ethics**, including the balance between open justice and protecting vulnerable individuals from harm.
  - **Risk and rehabilitation**, particularly whether (and how) severe childhood offenders can reintegrate.
