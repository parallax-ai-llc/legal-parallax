## Summary

Polish-born South African who assassinated anti-apartheid leader Chris Hani, nearly derailing peace process.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1993 | Assassinated Chris Hani |

## Background

- Born in Zakopane, Poland (Polish People's Republic); later emigrated to South Africa in the early 1980s.[^1]
- Became involved in far-right, pro-apartheid politics in South Africa, including membership in the National Party and the Afrikaner Weerstandsbeweging (AWB).[^1]

## Assassination of Chris Hani (1993)

- Date: 10 April 1993 (Easter Saturday), during negotiations to end apartheid.[^1]
- Location: outside Hani's home in Boksburg (Johannesburg area).[^1]
- Method: shot Hani at close range; Hani died at the scene.[^1]
- Capture: a neighbour recorded the fleeing car's registration, leading to his arrest.[^1]
- Investigation: evidence connected Conservative Party MP Clive Derby-Lewis to instigating and arranging the murder weapon; police also found a hit-list that included other political leaders.[^1]

## Legal Proceedings and Sentence

- Convicted of murder and initially sentenced to death; after South Africa abolished capital punishment, the sentence was commuted to life imprisonment.[^1]
- Applied for amnesty through the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC), which was not granted.[^1]

## Parole and Later Status

- Parole was refused multiple times; South Africa's Constitutional Court ordered his release on parole in November 2022.[^1]
- Released on parole on 7 December 2022; later returned to Poland.[^1]

## Impact and Historical Significance

- The assassination contributed to heightened political tension and fears of destabilizing South Africa's transition; it is widely treated as a major event in late-apartheid and transition-era history.[^1]
- The case is frequently discussed alongside the role of far-right networks and political violence during the early 1990s.

## Quick Facts

- Known for: assassination of Chris Hani (South African Communist Party leader; ANC armed wing MK chief of staff).[^1]
- Co-accused/associate: Clive Derby-Lewis (linked by investigators to planning/instigation).[^1]
