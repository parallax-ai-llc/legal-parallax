## Summary

Kim Collins won the 2003 World Championship 100m gold, making Saint Kitts and Nevis the smallest country to produce a World Champion.

## Key Achievements

- **2003**: Won the **World Championships 100 m** title in Paris, defeating a field that included several of the era’s top sprinters.
- Represented **Saint Kitts and Nevis** at multiple **Olympic Games** and **World Championships** across a long international career.

## Competitive Profile

- **Primary event**: 100 metres (sprint)
- Known for **longevity** at the elite level, continuing to compete internationally well beyond the typical peak years for sprinters.

## Additional Career Notes (high level)

- Competed regularly on the global circuit (including major championships and international meets), helping keep Saint Kitts and Nevis visible in top-level sprinting.
- His 2003 world title is frequently cited as a landmark result for **small nations** in athletics.

## Legacy

Kim Collins is remembered primarily for his **2003 100 m world title** and for demonstrating that sustained elite sprinting success can come from a **small Caribbean federation** with limited resources.
