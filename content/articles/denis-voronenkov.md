## Background and Public Profile

- Denis Nikolayevich Voronenkov (Russian: Денис Николаевич Вороненков) was a Russian politician and former member of the State Duma.
- After leaving Russia, he became a high-profile defector in Ukraine and was publicly associated with Russian domestic politics and security issues.

## Political and Professional Context

- He served as a deputy in the State Duma (the lower house of Russia's Federal Assembly).
- His post-Duma period was marked by legal and political controversy, which contributed to his decision to relocate.

## Defection to Ukraine

- Voronenkov moved to Ukraine and positioned himself as a defector from Russia.
- In Ukrainian public discourse, he was discussed as a figure who could provide testimony or insight into Russian political affairs.

## Assassination (2017)

- Voronenkov was shot and killed in Kyiv (Kiev) on 23 March 2017.
- The killing took place in daylight and was widely treated as a targeted assassination.
- The incident drew international attention because of his defector status and the Russia–Ukraine political context.

## Immediate Aftermath

- Ukrainian authorities publicly investigated the killing as a political and security incident.
- Media coverage focused on possible motives linked to his defection and prior political role.
