## Summary

Crypto Ponzi scheme that defrauded Latino communities of millions with false mining promises.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2018 | IcomTech begins operating (reported by later court filings and news coverage) |
| 2019 | Operations and public visibility diminish as complaints and scrutiny increase |
| 2022 | U.S. authorities publicly announce criminal charges tied to the scheme |
| 2023 | A founder is convicted in U.S. federal court on fraud-related charges |

## What It Promised

- **High, steady returns** presented as coming from crypto mining and/or automated trading.
- **Low perceived risk** messaging (e.g., “guaranteed” or “consistent” payouts).
- **Exclusive access** framing through paid “packages” or memberships.

## How the Scheme Commonly Worked (High Level)

- Participants paid money (often in cash or crypto) to join and buy investment “packages.”
- Recruiters earned commissions for bringing in new members (MLM-style structure).
- Early withdrawals, when available, were typically funded by later deposits.
- Marketing emphasized community trust and word-of-mouth recruitment.

## Who Was Targeted / Where It Spread

- Frequently described in reporting as concentrating on **Spanish-speaking and Latino communities**.
- Promotion often relied on in-person events, social media, and local networks.

## Impact (Publicly Reported)

- Victims reported significant financial losses.
- Allegations and prosecutions describe losses reaching **millions of dollars**.

## Legal Outcome (General)

- U.S. federal prosecutors pursued fraud and related charges connected to fundraising, marketing claims, and movement of funds.
- Court proceedings resulted in at least one conviction reported in 2023.

## Red Flags (Educational)

- “Guaranteed” or unusually consistent returns in volatile markets.
- Heavy emphasis on recruiting rather than verifiable revenue.
- Vague explanations of the underlying trading/mining operations.
- Difficulty withdrawing funds or frequent “technical” delays.
