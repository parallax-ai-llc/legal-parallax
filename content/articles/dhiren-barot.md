## Background

- Dhiren Barot (also reported in some sources as Issa al-Hindi) was a British-Indian Islamist militant associated with al-Qaeda.[^1]
- Reporting on his early life is limited in open sources; biographies generally focus on his activities in the late 1990s and early 2000s.[^1]

## Activities and Alleged Plots (High-Level)

- UK authorities linked Barot to reconnaissance and planning for attacks, including targets in the United States and the United Kingdom.[^1]
- The most frequently cited reporting concerns plans aimed at financial and infrastructure targets, including the New York Stock Exchange and sites in London.[^1]

## Arrest, Trial, and Conviction

- Barot was arrested in the United Kingdom in the mid-2000s and prosecuted under terrorism-related offenses.[^1]
- In 2006, he was convicted of conspiracy to murder, reflecting the UK court’s finding that he participated in attack planning.[^1]

## Additional Timeline (Narrative)

- 2004: UK police investigations intensified against multiple suspects connected to alleged al-Qaeda-linked plotting.[^1]
- 2006: Convicted in the UK (conspiracy to murder).[^1]
