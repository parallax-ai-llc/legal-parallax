## Background

Henry Okah is associated with militant activism linked to the Niger Delta conflict in Nigeria, a long-running dispute involving oil-producing communities, environmental degradation, and demands for greater local control of resources and revenue distribution.

## Role in Niger Delta Militancy (High-level)

Public reporting commonly connects Okah with the Movement for the Emancipation of the Niger Delta (MEND), an umbrella label used by different armed groups active in the mid-to-late 2000s. MEND-related violence was often framed as pressure on the Nigerian state and oil industry through kidnappings, sabotage, and attacks on infrastructure.

Key themes often cited in coverage of Niger Delta militancy include:

- Grievances over pollution and livelihoods in oil-producing areas.
- Calls for increased revenue sharing and political representation.
- Tactics targeting oil facilities, pipelines, and personnel.

## South Africa Prosecution

Okah was tried in South Africa under laws that allow domestic prosecution for certain terrorism-related offenses with cross-border elements. The case is notable because it applied South African jurisdiction to attacks that occurred in Nigeria.

The best-known facts from widely cited summaries:

- The charges related to car bombings in Abuja, Nigeria.
- The bombings were reported to have killed 12 people.
- A South African court convicted Okah in 2013.

## Significance

Okah’s case is frequently referenced in discussions of:

- The internationalization of counter-terrorism investigations.
- Extraterritorial jurisdiction and when states prosecute acts committed abroad.
- The Niger Delta conflict’s evolution from local insurgency dynamics to cases addressed through international legal systems.

## Related Terms

- **Niger Delta conflict**: A broad term for militant activity and political-economic disputes in Nigeria’s oil-producing region.
- **MEND**: A label associated with various armed groups and claims of responsibility during the height of Niger Delta militancy.
- **Extraterritorial prosecution**: Legal approach where a country prosecutes conduct that occurred outside its borders under certain conditions.
