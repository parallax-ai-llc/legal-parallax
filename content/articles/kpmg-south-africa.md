## Summary

KPMG South Africa was implicated in state capture for conducting a discredited intelligence report used to justify tax investigations against Zuma's opponents. Senior partners resigned.

## Context

KPMG South Africa is the South African member firm of the global KPMG network. The firm became a prominent example in South Africa’s broader “state capture” era, when private interests were alleged to have exerted improper influence over public institutions.

## What the controversy centered on

High-level allegations and public reporting commonly focused on:

- **A “rogue unit”/intelligence report**: work that was criticized for being methodologically weak or misleading, and that was referenced in political and tax-enforcement disputes.
- **Work for politically connected clients**: scrutiny of engagements involving figures and companies linked in the public narrative to state capture.
- **Governance and accountability failures**: questions about internal review, partner oversight, and risk management in accepting or delivering sensitive assignments.

## Consequences and aftermath

Publicly reported consequences in 2017 included:

- **Senior leadership changes**: multiple partners and senior executives resigned or were removed amid the fallout.
- **Reputational damage**: loss of trust from parts of the public, clients, and civil society.
- **Remedial actions**: commitments to strengthen controls, review past work, and address independence and quality processes.

## Why it mattered (in the state capture story)

The episode was notable because it:

- Illustrated how **professional services work products** (reports, opinions, and advisory outputs) can be leveraged in political narratives.
- Raised concerns about **ethical boundaries** in high-stakes engagements involving government and politically exposed persons.
- Became a reference point in debates about **audit quality, independence, and accountability** in South Africa.
