## Summary

British-Irish arms trader in East Africa hanged by Belgians for selling weapons to Arab slave traders.

## Context

- Stokes operated during the late-19th-century scramble for influence in the Great Lakes and Congo regions.
- European colonial administrations often treated arms trading as a political and security issue rather than a normal commercial activity.
- In that setting, supplying firearms to rival groups (or to groups labeled as enemies) could be prosecuted as a colonial crime.

## Activities in East Africa (high level)

- Worked as a trader with routes that connected interior markets to coastal supply lines.
- Became associated with arms sales in the region, including sales to actors involved in slave-raiding networks.
- His commercial activity intersected with competing authorities: local rulers, coastal intermediaries, and European colonial forces.

## The "Stokes Affair"

- Stokes was arrested by Belgian authorities and executed in 1895.
- The execution became a diplomatic scandal commonly referred to as the **Stokes Affair**.
- Contemporary criticism focused on due process (a rapid military-style proceeding and the lack of a standard civilian trial).
- The incident fed broader European debates about the legitimacy and methods of rule in the Congo Free State.

## Significance and Legacy

- Often cited as an early, high-profile example of international criticism of abuses and arbitrary justice in the Congo Free State.
- Illustrates how colonial regimes used legal process and punishment to project authority and control.
- Remembered primarily through the diplomatic controversy rather than through a long personal political career.

## Key Terms

- **Congo Free State**: Personal colony of King Leopold II of Belgium (1885–1908), administered separately from Belgium proper.
- **Scramble for Africa**: Period of intensified European colonization and territorial claims in Africa (late 19th century).
- **Arms trading**: In colonial contexts, frequently regulated and criminalized when viewed as enabling resistance or rival forces.
