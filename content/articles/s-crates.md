## Additional Context

### Player profile (high-level)

- Often remembered as a technically gifted, intelligent midfielder associated with an elegant style of play.
- Commentary about his game typically emphasizes vision, passing, and late runs into scoring positions rather than pure athleticism.

### 1982 Brazil and football culture

- The 1982 Brazil national team is frequently cited as a symbol of an expressive, attack-minded tradition, even though it did not win the tournament.
- As captain, Sócrates is commonly positioned as a central figure in the narrative of that team’s identity and influence on how fans remember the era.

### Physician-athlete identity

- His reputation as a trained physician is often highlighted as unusual among elite footballers and as part of his public persona.
- This dual identity contributed to portrayals of him as an articulate public intellectual in Brazilian sports life.

### Corinthians Democracy (high-level)

- The Corinthians Democracy movement is widely described as a player-led effort advocating for more democratic decision-making within the club.
- In broader retellings, it is also used as a cultural reference point for the relationship between sport, civic participation, and political expression in Brazil.

### Political and social symbolism

- He is frequently referenced in discussions of Brazilian public life where football intersects with debates about democracy and civic engagement.
- The story is often told as an example of athletes using prominence to signal values, not only to win matches.

### Legacy

- Remains a touchstone figure for conversations about football as culture: aesthetics, leadership, and the responsibilities of public figures.
- In club history, the Corinthians Democracy era continues to be cited as part of the club’s identity and mythology.
