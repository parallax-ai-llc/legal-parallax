## Summary

Dutch criminal who helped kidnap beer magnate Freddy Heineken before being assassinated.

## Background

- Cornelis ("Cor") van Hout was a Dutch criminal associated with the Amsterdam underworld.
- Public accounts primarily describe him in connection with the 1983 kidnapping of Freddy Heineken.

## Heineken kidnapping (1983)

- On **9 November 1983**, Freddy Heineken and his driver **Ab Doderer** were abducted in Amsterdam.
- Van Hout is widely described as a **mastermind/organizer** of the operation, working with Willem Holleeder and several accomplices.[^1]
- The victims were held for roughly **three weeks** (including confinement in a hut/warehouse setting in Westpoort), while the kidnappers demanded a ransom reported at **35 million Dutch guilders**.[^1]
- The hostages were released on **30 November 1983**.[^1]

## Arrest, trial, and imprisonment

- Van Hout and Holleeder fled to **Paris** after the release, but were arrested by French police on **29 February 1984**.[^1]
- After extradition proceedings, van Hout was ultimately extradited back to the Netherlands on **31 October 1986**.[^1]
- He was sentenced in **February 1987** to **11 years** in prison (with credit for time served).[^1]

## Later years and death

- Later reporting describes additional criminal cases and prison time after his release(s), though some details are inconsistently sourced in public summaries.[^1]
- After earlier failed attempts, he was assassinated on **24 January 2003** in **Amstelveen**.[^1]
- Dutch reporting later connected the assassination to broader underworld violence tied to figures involved in the Heineken kidnapping.[^1]
