## Background

- Described by major secondary sources as a lawyer and legal academic working across Pacific jurisdictions.
- Sources report he was born in the Colony of Fiji and educated in Australia.
- Wikipedia and some reporting also list professional honors (e.g., QC) and a Solomon Islands decoration (CSI).

## Pacific legal and academic work (high-level)

- Reported to have taught and written in areas including constitutional law and international law.
- Cited as having held visiting or adjunct academic roles (including at Bond University) and participating in international-law associations.
- Became a prominent legal adviser within Solomon Islands politics, culminating in his appointment as Attorney General.

## Extradition and political controversy

- Allegations concerned an incident said to have occurred in Vanuatu in 1997; the case became politically sensitive because it intersected with regional diplomacy and domestic Solomon Islands politics.
- In 2006 he was arrested in Papua New Guinea in connection with Australian efforts to have him extradited, later obtained bail, and then sought refuge at the Solomon Islands High Commission in Port Moresby (per Wikipedia).
- In October 2006 he was flown from Papua New Guinea to the Solomon Islands and then detained on arrival by RAMSI officers (per Wikipedia).
- In July 2007 Prime Minister Manasseh Sogavare appointed him Attorney General of the Solomon Islands.
- Following a change of government in late 2007, his appointment was terminated and he was extradited to Australia in December 2007, where he was arrested on arrival.
