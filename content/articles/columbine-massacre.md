## Summary

Eric Harris and Dylan Klebold killed 13 and wounded 21 at Columbine High School before committing suicide. The massacre became a touchstone for debates about guns, bullying, and school safety.

## Background and Context

- Columbine High School is in Columbine, an unincorporated area of Jefferson County, Colorado, near Littleton.
- The attack occurred during a period of heightened concern in the United States about youth violence and school safety.
- Early public narratives emphasized bullying as a primary driver; later research and investigative reporting described a more complex set of factors including ideology, personal grievances, mental health, and planning.

## Perpetrators

- The perpetrators were students Eric Harris and Dylan Klebold.
- Investigations documented extensive planning, including written material and recorded statements left behind by the perpetrators.
- Law enforcement and academic discussions often note that the perpetrators sought notoriety, which has influenced later media-coverage guidelines.

## Overview of the Attack (High-Level)

- The assault involved firearms and attempted explosives; many of the explosive devices failed to detonate as intended.
- The incident ended when both perpetrators died by suicide inside the school.
- The event resulted in 13 fatalities and 21 people wounded by gunfire, with additional injuries occurring during evacuation.

## Immediate Response and Investigation

- The initial police response and tactical decisions were heavily scrutinized; Columbine became a case study for active-shooter response doctrine.
- Multiple investigations followed, including official reports and extensive media coverage.
- Evidence from the case contributed to changes in emergency preparedness training for schools and law enforcement agencies.

## Aftermath and Broader Impact

- The massacre strongly influenced debates on gun policy, school security measures, and youth mental health.
- It shaped cultural conversations about violent media, social dynamics in schools, and the ethics of perpetrator-focused attention.
- Researchers later discussed possible "contagion" or copycat effects, leading to recommendations to reduce sensationalism and avoid amplifying attacker identities.

## Memorials and Commemoration

- Victims were commemorated through community memorials and ongoing remembrance events.
- The site and surrounding community became focal points for discussions about healing, trauma, and prevention.
