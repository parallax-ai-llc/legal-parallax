## Summary

19-year-old gunman killed 9 people including 7 children and 2 teachers at School No. 175 in Kazan. Led to tighter gun ownership laws in Russia.

## Background

- The shooting occurred in the city of **Kazan**, the capital of the Republic of **Tatarstan** in the Russian Federation.
- The incident took place at **School No. 175** (often described in reporting as a gymnasium/secondary school).
- Public reporting identified the perpetrator as **Ilnaz Galyaviev**, who was 19 years old at the time of the attack.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- On **11 May 2021**, the attacker entered the school and opened fire, killing **9 people** in total.
- Among the victims were **children** (students) and **adult staff** (teachers).
- Additional people were **injured**, and emergency services responded to evacuate and treat victims.
- The attacker was **detained/arrested alive** shortly after the incident.

## Immediate Aftermath

- The attack prompted a national response in Russia, including mourning and public statements from federal and regional authorities.
- Security and access-control measures around schools were discussed and, in many places, reviewed or strengthened.

## Legal Outcome (Reported)

- Russian authorities prosecuted the perpetrator for the killings.
- Public reporting later stated that the perpetrator received a **life sentence** in connection with the attack.

## Broader Impact

- The Kazan school shooting was widely cited in discussions of **firearms regulation** and **public safety**.
- It contributed to changes described as **tighter gun-ownership rules**, alongside renewed attention to how weapons are acquired, stored, and controlled.
