## Summary

See John Gotti. Nickname reference.

This entry captures “Dapper Don” as a **nickname associated with John Joseph Gotti Jr. (1940–2002)**, an American mafia figure who led the **Gambino crime family**. The moniker is most often used in retrospective coverage to describe his **stylish, public-facing presentation** during a period of unusually high media attention.

## Notes on the Nickname

- **“Dapper Don”** is commonly cited as one of the press nicknames for **John Gotti** (Gambino crime family boss), alongside terms like “Teflon Don.”
- The phrase refers to his **well-tailored suits and polished public image**, which contrasted with the typically low-profile approach of many organized-crime figures.
- In most historical contexts, “Dapper Don” is **not a separate person** but a **moniker** used for Gotti.

## Historical Context (High Level)

- Gotti’s rise occurred during a period of intense law-enforcement focus on U.S. organized crime, including the use of **RICO** statutes.
- His visibility and relationship with tabloid/TV coverage became part of the broader story of how media exposure intersected with late-20th-century organized-crime prosecutions.
