## Summary

Lawyer who pleaded guilty to conspiracy for creating the fake electors scheme.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2020 | Helped develop legal strategies and written memos related to attempts to keep Donald Trump in office after the 2020 election. |
| 2021 | Associated with efforts to organize slates of pro-Trump "alternate" electors in several states. |
| 2023 | Pleaded guilty in the Georgia election-interference case to a conspiracy charge related to filing false documents. |

## Role in the "fake electors" plan

- Widely reported as one of the key legal architects who produced memos outlining how alternate elector slates might be used if official results were contested.
- Worked with other lawyers and political operatives involved in post-election litigation and coordination across states.
- The plan has been described in public reporting as attempting to create a paper trail of competing electoral votes to influence certification or subsequent legal proceedings.

## Legal proceedings (high level)

- Became a named defendant in investigations and prosecutions concerning 2020 election subversion.
- In 2023, entered a guilty plea in Georgia, which reduced potential exposure at trial and placed him in a cooperating posture (subject to the terms of the plea agreement).
- His case is frequently referenced as an example of how attorneys can face criminal liability for advising or coordinating actions that prosecutors frame as fraudulent documentation schemes.

## Why he matters

- Illustrates the intersection of legal memo-writing, political pressure campaigns, and operational coordination in modern election-dispute strategies.
- Serves as a commonly cited node in the network of individuals connected to the broader "fake electors" effort.
- The public record around his memos is used by journalists, researchers, and prosecutors to reconstruct timelines and intent.
