## Expanded Context

### Role and significance

Habib al-Adly served as Egypt’s Minister of Interior for a long period during the Hosni Mubarak era, overseeing police and internal security institutions that were central to regime stability.

### Public controversy

He became a prominent symbol of police power and alleged abuses, particularly as public anger about policing and accountability intensified in the late 2000s and during the 2011 uprising.

### 2011 revolution and allegations

During the January–February 2011 revolution, Egypt experienced mass protests and violent clashes. Al-Adly was later prosecuted in connection with allegations that security forces used lethal force against demonstrators.

### Legal proceedings (high-level)

Court cases after 2011 included convictions and retrials in different proceedings related to protester deaths and corruption allegations. Reporting and court outcomes shifted over time across separate cases, making it useful to treat “convicted” as case-specific rather than a single final status.
