## Summary

Dina Asher-Smith was the first British woman to win a World sprint gold (200m, 2019).

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2011 | Began competing internationally (youth level). |
| 2013 | Won European Junior titles (including 200m) and relay success at junior level. |
| 2014 | Won World Junior 100m title. |
| 2015 | Became the first British woman to run under 11 seconds for 100m; broke the British 200m record at the World Championships. |
| 2016 | Won European Championship 200m title; also part of Great Britain’s Olympic 4×100m relay bronze-medal team (Rio). |
| 2018 | Won the European Championship 100m/200m double; also won European 4×100m relay gold. |
| 2019 | Won World Championship 200m gold (Doha), plus additional medals at the same championships; set British records in 100m and 200m (10.83 / 21.88). |
| 2020 | Part of Great Britain’s Olympic 4×100m relay bronze-medal team (Tokyo). |
| 2022 | Won World Championship 200m bronze (Eugene). |
| 2024 | Part of Great Britain’s Olympic 4×100m relay silver-medal team (Paris). |

## Notable Achievements

- First British woman to win a World Championships sprint gold medal (200m, 2019).
- Holds British national records across multiple sprint events (100m, 200m, and indoor 60m).
- European champion in both 100m and 200m, including a major-championship sprint double (2018).
- Diamond League champion over 100m (2019).
