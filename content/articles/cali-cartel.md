## Overview

The **Cali Cartel** (Spanish: *Cartel de Cali*) was a major Colombian drug trafficking organization centered in **Cali**. In public reporting it is often contrasted with the Medellín Cartel: the Cali organization is commonly described as placing greater emphasis on **corruption, intelligence gathering, and financial operations** (including money laundering) rather than overt, highly visible violence.

## Organization and Key Figures

Commonly cited leadership and affiliates include:

- **Gilberto Rodríguez Orejuela** — frequently described as a principal leader.
- **Miguel Rodríguez Orejuela** — frequently described as a principal leader.
- **José Santacruz Londoño** — commonly described as a senior figure.
- **Hélmer "Pacho" Herrera** — commonly described as a senior figure.

(Attribution and exact internal roles vary by source; high-level summaries typically focus on the figures above.)

## Operations (High-Level)

Reported operational characteristics include:

- **Cocaine trafficking** from Colombia into international markets.
- Use of **front companies** and complex financial arrangements to move and launder proceeds.
- **Bribery and political influence** as a means to reduce enforcement risk.
- **Counterintelligence** practices (e.g., monitoring, informant networks) intended to anticipate police activity.

## Law Enforcement and Decline

By the mid-1990s, arrests of top leadership substantially disrupted the organization’s command structure. Over time, the cartel label is generally used historically, while subsequent trafficking activity is often attributed to **fragmented successor networks** rather than the same centralized entity.
