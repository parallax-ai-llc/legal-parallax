## Summary
Olga Hepnarová was the last woman executed in Czechoslovakia. She drove a truck into a crowd of pedestrians in Prague in 1973, killing eight. She was executed by hanging.

## Background (high-level)
- Hepnarová grew up in Prague and later reported long-running social isolation and conflicts with people around her.
- Sources describe intermittent psychiatric assessment and treatment during her adolescence.
- In the early 1970s she worked as a driver, which gave her access to a heavy vehicle used in the 1973 attack.

## 1973 Prague Truck Attack
- **Date/Place:** 10 July 1973, Prague (Czechoslovakia).
- **Method:** Vehicle-ramming attack using a truck.
- **Casualties:** 8 killed; 12 injured.[^2]
- **Immediate outcome:** She was apprehended the same day.[^2]

## Investigation, Trial, and Execution
- Hepnarová was charged and convicted on multiple counts of murder related to the attack.
- She was sentenced to death under the laws in force at the time.
- **Execution:** 12 March 1975, by hanging, at Pankrác Prison in Prague.[^2]

## Historical Significance and Legacy
- Hepnarová is frequently noted as **the last woman executed in Czechoslovakia**.[^2]
- The case is discussed in broader studies of mass violence, capital punishment in Central Europe, and the social/psychiatric framing of criminal responsibility.
- Her actions have been revisited in later journalism and cultural works, often focusing on her writings and stated motives.
