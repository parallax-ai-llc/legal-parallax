## Summary

Former FBI agent who warned about al-Qaeda before 9/11 and interrogated key terrorists.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2001 | Key interrogator of al-Qaeda detainees |

## Additional Context

- **Role and reputation:** Soufan is widely known in public counterterrorism discourse for emphasizing **rapport-based interrogation** and for arguing that coercive methods undermine intelligence collection and legitimacy.
- **Pre-9/11 warnings:** In various accounts and interviews, he has described internal efforts to elevate concerns about al-Qaeda’s threat trajectory prior to the September 11 attacks.
- **Public-facing work:** After government service, he became a frequent commentator on terrorism, intelligence, and national security policy, often translating operational lessons into policy arguments for broader audiences.

## Organizations

- **The Soufan Group (TSG):** Private intelligence and security consultancy associated with Soufan’s post-government work.
- **The Soufan Center:** Nonprofit research and policy organization focused on global security issues, including violent extremism and geopolitical risk.

## Selected Publications (Books)

- *The Black Banners: The Inside Story of 9/11 and the War Against al-Qaeda* (2011)
- *The Way of the Strangers: Encounters with the Islamic State* (2017)

## Key Themes (High-Level)

- **Interrogation ethics and effectiveness:** Consistent advocacy for lawful, evidence-based interrogation practices.
- **Institutional learning:** Emphasis on improving intelligence sharing, analytic rigor, and operational coordination.
- **Evolving threat landscape:** Commentary that distinguishes between different jihadist organizations (e.g., al-Qaeda vs. Islamic State) and how their strategies and recruitment differ over time.
