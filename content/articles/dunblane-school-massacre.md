## Summary

Thomas Hamilton shot dead 16 children and their teacher at Dunblane Primary School in Scotland. Led to the UK banning virtually all handguns within months.

## Background

- **Location:** Dunblane Primary School, near Stirling, Scotland.
- **Perpetrator:** Thomas Hamilton, a local man with a history of concerns raised about his conduct and involvement with youth groups.
- **Weapon access:** The attack became a focal point in debates about private handgun ownership in Great Britain.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The shooting occurred on **13 March 1996** during the school day.
- **Fatalities:** 16 children and one teacher were killed.
- The event is widely regarded as one of the worst mass shootings in modern UK history.

## Immediate Aftermath

- Emergency services responded quickly; survivors were treated and evacuated.
- The perpetrator died at the scene after the attack.
- The incident prompted extensive public mourning and national scrutiny of firearms policy.

## Inquiry and Public Debate

- A formal public inquiry (commonly associated with the **Cullen Report**) examined the circumstances, including licensing and oversight.
- The findings and political pressure contributed to rapid legislative change.

## Policy Impact and Legacy

- In 1997, Parliament enacted major reforms that **effectively banned private ownership of most handguns** in Great Britain.
- The Dunblane massacre is frequently cited in discussions of UK gun-control policy and its long-term effects.
- Memorials and commemorations have remained central in Dunblane and across Scotland.
