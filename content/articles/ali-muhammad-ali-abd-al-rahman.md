## Overview

- **Also known as**: "Ali Kushayb" (commonly used name in reporting).
- **Role**: A senior Janjaweed/Militia leader linked to attacks during the Darfur conflict.
- **International case**: Prosecuted at the **International Criminal Court (ICC)** for crimes committed in Darfur.

## Context (Darfur)

- The Darfur conflict involved widespread violence against civilian populations, including village raids.
- International investigations and UN reporting helped document patterns of attacks, displacement, and alleged atrocities.
- The ICC opened cases connected to Darfur as part of its mandate to prosecute individuals for the gravest international crimes.

## ICC Proceedings (high level)

- **Allegations**: Involvement in attacks on civilians and related crimes during the Darfur conflict.
- **Charges (category level)**: Proceedings have centered on **war crimes** and **crimes against humanity**.
- **Outcome noted in this repository**: A conviction reported in 2024 (see timeline).
- **Why it matters**: The case is frequently cited as part of broader international accountability efforts regarding Darfur.

## Key Takeaways

- Janjaweed leadership roles are often analyzed in terms of command structures, militia coordination, and links to state security actors.
- ICC cases typically focus on **individual criminal responsibility** (who did what, ordered what, or enabled what).
- The Darfur docket remains a prominent example of international criminal justice engagement with mass-atrocity situations.
