## Background

Craig Federighi is an American software executive known for leading major parts of Apple’s software engineering organization.
He is widely recognized as a long-time on-stage presenter at Apple’s annual Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC).

### Early Career (high level)

- Worked in software engineering roles in the 1990s, including time at NeXT (the company founded by Steve Jobs after leaving Apple).
- Held senior engineering leadership roles in enterprise software, including at Ariba (where he served as a senior technology leader).

## Role at Apple

Federighi joined Apple in 2009 to lead Mac software engineering.
He later became Apple’s Senior Vice President of Software Engineering, overseeing the teams responsible for operating systems and core platform software.
In this capacity, his organization has covered major releases across iOS, iPadOS, and macOS, along with many developer-facing frameworks.

### What the job typically includes

- Setting engineering priorities and coordinating schedules across multiple OS release trains.
- Managing large cross-functional organizations spanning system software, apps, and developer platforms.
- Representing product direction publicly (e.g., WWDC keynotes) in partnership with other Apple executives.

## Notable Themes and Contributions (high level)

- **Platform transitions:** Helping guide macOS and Apple’s software ecosystem through major architectural shifts and long-term roadmap changes.
- **Developer relations via WWDC:** Serving as a consistent communicator of platform changes to developers, especially around OS releases.
- **Security and privacy posture:** As a senior software executive at Apple, contributing to the leadership responsible for OS-level security and privacy features.
