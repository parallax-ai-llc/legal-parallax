## Background

- Andrew Daulton Lee (born 1952) is an American who became known publicly through a late-1970s U.S. espionage case.
- He was associated with Christopher John Boyce, a former defense contractor employee who passed classified information to the Soviet Union.
- In public accounts, Lee is often described as a friend and associate of Boyce who acted as a courier in the spy ring.

## Role in the Boyce Espionage Case (High Level)

- The case centered on the unauthorized disclosure of U.S. national security information to Soviet intelligence during the Cold War.
- Lee’s reported role was to transport or help transmit materials and communications as part of the scheme.
- U.S. authorities ultimately prosecuted both men, and the case became one of the better-known U.S. spy stories of the era.

## Legal Outcome

- Lee was convicted of espionage-related offenses in 1977.
- His conviction is frequently summarized as part of the broader “Boyce and Lee” espionage narrative.

## Cultural / Historical Notes

- The story of Boyce and Lee helped shape popular understanding of Cold War-era espionage in the United States.
- In many summaries, Lee is portrayed as a secondary figure to Boyce, who is often described as the principal source of classified material.
