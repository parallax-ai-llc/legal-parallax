## Summary

North Korean agents bombed Korean Air Flight 858 in midair killing all 115 aboard. The attack was intended to disrupt the 1988 Seoul Olympics.

## Background

- The bombing occurred during heightened tensions on the Korean Peninsula and ahead of the 1988 Seoul Olympics.
- The incident is widely described as a state-sponsored terrorist attack intended to intimidate South Korea and undermine the upcoming Olympics.

## Attack (high-level)

- Korean Air Flight 858 was destroyed in flight by an explosive device, killing all **115** passengers and crew.
- Reporting commonly describes the device as a time bomb placed among passenger belongings and detonated midair.

## Investigation and arrests

- Two suspects traveling on forged Japanese passports were detained after the incident.
- One suspect died by cyanide poisoning during custody; the other, **Kim Hyon-hui**, survived and was later extradited to South Korea.
- Kim Hyon-hui confessed and said she had been trained by North Korea and acted under orders; North Korea denied responsibility.

## Aftermath and impact

- The attack drew international condemnation and increased focus on aviation security and counterterrorism in the region.
- It became part of the broader political context of the late Cold War period in East Asia and is frequently referenced in accounts of South Korea’s Olympic-era security environment.

## Ongoing questions

- Families of victims and commentators have raised questions over aspects of the investigation and accountability; some claims remain disputed in public debate.
