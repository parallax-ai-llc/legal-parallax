## Background and Education

- Nigerian artist, art historian, curator, and writer focused on African and African diaspora art history[^1]
- Studied and earned degrees across Nigeria and the United States (Nsukka, South Florida, Emory)[^1]
- Based in Princeton, New Jersey (as reported by Wikipedia)[^1]

## Academic and Institutional Roles

- Taught at institutions including Yaba College of Technology (Lagos) and the University of Nigeria, Nsukka, as well as universities in the United States (including Penn State and Williams College)[^1]
- At Princeton University, holds a professorship in Art & Archaeology and African American Studies and has served in leadership roles connected to African Studies and Africa-focused initiatives[^1]

## Research and Writing

- Publishes scholarship and criticism in art and humanities venues (e.g., *African Arts*, *Artforum International*, *South Atlantic Quarterly*)[^1]
- Serves as editor of *Nka: Journal of Contemporary African Art* (Duke University Press)[^1]
- Has written for mainstream platforms and maintains a blog (per Wikipedia)[^1]

## Curatorial Work (Selected)

- Co-organized major exhibitions and biennial-related projects focused on modern and contemporary African art, including *The Short Century* traveling exhibition project[^1]
- Has been involved in exhibitions and programs across Europe, Africa, and the United States (per Wikipedia)[^1]
