## Summary
Klaas Bruinsma was the Netherlands' biggest drug lord in the 1980s. He built a hashish and cocaine empire worth billions. He was assassinated outside the Amsterdam Hilton hotel in 1991.

## Operations and Influence
- **Primary markets:** Bruinsma is most commonly associated with **hashish trafficking**, and is also described in reporting as having links to **cocaine** distribution.
- **Scale:** Contemporary accounts portray him as a central figure in the Dutch narcotics scene during the 1980s, operating at a level that drew sustained attention from police and media.
- **Networked crime:** Like many major traffickers, his power depended on **logistics, trusted intermediaries, and protection relationships**, rather than direct street-level dealing.

## Law Enforcement Attention
- **High-profile target:** Bruinsma’s notoriety in the Netherlands made him a frequent subject of investigations and public discussion about organized crime.
- **Cross-border dimension:** Large-volume drug trafficking in Western Europe typically involved **international supply routes** and coordination beyond one country, which is consistent with how his organization is described.

## Death
Bruinsma was killed on **27 June 1991**. Sources commonly note that he was **shot outside the Amsterdam Hilton**. His death is often cited as a turning point illustrating the violence and internal rivalries that can accompany high-level trafficking.

## Legacy
- **Symbol of an era:** In Dutch crime history, he is frequently referenced as a symbol of the 1980s period when organized narcotics trafficking grew in visibility and sophistication.
- **Public memory:** His story is revisited in crime journalism and documentaries as part of broader narratives about Dutch organized crime.
