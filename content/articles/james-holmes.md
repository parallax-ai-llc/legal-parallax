## Summary
James Holmes killed 12 and injured 70 at a movie theater in Aurora, Colorado on July 20, 2012. He opened fire during a midnight screening of The Dark Knight Rises. Sentenced to life.

## Background
- Holmes was a graduate student in neuroscience at the University of Colorado Denver before the attack.
- Reporting from the period described escalating personal and academic difficulties prior to July 2012.

## Aurora Theater Shooting (2012)
- Date/Location: July 20, 2012, Century 16 theater in Aurora, Colorado.
- The attack occurred during a midnight screening of *The Dark Knight Rises*.
- Victims: 12 people were killed and dozens were wounded.
- The event prompted renewed national debate in the United States about mass shootings, emergency response, and firearm access.

## Investigation and Arrest
- Holmes was arrested shortly after the shooting.
- Law enforcement subsequently searched his residence as part of the investigation.

## Criminal Proceedings
- Prosecutors pursued charges related to multiple counts of murder and attempted murder.
- The defense raised issues related to mental health in court proceedings.
- The case became a major, closely followed prosecution given the number of victims and the public setting.

## Sentencing and Incarceration
- Holmes was sentenced to life imprisonment without the possibility of parole.
- In U.S. reporting, this outcome is often contrasted with capital punishment decisions in other mass-casualty cases.

## Public Impact
- The shooting influenced discussions about theater security practices and event safety planning.
- Survivors and families of victims have been central voices in subsequent advocacy and memorial efforts.
