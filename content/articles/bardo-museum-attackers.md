## Summary

ISIS-inspired gunmen who attacked the Bardo National Museum in Tunis killing 22 tourists.

## Attack Overview (2015)

- **Date:** 18 March 2015.
- **Location:** Bardo National Museum complex, adjacent to Tunisia’s parliament, Tunis.
- **Method:** Armed assault with firearms and hostage-taking inside the museum.
- **Casualties:** Widely reported as **22 killed**, the majority being foreign tourists, with additional injuries.

## Identities and Attribution

Public reporting and later investigations identified the two primary attackers as:

- **Seifeddine Rezgui** (Tunisian)
- **Yassine Labidi** (Tunisian)

The attack was **claimed by ISIL/ISIS**, and the perpetrators were described in press coverage as being inspired by or linked to jihadist networks operating in Tunisia and the region.

## Context

- The Bardo Museum attack occurred during a period of heightened security concern in Tunisia following the **2011 revolution**.
- Tunisia’s **tourism sector** and high-visibility sites were considered attractive targets for militants seeking international attention.

## Aftermath and Impact

- Tunisian security forces responded rapidly; the attackers were ultimately **killed**.
- The attack triggered heightened security measures for cultural sites and tourist areas.
- It had a chilling effect on tourism and became a widely cited turning point in discussions about Tunisia’s counterterrorism posture.
