## Summary

39 people, mostly Italian fans, were crushed when a wall collapsed after Liverpool fans charged at them before the European Cup Final. English clubs were banned from European competition for 5 years.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The 1985 European Cup Final was held at Heysel Stadium in Brussels between Liverpool and Juventus.
- Before kickoff, a confrontation in a mixed/adjacent section escalated and a crowd surge pushed supporters toward a retaining wall.
- The wall gave way and people were crushed; many victims were Juventus supporters.
- Despite the fatalities and chaos, the match was played and Juventus won 1–0 (penalty).

## Contributing Factors Often Cited

- Poor stadium infrastructure and fencing, with sections that were difficult to separate safely.
- Inadequate crowd control and policing for a high-risk fixture.
- Ticketing and segregation failures that placed rival supporters in close proximity.

## Consequences

- UEFA imposed a ban on English clubs from European competitions for five seasons; Liverpool received an additional season.
- Criminal proceedings followed; some Liverpool supporters were convicted for their roles in the events.
- Belgian officials and safety planning were also scrutinized in subsequent investigations.

## Legacy

- The disaster accelerated reforms in European matchday safety, including stronger standards for stadium maintenance, crowd management, and segregation.
- It remains a reference point in discussions about hooliganism, event security, and infrastructure risk in large gatherings.
