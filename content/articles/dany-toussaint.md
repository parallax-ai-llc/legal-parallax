## Background

- Dany Toussaint is described (in widely cited public reporting) as a Haitian politician and former security official.
- According to Wikipedia, he served as a major in the Haitian Army, later headed the Haitian police, and also worked as a bodyguard to President Jean-Bertrand Aristide.[^1]
- He is also described as having served as a Senator and as the leader of the Haitian Democratic and Reformist Movement Party (as named on Wikipedia).[^1]

## Jean Dominique assassination allegations (context)

- Haitian journalist Jean Dominique was assassinated in 2000; the case became one of Haiti’s most prominent unresolved political murders.
- Wikipedia reports that in 2003 Judge Claudy Gassant requested that Toussaint’s parliamentary immunity be lifted in connection with the Dominique assassination investigation; the Haitian Senate rejected the request.[^1]
- Reporting about the case often frames Toussaint as an accused or alleged figure rather than a convicted perpetrator; many accounts emphasize the political and institutional obstacles that hindered prosecution.

## 2006 weapons arrest

- Wikipedia states that on 2 January 2006, MINUSTAH (the UN Stabilization Mission in Haiti) arrested Toussaint for possession of illegal weapons, then handed him to the Haitian National Police, which released him hours later.[^1]
- A contemporaneous UN news report also summarized the detention and handover in similar terms.[^3]
