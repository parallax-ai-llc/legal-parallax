## What It Was (Clarification)

Although this entry is formatted like a person profile, the Lac-Mégantic rail disaster was a specific industrial accident and its aftermath (not an individual).

## Background

- **Rail operator**: Montreal, Maine & Atlantic Railway (MMA), a regional rail carrier operating in Canada and the U.S.
- **Cargo**: A train of tank cars carrying **crude oil** (Bakken crude) destined for an oil refinery.
- **Location**: Lac-Mégantic, Quebec, Canada.

## What Happened

- The train was **left unattended** overnight on a grade near Nantes, Quebec.
- It **rolled downhill** toward Lac-Mégantic, **derailed** in the town center, and multiple tank cars ruptured.
- The spilled crude oil **ignited**, producing fires and explosions that destroyed buildings and infrastructure.

## Human and Community Impact

- **Fatalities**: 47 people were killed.
- The blast and subsequent fire **devastated the downtown core**, forcing evacuations and long-term rebuilding.

## Contributing Factors (High-Level)

Public reporting and investigations highlighted a combination of risks, including:

- **Securement/braking practices** for unattended trains.
- **Tank car vulnerability** (older designs were more prone to puncture and release in derailments).
- **Operational and oversight pressures**, including debates about cost-cutting, staffing, and maintenance.

## Aftermath and Accountability

- The disaster became a major case study in **rail safety** and **hazardous materials transport**.
- MMA faced severe financial consequences and later entered bankruptcy proceedings.
- The company’s leadership, including CEO **Ed Burkhardt**, was widely criticized in public discourse.

## Legal Proceedings (Summary)

- Criminal charges were brought against certain MMA employees.
- **Three employees were ultimately acquitted**, a result frequently cited in discussions about how responsibility is allocated in complex system failures.

## Why It Matters

- Accelerated reforms around:
  - **Hazardous materials classification and routing**
  - **Train securement rules** and operating practices
  - **Tank car standards** (phase-out/retrofits of older cars)
- Commonly referenced in broader debates about the safety of moving crude oil by rail.
