International operation took down one of the largest CSAM websites run by South Korean Jong Woo Son. 337 users were arrested across 23 countries using Bitcoin blockchain analysis.

## Overview

"Welcome to Video" was described as a large child sexual abuse material (CSAM) website that used cryptocurrency payments and anonymity services. The international law-enforcement action known as the "Welcome to Video" takedown targeted the site, its operator, and users in multiple jurisdictions.[^1]

## Key Figures

The operation centered on the site operator, Jong Woo Son (also reported as Son Jung-woo), and a broader network of paying users. Court outcomes and extradition-related reporting varied by country and legal system.[^1]

## Investigation Methods

Public reporting highlighted the use of Bitcoin transaction tracing to identify payments linked to user accounts, along with seized servers and other digital evidence. Investigators combined blockchain analysis with conventional digital forensics and cross-border information sharing.[^1]

## International Coordination

The case is commonly cited as an example of a coordinated effort among law-enforcement agencies across many countries, including arrests and follow-on prosecutions in local courts. It also became part of broader discussions about cryptocurrency-enabled crime and online child exploitation investigations.[^1]

## Outcomes and Impact

According to published summaries, hundreds of suspects were identified, and many were arrested across numerous countries. The operation was frequently presented as a milestone in applying cryptocurrency analytics to dismantle an online CSAM marketplace.[^1]
