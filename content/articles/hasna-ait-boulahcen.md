## Summary

French woman initially reported to be Europe's first female suicide bomber during the Saint-Denis raid after the Paris attacks. It was later confirmed she was killed by Abaaoud's suicide vest blast, not her own.

## Background

- Hasna Ait Boulahcen was a French citizen from the Paris region.
- She was a cousin of Abdelhamid Abaaoud, a key figure linked to the November 2015 Paris attacks.

## Context: Saint-Denis raid (18 Nov 2015)

- After the 13 November 2015 attacks in Paris, French police conducted an operation in Saint-Denis targeting suspects believed to be connected to the attacks.
- Ait Boulahcen was present at the apartment during the raid and was killed during the operation.

## Early reporting and later clarification

- Some early media reports described her as a suicide bomber and, in particular, as the first female suicide bomber in Europe.
- Subsequent official and forensic accounts reported that the explosive detonation came from Abaaoud's suicide vest, and that Ait Boulahcen did not detonate explosives herself.

## Key points (high-level, widely reported)

- Associated primarily through family connection to Abdelhamid Abaaoud.
- Died during the Saint-Denis operation that followed the Paris attacks.
- Public narrative around her death became a prominent example of how early breaking-news reports can be corrected by later investigation.
