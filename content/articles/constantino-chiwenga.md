## Summary

Zimbabwean military commander who orchestrated the 2017 coup against Robert Mugabe.

## Background

- **Military affiliation:** Long-serving officer in the Zimbabwe Defence Forces (ZDF), associated with the ZANU–PF-era security establishment.
- **International engagement:** His period as commander coincided with regional security cooperation and participation in multilateral forums typical for ZDF leadership.

## Role in the 2017 intervention

- In **November 2017**, the Zimbabwean military intervened amid an internal ZANU–PF succession struggle.
- The operation placed **Robert Mugabe** under house arrest and helped trigger Mugabe’s resignation after long rule.
- Chiwenga, then the **Commander of the Zimbabwe Defence Forces**, was widely identified as the senior military figure behind the intervention.

## Political career (post-2017)

- After the 2017 transition, he moved from military leadership into senior state office.
- He has served as **Vice President of Zimbabwe** during the administration that followed Mugabe’s resignation.

## Why he matters

- Illustrates how senior military leadership can become decisive during **elite political transitions**.
- Often cited in analyses of Zimbabwe’s 2017 change in leadership as a key actor linking the military and ruling-party power centers.
