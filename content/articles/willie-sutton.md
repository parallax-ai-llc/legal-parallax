## Summary
Willie Sutton robbed over 100 banks during a 40-year career. When asked why he robbed banks, he supposedly said, 'Because that's where the money is' (a quote he later denied). He escaped prison three times.

## Early Life and Background
- Born in Brooklyn, New York, and raised in a working-class environment during an era of rapid urban growth.
- Became associated with street gangs and petty crime in his teens, gradually moving toward armed robbery.

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)
- Known for careful planning and disguises that helped him avoid immediate identification.
- Targeted banks and similar institutions where cash was concentrated, typically aiming to minimize time at the scene.
- His reputation was shaped as much by the longevity of his criminal career as by any single robbery.

## Arrests, Sentencing, and Prison Escapes
- Faced repeated arrests and long sentences across multiple decades, reflecting the scale and persistence of his robberies.
- Escaped confinement three times, contributing to his notoriety and the public image of him as a “master” bank robber.
- Escapes and re-captures became a recurring feature of his story and helped cement his place in American crime lore.

## Quote: “Because that’s where the money is”
- Sutton is widely linked to the line, but he later denied saying it in that form.
- Regardless of attribution, the quote entered popular culture as a concise explanation of rational target selection.

## Later Life
- After serving substantial prison time, he eventually gained release and lived out his remaining years outside prison.
- In later interviews and public accounts, his story was often framed as a blend of criminality, planning, and celebrity.

## Legacy and Cultural Impact
- Frequently cited in discussions of bank security, law enforcement tactics, and the “folk anti-hero” framing of some criminals.
- The enduring association with the famous quote made him a shorthand reference in economics, criminology, and business writing.
