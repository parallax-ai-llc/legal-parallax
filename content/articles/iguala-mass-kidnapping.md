## Summary

Police in Iguala, Guerrero attacked student buses and turned 43 students over to the Guerreros Unidos cartel. Investigation revealed deep state-cartel collusion.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2014 | 43 students handed to cartel by police |

## Key Details

- **Location:** Iguala, Guerrero, Mexico; students were traveling from the **Ayotzinapa Rural Teachers' College**.
- **Victims:** **43 students** who were forcibly disappeared after coordinated attacks on buses.
- **Alleged perpetrators:** Local police forces in Iguala and Cocula, acting in coordination with the **Guerreros Unidos** criminal group.
- **Core allegation:** A pattern of **state–cartel collusion**, with municipal authorities facilitating organized crime violence.

## Background

- Rural teacher-training colleges in Mexico have a history of political activism and frequent conflict with authorities.
- Guerrero has long been affected by high levels of violence, corruption, and disputes among organized crime groups.

## Immediate Events (High-Level)

- Students commandeered or traveled on buses and were attacked at multiple points in Iguala.
- Survivors reported shootings and detentions; the 43 were later reported as taken into custody and then disappeared.

## Investigations and Dispute

- Mexican authorities pursued a narrative that the students were killed and their remains disposed of; this account became widely contested.
- Independent forensic and investigative efforts raised questions about evidence handling, chain of custody, and coerced testimony.
- The case became a prominent example in debates over **impunity**, **forced disappearance**, and **institutional corruption** in Mexico.

## Aftermath and Impact

- Large-scale protests and international attention pressured federal and state authorities to continue investigations.
- The event intensified scrutiny of municipal policing, the role of federal security forces, and reforms to criminal justice institutions.
- Families of the disappeared remained central actors, sustaining long-running campaigns for truth, accountability, and identification of remains.

## Why It Matters

- Highlights how local state institutions can be captured by organized crime.
- Demonstrates the difficulties of establishing truth in complex cases involving multiple jurisdictions and compromised evidence.
- Became a symbolic case for human-rights advocacy concerning forced disappearances in Latin America.
