## Summary

Nine suicide bombers from NTJ attacked three churches and three luxury hotels on Easter Sunday, killing 269. The largest ISIS-inspired attack outside the Middle East.

## Background and Context

- **Date and setting:** The bombings occurred on **Easter Sunday (21 April 2019)** during church services and busy hotel hours in and around **Colombo**, Sri Lanka.[^1]
- **Targets:** The attacks struck **Christian places of worship** and **high-end hotels**, maximizing casualties among congregants and travelers.[^1]
- **Broader significance:** The event became one of Sri Lanka's deadliest incidents since the end of the civil war, drawing extensive international attention and assistance.[^1]

## Attack Details (High-Level)

- **Nature of attack:** Coordinated **suicide bombings** carried out by multiple attackers in a short time window.[^1]
- **Primary locations:** Three churches and three luxury hotels were hit in the initial wave, followed by additional explosions later that day.[^1]
- **Casualties:** Widely reported toll is **269 killed** with hundreds injured (counts vary by subsequent revisions and reporting methodology).[^1]

## Perpetrators and Claims

- **Domestic extremist group:** Investigations attributed the bombings to members linked to **National Thowheeth Jama'ath (NTJ)**, a local Islamist extremist network.[^1]
- **International linkage claim:** **Islamic State (IS/ISIS)** claimed responsibility; reporting and investigations discussed ideological inspiration and possible external facilitation, with details contested across sources.[^1]

## Warnings, Security, and Aftermath

- **Prior intelligence:** Public reporting emphasized that **warnings** about potential attacks circulated before the incident, becoming a major focus of political and institutional scrutiny afterward.[^1]
- **Emergency response:** Sri Lanka imposed heightened security measures, including large-scale arrests and expanded security operations in the weeks that followed.[^1]
- **Political impact:** The attacks triggered sustained debate about intelligence-sharing, chain-of-command failures, and national security reform.[^1]

## Long-Term Impact

- **Interfaith relations:** The bombings deepened concerns about religious violence and prompted efforts toward reconciliation and community protection.[^1]
- **Tourism and economy:** Sri Lanka's tourism sector faced a significant shock in 2019 as travelers reassessed safety, and businesses absorbed immediate losses.[^1]
- **Commemoration:** Annual memorials and religious services have marked the anniversary, with attention to victims and survivors.[^1]
