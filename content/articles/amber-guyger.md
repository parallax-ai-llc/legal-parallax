## Background

- Amber Joy Guyger was a police officer with the Dallas Police Department in Texas.
- She became widely known following the 2018 shooting death of Botham Shem Jean, a civilian neighbor in the same apartment complex.

## 2018 Shooting of Botham Jean (high-level)

- The shooting occurred on September 6, 2018, at Jean's apartment in Dallas.
- Prosecutors said Guyger entered Jean's apartment and shot him, later claiming she mistakenly believed it was her own apartment and that she encountered an intruder.
- The case drew extensive public attention in the United States, including scrutiny of law enforcement use of force and accountability.

## Legal Proceedings

- Guyger was charged with murder and tried in Dallas County.
- In 2019, a jury convicted her of murder; she was sentenced to prison (sentencing details are covered in the referenced sources).
- The trial and sentencing were closely followed nationally, including statements from Jean's family and discussions about policing standards.

## Aftermath and Public Impact

- The case became part of broader conversations about racial bias, police training, and criminal justice reforms.
- It also prompted debates about the appropriate handling of off-duty conduct, apartment-complex security, and how mistakes are evaluated when a firearm is used.
