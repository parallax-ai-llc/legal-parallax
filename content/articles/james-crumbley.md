## Background

- James Crumbley is the father of Ethan Crumbley, who carried out the Oxford High School shooting in Oxford Township, Michigan (Nov 30, 2021).
- Prosecutors argued that the handgun used in the shooting was accessible to Ethan due to failures in safe storage and supervision.

## Legal Case (Involuntary Manslaughter)

- **Charges:** James Crumbley was charged with four counts of involuntary manslaughter in connection with the four student deaths.
- **Theory of liability:** The case centered on alleged negligent conduct (rather than direct participation in the shooting), including enabling access to a firearm and not responding adequately to warning signs.
- **Trial and verdict (2024):** He was convicted in 2024; his spouse, Jennifer Crumbley, was tried separately and also convicted.
- **Sentencing:** Reporting associated with the Wikipedia summary notes a prison sentence in the range of 10–15 years, with eligibility for parole after 10 years.

## Why the Case Drew National Attention

- The prosecutions were widely discussed as a rare (and in Michigan, historically notable) example of parents being held criminally responsible for a child’s school shooting under an involuntary manslaughter theory.
- The case is often cited in broader debates about:
  - firearm access and safe storage in homes
  - duty of care and supervision by guardians
  - how the legal system assigns responsibility for foreseeable harm
