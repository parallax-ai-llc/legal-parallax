## Summary

Al-Shabaab militants besieged the Hayat Hotel in Mogadishu for 30 hours, killing 21 civilians. A car bomb breached the hotel before gunmen stormed inside.

## Key Facts

- **Location:** Hayat Hotel, Mogadishu, Somalia
- **Date:** 19–21 August 2022 (multi-day siege)
- **Type:** Coordinated assault beginning with an explosion followed by an armed attack
- **Perpetrator:** Al-Shabaab (claimed responsibility)
- **Outcome:** Siege ended after Somali security forces cleared the site

## Background

The Hayat Hotel is located in Mogadishu and has been used by politicians, businesspeople, and travelers. The attack took place amid a long-running insurgency in Somalia in which Al-Shabaab has repeatedly targeted hotels and public venues in the capital.

## Attack (High-Level)

- The assault began with an initial blast near the hotel entrance area, after which armed militants entered the building.
- Fighting continued for many hours as security forces engaged the attackers and attempted to evacuate civilians.
- Reports describe the event as a prolonged siege that lasted roughly 30 hours before the operation concluded.

## Casualties and Impact

- Public reporting cited **dozens of casualties**, including **at least 21 fatalities** and many injuries.
- The incident drew international condemnation and renewed attention to civilian security in Mogadishu.

## Aftermath

- Somali authorities stated that the attackers were neutralized and the area was secured after the siege.
- The attack contributed to further security measures around hotels and major public sites in the capital.
