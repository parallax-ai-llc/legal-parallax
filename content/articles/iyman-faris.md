## Summary

Pakistani-American truck driver convicted of plotting to destroy the Brooklyn Bridge for al-Qaeda.

## Background

- Faris was born in Pakistan and later lived in the United States.
- Reporting around his case commonly described him as a truck driver.
- He became associated with al-Qaeda during the period leading up to his arrest and conviction.

## Brooklyn Bridge Plot (High Level)

- U.S. prosecutors alleged a plan to attack the Brooklyn Bridge in New York City.
- Public accounts of the case describe reconnaissance and exploration of potential methods to damage the bridge.
- The plot is frequently discussed in the broader context of post-9/11 counterterrorism investigations.

## Arrest, Cooperation, and Court Outcome

- Faris was arrested and prosecuted in U.S. federal court.
- He ultimately pleaded guilty and was convicted on terrorism-related charges.
- Court reporting and later summaries note that he provided cooperation to authorities as part of the case process.

## Significance

- The case is often cited as an example of early-2000s U.S. efforts to disrupt attacks on major infrastructure targets.
- It is referenced in discussions of al-Qaeda’s interest in symbolic, high-visibility targets within the United States.
