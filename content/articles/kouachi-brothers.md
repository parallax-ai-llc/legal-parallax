## Summary
Saïd and Chérif Kouachi were the brothers who carried out the Charlie Hebdo shooting on January 7, 2015, killing 12 people at the satirical magazine's Paris office. They were killed by police.

## Overview
- **Names**: Saïd Kouachi (b. 1980) and Chérif Kouachi (b. 1982)
- **Notability**: Identified as the perpetrators of the *Charlie Hebdo* attack in Paris (2015)
- **Outcome**: Killed by French police on January 9, 2015 after a manhunt

## Background (high level)
Public reporting describes the Kouachi brothers as French citizens of Algerian descent who were raised in France.

Key points commonly cited in reporting:
- **Radicalization and networks**: Chérif Kouachi was linked in court reporting to extremist recruitment efforts in the mid-2000s.
- **Yemen travel**: Multiple outlets reported travel to Yemen and alleged contact/training connected to AQAP.

## The 2015 attack and aftermath (non-exhaustive)
- The attack on *Charlie Hebdo* was widely treated as a major terrorist incident in France.
- French authorities conducted a large-scale manhunt.
- The brothers were located and killed by police on January 9, 2015.

## Public and political impact
The attack prompted major public responses and policy debate, including:
- Large public demonstrations in France in support of free expression and against terrorism.
- Renewed scrutiny of domestic extremism, intelligence coordination, and counterterrorism laws.
- International discussion about satire, religion, and freedom of speech.

## Related people and organizations
- **Charlie Hebdo**: French satirical magazine targeted in the attack.
- **AQAP (reported)**: Al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula was frequently referenced in reporting regarding alleged training and claims of responsibility.
