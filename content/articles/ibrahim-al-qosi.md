## Background

- Ibrahim al-Qosi (also spelled **al Qosi**) is a Sudanese national known primarily through public reporting and U.S. government proceedings connected to Guantánamo Bay.
- Many biographical details are not consistently available in open sources; accounts focus on his alleged role within al-Qaeda networks and later appearances with AQAP.

## Detention and Legal Proceedings (High-level)

- **Guantánamo detention:** Detained by U.S. authorities and held at the Guantánamo Bay detention facility.
- **Military commission case:** He faced charges under the U.S. military commissions system.
- **Disposition:** Public reporting describes a **plea agreement** that preceded his transfer/repatriation; specific charge counts and terms vary by summary source.

## Post-release Activities (Public reporting)

- After repatriation, he was later reported to have traveled and re-emerged in **AQAP** media.
- Open-source accounts have described him as appearing in or being referenced by AQAP as a **religious/organizational** figure rather than as an on-the-ground commander; exact responsibilities are often described in general terms.
