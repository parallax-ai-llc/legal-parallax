## Background

Karim Masimov (Kazakh: Kd1rim Md1simov) is a Kazakhstani politician and security official who held multiple senior posts in the government of Kazakhstan. He is widely known internationally for serving as Prime Minister during the long presidency of Nursultan Nazarbayev and later as head of Kazakhstan's main security service.

## Key Roles

- **Prime Minister of Kazakhstan** (served two separate terms).
- **Head of the Presidential Administration** (senior office managing executive coordination).
- **Chairman of the National Security Committee (KNB)**, Kazakhstan's principal intelligence and security agency.

## January 2022 Unrest and Aftermath

In early January 2022, Kazakhstan experienced mass protests and violence often referred to as the "January events". Following the unrest, Masimov was detained by authorities and later prosecuted.

High-level reporting commonly frames the case in the context of an internal political crackdown and a broader reshuffle of power among Kazakhstan's elite after the protests.

## Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- **Arrest (2022):** Detained shortly after the January events.
- **Charges:** Public statements by Kazakh authorities described the case in terms of treason and related offenses.
- **Conviction:** He was reported convicted and sentenced by a Kazakh court.

Because court materials and evidentiary details are not fully transparent to outside observers, many accounts summarize outcomes while noting limited public documentation.

## Why This Figure Matters

- Illustrates how leadership changes and security politics can accelerate after large-scale civil unrest.
- Provides a case study in post-crisis consolidation: reorganization of security institutions and removal of senior officials.
- Often cited in discussions of Kazakhstan's transition period from the Nazarbayev era to the Tokayev administration.
