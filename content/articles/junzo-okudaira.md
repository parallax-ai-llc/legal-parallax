## Summary

Japanese Red Army member who participated in the Lod Airport massacre.

## Background

- Junzo Okudaira (奥平純三) was a member of the Japanese Red Army (JRA), a militant organization active in the late 1960s–1970s.
- He is primarily known for his role in the 1972 Lod Airport attack in Israel, an incident that brought international attention to the JRA.

## Notability

Okudaira is notable in historical records because the Lod Airport attack is frequently cited as an early example of an internationally coordinated operation involving a Japanese militant group operating outside Japan.

## Lod Airport Attack (1972)

- **Date**: 1972
- **Location**: Lod Airport (now Ben Gurion Airport), Israel
- **Method**: Firearms and grenades were used during the assault.
- **Perpetrators**: Okudaira acted as part of a three-person team linked to the Japanese Red Army.
- **Outcome**: Okudaira was killed during the attack.

## Associated Individuals

Historical summaries commonly list the other attackers as:

- **Kōzō Okamoto** (captured)
- **Yasuyuki Yasuda** (killed)

## Legacy and Historical Context

- The event is discussed in terrorism and security histories as a case of cross-border militancy and the globalization of political violence in the 1970s.
- Coverage typically focuses on the high civilian impact of the attack and the subsequent security responses at international airports.
