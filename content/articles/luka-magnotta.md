## Summary
Luka Magnotta was a Canadian murderer who killed and dismembered Chinese international student Jun Lin in 2012, filming and posting the murder online. He fled to Europe and was captured in Berlin. Sentenced to life.

## Background
- Born Eric Clinton Newman in Scarborough, Ontario (later legally changed his name to Luka Rocco Magnotta).
- Reported a history of seeking notoriety online and pursuing modeling/entertainment opportunities.

## 2012 Killing of Jun Lin (High-level)
- Victim: Jun Lin, a Chinese international student studying in Canada.
- The killing occurred in Montreal in 2012 and involved dismemberment.
- A video of the homicide was distributed online, which accelerated public attention and law-enforcement response.

## Flight and Arrest
- After the killing, Magnotta left Canada and traveled through parts of Europe.
- He was arrested in Berlin, Germany, after being identified and located.
- He was returned to Canada to stand trial.

## Trial and Sentence
- Convicted in Canada for first-degree murder and related offenses.
- Sentenced to life imprisonment (Canadian law includes parole ineligibility periods for first-degree murder).

## Public Impact and Media Coverage
- The case drew significant international media coverage, in part due to the online dissemination of the video.
- It prompted broader discussion about:
  - platform responsibility and content moderation,
  - the speed at which violent material can spread online,
  - digital investigation and crowdsourced identification.
