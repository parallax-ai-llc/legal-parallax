## Summary

6'9" serial killer who murdered his grandparents at 15 then killed six college students and his mother.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1964 | Killed grandparents as a teenager; committed to state hospital (California). |
| 1969 | Released and moved to the Santa Cruz area; later had ongoing conflicts with his mother. |
| 1972–1973 | Murdered multiple young women in Northern California ("co-ed killings"). |
| 1973 | Killed his mother and her friend; then called police and surrendered. |
| 1973 | Convicted in California and sentenced to prison (life term). |

## Notable Details (High-Level)

- **Physical profile**: Reported to be unusually tall (commonly cited around 6'9").
- **Victim count**: Commonly reported as **10 murders** (grandparents + 6 students + mother + mother's friend), though summaries often focus on the 1972–1973 series.
- **Case notoriety**: Often cited in criminology discussions because he **self-reported** to law enforcement and provided extensive interviews.

## Legal & Incarceration

- **Jurisdiction**: California state courts.
- **Disposition**: Pleaded/was found guilty and received a life sentence.
- **Custody**: Long-term incarceration within the California prison medical/correctional system.
- **Parole**: Public sources note repeated parole hearings with continued confinement.

## Cultural Impact

- Became a well-known figure in true-crime media due to the combination of the crimes, surrender, and later interview material.
- His case is frequently referenced in discussions about offender profiling and post-offense behavior (e.g., surrendering and cooperating).
