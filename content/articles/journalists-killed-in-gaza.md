## Summary

Over 150 journalists and media workers have been killed in Gaza since October 2023, making it the deadliest conflict for journalists in modern history.

## Context

- The fighting in and around Gaza after 7 October 2023 created sustained, high-intensity risk for reporters, camera operators, fixers, and other media workers.
- Many deaths have occurred while journalists were reporting from areas experiencing airstrikes, ground fighting, or artillery fire.
- Casualty totals vary by source because definitions differ (e.g., “journalist” vs. “media worker”), and because verification is difficult during active conflict.

## What the Wikipedia list represents

- The Wikipedia article is a compiled list of named individuals reported killed while working as journalists or media workers in Gaza during the conflict.
- Entries typically include the person’s name, employer/affiliation, date of death, and a citation to reporting or monitoring organizations.
- As with any crowdsourced list, the contents can change over time and should be cross-checked against primary reporting and specialist monitors.

## Key factual points (high-level)

- **Scale:** Multiple monitoring groups and news organizations have described the period after October 2023 as unusually lethal for media workers.
- **Attribution:** Causes of death reported in public sources include strikes/explosions, crossfire during combat, and other conflict-related incidents; details are often contested or incomplete.
- **Impunity risk:** International press-freedom organizations frequently emphasize that investigations and accountability mechanisms are critical when journalists are killed.

## Documentation and verification

Common elements used to document journalist deaths during conflicts include:

- Full name and known transliterations
- Employer/outlet (or independent/freelance status)
- Date and location of death
- Circumstances described by witnesses, local authorities, and/or the employer
- Corroborating reporting from multiple outlets
- Inclusion in datasets maintained by press-freedom monitors

## Why this matters

- Journalists provide independent information to the public, including documentation of civilian harm and humanitarian conditions.
- When journalists are killed, the ability to report from the affected area can diminish, increasing the “information blackout” risk.
- Protection of journalists is part of broader protections for civilians under international humanitarian law; the practical application depends on compliance, investigation, and enforcement.
