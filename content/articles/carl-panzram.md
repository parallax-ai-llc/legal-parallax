## Summary

American serial killer who murdered 22 people and committed thousands of burglaries and rapes worldwide.

## Background

- Panzram was born in Minnesota and grew up in an environment marked by instability and early contact with the penal system.
- He spent much of his adolescence and adult life in reform schools, jails, and prisons, where repeated incarceration reinforced his hostility toward authority and society.

## Crimes (High-Level)

- He later claimed responsibility for **22 murders** and admitted to extensive sexual violence and burglary, describing offending across multiple regions and, at times, outside the United States.
- Many of his asserted crimes were never independently corroborated in full; however, the scale and pattern of violence described in his accounts have made him a notable figure in criminology and true-crime history.

## Imprisonment and Execution

- Panzram was ultimately convicted for the murder of a prison guard while incarcerated.
- He was executed in 1930.

## Writings and Legacy

- While in prison, he produced autobiographical writings and letters that circulated after his death.
- These writings are often cited for their blunt, nihilistic tone and for the way they document a self-narrated criminal identity.
- His story is frequently referenced in discussions of prison conditions, recidivism, and the limits of self-reported confessions.
