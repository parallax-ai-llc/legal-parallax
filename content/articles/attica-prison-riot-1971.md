## Summary

1,281 inmates seized control of Attica Correctional Facility taking 42 staff hostage. The state police assault killed 43 people, including 10 hostages shot by police.

## Context and Causes

- **Overcrowding and living conditions:** Attica was widely reported as overcrowded, with complaints about sanitation, medical care, and daily living conditions.
- **Racial and social tensions:** A large share of inmates were Black and Puerto Rican, while most guards were white, contributing to long-running grievances and distrust.
- **Rights and governance inside prison:** Prisoners sought greater access to education, religious practice, political expression, and fairer internal rules and discipline.

## Prisoners’ Demands (High-Level)

Commonly summarized themes included:

- **Humane treatment:** Improvements to food, healthcare, and sanitation.
- **Religious and cultural rights:** Less restrictive practice for different faiths and cultural groups.
- **Due process and oversight:** Clearer rules, limits on abuse, and mechanisms to address complaints.
- **Access to programs:** Education, vocational training, and opportunities viewed as necessary for rehabilitation.

## Negotiation and Public Visibility

- **Hostages and control:** During the uprising, inmates held dozens of correctional staff as hostages while maintaining control over parts of the facility.
- **Observers and intermediaries:** Negotiations involved outside observers and prominent figures invited to witness discussions and reduce the risk of violence.
- **Public attention:** The events became a national story, intensifying scrutiny of prison conditions and state decision-making.

## State Assault (September 13, 1971)

- **Retaking the prison:** New York State authorities launched an armed assault to regain control of the facility.
- **Use of force:** The assault involved extensive gunfire and resulted in substantial loss of life.
- **Immediate aftermath:** Confusion and misinformation circulated in the early hours; later investigations clarified key facts about the causes of deaths.

## Casualties and Controversy

- **Total deaths:** 43 people died in connection with the uprising and retaking of Attica.
- **Breakdown often cited:** 33 inmates and 10 hostages were killed.
- **Cause of hostage deaths:** Subsequent findings attributed the hostages’ deaths to gunfire from law enforcement during the assault, contrary to initial public claims.

## Aftermath

- **Investigations:** Multiple inquiries examined prison conditions, the negotiation process, and the decision to use force.
- **Legal proceedings:** The aftermath included criminal prosecutions and extensive civil litigation spanning years.
- **Settlements and compensation:** Over time, settlements were reached involving families of hostages and inmates.
- **Policy impact:** Attica became a reference point in debates about prison reform, oversight, and the limits of coercive responses to unrest.

## Historical Significance

- **Prison reform symbol:** The Attica uprising is frequently cited as a turning point that pushed prison conditions and prisoners’ rights into mainstream political discussion.
- **Public memory:** “Attica” became shorthand in U.S. culture for both resistance to abusive conditions and the consequences of state violence in carceral settings.
