## Summary

Lebanese communist militant imprisoned in France since 1984 for involvement in assassinations of diplomats.

## Background

- Born in 1951 in northern Lebanon and educated as a teacher before becoming involved in left-wing political activity.[^1]
- Often described in secondary sources as aligned with Marxist/communist currents in the region and supportive of Palestinian armed factions during the Lebanese Civil War era.[^1]

## Organizations and Alleged Affiliations

- Widely reported as a leading figure associated with the Lebanese Armed Revolutionary Factions (LARF), a small militant group active in the early 1980s.[^1]
- The LARF has been linked in reporting to attacks against U.S. and Israeli targets in Europe during that period.[^1]

## Arrest, Conviction, and Detention in France

- Arrested in France in 1984; French courts later convicted him for complicity in the 1982 killings of U.S. military attaché Charles R. Ray and Israeli diplomat Yaakov Barsimantov in Paris.[^1]
- He has remained incarcerated in France for decades, making his case one of the longest-running political-militancy-related imprisonments in the country.[^1]
- Sources note repeated requests for release/parole and recurring political and diplomatic controversy surrounding those requests.[^1]

## Public and Political Controversy

- Supporters frame him as a political prisoner tied to broader Middle East conflicts; opponents and French authorities emphasize the underlying convictions related to assassinations.[^1]
- The case is frequently cited in discussions about terrorism-era sentencing, parole practice, and the role of politics in high-profile criminal detention decisions.[^1]
