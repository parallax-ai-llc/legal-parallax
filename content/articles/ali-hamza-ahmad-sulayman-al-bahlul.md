## Summary

Al-Qaeda propagandist convicted by US military tribunal of conspiracy and creating recruitment materials.

## Detention and Legal Case (high-level)

### Capture and transfer

- Public sources describe al-Bahlul as having been captured in late 2001 while attempting to move from Afghanistan toward Pakistan.
- He was transferred into U.S. custody in December 2001 and has been held at the Guantanamo Bay detention camp since 2002.

### Role attributed by U.S. authorities

- He has been described in official and journalistic summaries as a media/propaganda figure associated with al-Qaeda, including producing or supporting recruitment and propaganda materials.
- Some accounts describe work connected to Osama bin Laden’s media presence and the distribution of messaging intended to promote attacks.

### Military commission proceedings

- Al-Bahlul boycotted the Guantanamo military commission process, arguing that the tribunal lacked a proper legal basis.
- In November 2008, a U.S. military commission convicted him and imposed a life sentence.
- Appellate litigation that followed focused on what offenses could be tried by military commission and how those charges applied to conduct predating statutory changes.

### Appeals and outcome (overview)

- A U.S. appeals court decision in 2013 overturned portions of the 2008 convictions, leaving further disputes to subsequent review.
- Later appellate rulings ultimately affirmed a remaining conviction for conspiracy, and the U.S. Supreme Court declined to take up his petition (reported in 2017).
