## Summary

Anders Breivik bombed government buildings in Oslo killing 8, then massacred 69 people at a youth camp on Utøya island. The worst peacetime attack in Norway's history.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Date**: 22 July 2011.
- **Locations**: Oslo (government district) and **Utøya** island (Buskerud/Viken; at the time within Buskerud).
- **Attack sequence**: A bombing in central Oslo followed by a mass shooting at a youth summer camp.
- **Fatalities**: **77** people total (commonly cited breakdown: **8** in Oslo and **69** on Utøya).

## Perpetrator and Motive (Summary)

- The attacks were carried out by **Anders Behring Breivik**.
- Breivik described himself as motivated by **far-right, anti-immigration** and anti-government ideology.
- He published a lengthy document (often referred to as a manifesto) outlining his political views prior to the attacks.

## Immediate Response

- Norwegian emergency services and police responded to the Oslo blast and then to reports from Utøya.
- The scale and multi-location nature of the attacks prompted a nationwide crisis response and rapid mobilization.

## Legal Proceedings and Sentence

- Breivik was arrested the same day.
- In 2012, a Norwegian court found him criminally responsible and sentenced him to **forvaring** (preventive detention), Norway’s most severe penalty, with a minimum term commonly reported as **21 years** and the possibility of extension if considered a continuing threat.

## National and International Aftermath

- Norway held large public memorials and gatherings emphasizing **democracy, openness, and solidarity**.
- A public commission reviewed preparedness and response; its findings contributed to ongoing debate and reforms in security, policing coordination, and emergency readiness.

## Legacy

- The 2011 Norway attacks remain a defining modern event in Norwegian history and a reference point in discussions on **domestic extremism**, public safety, and societal resilience.
