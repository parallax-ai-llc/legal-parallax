Dutch colonial governor who ordered the genocide of the Banda Islands population to control the nutmeg trade.

## Background

- Coen was a Dutch East India Company (VOC) officer who rose to senior leadership during the early 17th century, when the VOC was consolidating control over Asian maritime trade.
- He is most associated with the VOC's attempt to secure a monopoly over high-value spices, especially nutmeg and mace from the Banda Islands.

## Banda Islands (1621)

- In 1621, Coen led and authorized a violent campaign against the Banda Islands' population as part of enforcing VOC monopoly policies.
- Contemporary and later historical accounts describe mass killings, forced deportations, and the reorganization of land and labor to serve VOC production and export goals.
- The result was a drastic demographic collapse on Banda, followed by the importation of enslaved and coerced labor to maintain spice cultivation under VOC oversight.

## Batavia (Jakarta) and VOC State-Building

- Coen is often linked to the VOC's establishment and fortification of Batavia as a key administrative and military hub in the region.
- Batavia functioned as a center for VOC governance, warehousing, and naval power projection across the Indonesian archipelago and beyond.

## Legacy and Controversy

- In the Netherlands, Coen was long celebrated in some civic and nationalist narratives as a symbol of commercial expansion.
- In modern historiography and public debate, he is widely criticized for colonial violence and crimes against local populations, particularly the Banda genocide.
- Statues, street names, and commemorations connected to Coen have been contested, contextualized, or re-evaluated as part of broader debates about colonial memory.

## Additional Notes

- **Spice monopoly logic:** VOC policy combined military force, treaty-making, and destruction of "unauthorized" trees or trade networks to suppress competition.
- **Human cost:** The Banda campaign is frequently cited as an extreme example of early-modern corporate-colonial violence.
