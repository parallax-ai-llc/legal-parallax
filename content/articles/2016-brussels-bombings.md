## Summary

ISIS suicide bombers attacked Brussels Airport and Maalbeek metro station killing 32 people. The cell was linked to the November 2015 Paris attackers.

## Background and Context

- The attacks occurred during a period of heightened terrorism alert in Belgium following the **November 2015 Paris attacks**.
- Belgian and French investigations had identified networks operating across borders, including individuals associated with the Paris cell.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Date:** 22 March 2016
- **Locations:**
  - **Brussels Airport (Zaventem)** — two explosions in the departure hall.
  - **Maalbeek/Maelbeek metro station** — one explosion on a train near EU institutions.
- **Impact:** 32 people were killed (excluding the attackers) and **300+** were injured.

## Perpetrators and Attribution

- The bombings were claimed by the **Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL/ISIS)**.
- Investigations attributed the attacks to a cell linked to the broader network involved in the **Paris 2015** operations.
- Commonly reported perpetrators included **Ibrahim El Bakraoui**, **Khalid El Bakraoui**, and **Najim Laachraoui** (attribution based on investigative reporting and official identification).

## Immediate Aftermath

- Brussels introduced emergency measures including transport disruptions, public venue closures, and a sustained security lockdown.
- A large-scale manhunt and investigative operation followed, with raids and coordinated international intelligence activity.

## Longer-Run Effects

- **Security policy:** The attacks intensified debate and policy changes around counterterrorism, border controls, surveillance authorities, and information-sharing within the EU.
- **Public life:** Memorials and public commemorations became recurring civic events, and the attacks remained a reference point for European security posture in the late 2010s.
