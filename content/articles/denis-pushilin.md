## Summary

Head of the Russian-backed Donetsk People's Republic involved in the conflict that included the MH17 shoot-down.

## Background (high level)

- Born in Makiivka (Donetsk Oblast) on 9 May 1981.
- Served in the National Guard of Ukraine (late 1990s) per biographical accounts.
- Studied economics-related coursework at Donbas National Academy of Civil Engineering and Architecture (did not complete a degree, per Wikipedia).

## Public profile and earlier activity

- Reported by Wikipedia to have been involved (2011–2013) with a successor version of the “MMM” pyramid / Ponzi-style scheme promoted by Sergei Mavrodi.
- Entered politics around the 2014 pro-Russian separatist movement in Donetsk.

## Role in the Donetsk People’s Republic (DPR)

- Became one of the visible political figures associated with DPR governance structures created during the conflict in eastern Ukraine.
- Held senior parliamentary/legislative leadership roles (chairmanship of the DPR’s council/“People’s Council” is listed on Wikipedia in multiple periods).
- After Alexander Zakharchenko’s assassination (2018), Wikipedia describes Pushilin as moving into top leadership and later winning the disputed 2018 DPR election.

## Sanctions and international status

- Wikipedia reports he has been subject to sanctions by multiple jurisdictions (including the EU and the US), with additional listings by other countries.
- The “Head of the Donetsk People’s Republic” title is described as “disputed” in common international references.
