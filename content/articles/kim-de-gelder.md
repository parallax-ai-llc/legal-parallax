## Summary

Belgian man who stabbed three people to death at a daycare center, including two babies.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2009 | Committed Dendermonde nursery attack |

## Key Facts (High Level)

- **Incident**: The attack took place in **Dendermonde, Belgium**, at a daycare center (often reported as the *Fabeltjesland* nursery).
- **Date**: Widely reported as **January 23, 2009**.
- **Method**: The killings were carried out with a **knife**; multiple other people were injured.
- **Victims**: Two **infants** and one **adult caregiver** were killed.
- **Public reporting**: Contemporary coverage frequently noted that the attacker was **disguised** and that the event shocked the country.

## Background (Reported)

- De Gelder is commonly described in reporting as a **Belgian man born in 1988**.
- Media accounts around the case have described him as socially isolated and suffering from mental health issues; details vary by source.

## Legal Proceedings and Outcome

- Belgian authorities arrested and prosecuted De Gelder for the nursery attack.
- He was **convicted** in Belgian court and received a **life sentence** (as reported in major coverage and summarized by Wikipedia).
- Court reporting around the trial discussed questions of **criminal responsibility** and mental health assessment.

## Broader Impact

- The Dendermonde nursery attack became a prominent case in Belgium and was widely discussed in terms of:
  - **Childcare and school security** practices
  - **Emergency response** readiness
  - **Public debate** about mental health care and risk assessment
