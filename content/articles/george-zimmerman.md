## Background

- Zimmerman became a national figure due to the 2012 shooting of Trayvon Martin in Sanford, Florida.
- Prior to the incident, he was involved in a neighborhood watch group in the Retreat at Twin Lakes community.

## Trayvon Martin Shooting (2012)

- On February 26, 2012, Zimmerman shot 17-year-old Trayvon Martin during an encounter after Zimmerman reported a “suspicious person” to a non-emergency dispatcher.
- The incident and subsequent investigation drew intense national attention, including debates about racial profiling, self-defense, and policing.

## Criminal Case and Trial

- Zimmerman was charged in Florida state court; prosecutors pursued a second-degree murder theory at trial.
- In July 2013, a jury acquitted Zimmerman of second-degree murder and manslaughter.
- The trial featured competing accounts of the confrontation and the application of Florida self-defense law.

## Public Reaction and Broader Impact

- The case became a flashpoint for discussions on race, the criminal justice system, and the limits of self-defense claims.
- The shooting and acquittal were widely cited in later activism and public discourse, including the early growth of the Black Lives Matter movement.

## Aftermath

- Zimmerman remained a polarizing public figure in the years following the verdict.
- He faced additional legal issues unrelated to the Martin case, which continued to keep him in the news.
