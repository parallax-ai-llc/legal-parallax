## Background

- Kim Potter served as a police officer in Minnesota and later became associated with the Brooklyn Center Police Department.[^1]
- Public attention around her primarily stems from an on-duty shooting during a traffic stop that resulted in the death of Daunte Wright.[^1]

## 2021 Shooting of Daunte Wright (Context)

- The incident occurred during a traffic stop in Brooklyn Center, Minnesota, in April 2021.[^1]
- Reporting and court records commonly describe the shooting as involving Potter discharging a handgun at close range during an attempt to arrest Wright.[^1]
- The case received broad media coverage and was widely discussed in the context of U.S. policing, training, and use-of-force practices.[^1]

## Legal Proceedings (High-level)

- Potter was charged in connection with Wright's death and later stood trial in Minnesota state court.[^1]
- She was convicted of manslaughter (as reflected in many summaries of the case).[^1]
- Sentencing and post-trial developments are summarized in major reference accounts such as Wikipedia and contemporaneous news coverage.[^1]

## Public and Institutional Impact

- The shooting and subsequent trial contributed to public debate about:
  - training and weapon-handling procedures
  - traffic-stop enforcement practices
  - accountability mechanisms for officers and departments
- The event also occurred during a period of heightened scrutiny of policing in the Minneapolis–Saint Paul region.[^1]
