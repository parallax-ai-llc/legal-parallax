## Additional Context

### Early life and education

- Baburova was born in Sevastopol and later moved to Moscow for higher education.
- She studied in multiple programs before focusing on journalism at Moscow State University.
- She was reported to speak Russian and Ukrainian as native languages, and also spoke English and French.

### Journalism and political activity

- Baburova worked as a journalist and/or freelance contributor while studying, publishing across several Russian newspapers.
- She became associated with anti-fascist, anarchist, and environmentalist circles and participated in protests and social forum events.
- In late 2008 she began investigating neo-Nazi groups for *Novaya Gazeta*, work that is frequently cited in accounts of her public profile.

### Assassination (2009)

- On 19 January 2009, Baburova was shot in central Moscow in an attack that also killed human rights lawyer Stanislav Markelov.
- Contemporary reporting and later court findings linked the attack to far-right / neo-Nazi extremists.

### Aftermath and legal proceedings

- The case became part of broader public scrutiny of violent far-right networks in Russia in the late 2000s.
- In 2011, a Moscow court convicted Nikita Tikhonov and Yevgenia Khasis for the murders of Markelov and Baburova; Tikhonov received a life sentence and Khasis a lengthy prison term.
