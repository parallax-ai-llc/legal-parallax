## Additional Details (Sourced)

- Wikipedia summarizes Jimmy Maketta as a South African rapist and serial killer born in 1964/65.[^1]
- According to reporting cited by Wikipedia (News24), he pleaded guilty in 2007 and was convicted on 16 counts of murder and 19 counts of rape.[^2]
- The crimes are described as occurring over a span from April to December 2005.[^1]
- The attacks were reported to have taken place near the township of Philippi, in Cape Town, where he would watch from a hill and target farm labourers on Friday evenings.[^3]
- A state psychiatrist (as reported by News24 and cited by Wikipedia) described him as a psychopath.[^2]

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)

- Targeted vulnerable victims in and around informal settlements and farm-labour areas.
- Used opportunistic, repetitive attacks over a limited time window (months rather than years).
- Sexual violence was a central element of the crime pattern (rape convictions reported alongside murder convictions).[^2]

## Legal Outcome

- Pleaded guilty (reported in 2007) and was convicted on multiple counts of murder and rape.[^2]
- Public reporting framed the case as a high-count serial offense with dozens of charges considered together at conviction.[^2]

## Context

- The case is frequently listed among South African serial killer cases due to the high number of victims attributed in the conviction record.[^1]
- Coverage emphasizes the concentrated crime spree period in 2005 and subsequent court proceedings reported in 2007.[^1]
