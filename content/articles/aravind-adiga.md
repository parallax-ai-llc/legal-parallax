## Background

- **Born:** 1974-10-23 (as listed in frontmatter).
- **Nationality:** Indian.
- Adiga is widely known as a contemporary Indian novelist and journalist whose fiction often examines class, corruption, and the moral costs of economic change in modern India.[^1]

## Notable Works (selected)

- **The White Tiger** (debut novel; often discussed as a darkly comic social satire).[^1]
- **Between the Assassinations** (linked short stories/novella-in-stories).[^1]
- **Last Man in Tower** (novel).[^1]
- **Selection Day** (novel).[^1]

## Themes and Approach

- **Class and inequality:** frequent focus on structural inequality and everyday exploitation in urban and semi-urban settings.[^1]
- **Power and corruption:** attention to how institutions, patronage, and informal systems shape outcomes.[^1]
- **Moral ambiguity:** protagonists are often placed in ethical traps rather than clean hero/villain arcs.

## Style (high-level)

- Uses **first-person narration** and **satirical tone** in major works (especially the debut), mixing dark humor with social critique.[^1]
- Often foregrounds **voice** (a strong, distinct narrator) and **observational detail** about workplaces, housing, and informal economies.

## Recognition

- Adiga’s debut novel **The White Tiger** won the **Man Booker Prize** (now the Booker Prize).[^1]
