## Background

Wellington Menezes de Oliveira was a Brazilian man and former student of Escola Municipal Tasso da Silveira (a municipal school in the Realengo neighborhood of Rio de Janeiro). Reporting on the attack noted that he left written material describing grievances and ideological views, which investigators and journalists used to reconstruct aspects of his motive and planning.[^1]

## Realengo school shooting (2011)

On 7 April 2011, he entered the school and opened fire on students and staff, killing 12 children and injuring others. The attack became known in Brazil as the "Realengo school massacre" (or Realengo school shooting) and was one of the country’s most prominent school attacks.[^1]

High-level sequence, as commonly reported:

- He arrived at the school during the morning and began shooting in classrooms and corridors.
- Police were called to the scene; when confronted, he shot himself and died at the location.

## Aftermath and public response

The killings triggered nationwide mourning and renewed debate in Brazil about school security and firearms access, along with broader discussion about online radicalization and the warning signs of planned violence.[^1]
