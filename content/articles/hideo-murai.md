## Summary

Aum Shinrikyo's science minister stabbed to death on camera by a Korean-Japanese man.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1995 | Stabbed to death on live television |

## Background

- Murai held a doctorate in astrophysics and became one of Aum Shinrikyo's most prominent technical figures.[^1]
- In Aum's internal hierarchy he was widely reported as a top leader (often described as third in command), serving under Shoko Asahara and Kiyohide Hayakawa.[^1]

## Role in Aum Shinrikyo

- Headed Aum Shinrikyo's "Ministry of Science and Technology", a portfolio that covered technical projects and the group's efforts to acquire and develop unconventional capabilities.[^1][^3]
- Named in reporting and later accounts as one of the perpetrators responsible for the Sakamoto family murder case, an early high-profile crime linked to the organization.[^1]
- Helped plan the 1995 Tokyo subway sarin attack, one of the most consequential acts of domestic terrorism in modern Japanese history.[^1]

## Assassination (1995)

- Murai was stabbed repeatedly in public by Hiroyuki Jo (徐裕行), described in reporting as an ethnic Korean man with ties to the Yamaguchi-gumi yakuza organization.[^1][^2]
- The attack occurred in the presence of police officers and numerous reporters; footage was recorded and broadcast as the event unfolded.[^1][^2]
- Murai was arrested at the scene and died shortly afterward in an ambulance.[^1][^2]

## Aftermath and Interpretations

- Court proceedings later dismissed allegations that a particular gang figure ordered the killing, amid claims that the motive was to prevent public disclosure of alleged yakuza connections to the cult.[^1]
- Jo was sentenced to 12 years in prison for the murder in subsequent proceedings.[^1]
