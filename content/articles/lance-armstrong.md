## High-Level Profile

- American professional road racing cyclist best known for his Tour de France era and subsequent doping scandal.
- Also known for his public cancer-survivor narrative and charity fundraising through the Livestrong Foundation (founded in the late 1990s).

## Notable Achievements (Context)

- 1993: World Champion in the men’s road race.
- 1999–2005: Seven Tour de France wins were recorded at the time, then later voided due to anti-doping rulings.

## Doping Controversy (Overview)

- Armstrong was the subject of long-running allegations of systematic doping during his peak years.
- Sanctions and subsequent admissions led to the annulment of key results, including Tour de France titles.
- This topic is central to his modern historical reputation and is typically the lead framing in reference sources.

## Cancer, Recovery, and Public Impact

- In 1996 he was diagnosed with advanced testicular cancer and underwent surgery and chemotherapy.
- After returning to competition, his comeback story became a major part of his public profile.
- The Livestrong brand became widely associated with cancer awareness and fundraising, particularly in the 2000s.

## Why He Matters (Historical/Media)

- A prominent example of how elite sports, celebrity narratives, and anti-doping enforcement intersect.
- His case is frequently cited in discussions about institutional pressures in endurance sports and the evolution of anti-doping policy.
