## Background

- Kudirat Abiola was the wife of Moshood Kashimawo Olawale (M. K. O.) Abiola, the Nigerian businessman and politician widely associated with the annulled 1993 presidential election.
- She became a prominent public figure during Nigeria’s 1990s political crisis, particularly in the period following the annulment of the June 12, 1993 election and the consolidation of military rule.

## Historical Context

- The early-to-mid 1990s in Nigeria were marked by military governance, crackdowns on civil society, and efforts by pro-democracy groups to restore civilian rule.
- The June 12 movement and broader democratic agitation centered on demands to validate the 1993 election results, release political detainees, and end military dictatorship.

## Pro-democracy Activism

- Abiola was known for public advocacy on behalf of democratic governance and civil liberties during the Abacha-era dictatorship.
- Her activism is often described in connection with protests, public statements, and the mobilization of civil society actors calling for a return to constitutional order.

## Assassination (1996)

- She was assassinated in Lagos on 4 June 1996.
- Her killing became a high-profile symbol of political violence in Nigeria during the period of military rule.
- In later years, Nigerian public discourse and legal proceedings linked her assassination to state-linked actors from that era.

## Legacy

- Kudirat Abiola is remembered in Nigeria as a pro-democracy martyr whose death underscored the risks faced by activists and opposition figures under authoritarian governments.
- Her name has been referenced in commemorations of the June 12 struggle and broader narratives about Nigeria’s transition to civilian rule.
