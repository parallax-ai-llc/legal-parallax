## Expanded Context

This entry appears to be a thin/duplicate stub that points at **Shoko Asahara** (Chizuo Matsumoto), the founder and spiritual leader of **Aum Shinrikyo**. In practice, "Aum Shinrikyo leadership" is usually discussed as:

- the role of the founder at the top of the organization
- the senior disciples/executives who ran day-to-day operations
- the organization’s internal governance, titles, and discipline

### Leadership model (high-level)

Aum Shinrikyo was structured around a charismatic central leader and a cadre of senior disciples who held minister-like roles. Reporting flowed upward toward the founder, and internal titles and departments were used to manage recruitment, finance, facilities, and (historically) technical capabilities.

Key characteristics that are widely described in reporting and secondary sources include:

- **centralized authority** around the founder/"guru"
- **ideological control** and heavy internal discipline
- **delegation to trusted lieutenants** (senior disciples) who executed operational plans

### Notable leadership figures (examples)

The exact set of "leadership" names depends on the period being discussed; commonly cited senior figures include:

- **Shoko Asahara (Chizuo Matsumoto)** — founder and supreme authority
- **Kiyohide Hayakawa** — senior disciple; associated with facilities/operations (historical reporting)
- **Hideo Murai** — senior disciple; public-facing figure in the early 1990s (killed in 1995)
- **Fumihiro Joyu** — spokesperson and senior figure, particularly visible in the 1990s

(These examples are included to give the topic substance; they are not an exhaustive list.)

### Selected organizational milestones (context)

- **1980s**: group develops from a yoga/spiritual movement into a larger religious organization
- **1995**: Tokyo subway sarin attack becomes the defining event shaping legal action and public perception
- **2000s–2010s**: continued monitoring of successor and related groups by Japanese authorities
- **2018**: execution of Shoko Asahara and multiple senior members convicted for major crimes
