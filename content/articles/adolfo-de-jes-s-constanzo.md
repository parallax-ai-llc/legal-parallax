This entry preserves the name variant (with diacritics) used in some sources; most English-language coverage uses **Adolfo Constanzo**. The underlying subject is the same person: a Cuban-born occult practitioner who led a small criminal group in Mexico in the late 1980s and became associated with a series of murders tied to a Palo Mayombe–influenced cult.

## Background

- **Origins:** Born in Cuba and later lived in the United States before relocating to Mexico.
- **Religious/occult framing:** Reports describe his practices as drawing on **Palo Mayombe** traditions (an Afro-Caribbean religion with syncretic elements), though media accounts often sensationalize or flatten the distinctions between belief, ritual, and criminal activity.
- **Criminal association:** He is commonly described as leading a small, tightly controlled group that mixed superstition, coercion, and organized crime.

## Crimes and cult activity (high level)

- **Purpose of violence (as reported):** Sources describe killings as part of ritualized acts intended to obtain protection, luck, or power.
- **Victim profile:** Accounts include both targeted victims tied to the group’s criminal disputes and individuals killed opportunistically.
- **Public notoriety:** The case became widely known in Mexico and internationally due to the combination of alleged occult ritual, narcotics-linked crime, and the discovery of a rural site connected to the group.

## Investigation and death

- **Catalyst:** The disappearance of an American student in Mexico drew significant attention and intensified law-enforcement efforts.
- **Manhunt:** Following raids and arrests of associates, authorities located him in Mexico City.
- **Death:** He died in 1989; widely cited accounts state he was killed by an associate at his request during a police standoff.
