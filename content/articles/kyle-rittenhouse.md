## Summary

American teenager acquitted of all charges after fatally shooting two people during Kenosha protests.

## Background

- Raised in Antioch, Illinois, near the Wisconsin border.
- Became widely known in connection with events during the 2020 unrest in Kenosha, Wisconsin.

## Kenosha shootings (2020)

- On August 25, 2020, Rittenhouse shot three people during protests and unrest in Kenosha; two of the individuals later died.
- He was arrested the following day and faced multiple criminal charges in Wisconsin.

## Trial and verdict (2021)

- The case centered on whether the shootings were justified as self-defense under Wisconsin law.
- On November 19, 2021, a jury found him not guilty on all counts.

## Public impact

- The incident and trial became a major national flashpoint in U.S. debates about:
  - firearms and open carry
  - protest policing and civil unrest
  - self-defense law and use-of-force standards
  - media framing and political polarization

## Key facts (high-level)

- Notable for the scale of media coverage and the speed with which the case became politicized.
- "Acquitted" refers to the criminal verdict; it does not necessarily resolve broader moral, political, or civil-liability debates.
