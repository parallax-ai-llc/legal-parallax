## Summary

Boko Haram kidnapped 276 schoolgirls from their dormitory in Chibok, northeastern Nigeria. The mass abduction sparked the #BringBackOurGirls campaign; some girls remain missing.

## Context and Background

- **Location**: Chibok is a town in Borno State, northeastern Nigeria, a region heavily affected by the Boko Haram insurgency.
- **Target**: The abducted students were at the Government Girls Secondary School in Chibok, where some had been taking exams.
- **Security environment**: The incident occurred amid broader attacks on civilians and institutions, including schools, during the conflict.

## Perpetrators

- The kidnapping is widely attributed to **Boko Haram**, an Islamist militant group operating primarily in northeastern Nigeria.
- The group has been known for using mass abductions and violence against education as part of its campaign.

## Immediate Impact

- The kidnapping drew national and international attention due to the scale of the abduction and the vulnerability of the victims.
- Families and local communities faced prolonged uncertainty, while Nigeria’s security response became a subject of intense scrutiny.

## International Response and Advocacy

- The **#BringBackOurGirls** campaign amplified global awareness and sustained public pressure for action.
- Governments and international organizations offered support to Nigeria, including advisory, intelligence, and humanitarian assistance (details vary by source).

## Aftermath and Ongoing Status

- Over time, **some of the abducted girls were released or escaped**, while others were reported missing for years afterward.
- The situation has remained a reference point in discussions of civilian protection, gender-based violence in conflict, and attacks on education.

## Notes on the Numbers

- The commonly cited figure is **276 abducted**, but public reporting has sometimes differed due to evolving lists and the complexity of verification during crisis events.
- Where exact counts are uncertain, secondary sources typically rely on official statements and later reconciled records.
