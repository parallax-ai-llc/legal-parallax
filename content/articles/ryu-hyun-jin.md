## Additional Context

### Early career in Korea (high-level)

- He first became widely known through professional play in South Korea before moving to Major League Baseball.
- Discussions of his early years often emphasize his ability to pitch effectively as a starter at a young age and to carry significant workloads.

### Transition to MLB

- His MLB move is commonly framed as part of the broader trend of top KBO players testing themselves in North American leagues.
- Early MLB coverage frequently highlighted his command, mix of pitches, and ability to limit hard contact when healthy.

### Pitching profile (high-level)

- Generally described as a command-focused left-handed starter who mixes multiple pitches to disrupt timing.
- Evaluations often mention an emphasis on changing speeds, locating to both sides of the plate, and using off-speed offerings to set up the fastball.

### Injuries and durability narrative

- A recurring theme in his career storyline is the balance between peak performance and periods affected by injuries.
- When available, he has been discussed as a rotation stabilizer; when unavailable, teams have had to plan around his health-related uncertainty.

### Why the 2019 season is remembered

- The 2019 campaign is often cited as a high point due to league-leading run prevention and award recognition.
- Commentators sometimes use it as an example of how elite command and sequencing can produce top-tier results even without consistently overpowering velocity.

### Legacy and broader significance

- In Korean baseball discourse, he is frequently referenced as a prominent example of a pitcher who built a career across both KBO and MLB.
- His career is also used in discussions about international scouting, posting systems, and the adaptation challenges pitchers face when moving between leagues.
