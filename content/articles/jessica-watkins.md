## Summary

Oath Keepers member convicted of conspiracy for storming the Capitol on January 6.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2023 | Convicted of conspiracy |

## Key Details

- **Affiliation**: Identified in court filings and reporting as a member of the **Oath Keepers**, a far-right militia organization.
- **January 6, 2021**: Participated in events at the **U.S. Capitol** during the attack on Congress as it met to certify the 2020 presidential election results.
- **Core allegation**: Prosecutors argued she was part of coordinated activity with other Oath Keepers members, including communications and planning around January 6.

## Background

Public reporting about Watkins has described her as:

- A former **U.S. Army** service member.
- Based in **Ohio** in the period leading up to January 6.
- Associated with local militia-style organizing aligned with the Oath Keepers brand and networks.

## Legal Case (High-Level)

Watkins was among defendants prosecuted in the large, multi-year federal investigation into January 6. In broad terms, the case involved:

- **Federal charges** related to conspiracy and obstruction tied to the certification proceeding.
- The use of **digital evidence** (messages, group chats, and travel/coordination records) to establish coordination.
- A trial process that produced multiple verdicts across different Oath Keepers-linked defendants.

## Why She Is Notable

- She is frequently cited as one of the better-known **Oath Keepers-linked** defendants from the January 6 prosecutions.
- Her case is commonly discussed in the context of how federal prosecutors approached **group-based conspiracy** theories for January 6.
