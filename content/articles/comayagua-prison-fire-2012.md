## Summary

Fire at Comayagua Prison in Honduras killed 361 inmates, the deadliest prison fire in modern history. The prison was at over 200% capacity with locked cells and no fire plan.

## Context and Conditions

- **Location**: Comayagua, Honduras (regional prison complex).
- **Overcrowding**: Reports describe occupancy far above designed capacity, with limited separation between cell blocks.
- **Security-first design**: Locked cells and restricted movement were emphasized over rapid evacuation.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- A fire broke out inside the prison complex during the night.
- Many prisoners were unable to exit due to **locked cell doors** and limited access to keys.
- Smoke inhalation and burns were reported as leading causes of death, consistent with large enclosed-structure fires.

## Contributing Factors (Commonly Cited)

- **Overcrowding** increased the number of people exposed and complicated evacuation.
- **Infrastructure and safety gaps**: limited fire detection, suppression, or emergency planning capacity has been widely discussed.
- **Delayed access** to affected areas due to security protocols.

## Immediate Response and Aftermath

- Emergency responders and prison staff attempted rescue and suppression, but conditions inside the facility limited speed and reach.
- The incident became a national and international news event because of the exceptionally high death toll.
- Families gathered outside the prison seeking information, highlighting the difficulty of rapid identification and notification after mass-casualty incidents.

## Significance

- Often cited as the **deadliest prison fire in modern history**.
- The event is frequently referenced in discussions about:
  - prison overcrowding,
  - fire safety standards in detention facilities,
  - and the trade-off between security controls and emergency egress.
