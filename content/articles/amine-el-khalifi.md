## Summary

Moroccan convicted of planning a suicide bombing at the US Capitol.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2012 | Convicted of Capitol bomb plot |

## Background

- Entered the United States on a visitor visa as a teenager; remained after the visa expired (reported to have expired in 1999).[^1]
- Lived in Northern Virginia (Arlington County area) and worked various jobs.
- Came to the attention of law enforcement prior to the plot, and was later placed under federal surveillance (reported as beginning by early 2011).[^1]

## Plot and Investigation (FBI Sting)

- The case is widely described as a sting operation in which El Khalifi believed he was dealing with al-Qaeda operatives, but was communicating with undercover FBI personnel and/or confidential sources.[^1]
- Public reporting states authorities believed he had no actual ties to al-Qaeda, and that all weapons/material support involved in the plan were controlled and provided by the FBI, with the operation structured so the public was not in danger.[^1]
- Early concepts attributed to him included ideas ranging from a mass shooting to a suicide bombing, before focusing on an attack near the U.S. Capitol.[^1]

## Arrest, Charges, and Court Outcome

- Arrested after attempting to proceed with what he understood to be a suicide bombing plan targeting the U.S. Capitol area.[^1]
- Charged federally with **attempted use of a weapon of mass destruction** against federal property (18 U.S.C. § 2332a).[^1]
- Pleaded guilty in June 2012 and was sentenced later that year to **30 years in prison**.[^1]

## Incarceration

- Reported to be incarcerated within the U.S. federal prison system (e.g., listed as FCI Memphis in some summaries).[^1]
