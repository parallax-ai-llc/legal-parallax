## Additional Context

### Early years and background (high-level)

- According to Wikipedia, he is one of the Ochoa brothers (Juan David and Fabio) and emerged from a family involved in cattle-breeding and restaurant businesses before moving into narcotics trafficking in the mid-1970s.
- Wikipedia describes him as presenting a "businessman" profile and being associated with cattle ranching (including fighting bulls) as well as collecting Harley-Davidson motorcycles.

### Role in the Medellín Cartel

- Wikipedia lists him as a founding member of the Medellín Cartel (late 1970s) alongside figures such as Pablo Escobar, Carlos Lehder, José Gonzalo Rodríguez Gacha, and Gustavo Gaviria.
- Wikipedia states he coordinated cartel operations in the United States and Western Europe, and claims are attributed to him about shipping large quantities of cocaine per month during the early 1980s.

### Extradition pressure and legal milestones

- Wikipedia notes that following intensifying Colombian anti-cartel efforts (including extradition policy shifts in the mid-1980s), he faced U.S. indictments and was arrested in Spain (Madrid) in 1984 on a U.S. warrant.
- Wikipedia reports a Spanish court decision (1986) to extradite him to stand trial in Colombia, and that subsequent U.S. extradition requests were a major political flashpoint.
- Wikipedia states he surrendered to Colombian authorities in January 1991 under policies offering reduced sentences served in Colombia, and that he was released in July 1996 after serving about five and a half years.
