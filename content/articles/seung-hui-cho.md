## Summary
Seung-Hui Cho killed 32 people and wounded 17 at Virginia Tech on April 16, 2007. It was the deadliest school shooting in US history. He then killed himself.

## Context and Background
- Cho was born in South Korea and later immigrated to the United States with his family as a child.
- Multiple retrospective accounts describe longstanding difficulties with communication, social isolation, and mental health concerns.
- In public discussion, the case is often cited in debates about campus threat assessment, mental health intervention, and firearm access.

## Education
- He attended Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University (Virginia Tech) in Blacksburg, Virginia.
- He studied English and wrote creative work as part of his coursework; some classmates and faculty later described the work as disturbing.

## 2007 Virginia Tech Attack (High-Level)
- Date: April 16, 2007.
- Location: Virginia Tech campus.
- Outcome: 32 victims killed and 17 wounded; Cho died by suicide at the scene.
- Cho also sent a package of writings and videos to NBC News during the incident, which was later widely reported.

## Immediate Aftermath
- Virginia Tech and surrounding communities held memorials and established scholarship and remembrance efforts.
- Multiple official reviews examined campus procedures, communications, and law enforcement response.

## Policy and Institutional Impact
- The case contributed to the growth of campus threat assessment teams and emergency notification systems.
- It is frequently referenced in discussions about:
  - information sharing between institutions and mental health providers
  - court-ordered treatment and follow-up
  - background check reporting and implementation
