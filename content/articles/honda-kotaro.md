## Summary

Dup.

Honda Kotaro (本多 光太郎, 1870–1954) was a Japanese metallurgist known for influential work on magnetic materials and steel.

## Key Contributions (High Level)

- **Magnet steels:** He is widely associated with the development of **KS steel**, an early high-performance permanent-magnet steel.
- **Metallurgy & physical properties:** His research connected metallurgical processing with measurable magnetic/physical properties—work that helped shape modern materials science.
- **Institution building:** He is often credited as an important figure in establishing and advancing Japan’s academic research infrastructure for metals/materials.

## Context: Why KS Steel Matters

Permanent magnets require materials with properties such as high coercivity and stable magnetization. In the early 20th century, improvements in magnet steels materially affected:

- electric motors and generators
- measuring instruments
- communications and early electrical technologies

KS steel is frequently cited as one of the notable steps in the evolution from traditional steels toward more specialized magnetic alloys.

## Legacy

- Remembered as a foundational Japanese figure in **metallurgy** and **magnetic materials**.
- Commonly referenced in historical accounts of the rise of **materials research** in Japan.
