## Overview

Warren Edward Buffett (born 1930) is an American investor known for building Berkshire Hathaway into a large conglomerate and for a public track record associated with long-term, fundamentals-driven investing. He is closely associated with Omaha, Nebraska, where Berkshire Hathaway is headquartered.

## Early Interest and Education (High Level)

Public biographies commonly describe Buffett as developing an early interest in business and investing, later studying under Benjamin Graham’s value-investing tradition and applying those ideas in his own partnerships and vehicles before consolidating control at Berkshire Hathaway.

## Berkshire Hathaway and Business Model

Berkshire Hathaway’s structure is frequently summarized as a combination of:

- Wholly owned operating subsidiaries (e.g., insurance, transportation, manufacturing, retail)
- A large portfolio of publicly traded equities
- Significant cash and short-term investments used for flexibility and risk management

A key concept often highlighted is insurance “float,” which can provide investable funds when underwriting is disciplined.

## Public Reputation and Communication

Buffett is well known for plainspoken communication to shareholders, especially through Berkshire Hathaway’s annual shareholder letters. These letters are widely read for commentary on business fundamentals, capital allocation, corporate governance, and market psychology.

## Commonly Cited Long-Term Holdings

In public filings and reporting, Berkshire has been associated with sizable or long-duration positions in companies such as Coca-Cola, American Express, and Apple (among others), though the size and composition of holdings can change over time.

## Philanthropy

Buffett has made prominent philanthropic commitments, including plans to donate most of his wealth, and has made large, recurring donations reported in public sources.
