### The Great Nordic Biker War (overview)

The Great Nordic Biker War was a violent, multi-year conflict primarily associated with rival outlaw motorcycle clubs in Scandinavia—most commonly described as involving **Hells Angels** and the **Bandidos**. Most summaries place the war in the **mid-1990s (often 1994–1997)** and note that it included incidents across multiple countries in the Nordic region.

Commonly described features of the conflict include:

- **Retaliatory violence and escalation**: shootings, bombings, and attacks on clubhouses.
- **Public safety impact**: bystander risk, property damage, and significant policing efforts.
- **Cross-border dynamics**: clubs and affiliated support networks operating across national boundaries.
