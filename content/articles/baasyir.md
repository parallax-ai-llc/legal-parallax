## Summary

See Abu Bakar Bashir. Indonesian cleric and spiritual leader of Jemaah Islamiyah.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1938 | Born (17 Aug) in Jombang Regency, East Java, in the Dutch East Indies (now Indonesia). |
| 1972 | Co-founded the Al-Mukmin Islamic boarding school (Pesantren Al-Mukmin) at Ngruki, Sukoharjo, Central Java (commonly associated with the Ngruki pesantren). |
| 1970s | Active in Islamic preaching and education; later associated with the Darul Islam/Tentara Islam Indonesia milieu in Indonesian security reporting and journalism. |
| 1978–1982 | Imprisoned by the Suharto-era government (period commonly described as tied to charges related to subversion/links to Darul Islam). |
| 1980s–1990s | Lived in exile in Malaysia for years after leaving Indonesia; remained influential among Indonesian Islamist networks. |
| 1999 | Returned to Indonesia after the fall of Suharto. |
| Early 2000s | Frequently described by regional analysts and Indonesian authorities as a leading cleric associated with Jemaah Islamiyah (JI); he is often characterized as a spiritual figure rather than an operational commander. |
| 2010 | Convicted in Indonesia in connection with militant training activities in Aceh (legal proceedings described in Indonesian and international reporting). |
| 2011–2020s | Periods of imprisonment and health-related reporting; his public role continued to be discussed in Indonesian counterterrorism context. |

## Notes and Context

- **Name/alias:** The spelling “Baasyir” is commonly a variant for **Abu Bakar Bashir** (also spelled Ba’asyir/Baasyir/Bashir in English-language sources).
- **Role characterization:** Many summaries describe him as an **ideological or spiritual leader** linked to JI and related networks, with disputes in public reporting and legal filings over the degree of operational involvement.
- **Institutional influence:** The **Ngruki pesantren** is widely cited in regional security literature as an influential educational institution; discussions often distinguish between the school as an institution and the independent trajectories of some alumni.

## Associated Organizations (as described in open sources)

- **Jemaah Islamiyah (JI):** Frequently referenced in reporting and analysis as an organization with which Bashir was associated; JI is known for involvement in major terrorist attacks in Indonesia in the early 2000s.
- **Majelis Mujahidin Indonesia (MMI):** Often described in open sources as an umbrella organization Bashir helped found or lead, associated with Islamist activism in the post-1998 reform era.
- **Pesantren Al-Mukmin (Ngruki):** Educational institution co-founded and commonly linked to his influence.
