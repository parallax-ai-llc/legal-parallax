## Summary

31-man North Korean commando team that attempted to assassinate the South Korean president.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1968 | Failed assassination raid on Blue House |
| 1968-01 | Team infiltrated South Korea and moved toward Seoul in an assassination attempt targeting the Blue House (the presidential residence).[^1] |
| 1968-01 | The raid failed; most of the team members were killed in ensuing fighting or later operations, with a small number captured or escaping north.[^1] |

## Background

- The incident occurred during a tense Cold War period on the Korean Peninsula, with frequent infiltration attempts and clashes along the DMZ.
- The team is commonly referred to as a North Korean commando unit; in Korean sources the event is often discussed as the 1.21 사태 (January 21 incident).[^1]

## Mission and Organization

- **Stated objective:** assassinate the President of South Korea at the Blue House (Cheong Wa Dae).[^1]
- **Unit size:** 31 operatives (often described as a “31-man team”).[^1]
- **Approach:** covert infiltration and movement disguised to blend in with local forces/civilians during approach, aiming to reach the target area before detection.[^1]

## Infiltration and Approach to Seoul

- The team crossed into South Korea and traveled toward Seoul using an overland route designed to avoid major checkpoints.[^1]
- As they neared the capital, security forces became aware of the intrusion and attempted to intercept the group.[^1]

## Engagement and Outcome

- The commando team came into contact with South Korean security forces near Seoul, leading to firefights and a major manhunt.[^1]
- The assassination attempt did not reach the Blue House successfully.
- The incident produced significant casualties among the raiders and also involved South Korean military/police losses and civilian impacts in the course of the pursuit.[^1]

## Aftermath and Impact

- The event drove a sharp rise in domestic security attention and counter-infiltration measures in South Korea.
- It is frequently cited as one of the most serious infiltration/assassination attempts against the South Korean presidency in the post-war era.[^1]

## Legacy

- The “Blue House raid” is used as shorthand for the broader incident and the associated security response.
- The episode remains a recurring reference point in discussions of inter-Korean covert operations, escalation management, and the vulnerability of political leadership to asymmetric attacks.

## Quick Facts

- **Target:** Blue House (Cheong Wa Dae), Seoul
- **Actors:** North Korean commando unit vs. South Korean security forces
- **Result:** failed assassination attempt; extensive pursuit and engagements[^1]
