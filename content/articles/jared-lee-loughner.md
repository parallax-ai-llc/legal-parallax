## Summary

Shot US Congresswoman Gabrielle Giffords in the head and killed 6 others including a federal judge and a 9-year-old girl at a Tucson shopping center. Sentenced to life.

## Overview

- **Notability:** Perpetrator of the 2011 Tucson shooting during a constituent event for U.S. Representative Gabrielle Giffords.
- **Outcome:** Pled guilty in federal court and received **multiple life sentences**.

## Background (high level)

- An American man born in 1988.
- Public reporting describes a history of disruptive behavior and mental health concerns prior to the attack, which later became central in competency proceedings.[^1]

## 2011 Tucson shooting (high level)

- The attack occurred at a public event in **Tucson, Arizona** in January 2011.[^1]
- Representative **Gabrielle Giffords** was seriously injured.
- **Six people were killed** and several others were wounded.[^1]

## Investigation and federal prosecution

- The case was handled in **federal court** due to the target being a member of Congress and related federal charges.[^1]
- Proceedings included competency evaluations and restoration treatment before the case could move forward.[^1]

## Plea and sentencing

- Loughner entered a **guilty plea** to federal charges connected to the shooting.[^1]
- He was sentenced to **life imprisonment**, with the sentence structured to keep him in federal custody for the remainder of his life.[^1]

## Significance and public impact

- The shooting became a prominent U.S. case study in:
  - security at public political events,
  - the intersection of violent crime and competency to stand trial,
  - victim recovery and long-term community impact.[^1]
