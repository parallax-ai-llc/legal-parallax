## Summary

See Warren Anderson. The 1984 Bhopal gas leak at the Union Carbide plant in India killed over 15,000 people and affected 600,000. It was the world's worst industrial disaster. Chairman Warren Anderson was charged with homicide but never faced trial.

## Context and Background

- **Location**: Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh, India.
- **Facility**: Union Carbide India Limited (UCIL) pesticide plant (major shareholder: Union Carbide Corporation).
- **Hazardous chemical**: **Methyl isocyanate (MIC)**, a highly reactive and toxic intermediate used in pesticide production.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- In the night of **2–3 December 1984**, a large quantity of toxic gas escaped from the plant.
- A dense cloud spread into surrounding neighborhoods while most residents were asleep.
- Acute exposure caused mass casualties and long-term health impacts in the affected population.

## Causes and Contributing Factors (Reported)

High-level factors commonly discussed in investigations and reporting include:

- **Water ingress** into a MIC storage tank, triggering an exothermic reaction and rapid pressure increase.
- **Safety systems** that were inadequate, offline, bypassed, or not effective under the conditions that occurred.
- **Maintenance and operational practices** that increased risk (e.g., equipment condition, staffing/training, process controls).

*(Specific technical findings vary by source and inquiry; the above is a consolidated overview.)*

## Human Impact

- **Deaths**: Estimates vary; many sources cite **thousands** of immediate deaths and **tens of thousands** in total over time.
- **Injuries/illness**: Large-scale respiratory, ocular, and other acute injuries; chronic conditions reported for years afterward.
- **Intergenerational effects**: Ongoing concerns in affected communities regarding long-term public health and environmental exposure.

## Legal and Corporate Aftermath (Overview)

- The disaster led to criminal and civil proceedings in India and international litigation.
- Union Carbide reached a **settlement with the Government of India** (widely reported as **US$470 million**, 1989).
- Warren Anderson (then Union Carbide CEO/chairman) faced charges in India but did not appear for trial; his status remains central in many summaries of accountability.
- Union Carbide later became a subsidiary of **Dow Chemical** (2001), which has been referenced in ongoing debates about responsibility and remediation.

## Legacy and Significance

- Frequently cited as the **worst industrial disaster** in modern history.
- A watershed event for:
  - **Industrial safety regulation** and emergency planning.
  - **Corporate accountability** and transnational liability debates.
  - **Community right-to-know** policies and hazardous chemical risk management.
