## Summary

Kenyan-born al-Shabaab commander who led the Garissa University attack that killed 148 students. Son of a Kenyan government official who had been radicalized.

## Background

- Abdullahi was a Kenyan national associated with the militant Islamist group al-Shabaab.[^1]
- Public reporting has described him as coming from a relatively well-known family in Kenya, which drew additional attention to his radicalization.[^1]

## Role in the Garissa University attack (2015)

- The Garissa University College attack took place in April 2015 and resulted in the deaths of 148 people, primarily students.[^1]
- Abdullahi is widely reported as having been one of the principal commanders involved in planning or directing the assault.[^1]
- The attack was claimed by al-Shabaab and became one of the deadliest militant attacks in Kenya in recent decades.[^1]

## Death and immediate aftermath

- Kenyan security forces ended the siege after an extended operation at the campus.[^1]
- Abdullahi was reported killed during the security response in April 2015.[^1]
- In Kenyan public discourse, his case was frequently cited in debates about recruitment networks, border security, and counterterrorism strategy.[^1]

## Context

- al-Shabaab has conducted cross-border attacks and operations in Kenya, often framing them as retaliation for Kenya's military involvement in Somalia.[^1]
- The Garissa attack intensified domestic and international focus on regional counterterrorism coordination in East Africa.[^1]
