## Summary

Belgian teenager who shot and killed a Malian nanny and toddler in a racist attack in Antwerp.

## Background (high-level)

- Raised in Belgium; accounts note exposure to far-right ideology in the period leading up to the attack.[^1]
- The case became a focal point in Belgian public debate about racism, political extremism, and integration.[^1]

## 2006 Antwerp shooting (overview)

- Date: 11 May 2006 (Antwerp, Belgium).[^1]
- Attack type: firearm shooting targeting people perceived as non-White; widely described as racially motivated.[^1]
- Victims: a Malian-born nanny and a two-year-old child were killed; another woman was injured.[^1]

## Legal process and sentence

- Prosecutors framed the act as racist violence; the case proceeded through Belgian criminal courts.[^1]
- Outcome: convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment (reported in public sources); subsequent appeals were addressed in later proceedings.[^1]

## Impact and legacy

- The killings were referenced in discussions of hate crime policy and the monitoring of extremist networks in Belgium.[^1]
- Public reactions included demonstrations and renewed attention to anti-racism initiatives in Antwerp and nationally.[^1]
