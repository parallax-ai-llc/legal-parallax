## Background

- Chanda Kochhar is an Indian banking executive best known for leading ICICI Bank, one of India’s largest private-sector banks.
- Her career is frequently discussed in the context of corporate governance, credit underwriting, and conflict-of-interest controls in large financial institutions.

## ICICI Bank and Leadership (high level)

- Kochhar rose through ICICI/ICICI Bank’s leadership ranks and became CEO and Managing Director.
- During her tenure, ICICI expanded its retail and corporate banking footprint, and the bank operated under increasing public scrutiny as India’s credit cycle tightened.

## Videocon/Corporate Loan Controversy (overview)

- Indian authorities and media reporting linked Kochhar to allegations that ICICI extended credit to entities associated with the Videocon Group.
- The core allegation discussed publicly is a conflict-of-interest: loan decisions at ICICI were allegedly connected to benefits received by parties linked to Kochhar’s family.
- The matter has been framed as involving bank-fraud and money-laundering investigations rather than routine credit losses.

## Legal and Regulatory Aftermath

- Kochhar was removed from her leadership role and later faced investigative and legal actions.
- Arrest and court proceedings have been reported as part of broader enforcement efforts around financial misconduct and governance failures.
