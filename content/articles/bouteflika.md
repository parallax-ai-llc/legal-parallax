## Summary

High-level notes:
- Abdelaziz Bouteflika was a longtime figure in Algeria’s post-independence political establishment and served as president from 1999 to 2019.
- His presidency is often associated with efforts to end or de-escalate violence after the 1990s civil conflict, alongside entrenched elite bargaining and limits on political competition.
- After a 2013 stroke, his reduced public presence became a defining feature of late-period governance and public debate.
- The 2019 Hirak protest movement opposed his proposed fifth term and contributed to his resignation.

## Presidency (1999–2019)

- **National reconciliation framing:** Bouteflika pursued policies presented as a route to restore stability after the 1990s conflict, including amnesty-related measures that remained contested by victims’ families and rights advocates.
- **State capacity and hydrocarbons:** Algeria’s political economy during his tenure remained heavily dependent on oil and gas revenues, shaping fiscal space, social spending, and state patronage.
- **Institutional changes:** The 2008 constitutional revision removing term limits enabled him to seek additional terms beyond the earlier two-term constraint.
- **Governance late period:** Following the 2013 stroke, day-to-day governance was widely perceived as increasingly managed by close advisers and political-business networks.

## Controversies and End of Tenure

- **Corruption allegations:** His era is frequently discussed in connection with corruption and elite capture; multiple figures associated with the period faced legal proceedings after his resignation.
- **Fifth-term crisis:** The announcement of a fifth-term candidacy catalyzed sustained nationwide demonstrations (Hirak), which demanded systemic political change.
- **Resignation:** Under intensifying protests and institutional pressure, he resigned in April 2019.

## Legacy (high-level)

- Remembered by supporters for relative stabilization after the 1990s and by critics for democratic backsliding, entrenched patronage, and constrained accountability.
- The Hirak movement is often cited as a major civic turning point in contemporary Algeria, with Bouteflika’s attempted fifth term serving as a trigger.
