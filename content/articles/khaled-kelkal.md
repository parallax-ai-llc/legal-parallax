Franco-Algerian terrorist behind the 1995 Paris Metro bombings, shot dead by police on live TV.

## Background

- **Known for**: Being identified by French authorities as a key participant in the **1995 Islamist terrorist campaign in France**, including the Paris public-transport bombings.
- **Profile**: Often described in reporting as a young Franco-Algerian man whose trajectory combined **petty crime** and later **militant networks**, a pattern that became a reference point in later French debates about radicalization.

## 1995 Attacks (High-Level)

The 1995 bombing campaign in France targeted **public spaces and transit** and caused civilian casualties. Kelkal’s name became prominent after investigators linked him to the network responsible for the attacks and circulated his image widely.

Key points commonly noted in accounts:

- The campaign’s most infamous incident was the **Saint-Michel RER bombing** in Paris (July 1995), part of a sequence of attacks and attempted attacks that year.
- The investigation relied on **forensic and identification leads** (including fingerprint-related reporting), which contributed to Kelkal’s rapid identification.

## Manhunt and Death

Kelkal was the subject of an intensive manhunt. He was **killed by French police** on 29 September 1995 during an attempted arrest in the Lyon area, an event that was **filmed and broadcast**, becoming a highly visible moment in French counterterrorism history.

## Aftermath and Significance

- **Public impact**: The televised footage and the broader 1995 attacks contributed to a lasting public memory and to increased attention on counterterrorism methods and civil-liberties questions.
- **Policy context**: The 1995 campaign is frequently cited as a turning point in modern French counterterrorism posture and public awareness of jihadist-linked violence.
