## Overview

- **Brian Chesky** is an American entrepreneur best known as a **co-founder and CEO of Airbnb**.
- His background is in **design**, and he is frequently cited as an example of a design-led founder in Silicon Valley.

## Early Life and Education

- Born in **1981** in the United States.
- Studied **industrial design** at the **Rhode Island School of Design (RISD)**.

## Airbnb (Company-Building)

- **2008**: Co-founded Airbnb (originally associated with the early "AirBed & Breakfast" concept) with **Joe Gebbia** and **Nathan Blecharczyk**.
- **2009**: Airbnb participated in **Y Combinator**, which helped the company accelerate early growth.
- Airbnb developed into a marketplace connecting **hosts** and **guests** for short-term (and later longer-term) stays, operating across many countries and major cities.

## Notable Milestones

- **2014**: Airbnb introduced the "**Blo**" symbol as part of a major brand refresh.
- **2016**: Airbnb expanded beyond stays with products like **Airbnb Experiences** (activities hosted by locals).
- **2020**: Airbnb was heavily impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic and publicly communicated cost cuts and layoffs as it restructured.
- **2020-12**: **Airbnb went public** via an IPO on **NASDAQ (ticker: ABNB)**.

## Leadership and Themes

- Chesky has been associated with:
  - **Product and brand craft** (design-driven product decisions).
  - Building trust mechanisms for a two-sided marketplace (reviews, identity/verification, host-guest policies).
  - Navigating **regulatory** and **community** constraints (housing concerns, local rules, safety policies).
