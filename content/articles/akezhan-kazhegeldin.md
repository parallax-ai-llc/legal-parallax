## Summary

Former Kazakh PM charged with corruption and tax evasion who fled abroad and was convicted in absentia.

## Background

- Born in Georgiyevka (now Qalbatau) in the Kazakh SSR (Soviet Union).
- Educated at the Semipalatinsk Pedagogical Institute (graduated 1974) and later studied economics/management (graduated 1985).
- Served in the Soviet armed forces (1974–1978).

## Government Roles

Kazhegeldin rose rapidly in Kazakhstan’s post-Soviet executive branch and is best known for leading the government during a key period of early economic transition.

- **First Deputy Prime Minister** (Dec 1993 – Oct 1994)
- **Prime Minister of Kazakhstan** (Oct 1994 – Oct 1997) under President Nursultan Nazarbayev

## Political Activity and Exile

After leaving the premiership, he became identified with opposition politics and public criticism of the Nazarbayev administration, including claims of authoritarianism, nepotism, and human-rights abuses.

- Led/chaired the **Republican People’s Party** (late 1990s–early 2000s).
- Lived outside Kazakhstan for extended periods and is commonly described as being in **exile**.

## Allegations and Legal Status

Public reporting describes a series of legal cases and restrictions affecting his return and political participation:

- Accusations have included **corruption** and **tax evasion**.
- He was **convicted in absentia** (reported in the early 2000s).
- He has been reported as **barred from running for president** due to prior administrative offenses related to an unsanctioned demonstration.
