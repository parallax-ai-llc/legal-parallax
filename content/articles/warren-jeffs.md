## Summary
Warren Steed Jeffs is the president of the Fundamentalist Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints (FLDS). He was convicted of child sexual assault for marrying underage girls. Sentenced to life plus 20 years.

## Background
- Raised in the Fundamentalist Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints (FLDS), a polygamist sect that broke from the mainstream LDS Church.
- Son of Rulon Jeffs, who led the FLDS before Warren Jeffs.

## Rise to FLDS Leadership
- In the late 1990s, Jeffs became a senior figure within the FLDS hierarchy and was widely viewed as an enforcer of internal discipline.
- After Rulon Jeffs’ death (2002), Warren Jeffs consolidated control and was treated by followers as the church’s “prophet.”
- Under his leadership, the FLDS maintained closed communities including areas around the Arizona–Utah border (Colorado City, AZ / Hildale, UT) and the Texas compound near Eldorado (Yearning for Zion Ranch).

## Criminal Conduct and Convictions (High-Level)
- Jeffs is known primarily for sexual abuse of minors within a coercive, authoritarian religious structure.
- He arranged marriages involving underage girls and performed marriage ceremonies that became central evidence in later prosecutions.
- He was placed on law-enforcement most-wanted lists before his arrest.

## Incarceration
- Jeffs is serving a life sentence plus additional years, and remains imprisoned as of the last update.

## Why He Matters (Historical/Institutional Impact)
- Jeffs’ case became a high-profile example of criminal prosecution intersecting with closed religious communities.
- It accelerated legal, social-service, and media scrutiny of the FLDS and similar groups, especially regarding child protection, forced marriage, and coercive control.
- The prosecutions also contributed to leadership fragmentation and ongoing disputes over FLDS governance and assets.
