## Summary

US Army Sgt. Robert Bales murdered 16 Afghan civilians including 9 children in a nighttime rampage through two villages. He was sentenced to life in prison without parole.

## What happened

- Date: 11 March 2012 (local time).
- Perpetrator: U.S. Army Staff Sergeant Robert Bales.
- Bales left a U.S. military outpost in Kandahar Province, entered nearby villages at night, and killed civilians at close range.
- Reports described shootings, stabbings, and in some cases bodies being set on fire.

## Victims and locations

- The killings occurred in Panjwai District, Kandahar Province.
- The attacks hit two villages (commonly reported as Alkozai and Najiban).
- Total killed: 16 Afghan civilians.
- Children killed: 9.
- The event is frequently cited as one of the deadliest single-person attacks on Afghan civilians during the war.

## Investigation and accountability

- U.S. authorities took Bales into custody soon after the attacks.
- The case proceeded under the U.S. military justice system (court-martial), not Afghan courts.
- In 2013, Bales pleaded guilty to multiple counts including murder.
- Sentence: life imprisonment without the possibility of parole.

## Political and social impact

- The massacre triggered widespread outrage in Afghanistan and intensified criticism of the foreign military presence.
- U.S. officials issued apologies and condemned the killings; Afghan leaders demanded accountability.
- The incident became a recurring reference point in debates about civilian protection, rules of engagement, and oversight during counterinsurgency operations.

## Legacy

- The Kandahar massacre is remembered primarily as a civilian mass killing and a major trust rupture between local communities and coalition forces.
- It is often discussed alongside other wartime civilian-casualty incidents as evidence of the human costs of prolonged conflict.
