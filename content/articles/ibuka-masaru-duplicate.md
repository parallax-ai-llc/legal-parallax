## About Masaru Ibuka (canonical subject)

Masaru Ibuka (1908–1997) was a Japanese engineer and entrepreneur best known as a co-founder of Sony.
He is commonly credited with helping shape Sony's early product strategy and engineering culture in the postwar period.

### High-level significance

- Co-founded the company that became **Sony Group** (originally Tokyo Tsushin Kogyo / Tokyo Telecommunications Engineering Corporation).
- Helped commercialize consumer electronics in Japan during the post–World War II rebuilding era.
- Advocated for practical engineering, miniaturization, and product quality as competitive advantages.
