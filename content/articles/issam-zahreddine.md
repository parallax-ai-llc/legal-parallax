## Summary

Syrian general who defended Deir ez-Zor during the ISIS siege, killed by a landmine.

## Background

- Born in the Suwayda Governorate region (often reported as part of Syria’s Druze-majority areas).[^1]
- Rose through the Syrian Arab Army and became associated with elite formations tied to the Republican Guard.[^1]

## Military Career (High Level)

- Served in the **Republican Guard**, a force considered among Syria’s most capable and politically important units.[^1]
- Commonly linked to the **104th Brigade** of the Republican Guard during the Syrian civil war period.[^1]
- Participated in multiple fronts during the conflict; international coverage frequently referenced his role in key government offensives.[^2]

## Deir ez-Zor and the ISIS Siege

Deir ez-Zor city and its airbase became a major government-held enclave in eastern Syria, surrounded for years by ISIS forces. Zahreddine is widely credited in pro-government narratives with helping maintain the defense of remaining positions and coordinating efforts to hold supply routes and defensive lines.[^1]

Key points often highlighted in reporting and summaries:

- The siege constrained food, fuel, and medical supplies, increasing reliance on airlifts and limited corridors.[^1]
- Government operations in 2017 aimed to break the encirclement and reconnect the enclave with other held territory.[^1][^2]

## Public Statements and Controversy

Zahreddine attracted attention not only for battlefield leadership but also for comments reported in regional media. Some of his statements about Syrian refugees (and whether they should return) were criticized and became part of his public reputation outside Syria.[^1]

## Death

- Died on **18 October 2017** near Deir ez-Zor after an incident widely reported as a **landmine** or explosive device while operations were ongoing.[^1][^2]
- His death was reported by Syrian state-linked outlets and widely reprinted by international news organizations.[^2]
