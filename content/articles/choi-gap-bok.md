## Additional Context

### What the story is known for

- Choi Gap-bok is widely described in English-language summaries as a South Korean prisoner who escaped confinement by squeezing through a small meal/food slot.
- The escape is commonly framed as an unusual combination of **physical flexibility** and **institutional oversight**, which is why the incident circulated internationally.

### Reported method (high-level)

- The core claim repeated across retellings is that he reduced his body profile enough to pass through an opening intended only for passing meals.
- Many brief accounts attribute this to “yoga” or flexibility training; the precise training history is not consistently documented in the available short sources.

### Why it mattered

- **Operational lesson:** even small access ports (food slots, service hatches) can become egress points if assumptions about human movement are wrong.
- **Media effect:** the simplicity of the narrative (“escaped through the food slot”) made it memorable and easy to headline.
