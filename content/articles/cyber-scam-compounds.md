## Summary

Criminal organizations in Myanmar, Cambodia, and Laos operate massive cyber scam compounds staffed by trafficked workers. Victims are forced to run romance and investment scams under threat of violence.

## Scale and Structure

- **Compound model**: Large, guarded campuses (often in special economic zones or border towns) where scam operations and worker housing are colocated.
- **Workforce**: Many workers are **trafficked** or coerced, while some are voluntary recruits; conditions reported include confiscated passports, restricted movement, and debt bondage.
- **Revenue**: Compounds typically run multiple scam “desks” and rotate scripts to match market conditions and enforcement pressure.

## Common Scam Types

- **Romance scams** (“pig butchering” style grooming leading to investment fraud).
- **Fake investment platforms** (crypto/FX/commodities dashboards controlled by scammers).
- **Impersonation scams** (customer support, government, bank, or parcel-delivery pretexts).
- **Gambling and app-based fraud** (rigged platforms, chargeback/verification traps).

## Trafficking and Coercion Patterns

- **Recruitment**: Job ads promising call-center/marketing roles in Southeast Asia; victims are transported across borders and then held at compounds.
- **Control**: Threats, violence, surveillance, and financial penalties for missed quotas have been widely reported by survivors and journalists.
- **Language targeting**: Teams are often organized by language/region to target victims in East Asia, Southeast Asia, and increasingly the West.

## Responses and Ongoing Reporting

- **Law enforcement actions**: Periodic raids, diplomatic negotiations, and repatriations occur, but enforcement is uneven and complicated by corruption and jurisdictional limits.
- **International attention**: The issue is covered by governments, NGOs, and investigative outlets as a hybrid of **organized crime** and **human trafficking**.

## Practical Indicators (High-Level)

- Sudden relocation “job offers” requiring immediate travel and passport surrender.
- Online relationships that quickly pivot to “guaranteed” returns on unfamiliar platforms.
- Requests to install remote-access software or move communications to encrypted apps early.
