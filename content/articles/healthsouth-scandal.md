## Summary

HealthSouth CEO Richard Scrushy directed a $2.7 billion accounting fraud, inflating earnings to meet Wall Street expectations. First CEO charged under Sarbanes-Oxley; acquitted.

## Context

- **Company**: HealthSouth Corporation, a large U.S. provider of outpatient surgery and rehabilitation services.
- **Core allegation**: reported earnings were systematically inflated over multiple reporting periods to meet market expectations.
- **Scale (often cited)**: approximately **$2.7B** of overstated earnings reported when the fraud was uncovered.

## What Actually Happened (High-Level)

- Executives allegedly used **unsupported accounting entries** to increase reported income.
- Internal targets were set for earnings-per-share; when real results fell short, accounting adjustments were made to close the gap.
- The scheme relied on **control over finance functions** and insufficient internal controls, enabling repeated misstatement.

## Key Actors (Commonly Mentioned)

- **Richard Scrushy**: founder and CEO during the period; accused of directing the fraud.
- **Finance and accounting staff**: multiple insiders later cooperated with investigators and provided testimony.

## Legal / Regulatory Outcome (Summary)

- The scandal became one of the prominent early post-Enron corporate fraud cases.
- Scrushy was **charged** under the then-new Sarbanes–Oxley framework and was **acquitted** in the main criminal accounting-fraud case.
- The investigation led to additional prosecutions and settlements involving other executives and participants.

## Aftermath and Impact

- HealthSouth underwent leadership changes, governance reforms, and reputational damage.
- The case is frequently referenced in discussions of:
  - **internal controls** and financial reporting integrity,
  - **executive accountability** and board oversight,
  - the practical limits of relying on **earnings guidance** as a managerial scoreboard.
