## Key Facts

- **Full name**: Hisham Barakat (هشام بركات)
- **Position**: Prosecutor General of Egypt (served from 2013 until his death in 2015)
- **Known for**: High-profile prosecutions during a period of intense political polarization and security crackdowns.

## Background

- Barakat was an Egyptian judge and legal official who rose through the country’s judicial and prosecution system.
- In the wake of Egypt’s 2013 political upheaval, he became one of the most prominent legal figures associated with state efforts to pursue politically sensitive cases.

## Role as Prosecutor General (2013–2015)

- As Prosecutor General, he oversaw prosecutorial policy and major criminal investigations handled by the Public Prosecution.
- His tenure coincided with large-scale cases related to political violence, protests, and terrorism-related allegations.
- The office became a focal point of both domestic controversy and international attention regarding due process and human-rights concerns.

## Assassination (2015)

- On **29 June 2015**, Barakat was killed in **Cairo** when a **car bomb** struck his motorcade.
- The attack was widely treated as a major escalation against senior state officials and prompted intensified security operations.
- Egyptian authorities attributed responsibility to militant networks; the case remained politically and legally significant for years afterward.

## Aftermath and Impact

- His death led to renewed emphasis on counterterrorism measures and accelerated prosecutorial and security responses.
- The assassination was frequently cited in official narratives about combating insurgency and political violence.
