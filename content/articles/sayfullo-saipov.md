## Summary
Sayfullo Saipov drove a rented truck into cyclists and pedestrians on a Manhattan bike path on October 31, 2017, killing eight. He displayed ISIS flags. The jury rejected the death penalty; sentenced to life.

## Background
- **Origin:** Saipov was born in Uzbekistan and later moved to the United States.
- **Residency:** Lived and worked in multiple U.S. states prior to the 2017 attack.
- **Radicalization (reported):** Prosecutors and investigators described exposure to ISIS propaganda and online extremist material as part of the motive narrative.

## 2017 New York City Truck Attack (high level)
- **Date:** 2017-10-31 (Halloween).
- **Location:** Manhattan, New York City — Hudson River Greenway bike path and surrounding streets.
- **Method:** Rented pickup truck used as a vehicle-ramming weapon against cyclists and pedestrians.
- **Fatalities:** 8 people killed; others injured.
- **Claimed affiliation:** ISIS symbolism was reported (e.g., flags) and the attack was treated as ISIS-inspired.

## Investigation and Charges
- **Jurisdiction:** Federal (Southern District of New York).
- **Core allegations:** Terrorism-related offenses and murder counts tied to the vehicle attack.
- **Evidence types (typical in reporting):** Witness statements, physical evidence from the scene, digital communications, and statements attributed to the defendant.

## Trial and Sentencing
- **Outcome:** Convicted by a federal jury.
- **Death penalty decision:** The jury did not unanimously impose capital punishment.
- **Sentence:** Life imprisonment without parole (reported in mainstream coverage).

## Impact and Context
- The attack is frequently cited in analyses of **vehicle-ramming** as a low-complexity tactic encouraged by ISIS propaganda during the mid-to-late 2010s.
- It contributed to renewed public discussion in New York City and the U.S. about **hardening pedestrian spaces**, **bike-path barriers**, and rapid incident response.
