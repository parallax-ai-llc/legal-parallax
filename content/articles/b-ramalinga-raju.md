## Background

- B. Ramalinga Raju is best known as the founder and former chairman of Satyam Computer Services, an India-based IT services company.
- The Satyam case is often described as one of the most significant accounting and corporate-governance scandals in India’s modern business history.

## Satyam accounting fraud (high-level)

- In early 2009, Raju admitted to large-scale falsification of Satyam’s accounts, including overstated cash and bank balances and manipulated revenue/profit figures.
- The misconduct involved creating a sustained picture of financial health that did not match underlying business reality.
- The scale of misstatement damaged investor confidence in Satyam and prompted a rapid response from regulators, lenders, and customers.

## Legal proceedings and outcomes

- Indian authorities pursued criminal charges related to falsification, conspiracy, and associated corporate offenses.
- Raju and other defendants were convicted in a trial court in 2015 in connection with the fraud.
- Subsequent appeals and related proceedings have continued to shape how the case is discussed in legal and governance contexts.

## Immediate consequences for Satyam

- The scandal triggered an emergency effort to stabilize the company’s operations and preserve customer delivery.
- Satyam’s board was reconstituted and the firm underwent a government-facilitated sale process.
- The company was acquired by Tech Mahindra and later integrated under the Mahindra Group’s IT-services branding.

## Impact and legacy

- The case became a reference point for:
  - tighter audit expectations,
  - improved internal controls,
  - board oversight and independent-director responsibilities,
  - investor due diligence in emerging-market public companies.
- It also influenced public discussion about the role of auditors, the reliability of reported cash balances, and the incentives that can sustain long-running misstatements.
