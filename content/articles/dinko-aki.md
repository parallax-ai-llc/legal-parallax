## Background

- Born in 1921 in present-day Croatia (then part of the Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes/Yugoslavia).[^1]
- Became associated with the Ustaše movement during World War II and served within structures of the Independent State of Croatia (NDH).[^1]

## Role in the Jasenovac camp system

- Served as a commander at the Jasenovac concentration camp complex during World War II.[^1]
- Jasenovac was operated by the Ustaše regime; historical accounts describe it as a site of mass detention and killings of Serbs, Jews, Roma, and political opponents.[^1]

## Post-war flight and later life

- After World War II, he left Europe and lived for decades abroad, including in Argentina.[^1]
- Public attention to his whereabouts increased in the 1990s, leading to renewed legal action.[^1]

## Trial and conviction

- Returned/extradited to Croatia in the late 1990s and was tried for crimes committed at Jasenovac.[^1]
- In 1999, he was convicted by a Croatian court and sentenced for war crimes related to his camp command role.[^1]
