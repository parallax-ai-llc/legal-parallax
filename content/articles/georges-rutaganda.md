## Background

- Georges Rutaganda (born 1958) was a Rwandan businessman associated with the Interahamwe, a Hutu extremist militia linked to mass violence during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi in Rwanda.[^1]
- He was often described in reporting and later tribunal proceedings as a prominent figure within Interahamwe structures in Kigali during the period leading up to and during the genocide.[^1][^2]

## Role During the 1994 Genocide (High-Level)

- Tribunal findings and historical accounts describe Interahamwe roadblocks and targeted attacks as key mechanisms of violence; Rutaganda was convicted for participation in and leadership related to such crimes.[^1][^2]
- Accounts summarized by the ICTR include allegations that he was present at, and contributed to, attacks and killings at specific sites in Kigali in 1994.[^1][^2]

## ICTR Prosecution and Conviction

- Rutaganda was prosecuted by the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR) and convicted of genocide and crimes against humanity (including extermination and murder).[^1][^2]
- His conviction was issued in 1999, and later appellate proceedings altered parts of the judgment while leaving core responsibility for serious crimes affirmed in ICTR records.[^1][^2]

## Imprisonment and Death

- After conviction, he served his sentence in international custody; he died in 2010.[^1]
