## Summary
The murder of Meredith Kercher in Perugia, Italy in 2007 led to the prosecution and eventual acquittal of American Amanda Knox and Italian Raffaele Sollecito. Rudy Guede was convicted. The case became an international media sensation.

## Background
- Meredith Kercher was a 21-year-old British student spending a year abroad in Perugia, Italy.
- She shared a cottage with several roommates, including American student Amanda Knox.

## Key Events (2007–2015)
- **1 Nov 2007**: Kercher was found murdered in the shared home in Perugia.
- **Nov 2007**: Knox and Knox’s then-boyfriend Raffaele Sollecito were arrested; Patrick Lumumba was also detained briefly after Knox’s accusation.
- **2008**: Rudy Guede was tried separately via a fast-track procedure and convicted.
- **2009**: Knox and Sollecito were convicted at first instance.
- **2011**: An appellate court overturned the convictions and acquitted Knox and Sollecito.
- **2013**: Italy’s Supreme Court of Cassation annulled the 2011 acquittal and ordered a new appeal.
- **2014**: A retrial appeal court again convicted Knox and Sollecito.
- **2015**: The Supreme Court of Cassation definitively acquitted Knox and Sollecito, ending the Italian proceedings against them for the murder.

## Legal Outcomes (High Level)
- **Rudy Guede**: Convicted for Kercher’s murder in a separate case (sentence later reduced on appeal).
- **Amanda Knox & Raffaele Sollecito**: Ultimately **acquitted** with the final 2015 ruling by the Court of Cassation.
- **Calunnia/defamation issue**: Knox was found guilty of falsely accusing Lumumba (a separate offense from the murder charge) in Italian proceedings.

## Why the Case Drew Global Attention
- Intense international media coverage and competing narratives about evidence, interrogation practices, and forensic reliability.
- Complex multi-stage Italian process (trial → appeal → Cassation review → retrial → final Cassation decision).
