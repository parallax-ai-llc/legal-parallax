## Summary
Nikolas Cruz killed 17 people at Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School in Parkland, Florida on February 14, 2018. The shooting sparked the March for Our Lives gun control movement. He was sentenced to life.

## Context (High-Level)
- **Location**: Parkland, Florida (Broward County), United States.
- **Setting**: Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School.
- **Date**: February 14, 2018.
- **Fatalities**: 17 people killed; additional victims were injured.[^2]

## Incident (High-Level)
- The attack was carried out on school grounds and became one of the deadliest school shootings in U.S. history.[^2]
- The event triggered extensive national debate on gun policy, school security, and mental-health intervention.

## Legal Proceedings & Sentence
- Cruz pleaded guilty (2021) to multiple counts of murder and attempted murder.[^2]
- In 2022, a jury recommended life imprisonment without the possibility of parole; the court imposed a life sentence.[^2][^3]

## Public & Political Impact
- The shooting catalyzed **March for Our Lives**, a youth-led advocacy movement focused on gun violence prevention.[^2]
- The event influenced policy discussions at local, state, and federal levels, and prompted renewed attention to threat reporting and school safety measures.
