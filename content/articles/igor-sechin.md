## Background

- Igor Ivanovich Sechin (Игорь Иванович Сечин) is a Russian business executive and long-time political insider.
- He is widely described as a close ally of Vladimir Putin and a prominent figure in Russia’s state-capitalist system.
- His influence is commonly linked to the expansion of state control over strategic energy assets during the 2000s–2010s.

## Government & Political Roles (high level)

- Worked in the St. Petersburg city administration in the early 1990s, a network that later supplied many senior federal officials.
- Held senior roles in the Russian presidential administration during the 2000s.
- Served as Deputy Prime Minister of Russia (2008–2012), with responsibilities often connected to the fuel-and-energy sector.

## Role at Rosneft

- Rosneft is Russia’s largest oil company and a major state-controlled energy producer.
- As CEO, Sechin has been associated with Rosneft’s acquisition-led growth and consolidation of upstream assets.
- His tenure is frequently discussed in the context of Rosneft’s increased strategic importance to the Russian state.

## Notable Controversies & Public Criticism

- Sechin is frequently accused by critics and investigative outlets of benefiting from opaque state-company governance and political patronage.
- Allegations commonly focus on corruption, conflicts of interest, and the blurring of roles between public authority and state-owned enterprise leadership.

## Sanctions (overview)

- Sechin has been sanctioned by several Western governments and blocs in connection with Russia’s actions in Ukraine.
- Sanctions have typically targeted travel, asset access, and restrictions on dealings by listed entities/individuals.
- Rosneft and related energy-sector entities have also faced sectoral restrictions impacting financing and technology access.
