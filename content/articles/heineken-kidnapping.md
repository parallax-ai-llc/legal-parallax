## Summary

Kidnapping of beer magnate Freddy Heineken that captivated the Netherlands.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1983 | Freddy Heineken kidnapped for ransom |

## Context and Background

- **Target**: Freddy Heineken, then head of the Heineken brewing company, one of the Netherlands' most prominent business figures.
- **Location**: The kidnapping took place in Amsterdam and quickly became a national obsession due to the scale of the ransom and the high profile of the victim.
- **Ransom demand**: The kidnappers demanded a record ransom (reported as **35 million Dutch guilders**), making it one of the most notorious European kidnappings of its era.

## The Kidnapping (1983)

- **Date**: 9 November 1983.
- **Victims**: Freddy Heineken and his driver/chauffeur, Ab Doderer.
- **Captivity**: The victims were held for weeks in a specially prepared location, restrained and guarded while negotiations unfolded.
- **Release**: The victims were released alive on 30 November 1983.

## Investigation and Aftermath

- Dutch authorities pursued an intensive investigation after the release.
- Key perpetrators were identified and later arrested; some fled the Netherlands and were captured abroad.
- A significant portion of the ransom money was eventually recovered, though the case continued to influence Dutch debates about organized crime and policing.

## Notable Individuals (Perpetrators)

The kidnapping is widely associated with a group including:

- Willem Holleeder
- Cor van Hout
- Frans Meijer
- Jan Boellaard
- Martin Erkamps

## Cultural Impact

- The case became a landmark true-crime story in the Netherlands.
- It inspired multiple retellings in books and screen adaptations, helping cement it as a reference point for discussions of ransom kidnapping and organized crime.
