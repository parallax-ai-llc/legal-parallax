Norwegian master criminal who orchestrated the NOKAS robbery, Norway's largest cash heist.

## Background

- David Toska is a Norwegian criminal known primarily for his role in the 2004 NOKAS robbery.
- Public reporting describes him as one of the central organizers behind the operation that targeted a cash handling center in Stavanger.

## The NOKAS robbery (2004)

- The robbery took place on 5 April 2004 and is widely described as the largest cash robbery in Norway.
- The target was a NOKAS (Norsk Kontantservice) cash depot/cash handling facility.
- The theft is commonly reported at roughly 57 million Norwegian kroner (often cited as ~57.4 million NOK).
- The event is also remembered for the fatal shooting of a Norwegian police officer (Arne Sigve Klungland) during the response.

## Investigation, trial, and sentencing

- Following the robbery, Norwegian authorities conducted a major investigation into an organized group of participants.
- Toska was prosecuted as a leading figure in planning and organizing the operation.
- He was convicted and received a lengthy prison sentence (commonly reported as 19 years).

## Why the case is notable

- Scale: the amount stolen made it a benchmark case in modern Norwegian criminal history.
- Operational complexity: the robbery involved multiple participants, weapons, vehicles, and coordinated logistics.
- Legal and public impact: the killing of an officer and the scale of the theft influenced public debate around armed robbery, policing, and organized crime.
