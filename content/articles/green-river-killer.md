## Notes on Naming

- **"Green River Killer"** is the media and law-enforcement nickname for the serial killer later identified as **Gary Leon Ridgway**.
- This page is kept as an alias entry for search/navigation consistency; the primary biography is typically filed under **Gary Ridgway**.

## Overview

- **Active period:** Primarily the early to mid-1980s, with murders attributed across multiple years.
- **Victim profile (high-level):** Many victims were young women and girls, including sex workers and runaways.
- **Geography:** Most killings were associated with the Seattle–Tacoma area of Washington State; bodies were often left in wooded or river-adjacent locations.

## Investigation & Identification (High Level)

- The case became one of the largest serial-murder investigations in U.S. history due to the number of victims and the long time to identify the offender.
- Gary Ridgway was a suspect during the investigation but was not arrested until advances in forensic evidence and case development supported charges.

## Legal Outcome

- Ridgway ultimately **pleaded guilty** in Washington State to multiple counts of aggravated first-degree murder.
- The plea agreement avoided a death-penalty trial in exchange for cooperation and admissions about additional victims.
