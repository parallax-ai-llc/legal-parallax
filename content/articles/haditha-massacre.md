## Summary

US Marines killed 24 unarmed Iraqi civilians in Haditha including children and elderly after an IED killed one Marine. Only one Marine was convicted; he served no jail time.

## Context and Background

- **Location**: Haditha, Al Anbar Governorate, Iraq.
- **War context**: The killings occurred during the Iraq War amid intense insurgency and frequent roadside bomb (IED) attacks in western Iraq.
- **Immediate trigger**: An IED struck a U.S. Marine convoy, killing Lance Corporal Miguel Terrazas and injuring others, shortly before the civilians were killed.

## What Happened (High-Level)

Accounts summarized in public reporting describe Marines conducting house-to-house entries following the IED blast and firing on civilians in multiple locations, including private homes and a taxi.

- **Victims**: 24 Iraqi civilians, including children and elderly people.
- **Nature of victims**: Public sources characterize the victims as unarmed and not participating in hostilities.

## Investigations and Legal Outcomes

- **Initial reporting**: The incident drew wider attention after investigative reporting and subsequent official inquiries.
- **Military investigations**: The U.S. military opened investigations, and several Marines were charged.
- **Case outcomes**: Charges against multiple defendants were dismissed, dropped, or resulted in acquittals.
- **Conviction**: One Marine was convicted (in connection with misconduct related to the case) and received a sentence that did not include jail time.

## Significance

- **Public impact**: The Haditha killings became one of the most cited Iraq War incidents in discussions of civilian harm, rules of engagement, and accountability.
- **Policy relevance**: The case is frequently referenced in debates about command responsibility, battlefield decision-making under stress, and the adequacy of military justice processes for alleged war crimes.

## Key Facts (Quick Reference)

- **Date**: 19 November 2005
- **Perpetrators (as alleged/charged)**: Members of a U.S. Marine unit operating in Haditha.
- **Outcome summary**: Broad prosecutions did not lead to imprisonment for the central killings; the final legal record is often cited as a point of controversy.
