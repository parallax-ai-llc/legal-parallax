## Background

Cornelius Steynberg is a South African national who became widely known in connection with the collapse of Mirror Trading International (MTI), a Bitcoin-focused investment scheme.

## Mirror Trading International (MTI)

- **Role:** Identified in public reporting as the founder/CEO of MTI.[^1]
- **What MTI claimed:** Automated/algorithmic Bitcoin trading and consistent returns for members (as described in public sources).[^1]
- **Outcome:** MTI collapsed and was widely characterized by regulators and media as a Ponzi scheme.[^1]

## Allegations and Legal Actions

- **Regulatory action (US):** The U.S. Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) filed a civil enforcement action alleging fraud and misappropriation connected to MTI.[^2]
- **Scale (reported):** Public sources and regulators have described MTI as involving a very large quantity of bitcoin and losses to participants; figures are typically reported in the billions of USD equivalent.[^1][^2]
- **Status:** Details vary by jurisdiction and over time; primary public summaries are captured in Wikipedia and regulator releases.[^1][^2]

- MTI is often cited as a high-profile case study of crypto-era affinity fraud and “too-good-to-be-true” yield claims.
