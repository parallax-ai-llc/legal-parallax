## Summary

Nigeria's most feared armed robber known as 'The Law' who terrorized Benin City with police protection.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1987 | Executed by firing squad |

## Background and Notability

- Anini Lawrence became notorious in the mid-1980s around Benin City, Edo State, during a period of heightened urban crime and public distrust of policing.
- He was widely reported as operating with **inside assistance** from corrupt officials, which amplified fear and a sense of impunity.
- The nickname **"The Law"** is commonly explained as a taunt: a claim that he could evade or bend law enforcement.

## Modus Operandi (as commonly reported)

- Armed robbery operations were described as coordinated and bold, with attacks that targeted individuals and businesses believed to hold cash.
- Contemporary reporting and later summaries frequently emphasize:
  - use of firearms and intimidation
  - knowledge of police movements and checkpoints
  - a network of collaborators (drivers, informants, and alleged protectors)

## Capture, Trial, and Execution

- After an extended manhunt, he was captured and prosecuted by Nigerian authorities.
- He was sentenced to death and **executed by firing squad** in 1987, a punishment that was publicly framed as a deterrent.
- Accounts often note that his case became symbolic of the Nigerian state's attempt to reassert control over violent crime and corruption.

## Legacy

- In Nigerian popular memory, Anini's story is frequently cited in conversations about:
  - the impact of police corruption on public safety
  - the social conditions that enable organized violent crime
  - the state's use of high-profile punishment to restore confidence
- His name has been referenced in media and public discourse as a shorthand for a violent criminal with alleged official protection.
