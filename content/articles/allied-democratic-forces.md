## Summary

Ugandan rebel group allied with ISIS responsible for bombings and massacres in Congo and Uganda.

## Background and Origins

- The Allied Democratic Forces (ADF) emerged in the mid-to-late 1990s, with roots in Ugandan opposition networks and Islamist-leaning factions.
- Over time, the group relocated much of its activity to the border region between Uganda and the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC), operating primarily from eastern DRC.

## Areas of Operation

- **Primary base areas (commonly reported):** North Kivu and Ituri provinces in eastern DRC.
- **Cross-border impact:** attacks and raids have periodically affected communities in both eastern DRC and western Uganda.

## Leadership and Affiliations (High-Level)

- The group has had multiple leadership phases since its formation; reporting often links early leadership to **Jamil Mukulu** and later operational leadership to **Musa Baluku**.
- In the 2010s–2020s, the ADF was widely reported to have ties to the Islamic State’s regional networks, and some attacks in the region were claimed under the Islamic State’s Central Africa messaging.

## Tactics and Targeting Patterns

- The ADF has been associated with **mass-casualty violence**, including attacks on civilians in rural communities, assaults on security forces, and bombings in urban settings.
- Operations are frequently described as involving raids, ambushes, and the use of improvised explosive devices (IEDs).

## International Response

- The ADF has been described by multiple governments and international bodies as a terrorist or extremist organization.
- Regional military operations have periodically targeted ADF positions in eastern DRC, often in coordination between Ugandan and Congolese forces.
