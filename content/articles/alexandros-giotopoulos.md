## Background and Context

- **November 17 (17N)** was a Greek far-left, anti-imperialist urban guerrilla organization active from the **mid-1970s to 2002**.
- The group became known for **assassinations, bombings, and robberies**, often framing attacks as retaliation against foreign influence and domestic elites.
- Giotopoulos was widely described by prosecutors and Greek courts as the organization’s **strategic leader** and ideological guide, though he **denied** leadership.

## Notable Attacks Associated With 17N (High-Level)

The organization’s operations included high-profile assassinations and attacks, such as:

- **1975**: Assassination of **Richard Welch**, a senior U.S. official in Athens.
- **2000**: Assassination of **Stephen Saunders**, the British defense attaché in Greece.
- Attacks targeting **Greek political figures**, **security officials**, and **business interests**, as well as **U.S. and allied personnel**.

## Arrest, Trial, and Sentencing

- **2002**: A police investigation intensified after the organization suffered operational setbacks and arrests of suspected members.
- **2003**: Giotopoulos was **convicted** in Greece for involvement with 17N, including leadership-related charges.
- He received **multiple life sentences** under Greek anti-terrorism prosecutions connected to the group’s long-running campaign.

## Significance

- The dismantling of 17N in the early 2000s marked the end of one of Europe’s most enduring post-war militant organizations.
- The case became a major reference point in Greece for debates over **domestic terrorism**, **state capacity**, and the **politics of the post-junta era**.
