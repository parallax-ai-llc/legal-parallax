## High-Level Context

- Rodriguez Gacha (often nicknamed **"El Mexicano"**) was a prominent Colombian trafficker associated with the Medellín Cartel during the peak years of the cocaine trade.
- He is widely described as combining **drug trafficking wealth** with **armed enforcement**, including links (in public reporting) to paramilitary-style activity.
- His rise and death are frequently cited in discussions of Colombia’s late-1980s escalation between cartels and the state.

## Key Points (Factual, High-Level)

- **Role**: senior cartel-aligned operator; reported to have managed trafficking logistics and violent enforcement.
- **Geography**: influence concentrated in parts of central Colombia; operations connected to broader Medellín Cartel networks.
- **End of life**: killed in a major police operation on **1989-12-15**, a notable event in the state’s campaign against cartel leadership.
