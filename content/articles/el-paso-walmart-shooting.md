## Background and Motive (Reported)

- The attack was widely described by authorities and media as **racially motivated**, with a focus on targeting **Hispanic/Latino** people.
- A document described as a **manifesto** was posted online shortly before the shooting and referenced a so-called "Hispanic invasion"; investigators linked it to the suspect in subsequent reporting.
- The event occurred amid broader U.S. debates about immigration, domestic violent extremism, and online radicalization.

## What Happened

- The shooting took place at a **Walmart** in **El Paso, Texas**, a city near the U.S.–Mexico border.
- Reports described the suspect traveling a long distance to reach El Paso before carrying out the attack.
- The incident resulted in **23 deaths** and numerous injuries, making it one of the deadliest mass shootings in modern U.S. history.

## Immediate Response and Aftermath

- Local, state, and federal law enforcement responded; the case was investigated as a potential **hate crime** and act of **domestic terrorism** in public discourse.
- Community vigils and memorials were held, with particular focus on solidarity with El Paso’s Latino community.
- The shooting prompted renewed attention to security at large retail venues and emergency response planning.
