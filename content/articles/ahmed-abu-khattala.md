## Summary

Libyan militia leader convicted for his role in the 2012 Benghazi attack that killed the US ambassador.

## Background

- Ahmed Abu Khattala is commonly described in reporting as a Libyan militia commander active in Benghazi.
- Open-source profiles describe him as having been involved in the 2011 Libyan civil war and later operating in the post‑revolution militia environment in eastern Libya.[^1]

## Role in the 2012 Benghazi Attack (High-Level)

- The 11 September 2012 attack targeted U.S. diplomatic facilities in Benghazi, Libya, and resulted in the deaths of Ambassador J. Christopher Stevens and three other Americans.
- Abu Khattala was alleged by witnesses and U.S. investigators to have played a significant role in the events of that night; he publicly denied key allegations in contemporaneous media coverage.[^1]

## Arrest, Trial, and Sentencing

- **Capture (2014):** U.S. forces captured Abu Khattala in Libya and transferred him for prosecution in the United States.[^1]
- **Federal trial (2017):** He was acquitted of many of the most serious counts but convicted on a smaller set of terrorism‑related offenses tied to material support and weapons/property crimes.[^1]
- **Sentence:** Open sources report a multi‑decade prison sentence, with later resentencing reported years afterward.[^1]
