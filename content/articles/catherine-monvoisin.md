## Context: the Affair of the Poisons

- The **Affair of the Poisons** (Affaire des Poisons) was a major scandal in late-17th-century France involving allegations of **poisoning, clandestine fortune-telling, and illicit magical rites**, drawing attention because of links to people around the royal court.
- Monvoisin (often called **"La Voisin"**) is frequently described in modern summaries as a central figure in the affair due to her role as a **provider of occult services** and her alleged involvement in a **poison-selling network**.

## Activities and services (high-level)

Accounts typically attribute a mix of services to Monvoisin's practice:

- **Fortune-telling** and divination services.
- **Love charms** and "help" with relationship disputes.
- Alleged provision of **poisons** (sometimes framed in sources as "powders" or "inheritance powders").
- Facilitation of introductions within a broader **client and supplier network**.

## Investigation, arrest, and execution

- The affair led to investigations by royal authorities and the use of special judicial mechanisms (commonly referenced as the **Chambre Ardente**, the "Burning Chamber").
- Monvoisin was **arrested**, tried, and ultimately **executed in 1680**.
- Many details about accusations (especially sensational ones) are filtered through later retellings and should be treated with care unless supported by primary documentation.

## Why she remains historically notable

- Her case is often cited as an example of how **popular magic**, informal medicine, and criminal entrepreneurship could intersect in early modern European cities.
- The affair illustrates the period's tensions between:
  - elite political life and underground economies,
  - religious authority and folk practices,
  - fear of poisoning and the limits of forensic proof.

## Related people and institutions (commonly mentioned)

- **Louis XIV** (reign context; court sensitivity to scandal).
- **Madame de Montespan** (frequently named in popular accounts of the affair).
- The **Chambre Ardente** (special court associated with prosecutions).
