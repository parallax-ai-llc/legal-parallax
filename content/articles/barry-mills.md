## Background

- Barry Mills was an American prisoner who became known for his leadership role in the Aryan Brotherhood, a white supremacist prison gang in the United States.[^1]
- Public reporting about his early life is limited in many secondary summaries; most widely cited biographical details focus on his incarceration and later prosecutions.[^1]

## Role in the Aryan Brotherhood

- Often described as a prominent leader inside the organization, he was associated with the group’s efforts to exert control in prisons through violence and intimidation.[^1]
- Accounts commonly emphasize the extent to which the gang maintained structure and influence despite members being housed in high-security conditions.[^1]

## Federal prosecution and conviction

- In the mid-2000s, federal prosecutors brought a racketeering case that portrayed Aryan Brotherhood leadership as directing assaults and murders from prison.[^1]
- In 2006, Mills was convicted in federal court; summaries typically describe the convictions as including racketeering and murder-related charges connected to orders issued while incarcerated.[^1]

## Later years and death

- Mills remained in custody after the 2006 conviction and was still incarcerated at the time of his death in 2018.[^1]
- He is frequently referenced as an example of how prison-gang leadership structures can persist over long periods of confinement.[^1]

## Context and significance

- The Aryan Brotherhood has been treated by law enforcement as both a prison gang and an organized criminal group, with prosecutions often relying on conspiracy and racketeering frameworks.[^1]
- Cases involving alleged “orders from prison” are notable because they turn on communication channels, delegation, and organizational discipline under restrictive conditions.[^1]
