## Summary

Notorious Australian criminal who had his ears cut off in prison and became a bestselling author.

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

## Notability

- Widely known in Australia for a violent criminal history and an outsized public persona.
- Became an unusual case of a long-term prisoner who later built a mainstream media and publishing career.

## Background (high level)

- Birth name: **Mark Brandon Read**.
- Nickname "Chopper" is commonly associated with his prison notoriety and later public identity.

## Criminal history (overview)

- Spent many years in the Victorian prison system.
- Is frequently described in reporting and biographies as being involved in violent episodes and underworld disputes.
- A well-known anecdote from his time in custody is the **removal/cutting off of his ears**, which became part of his legend.

## Writing and public life

- Wrote and published multiple books (often marketed as autobiography/memoir or crime writing).
- His books and interviews helped turn him into a pop-culture figure beyond true-crime audiences.

## Legacy and portrayal

- Remembered as a controversial figure: simultaneously condemned for violence and studied as a case of criminal notoriety turning into celebrity.
- Often referenced in discussions of Australian underworld history, prison culture, and media sensationalism.
