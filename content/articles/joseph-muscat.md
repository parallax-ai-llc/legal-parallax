## Summary

Former Malta PM under whose government journalist Caruana Galizia was assassinated amid corruption scandals.

## Key Facts

- Served as Prime Minister of Malta from 2013 to 2020.[^1]
- Leader of Malta's Labour Party (Partit Laburista) during his rise to national office.[^1]
- Resigned after sustained public pressure related to investigations around the murder of journalist Daphne Caruana Galizia.[^1][^2]

## Background

- Born in 1974 and educated in Malta; worked as a journalist and later in European policy roles before entering national leadership.[^1]
- Became Labour Party leader in the late 2000s and positioned the party as more centrist and pro-business in the 2010s.[^1]

## Premiership (2013-2020)

High-level themes commonly associated with Muscat's time in office include:

- Economic growth and investment promotion, including high-profile programs such as the Individual Investor Programme (citizenship-by-investment).[^1]
- Governance controversies tied to allegations of corruption and conflicts of interest, frequently reported in international coverage.[^1][^2]
- Intensifying scrutiny after the 2016 Panama Papers revelations involving senior Maltese officials, which became a persistent political flashpoint.[^1][^2]

## Daphne Caruana Galizia Case (Context)

- Investigations into Caruana Galizia's 2017 assassination and related political fallout shaped Malta's domestic politics through 2019-2020.[^1][^2]
- Muscat's resignation is often described in major reporting as linked to the public crisis surrounding the case, though formal criminal responsibility for the assassination is treated separately in legal proceedings.[^1][^2]

## After Office

- Following his resignation, Muscat remained a prominent public figure and continued to attract attention in Malta's political and legal news cycles.[^1]
