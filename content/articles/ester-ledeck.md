## Summary

Ester Ledecká became the only person to win Olympic gold in both alpine skiing and snowboarding at the same Games, at 2018 PyeongChang.

## Expanded Overview

### Dual-discipline background

- Ledecká competes at the elite level in **two winter sports**: **alpine skiing** and **snowboarding**.
- Her international reputation is closely tied to being able to switch between these disciplines while remaining competitive.

### 2018 PyeongChang Olympic Games (high level)

- She won **alpine skiing** gold in the **women’s super-G** at PyeongChang 2018.
- At the same Olympics, she also won **snowboarding** gold in **parallel giant slalom**.
- The combination is widely noted because the two events require different equipment, technique, and race formats.

### Competition formats (quick context)

- **Super-G (alpine):** a single-run speed event that blends downhill-style speed with more turning than downhill.
- **Parallel giant slalom (snowboard):** head-to-head racing on mirrored courses, emphasizing start reaction, turning precision, and consistency.

### Notability and legacy

- Ledecká is frequently cited as a rare example of a modern winter athlete achieving top results across two distinct disciplines.
- Her 2018 results are often referenced in discussions about specialization vs. cross-discipline training in elite sport.
