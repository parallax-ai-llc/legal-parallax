- **Victim profile (general):** Primarily young men; details vary by source and case.
- **Modus operandi (general):** Lured victims to his residence; murders were typically committed indoors.
- **Discovery:** Triggered by practical evidence (notably issues involving disposal and drainage), leading investigators to the residence.

## Investigation & Trial (Overview)

- Nilsen’s arrest followed investigative work connected to suspicious material and human remains.
- He provided extensive statements to police; these confessions became central to the case narrative.
- The prosecution and defense contested aspects of intent and mental state, but he was ultimately convicted and received a life sentence.
