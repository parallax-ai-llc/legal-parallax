## Summary

Founder of the PKK Kurdish militant group responsible for an insurgency that killed over 40,000 people.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1999 | Captured and sentenced to life |

## Background

- **Full name**: Abdullah Öcalan (often referred to as **“Apo”** by supporters).
- **Born**: 4 April 1949, in southeastern Turkey.[^1]
- Became a prominent figure in Kurdish nationalism and leftist political organizing in Turkey in the 1970s.[^1]

## Role and significance

- Widely described as the **founder and longtime leader** of the Kurdistan Workers' Party (**PKK**), established in the late 1970s.[^1]
- The PKK launched an armed insurgency against the Turkish state in 1984; the broader conflict has resulted in **tens of thousands of deaths** over subsequent decades.[^1]
- Öcalan’s influence continued after his capture through statements, letters, and writings from prison that have been used to shape PKK positions and related Kurdish political movements.[^1]

## Expanded career timeline (high level)

- **1978** — PKK founded (Öcalan as a leading figure).[^1]
- **1984** — PKK begins armed campaign in Turkey.[^1]
- **1998** — Leaves Syria amid regional pressure (context for subsequent pursuit and arrest).[^1]
- **1999** — Captured and tried; ultimately given a life sentence (imprisoned on **İmralı Island**).[^1]
- **2000s–2010s** — Periodic calls associated with Öcalan for ceasefires/negotiations, amid shifting phases of the Turkey–PKK conflict.[^1]

## Detention and legal status

- Following capture in 1999, Öcalan was held under high-security conditions; reporting commonly associates his imprisonment with **İmralı Prison** in the Sea of Marmara.[^1]
- Court proceedings and sentencing are frequently discussed in the context of Turkey’s legal and political debates over the Kurdish issue and security policy.[^1]

## Legacy and controversy

- Considered by many Kurdish supporters a central political symbol; viewed by the Turkish state and various international actors as a militant/terrorist figure due to the PKK’s armed campaign.[^1]
- Frequently cited in discussions of Kurdish rights, armed insurgency, and attempted peace processes in modern Turkish history.[^1]
