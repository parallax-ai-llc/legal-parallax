## Summary

Real name of Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi, ISIS caliph who killed himself during a US raid.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1971 | Born in Iraq (near Samarra, according to multiple biographies). |
| 2003 | After the U.S. invasion of Iraq, became involved with insurgent networks tied to al-Qaeda in Iraq. |
| 2004–2009 | Detained by U.S. forces (often reported as held at Camp Bucca). |
| 2010 | Emerged as leader of the Islamic State of Iraq (ISI) after predecessor deaths. |
| 2013 | Expanded operations into Syria; group became widely known as ISIS/ISIL after a split with al-Qaeda. |
| 2014 | Proclaimed a “caliphate” from Mosul and adopted the name Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi; declared “Caliph Ibrahim.” |
| 2014–2019 | Led ISIS during its peak territorial control and subsequent losses under international military pressure. |
| 2019 | Died in northern Syria during a U.S. special operations raid, detonating an explosive vest. |

## Names and Aliases

- Most commonly known as **Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi**.
- Also described in reporting and government releases as **Abu Du’a** and by variations of his birth name.
- The “al-Baghdadi” surname is a *nisba* indicating an association with Baghdad, not necessarily a literal family name.

## Role in ISIS

- Served as the organization’s top leader and public face during the 2014 declaration of the Islamic State.
- Oversaw a hierarchy of military, security, media, and provincial governance structures used by ISIS in Iraq and Syria.
- ISIS used propaganda outputs (audio statements, video productions, magazines) to recruit and intimidate; al-Baghdadi’s rare public appearances amplified this strategy.

## Designations and Reward

- Designated a terrorist by multiple governments and international bodies.
- The U.S. State Department’s Rewards for Justice program offered a reward (reported up to **$25 million**) for information leading to his location.

## Death

- Died on **2019-10-27** near Barisha in Idlib Governorate, Syria, during a U.S. operation.
- U.S. statements described him fleeing into a tunnel and detonating an explosive vest, killing himself and others nearby.

## Legacy and Impact

- Widely associated with ISIS’s mass violence, sectarian targeting, and terror attacks internationally.
- His death was treated as a major symbolic blow to ISIS leadership, though the group continued operating through successor leadership and regional affiliates.
