## Summary
Dylan Klebold with Eric Harris carried out the Columbine massacre, killing 13 people at their high school. Klebold is often characterized as a depressive follower to Harris's psychopathic leadership.

## Background
- **Full name**: Dylan Bennet Klebold.
- **Born**: September 11, 1981, in Lakewood, Colorado, United States.
- **Family**: Son of Thomas and Susan (Sue) Klebold.
- **School**: Attended Columbine High School in Columbine, Colorado.
- **Interests (reported)**: Computers and technology; participated in school activities (e.g., video/theater-related projects).

## Columbine High School massacre (1999)
- On **April 20, 1999**, Klebold and **Eric Harris** carried out an attack at Columbine High School.
- The attack resulted in **13 deaths** (12 students and 1 teacher) and **numerous injuries**, and ended with both perpetrators’ deaths at the school.
- The event became one of the most widely covered U.S. school shootings and influenced subsequent debates on school safety, media effects, and youth violence.

## Interpretation and public narrative
- Early media narratives often framed Klebold as the more **depressed** or **reluctant** participant and Harris as the dominant planner.
- Later reporting and document-based research commonly emphasizes that both were active participants, and that simplified “leader/follower” labels can obscure the broader context.

## Legacy and impact
- The Columbine case is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - **Threat assessment** and warning signs in schools
  - **Copycat risk** and responsible media coverage
  - **Mental health** and adolescent crisis intervention
  - The evolution of **active shooter** response policies in U.S. law enforcement and school districts
