## Summary

Japanese mass murderer who stabbed and killed eight children at Ikeda Elementary School in Osaka. He was executed 15 months after conviction, unusually fast for Japan.

## Background

Takuma had a history of mental-health and employment difficulties and had been hospitalized and treated prior to the attack. Reporting around the case notes prior incidents and instability that preceded the stabbing.

## Ikeda Elementary School attack (2001)

On 8 June 2001, Takuma entered Ikeda Elementary School in Ikeda, Osaka Prefecture, carrying a knife. He stabbed multiple students and staff, killing eight children and injuring others.

## Arrest and investigation

Police arrested Takuma shortly after the attack. The case drew intense national attention in Japan, with public scrutiny focused on school security and on how authorities handle violent offenders with prior medical histories.

## Trial, conviction, and sentence

Takuma was tried for murder and related charges, convicted, and sentenced to death. Commentary on the case frequently highlights the court's findings regarding responsibility and the seriousness of the attack.

## Execution

He was executed by hanging in 2004, roughly 15 months after his conviction, which was widely described as unusually rapid by Japanese standards for capital cases.

## Aftermath and legacy

The Ikeda school stabbing became a reference point in discussions about campus access control, emergency response procedures, and the broader social impact of rare but high-profile mass-casualty attacks in Japan.
