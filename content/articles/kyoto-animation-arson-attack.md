## Summary

Shinji Aoba set fire to the Kyoto Animation studio killing 36 people. He doused the building with gasoline and ignited it, accusing the company of stealing his novel ideas.

## Context and Background

- **Kyoto Animation (KyoAni)** is a Kyoto-based animation studio known for producing TV series and films and for its in-house training model.
- The attack targeted **Kyoto Animation Studio 1** in Fushimi Ward, Kyoto; the building was constructed in **2007** and was used mainly by production staff.
- According to reporting summarized by Wikipedia, Kyoto Animation had received **hundreds of threats** in the year leading up to the attack, though whether they were related was not established.

## The Attack (18 July 2019)

- **Date/Time:** Morning of **18 July 2019** (about **10:31 a.m. JST**).
- **Method:** The perpetrator carried roughly **40 litres of gasoline**, poured it in and around the entrance area (and on people nearby), and ignited it.
- **Immediate outcome:** The fire spread rapidly through the building, resulting in mass casualties and severe injuries.
- **Casualties (widely reported):** **36 people killed** and **34 injured** (including the perpetrator).

## Perpetrator and Motive (High-Level)

- **Perpetrator:** **Shinji Aoba**.
- **Claimed motive:** Witnesses reported that he accused the studio of **plagiarism** (a belief that the company had stolen his ideas).
- Public summaries (e.g., Wikipedia) characterize the motive as involving **delusions** and retaliation against perceived wrongdoing.

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings

- The perpetrator was **apprehended near the scene** shortly after the attack.
- Police **arrested him on 27 May 2020** after he recovered from life-threatening burns.
- He was **indicted in December 2020**.
- In **January 2024**, he was **sentenced to death** by the Kyoto District Court (as summarized by Wikipedia).

## Impact on Kyoto Animation

- The fire destroyed large amounts of production materials and equipment at Studio 1.
- Kyoto Animation’s projects and collaborations were **delayed**, and multiple industry events were **cancelled or postponed** in the aftermath.
- The incident is widely described as one of Japan’s deadliest mass-casualty events in the post-war era and a major shock to the animation industry.

## Public Response and Support

- The attack prompted condolences from leaders and support messages from fans worldwide.
- Fundraising campaigns raised **billions of yen** in support for the studio and victims’ families (Wikipedia summarizes totals exceeding **¥3.3 billion** in Japan, plus additional international fundraising).
- Japan’s National Diet passed a **special measure** to make certain donations to the studio **tax-exempt**.

## Memorials and Recovery

- Kyoto Animation and the local community held memorial activities to honor victims and support survivors.
- The studio continued operations at other facilities and has been associated with ongoing rebuilding and long-term recovery efforts.
