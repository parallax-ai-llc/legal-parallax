## Summary

Japanese man convicted of the Kabukicho fire that killed 44 people in one of Japan's deadliest arsons.

## Context

- The incident Tsunoda is associated with occurred in Tokyo's Kabukichō district (Shinjuku), an area known for dense nightlife businesses and multi-tenant buildings.
- Large-loss fires in such buildings can be exacerbated by:
  - complex interior layouts and many rooms
  - locked or obstructed exits
  - delayed detection/notification
  - rapid smoke spread through stairwells and corridors

## What the Conviction Represents

This article focuses on the high-level historical record that Tsunoda was **convicted** in connection with a major Kabukichō fire. In that context, his case is commonly cited as:

- an example of the human causes behind mass-casualty building fires (intentional ignition rather than accidental failure)
- a reminder that accountability can extend beyond direct ignition to related criminal responsibility, depending on the facts proven in court

## Broader Aftermath and Significance

Even when a single individual is identified as criminally responsible, events of this scale typically drive broader changes, including:

- stricter enforcement of fire codes and occupancy rules
- renewed scrutiny of emergency egress (signage, unlocked exits, stairwell access)
- attention to landlord/tenant responsibilities in multi-use commercial buildings
- public debate about safety practices in high-density entertainment districts
