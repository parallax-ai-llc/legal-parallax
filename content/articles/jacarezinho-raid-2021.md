## Summary

Rio police killed 28 people during a raid on the Jacarezinho favela, the deadliest police operation in the city's history. Evidence suggested execution-style killings.

## Context and Background

- **Location:** Jacarezinho is a favela (informal community) in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
- **Public security setting:** Rio has long-running, highly militarized police operations in favelas, often justified as anti-gang actions.
- **Legal backdrop:** Brazil’s Supreme Federal Court (STF) issued restrictions on police operations in favelas during the COVID-19 pandemic (with exceptions), which became part of the public debate around this raid.

## The Operation (High-Level)

- **Date:** 2021-05-06.
- **Stated purpose:** Authorities described the operation as aimed at disrupting organized crime and investigating alleged recruitment of minors.
- **Scale:** The raid involved significant police deployment and resulted in widespread gunfire and casualties.

## Casualties and Key Claims

- **Deaths reported:** 28 people were reported killed during the operation, including a police officer.
- **Characterization:** It was widely described as the **deadliest police operation** in Rio de Janeiro’s history.
- **Allegations:** Multiple accounts and reporting raised concerns about **execution-style killings** and possible tampering with crime scenes.

## Aftermath and Oversight

- **Investigations:** The raid triggered calls for investigation by prosecutors, courts, and human-rights organizations.
- **Accountability debate:** The incident intensified scrutiny of lethal-force practices, body-camera policies, and operational rules in favelas.
- **Policy impact:** The case remained a prominent reference point in discussions about policing standards and judicial limits on raids.
