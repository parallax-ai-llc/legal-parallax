## Context

- Burkina Faso has faced a sustained insurgency involving jihadist armed groups since the mid-to-late 2010s, with violence spreading across multiple regions.
- Many of the deadliest episodes have targeted civilians in rural communities, sometimes alongside attacks on local security forces.

## Patterns and Characteristics

- **Targets:** Civilians in villages, markets, and transportation routes; in some cases, local auxiliaries and community defense groups.
- **Methods:** Armed assaults and raids on communities; incidents are often reported as mass killings with large civilian death tolls.
- **Attribution:** Public reporting frequently attributes attacks to jihadist groups operating in the Sahel; specific perpetrators may be contested or remain unconfirmed in early reporting.

## Reported Consequences

- **Humanitarian impact:** Large-scale displacement and heightened insecurity affecting access to food, schooling, and health services.
- **Political and security impact:** Major massacres have intensified domestic pressure on the state and influenced security policy and leadership changes.
