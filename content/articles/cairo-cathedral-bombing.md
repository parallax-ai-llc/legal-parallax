## Summary

ISIS-linked bombing of a Coptic Christian cathedral in Cairo killing 29 worshippers.

## Background

- Egypt has one of the world’s largest Coptic Christian communities, and sectarian attacks have periodically targeted churches and religious gatherings.
- The bombing occurred during a Sunday service at the Saint Peter and Saint Paul Church complex adjacent to Cairo’s Coptic Orthodox Cathedral.

## The Attack

- Date: 2016-12-11
- Method: An explosive device was detonated inside the church during worship.
- Impact: The blast killed 29 people and injured many others, making it one of the deadliest attacks on Coptic Christians in Cairo in that period.

## Attribution

- The incident was claimed by the Islamic State’s Egypt/Sinai affiliate in the aftermath, and Egyptian authorities publicly linked the bombing to ISIS.

## Aftermath

- Security and political leaders issued public condemnations and pledged increased protection for churches.
- The event intensified debates about domestic security, extremist networks, and the protection of religious minorities.
