## Summary

Italian fashion designer murdered on his doorstep in Miami Beach by spree killer Andrew Cunanan.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1972 | Began designing for established Italian fashion houses (including work associated with Callaghan, Genny, and Complice in the 1970s). |
| 1978 | Founded Gianni Versace S.p.A. and opened a boutique in Milan. |
| 1982 | Expanded the Versace brand into accessories, homeware, and additional product lines as the company grew internationally. |
| 1993 | Became widely identified with high-profile celebrity dressing and runway presentations in the 1990s. |
| 1997 | Murdered by Andrew Cunanan on the steps of his Miami Beach home. |

## Background

- **Origin**: Born in Reggio Calabria, Italy; learned aspects of tailoring and garment construction through early exposure to his mother's dressmaking work.
- **Reputation**: Known for bold colors, baroque motifs, and a recognizable Medusa logo that became synonymous with the brand.

## Death and Aftermath

- **Date and place**: Versace was shot outside Casa Casuarina (Miami Beach) on 15 July 1997.
- **Perpetrator**: Andrew Cunanan, later found dead by suicide.
- **Industry impact**: The murder received extensive international coverage and became one of the defining cultural moments in fashion news of the 1990s.

## Legacy

- **Brand continuity**: Versace continued as a major luxury fashion house after his death, with leadership transitioning within the Versace family and the company maintaining global retail presence.
- **Cultural influence**: His designs influenced late-20th-century fashion aesthetics, particularly the intersection of pop culture, celebrity, and runway spectacle.
