## Summary
Daniel Barrera Barrera, 'El Loco,' was considered the last great Colombian drug lord. He ran a cocaine empire worth billions. Captured in Venezuela in 2012 and extradited to the US in 2018.

## Additional Context

### Identity and Aliases
- Commonly known by the nickname **"El Loco"** ("The Crazy One").
- Reported as **Daniel Barrera Barrera** in major press and court coverage.

### Role in the Cocaine Trade
- Characterized by authorities and media as a **major broker/organizer** in the Colombian cocaine supply chain, especially during the post-cartel era.
- Linked in reporting to coordination among **Colombian armed groups and trafficking networks**, acting as an intermediary rather than a single, vertically integrated “cartel” leader.

### Areas of Operation (High-Level)
- Associated with trafficking routes connected to **eastern Colombia** and cross-border movement.
- Captured in **Venezuela (2012)** during an international operation and later moved through extradition processes.

### Capture and Extradition
- **2012**: Arrested in Venezuela following intelligence sharing and joint operations.
- **2018**: Extradited to the **United States**.
- US case coverage describes him as a high-value target from the era after the collapse of larger Colombian cartels.

## What He Is Known For
- Being described as the **"last great"** Colombian drug lord figure by some outlets, reflecting his prominence after earlier cartel leadership was dismantled.
- Operating through **alliances and logistics** across multiple actors rather than a single brand-name cartel.

## Legal Case (Overview)
- After extradition to the US, Barrera’s case proceeded in federal court; public summaries generally describe **drug-trafficking conspiracy** allegations tied to large-scale cocaine shipments.
- Publicly available write-ups emphasize his significance as an international target rather than providing a single definitive number for total drugs moved (figures can vary by source and indictment framing).

## Related Actors and Structure (High-Level)

  - **Armed groups** and local networks that controlled territory and production corridors
  - **Cross-border facilitators** supporting transit and laundering
