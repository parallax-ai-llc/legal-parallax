## Overview

- **Date:** 15 March 2019 (local time).[^1]
- **Location:** Christchurch, New Zealand — attacks targeted two mosques (Al Noor Mosque and Linwood Islamic Centre).[^1]
- **Casualties:** 51 people killed; dozens injured (widely reported as 40+ injured).[^1]
- **Characterization:** The incident is commonly described as a terrorist attack and mass shooting.[^1]

## What Happened (High-level)

- The attacker traveled between the two sites and carried out shootings during Friday prayers.[^1]
- The event was livestreamed online, and copies of the video and related material spread rapidly before being removed by platforms and authorities.[^1]

## Immediate Aftermath

- New Zealand authorities initiated a large-scale police response and investigations in Christchurch and elsewhere.[^1]
- The government and civil society organized vigils and memorial services; the national response emphasized solidarity with Muslim communities.[^1]

## Legal Proceedings

- The perpetrator was arrested shortly after the attacks and later faced charges including **murder**, **attempted murder**, and **engaging in a terrorist act** under New Zealand law.[^1]
- In 2020, he pleaded guilty and was sentenced to **life imprisonment without the possibility of parole** (reported as the first such sentence in New Zealand).[^1]

## Policy and Social Impact

- **Firearms law reform:** New Zealand moved quickly to tighten gun laws after the attacks, including restrictions on certain semi-automatic firearms.[^1]
- **Online extremism and content moderation:** The attacks became a global reference point in debates on violent extremist content online and platform responsibility.[^1]
- **Community relations:** The attacks had lasting effects on interfaith initiatives, security at places of worship, and public discussions about racism and far-right extremism.[^1]
