## Background

- Born 5 December 1979 in Ukraine.[^1]
- Became known in Ukrainian nationalist and far-right political circles in the 2000s.[^1]

## Azov movement (overview)

- Biletsky is associated with the formation and early leadership of the Azov Battalion, a volunteer unit that emerged in 2014 during the conflict in eastern Ukraine.[^1]
- Azov was later incorporated into Ukraine's National Guard (as the Azov Regiment), moving from a volunteer formation into a state structure.[^1]

## Political activity

- He has been described in public reporting and reference sources as a far-right political figure.[^1]
- Served as a member of Ukraine's parliament (Verkhovna Rada) after being elected in the mid-2010s.[^1]
- After leaving parliament, he remained active in political and veteran/volunteer networks linked to the broader Azov movement.[^1]

## Public characterization and controversy (high level)

- Biletsky and the Azov movement have drawn international scrutiny over far-right symbolism and allegations of extremist links; Ukrainian authorities and some participants have disputed aspects of these characterizations.[^1]
- Because many claims in this area are contested and politicized, summaries often rely on secondary sources and should be read with attention to attribution and time period.[^1]
