## Context and Notability

- José Gonzalo Rodríguez Gacha (often nicknamed **"El Mexicano"**) was a prominent Colombian narcotrafficker who became a key figure in the **Medellín Cartel** era.
- He is commonly described in secondary sources as one of the cartel’s major **enforcers** and **trafficking partners**, and is frequently mentioned alongside Pablo Escobar and other senior Medellín leaders.

## Background

- Rodríguez Gacha was born in Colombia (1947) and rose from regional criminal activity into large-scale narcotics trafficking during the late 20th century.
- The nickname "El Mexicano" is widely attributed to his reported affinity for Mexican culture and imagery; it is also used in media profiles about Medellín-era traffickers.

## Role in the Medellín Cartel (High Level)

- Sources typically associate him with:
  - **Cocaine trafficking logistics** and expansion of routes.
  - **Armed enforcement**, including the organization of gunmen and protection networks.
  - Influence in certain regions of Colombia where Medellín-related operations and violence intensified.

## Conflict with the Colombian State

- In accounts of the late 1980s, Rodríguez Gacha is often included among the Medellín figures implicated in escalations of violence tied to pressure from Colombian authorities and anti-cartel operations.
- Public reporting and historical summaries frequently place him within the broader wave of cartel-state confrontation that defined the Medellín period.

## Death

- He was killed in 1989 during operations involving Colombian security forces, a commonly cited endpoint in timelines of major Medellín-affiliated leaders.

## Legacy and Historical Notes

- Rodríguez Gacha appears in many summaries of the Medellín Cartel as an example of how cartel power combined **trafficking wealth** with **paramilitary-style enforcement**.
- His life and role have been referenced in journalism, documentaries, and dramatized portrayals of Colombia’s narcotics conflict.
