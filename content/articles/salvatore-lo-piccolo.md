## Summary
Salvatore Lo Piccolo was the boss of the Sicilian Mafia after Provenzano's arrest. Known as 'The Baron,' he was captured in 2007 with a list of Mafia commandments. Sentenced to life.

## Background
- Born in Palermo, Sicily (Italy) in 1942.
- Identified by Italian authorities and investigative reporting as associated with the Palermo area of Cosa Nostra.

## Role in Cosa Nostra
- Commonly described as one of the senior figures who helped manage the organization after major arrests in the mid-2000s.
- His leadership is often discussed in the context of the post-Totò Riina / Bernardo Provenzano era and the internal effort to reduce violence and public exposure.

## 2007 Arrest
- Arrested by Italian police in November 2007 in Sicily after a period as a fugitive.
- Captured together with his son Sandro Lo Piccolo during the same operation.

## “Mafia Commandments” Document
- During the 2007 operation, investigators reported finding written guidance attributed to the Palermo leadership.
- The document has been widely summarized as outlining rules of conduct (e.g., discretion, avoiding attention, managing contacts carefully) intended to limit operational risk.

## Legal Outcome
- Reported in multiple sources as receiving a life sentence in Italy for mafia-related crimes.
- Continues to be described as imprisoned rather than deceased.

## Notes
- Public reporting varies on the extent and duration of his effective leadership, reflecting the opacity of Cosa Nostra’s internal structure.
