## Summary
Michael Ryan shot and killed 16 people in Hungerford, Berkshire on August 19, 1987, before killing himself. The Hungerford massacre led to the Firearms (Amendment) Act 1988.

## Overview
- **Event**: Hungerford massacre (Hungerford, Berkshire, England)
- **Date**: 1987-08-19
- **Outcome**: Ryan killed 16 people and injured others before killing himself.
- **Broader impact**: The incident is widely cited as a catalyst for tightening UK firearms law, culminating in the **Firearms (Amendment) Act 1988**.

## Background
High-level reporting commonly notes that Ryan was a local resident whose actions on 19 August 1987 shocked the UK and prompted renewed public debate about civilian access to certain firearms.

## Aftermath and legacy
- **Public policy**: The massacre contributed to momentum for legislative change and stricter controls on particular categories of firearms in the UK.
- **Societal impact**: It remains one of the UK’s most notorious mass shootings and is frequently referenced in discussions of British gun policy.
- **Historical significance**: Along with later incidents, Hungerford forms part of the modern history of UK firearms regulation.
