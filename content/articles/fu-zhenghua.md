## Summary

Chinese justice minister sentenced to death with reprieve for taking $16 million in bribes.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2010 | Appointed Director of the Beijing Municipal Public Security Bureau (Beijing police chief). |
| 2013 | Appointed Deputy Minister of Public Security. |
| 2018 | Became Minister of Justice of the People's Republic of China. |
| 2020 | Left the Ministry of Justice; later served on the CPPCC Social and Legal Affairs Committee (reported 2020–2021). |
| 2022 | Convicted of bribery and “bending the law for personal gain”; publicly reported sentence was life imprisonment (some summaries also describe a death sentence with reprieve). |

## Notes on Notability

- Fu Zhenghua was a senior public-security and legal-affairs figure whose career included leadership roles in Beijing policing, the national Ministry of Public Security, and the Ministry of Justice.
- International reporting about his case often frames it within the Chinese Communist Party’s broader anti-corruption efforts and internal discipline system.

## Key Positions (reported)

- Director, Beijing Municipal Public Security Bureau (2010–2013).
- Deputy Minister, Ministry of Public Security (2013–2015).
- Executive Deputy Minister of Public Security (2015–2018).
- Minister of Justice (2018–2020).
- Deputy Director, CPPCC National Committee Social and Legal Affairs Committee (reported 2020–2021).

## Public Campaigns and High-Profile Work (as described in biographies)

- Worked as an investigator and later as a senior Beijing police official associated with several high-profile criminal investigations.
- Led or was associated with campaigns focused on cybercrime and online speech/rumor enforcement (terminology varies by source).

## Legal Case (high-level)

- In 2022, Chinese state media and secondary summaries reported that Fu was convicted of accepting bribes and abusing legal authority for personal gain.
- Sentencing is variously summarized; the English-language Wikipedia biography describes a life-imprisonment sentence, while some short summaries describe a death sentence with reprieve.
