## Summary

Neo-Nazi Stephan Balliet attempted to storm a synagogue on Yom Kippur but failed to break in. He then killed 2 bystanders and shot at a kebab shop; livestreamed the attack.

## Context

- **Date & place:** 9 October 2019, Halle (Saale), Saxony-Anhalt, Germany.
- **Target:** The local synagogue during **Yom Kippur**, when services were underway.
- **Ideology:** The attacker acted from a far-right, antisemitic worldview and framed the attack as part of extremist online culture.

## Attack Overview (High-Level)

- Balliet arrived armed with improvised firearms and explosives and attempted to force entry into the synagogue.
- **Entry failed** because the synagogue door held, preventing a mass-casualty attack inside.
- After failing to enter, he shot and killed two people nearby and continued moving through the area.
- The attack was **livestreamed** and accompanied by a manifesto-style document, reflecting the pattern of “performative” extremist violence aimed at online audiences.

## Victims and Immediate Aftermath

- Two bystanders were killed and additional people were injured.
- The attacker also fired at a nearby kebab shop and at other targets while trying to escape.
- Police pursued and arrested Balliet following the incident.

## Investigation, Trial, and Sentencing

- German authorities treated the incident as a terrorist attack with antisemitic motivation.
- The case highlighted the role of online radicalization, homemade weapons, and copycat tactics.
- In 2020, a German court convicted Balliet of murder and attempted murder and imposed a **life sentence** (with preventive detention ordered).

## Significance

- The Halle attack became a major reference point in Germany’s debate about:
  - Protection of Jewish institutions and community security
  - Far-right extremist networks and propaganda ecosystems
  - The connection between online spaces, manifestos, and real-world violence
- The failure to breach the synagogue underscored how basic physical security measures can materially reduce casualties.
