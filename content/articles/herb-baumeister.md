## Summary
Herb Baumeister was an Indianapolis businessman who murdered at least 11 men at his Fox Hollow Farm estate. Thousands of bone fragments were found on his property. He killed himself before arrest.

## Background
- **Early life**: Born in Indiana and raised in the Indianapolis area. He attended college and later worked in a mix of administrative and business roles.
- **Public persona**: Known locally as a successful suburban businessman and family man, which contrasted sharply with the later allegations.

## Crimes
- **Victim profile**: The confirmed and suspected victims were primarily adult men.
- **Location**: Many killings were linked to Baumeister’s property, **Fox Hollow Farm** (Westfield, Indiana).
- **Method (reported)**: Accounts in investigative reporting and witness statements commonly describe **strangulation** and the use of alcohol/drugs to incapacitate victims, though the full details of each homicide are not known.

## Investigation
- **Missing persons pattern**: Disappearances of men in the Indianapolis area drew attention in the early-to-mid 1990s.
- **Search of Fox Hollow Farm**: Law enforcement searched the estate after mounting suspicion, later recovering extensive human remains.
- **Forensic findings**: Investigators reported **thousands of bone fragments** and evidence consistent with attempts to destroy remains (including burning).

## Death
- **Flight and suicide**: After the investigation intensified, Baumeister left Indiana and later died by suicide in Canada, preventing a criminal trial.

## Legacy and public attention
- The Fox Hollow Farm case is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - serial homicide investigations involving fragmented remains
  - the limits of resolution when a suspect dies before trial
  - identification challenges when remains are incomplete
