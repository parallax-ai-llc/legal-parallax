## Summary

Attack on Charlie Hebdo offices in Paris on January 7, 2015 killing 12 people over cartoons of Muhammad.

## Background and Context

- **Charlie Hebdo** is a French satirical weekly known for political and religious caricatures.
- The publication had faced prior controversy and threats related to depictions of the Prophet Muhammad.
- In the years preceding the attack, France had experienced heightened concerns about jihadist violence and domestic radicalization.

## The Attack (High-Level)

- **Date:** 2015-01-07
- **Location:** Paris, France (Charlie Hebdo offices)
- **Type:** Armed assault on a media organization.
- **Immediate outcome:** 12 people were killed and multiple others were injured during the attack.

## Perpetrators (Overview)

- The attack was carried out by **two armed assailants**, identified in reporting as the Kouachi brothers.
- The perpetrators stated motivations linked to the magazine's cartoons of Muhammad.
- The event was widely discussed in the context of jihadist ideology and terrorism in Europe.

## Victims and Targets

- Those killed included **staff members and contributors** associated with Charlie Hebdo, as well as others present at the scene.
- The attack was widely characterized as an assault on **freedom of expression** and the press.

## Aftermath and Wider Events

- The attack triggered a large-scale **security and manhunt operation** in the Paris region.
- It was followed by additional connected incidents in the same period, contributing to a broader national crisis response.

## Public Response

- Large public demonstrations and vigils were held in France and internationally.
- The slogan **"Je suis Charlie"** became a prominent symbol of solidarity and debate about speech, satire, and pluralism.

## Policy and Security Impact (General)

- France intensified counterterrorism measures and public security posture in response to the attacks.
- The event influenced public discourse on **radicalization**, intelligence coordination, and protection of high-risk targets.

## Legacy

- The Charlie Hebdo attack remains a landmark case in modern European terrorism history.
- It continues to be referenced in discussions on the boundaries of satire, religious offense, and the risks faced by journalists and publishers.
