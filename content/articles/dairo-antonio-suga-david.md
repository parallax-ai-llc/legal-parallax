## Summary

Known as 'Otoniel,' leader of the Clan del Golfo, Colombia's most wanted drug trafficker.

## Also Known As

- **Aliases:** "Otoniel" (most commonly used), and other reported nom de guerre/aliases used in Colombian security reporting.[^1]
- **Organization names in reporting:** *Clan del Golfo* (also referred to in English-language sources as the Gulf Clan), with links in public reporting to successor networks associated with the *Autodefensas Gaitanistas de Colombia (AGC)*.[^1]

## Background (High-Level)

- Colombian national born in 1971, later becoming a prominent figure in organized crime and narcotics trafficking in Colombia.[^1]
- Public reporting commonly describes him as rising through armed/criminal structures in Colombia’s northwest (Urabá/Antioquia region) before becoming a top leader within the Gulf Clan structure.[^1]

## Role and Notability

- Frequently described by Colombian authorities and international media as one of the most significant drug-trafficking leaders in Colombia in the 2010s–early 2020s.[^1]
- Associated in reporting with coordinating cocaine trafficking routes and exercising influence through an armed network engaged in intimidation, extortion, and territorial control.[^1]

## Capture and Aftermath

- **October 2021:** Captured by Colombian security forces in an operation widely publicized by the Colombian government.[^1]
- **2022:** Extradited to the United States in connection with U.S. drug-trafficking charges reported in open sources.[^1]
