## Background

- Benjamin Yeaten is widely described in reporting and reference summaries as a senior security figure within Charles Taylor's inner circle during Liberia's civil war era.
- He is commonly associated with Taylor's personal security apparatus and units accused of politically motivated violence.
- Publicly available biographical details are limited and are often compiled from investigative reporting and sanctions documentation.

## Role and Allegations

- Frequently characterized as a key enforcer for Taylor's National Patriotic Front of Liberia (NPFL) and later Taylor-aligned state security structures.
- Often linked (in secondary sources) to the **Anti-Terrorist Unit (ATU)**, an elite force associated with intimidation, targeted killings, and abuses.
- Allegations described in public sources include directing or coordinating death squads and campaigns of terror affecting Liberia and neighboring conflicts.
- In most summaries, Yeaten is discussed in the context of **command responsibility** and the use of security units for regime protection and coercion.

## Sanctions and Legal Status (High-Level)

- Yeaten has been identified in international discourse about Liberia-era abuses, including through sanctions-related narratives and accountability efforts.
- Where legal outcomes are unclear or disputed across sources, descriptions should be treated as **allegations** unless supported by a primary legal document.
