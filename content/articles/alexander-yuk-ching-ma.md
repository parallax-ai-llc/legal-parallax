## Summary

Former CIA officer charged with selling classified information to Chinese intelligence for $700,000.

Public reporting describes him as a former CIA clandestine officer who later worked for the FBI as a contract linguist in Honolulu, and who was accused of conspiring to transmit U.S. national defense information to China’s Ministry of State Security (MSS). He was arrested in August 2020, later pleaded guilty (2024), and was sentenced to a 10-year prison term (2024).

## What he was accused of (high-level)

- **Conspiracy to transmit national defense information**: The core allegation was an agreement to provide classified or sensitive U.S. government information to Chinese intelligence.
- **Regaining access through employment**: Reporting says he sought FBI work after leaving the CIA in order to regain access to classified information.
- **Methods alleged in court filings/reporting**: Copying, photographing, or removing documents; traveling with materials; receiving cash and gifts.

## Investigation and case notes

- Undercover recordings (2019–2020) were cited in reporting as part of the investigation.
- The U.S. Department of Justice press releases and major news outlets are commonly referenced in summaries of the case.
- The case has been discussed in the broader context of counterintelligence risks associated with former intelligence personnel and contractor access.
