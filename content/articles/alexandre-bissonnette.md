## Background

- Born in 1989 in Quebec, Canada.
- Studied at Université Laval in Quebec City.
- Media and court records described him as socially isolated and affected by anxiety.

## Quebec City Mosque Shooting (2017)

- On 29 January 2017, he opened fire at the Islamic Cultural Centre of Quebec City during evening prayers.
- Six people were killed and others were injured.
- The attack is widely recognized in Canada as an anti-Muslim hate crime and act of terrorism.

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings

- Arrested shortly after the shooting.
- Charged with multiple counts of first-degree murder and attempted murder.
- In 2018, he pleaded guilty.

## Sentencing and Appeals

- Sentenced to life imprisonment without parole eligibility for 40 years (a sentence later changed on appeal).
- Canadian appellate courts reduced the parole ineligibility period; subsequent rulings set eligibility at 25 years.

## Public Impact

- The shooting prompted national debate in Canada about Islamophobia, hate crimes, and violent extremism.
- Vigils and memorials were held in Quebec and across Canada to honor the victims.
