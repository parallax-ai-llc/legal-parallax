## Background (high level)

- Dale Cregan is from the Greater Manchester area (England).
- He became associated with local criminal activity and disputes that escalated into violence.

## Notable crimes

- In 2011, he was involved in the killings of Mark Short and David Short.
- On 18 September 2012, he killed two Greater Manchester Police constables (Fiona Bone and Nicola Hughes) after luring officers to a property.
- The 2012 attack involved a firearm and an explosive device (a grenade), and it was widely reported as a deliberate ambush.

## Investigation and arrest

- The 2012 incident triggered a major police operation in Greater Manchester.
- Cregan was arrested shortly after the killings.

## Trial and sentence

- He was tried and convicted for multiple murders.
- UK courts imposed a whole-life order (a life sentence with no minimum term for release) for the police murders.

## Public impact

- The deaths of the two officers led to widespread public mourning and tributes across the UK.
- The case was frequently cited in discussions about attacks on police and the risks officers face when responding to routine calls.
