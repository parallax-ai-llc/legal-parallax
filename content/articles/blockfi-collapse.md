## Summary

Crypto lender that collapsed due to exposure to FTX and Three Arrows Capital.

## Career Timeline

## Context

- BlockFi was a centralized crypto lender, known for interest-bearing accounts and crypto-backed loans.
- Its business model depended on counterparties, collateral management, and liquidity (ability to meet withdrawals).

## What Happened (High Level)

- **Counterparty shock:** The collapse of major crypto firms (including Three Arrows Capital) stressed the lending market.
- **Contagion from FTX/Alameda:** BlockFi had material exposure to entities within the FTX ecosystem (reported), and the failure of FTX removed expected support and liquidity.
- **Run dynamics:** When confidence dropped, users sought withdrawals at scale; platforms that could not meet redemptions faced insolvency pressure.

## Bankruptcy & Aftermath

- BlockFi filed for **Chapter 11** bankruptcy protection in the United States in late 2022.
- Proceedings focused on:
  - identifying assets and liabilities,
  - reconciling customer and creditor claims,
  - pursuing recoveries from bankrupt counterparties (where applicable), and
  - designing a plan for distributions.

## Why It Mattered

- It became a prominent example of **CeFi (centralized finance) failure**, where opacity of balance sheets and concentrated counterparty risk amplified losses.
- The event influenced market expectations around:
  - proof-of-reserves / proof-of-liabilities discussions,
  - stricter risk management for rehypothecation and lending,
  - and regulatory attention on yield products marketed to retail customers.

## Key Takeaways

- **Liquidity mismatches are existential:** short-term withdrawal demand can overwhelm long-duration or illiquid positions.
- **Counterparty concentration compounds risk:** dependence on a small number of trading/lending partners raises tail risk.
- **Transparency matters:** clearer disclosures can reduce misinformation and panic, but cannot substitute for solvency.
