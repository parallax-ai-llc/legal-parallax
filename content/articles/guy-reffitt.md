First January 6 defendant convicted at trial for storming the Capitol with a gun and zip ties.

## Background

- Texas-based worker in the petroleum industry; later worked in home-improvement contracting (including sunroom installation).
- Associated with the **Three Percenters** militia movement prior to January 6, 2021.[^1]

## January 6, 2021 (U.S. Capitol attack)

- Traveled to Washington, D.C. for events around January 6, 2021 and participated in the breach of restricted Capitol grounds.[^1]
- Reported in court filings and coverage as carrying tactical gear; widely noted for allegations involving a firearm and zip ties (often cited as part of why the case drew attention).[^1]

## Criminal case (high-level)

- Notable for being the **first January 6 defendant convicted at trial** (as opposed to by plea agreement).[^1]
- Convictions described by Wikipedia include:
  - obstruction of an official proceeding,
  - entering and remaining on restricted grounds,
  - obstruction of justice.[^1]
- Sentenced (Aug 2022) to **87 months** in federal prison, a fine, and supervised release.[^1]

## Later developments

- Wikipedia notes that his case was impacted by post-2022 legal developments affecting January 6 obstruction charges (including reference to *Fischer v. United States*), with mention of potential resentencing proceedings.[^1]
- Wikipedia further notes a **presidential pardon** in January 2025 as part of a large set of January 6-related pardons.[^1]
