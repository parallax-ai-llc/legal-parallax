## Disambiguation / Data Notes

The name **Edward Dyer** is used by multiple notable figures. The metadata and summary in this file may reflect a conflation with other people named Dyer.

- **Sir Edward Dyer (1543–1607)**: English courtier and poet in the Elizabethan era.
- **Edward C. Dyer (1915–2015)**: U.S. Marine Corps major general (different person).
- **Reginald E. H. Dyer (1864–1927)**: British Indian Army officer associated with the 1919 Jallianwala Bagh massacre (often referenced simply as “Dyer”).

## Sir Edward Dyer (courtier and poet, 16th–17th c.)

High-level biography (as summarized in major reference sources):

- Born in **Somerset**; educated at **Oxford** (accounts vary by college/hall).
- Appeared at **Elizabeth I’s court** after time abroad; associated with prominent courtiers and writers.
- Served in royal administration, including stewardship at **Woodstock** (from 1570).
- Undertook diplomatic/royal missions, including travel to the **Low Countries (1584)** and **Denmark (1589)**.
- Served as **Member of Parliament for Somerset** (1589, 1593).
- **Knighted** and appointed **Chancellor of the Order of the Garter** (1596).
- Died in **1607**; buried at **St Saviour’s (Southwark Cathedral)**.

## Literary Reputation (overview)

- Remembered as an **Elizabethan courtier-poet**; contemporaries praised his skill in elegant verse.
- A limited portion of his work survives; some poems historically attributed to him have disputed authorship in modern scholarship.
- Later cultural references include modern television where lines attributed to Dyer are recited.
