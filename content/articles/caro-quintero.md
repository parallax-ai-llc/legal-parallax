## Additional Context

### Overview
- Rafael Caro Quintero is a Mexican drug trafficker widely associated with the early growth of large-scale marijuana trafficking from Mexico into the United States in the late 1970s and early 1980s.
- He is commonly described in sources as a co-founder and key figure of the Guadalajara Cartel, an organization that helped professionalize transnational drug trafficking networks in Mexico.

### Notable Allegations and Legal History (high level)
- He has been linked in public reporting and official accounts to the 1985 kidnapping, torture, and murder of U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) agent Enrique “Kiki” Camarena and pilot Alfredo Zavala.
- In 1985, Mexican authorities arrested him; he was convicted in Mexico for the Camarena case.
- In 2013, a Mexican court ordered his release on a procedural basis; the decision was later contested and he returned to fugitive status.
- In July 2022, Mexican forces announced his recapture.

### Legacy and Significance
- The Camarena case is frequently cited as a major inflection point in U.S.–Mexico counternarcotics cooperation and in the public profile of the Mexican drug trade.
- Caro Quintero remains a prominent figure in discussions of the historical evolution of Mexican organized crime from regional trafficking groups to large cartel structures.
