## Summary

Delhi gangster shot dead inside a courtroom by rival gang members disguised as lawyers.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2020 | Reported arrest in Delhi on charges including murder and extortion.[^2] |
| 2021 | Shot dead in a Delhi courtroom during a hearing; police returned fire and killed two attackers.[^2] |

## Background and Notability

Jitender Maan (often reported with the alias "Gogi") was described by authorities and media as a prominent gangster in Delhi and surrounding regions.[^2]

High-level points commonly mentioned in reporting include:

- He was described as being on Delhi police's "most-wanted" list at the time of his death.[^2]
- He was an accused in numerous criminal cases across multiple Indian states, including allegations such as murder, kidnapping, and fraud.[^2]

## Courtroom Shooting (2021)

The killing occurred inside the Rohini court complex in Delhi:

- Two assailants posed as lawyers and opened fire inside the courtroom where a case against him was being heard.[^2]
- Officials said police personnel returned fire at the scene, killing the two attackers.[^2]
- The incident drew public scrutiny and political commentary about court security and planning in Delhi.[^2]

## Reported Criminal Trajectory (as summarized in media)

Some reporting described a progression from petty crimes to more serious allegations:

- Reports cited by the BBC said his involvement in crime began as a teenager and initially included carjacking and theft.[^2]
- Later, he was described as a suspect in several high-profile killings, including the killing of a popular singer in Haryana.[^2]
