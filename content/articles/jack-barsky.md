KGB sleeper agent who lived as an American for over a decade before being discovered.

## Early Life

- Born as **Albrecht Dittrich** in East Germany.
- Studied chemistry and was recruited by Soviet intelligence during the Cold War.

## Recruitment and Training

- Selected and trained by the **KGB** for “illegal” (non-official-cover) work.
- Adopted the alias **"Jack Barsky"** and prepared to live long-term under a fabricated identity.

## Life in the United States (Cover Identity)

- Entered the United States and lived for years as a sleeper agent.
- Maintained a normal outward life (work and family life) while keeping his clandestine status hidden.
- Like many illegals, relied on:
  - careful personal documentation and identity management
  - compartmentation and strict operational discipline
  - avoiding attention from law enforcement and intelligence services

## Discovery and Aftermath

- U.S. authorities eventually identified him as a Soviet/Russian intelligence asset.
- The public timeline commonly highlights **1997** as the year he was discovered.
- In later years, Barsky became a public figure in discussions about Cold War espionage and sleeper operations.

## Why He Matters

- Illustrates how “illegal” intelligence officers can blend into society for extended periods.
- Highlights the long time horizons of Cold War tradecraft: patient placement, deep cover, and delayed tasking.
- Became a well-known case study in modern popular understanding of sleeper agents.
