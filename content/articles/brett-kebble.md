## Summary

South African mining magnate whose murder was arranged by his own associates in a death-for-hire plot.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2005 | Murdered in arranged assassination |

## Early Life and Education

- Born in Springs, Gauteng, South Africa.
- Attended St. Andrew's School in Bloemfontein.
- Earned a law degree from the University of Cape Town (1986).
- Worked as an articled clerk after graduation before moving into mining.

## Business Activities

Kebble became a prominent figure in South African gold and diamond mining from the mid-1990s, often associated with black economic empowerment (BEE) transactions.

Notable business involvements reported in major press coverage include:

- Rand Leases Gold Mining (early entry into mining, alongside his father Roger Kebble).
- Randgold & Exploration (leadership obtained after a hostile takeover).
- JCI Limited (acquired a controlling stake in 1995 via a BEE partnership with Mzi Khumalo; the relationship later deteriorated).
- Interests across several mid-size mining companies at different times (e.g., Harmony, Western Areas, Durban Deep).

## Allegations and Legal Scrutiny

After his death, reporting and subsequent investigations highlighted extensive allegations of corporate misconduct around some of Kebble's business dealings.

High-level themes commonly cited include:

- Allegations of corporate fraud and irregular transactions.
- Political connections and influence-peddling claims (especially related to figures linked to the African National Congress).
- Broader concerns about governance failures in parts of the mining sector during the period.

## Death and Aftermath

- Kebble was shot dead in Johannesburg on 27 September 2005.
- The murder investigation drew significant public attention because of the involvement of prominent businessmen and the suggestion of political entanglements.
- Later court testimony (reported in 2010) alleged the killing was arranged by hitmen hired by his security chief, and that it may have been carried out at Kebble's own request (sometimes characterized as “suicide-by-murder”).
- Despite public proceedings and media scrutiny, the complete circumstances around the death have been described as remaining unclear.
