## Background

- Abu Shekau (often reported as Abubakar Shekau) was a Nigerian Islamist militant best known as a long-time leader of the jihadist group Boko Haram.
- Public reporting commonly places his birth around 1969 in northeastern Nigeria, though precise biographical details are disputed.
- He became prominent inside Boko Haram after the group’s founder, Mohammed Yusuf, was killed in Nigerian custody in 2009.

## Role in Boko Haram

- After 2009, Shekau emerged as Boko Haram’s de facto leader and principal public voice, issuing audio and video messages.
- Under his leadership, Boko Haram expanded insurgent operations in northeastern Nigeria and conducted attacks targeting civilians and government/security forces.
- The group was responsible for mass abductions and forced recruitment; the 2014 kidnapping of schoolgirls from Chibok became one of the best-known incidents associated with Boko Haram.

## Relationship with the Islamic State

- In 2015, Boko Haram pledged allegiance to the Islamic State and began using the name “Islamic State’s West Africa Province” (ISWAP) in some propaganda.
- By 2016, the Islamic State publicly recognized a leadership change within its West Africa affiliate, contributing to a split between Shekau’s loyalists and the ISWAP faction.
- The split was marked by internal conflict and differing approaches to targeting and governance, as described in open-source reporting.

## Death (2021)

- In May 2021, ISWAP forces reportedly overran one of Shekau’s strongholds in the Sambisa Forest area.
- Multiple reports state that Shekau detonated an explosive device, killing himself rather than being captured.
