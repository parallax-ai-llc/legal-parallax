## Summary

Former Pakistani PM Benazir Bhutto was assassinated at a campaign rally in Rawalpindi. A shooter and suicide bomber struck as she waved from her car's sunroof.

## Context

- Benazir Bhutto (1953–2007) was a former Prime Minister of Pakistan and leader of the Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP), returning from exile in 2007 to campaign ahead of national elections.
- The months before her death featured major political tension, militant violence, and repeated warnings about threats against high-profile political figures.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The attack occurred on **27 December 2007** after a political rally in **Rawalpindi**, Pakistan.
- As Bhutto left the venue and appeared from her vehicle, an assailant fired shots; a blast followed moments later.
- Bhutto died shortly after the attack. Many others in the vicinity were injured or killed.

## Immediate Aftermath

- The assassination triggered widespread unrest and mourning across Pakistan, including protests and rioting in multiple cities.
- National election plans were disrupted; the election was subsequently postponed.
- Pakistan’s security posture and campaign event practices came under intense scrutiny.

## Investigations and Public Dispute

- Competing accounts circulated about the precise mechanism of death and the adequacy of security arrangements.
- Pakistani authorities and international observers discussed responsibility, including the role of militant networks, while political actors debated institutional accountability.
- The case remained politically sensitive, with investigations and court proceedings discussed for years afterward.

## Historical Significance

- Bhutto’s assassination is widely treated as a pivotal event in Pakistan’s modern political history.
- It reshaped party leadership dynamics within the PPP and influenced the 2008 electoral landscape.
