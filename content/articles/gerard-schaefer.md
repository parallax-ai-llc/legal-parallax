## Summary
Gerard Schaefer was a Florida deputy sheriff who murdered at least two women and was suspected of killing up to 30. He abducted women while on duty. Murdered in prison in 1995.

## Background
- Born in Wisconsin and later lived in Florida.
- Worked in law enforcement in Martin County, Florida.
- Became the subject of major controversy because his position gave him access to potential victims and helped him evade suspicion for a period of time.[^2]

## Known Victims (Confirmed)
High-level summaries commonly list at least two confirmed murder victims.
- **Roxanne Hayes (teenager)** — abducted and murdered in Florida; remains recovered and connected to Schaefer through investigation and prosecution reporting.[^2]
- **Virginia "Ginny" Carpenter (teenager)** — abducted and murdered in Florida; remains recovered and connected to Schaefer through investigation and prosecution reporting.[^2]

## Modus Operandi (As Reported)
- Targeted young women and girls, often when he was in a law-enforcement role or presenting himself as an authority figure.
- Abductions and restraints were a recurring element reported in the case.
- Later investigative accounts describe him as maintaining trophies/notes and producing violent writings in prison, which added to suspicion about additional crimes.[^2][^3]

## Investigation and Conviction (Overview)
- The 1972 incident involving two bound missing girls led to his arrest and was a pivotal turning point in the case.[^2]
- Subsequent searches and follow-up investigations connected him to at least two murders.
- Estimates of “up to 30” victims appear in some secondary accounts; these are not uniformly accepted as confirmed counts and are best treated as investigative suspicion rather than a proven total.[^2]

## Imprisonment and Death
- Schaefer served decades in Florida prisons.
- In 1995, he was killed by another inmate; sources generally report the killing occurred at a Florida state prison and was connected to inmate violence and prison dynamics.[^2]

## Notes
- Because the case involves alleged additional victims, different sources may describe different suspected totals; confirmed convictions are fewer than the highest estimates.
- When summarizing, separate **confirmed murders/convictions** from **suspected/attributed** cases to avoid overstating certainty.
