## Summary

Two brothers attacked the Charlie Hebdo magazine office in Paris killing 12, including the editor and cartoonists who had published caricatures of the Prophet Muhammad.

## Background and Context

- **Location**: Paris, France.
- **Target**: The offices of the satirical newspaper *Charlie Hebdo*.
- **Motivation (widely reported)**: Retaliation for *Charlie Hebdo*'s publication of caricatures of the Prophet Muhammad.
- **Related events**: The attack is commonly discussed as part of a broader series of jihadist-related attacks in France in the mid-2010s.

## Key Facts

- The attack occurred on **7 January 2015**.
- **12 people were killed** at the *Charlie Hebdo* office, including members of the editorial staff.
- The incident led to a major security response and a large-scale manhunt.

## Immediate Aftermath

- The event prompted widespread public demonstrations and vigils in France and internationally.
- The slogan **"Je suis Charlie"** became a prominent symbol of solidarity and debates around free expression.
- French authorities raised national security alert levels and increased protections for potential targets.

## Broader Impact

- The attack intensified public discussion about:
  - freedom of speech vs. hate speech and religious offense,
  - secularism (*laïcité*) and integration in France,
  - counterterrorism policy and surveillance.
