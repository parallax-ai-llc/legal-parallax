## Summary

A fishing trawler carrying up to 750 migrants sank off the Greek coast killing approximately 600 people. The Greek coast guard was accused of failing to rescue the vessel.

## Background

- The incident occurred on the central Mediterranean migration route, where overcrowded boats attempt to reach the European Union from North Africa.
- The vessel was reported to be a fishing trawler that had departed from Libya and was carrying hundreds of people from multiple countries.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- On 14 June 2023, the trawler sank in international waters southwest of Pylos, Greece, within the Greek search-and-rescue region.
- Reports described a long period of distress and contact with authorities prior to the sinking, followed by a rapid capsize and submersion.
- Survivors and reporting later raised allegations about interactions between the boat and the Hellenic Coast Guard, including whether towing or maneuvers contributed to the capsize; these points have been disputed and investigated.

## Casualties and Survivors

- The exact number of people onboard remains uncertain; widely cited estimates ranged up to roughly 750.
- Confirmed survivors numbered just over 100 in early reporting.
- The death toll is often described as one of the deadliest known Mediterranean migrant shipwrecks in recent years, with hundreds missing or presumed dead.

## Aftermath

- Greek authorities and international organizations faced renewed scrutiny over search-and-rescue practices, the responsibilities of coastal states, and deterrence policies.
- The shipwreck prompted calls for accountability, independent investigation, and improved rescue capacity on major migration routes.

## Key Takeaways

- **Uncertainty in totals:** Because passenger manifests are typically absent, casualty figures rely on survivor testimony, phone records, and investigative reconstructions.
- **Policy context:** The event became a focal point in debates over EU border enforcement versus humanitarian rescue obligations.
