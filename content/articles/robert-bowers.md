## Summary
Robert Bowers killed 11 worshippers at the Tree of Life synagogue in Pittsburgh on October 27, 2018. It was the deadliest attack on Jewish people in US history. He was sentenced to death.

## Background
- Bowers is a U.S. citizen born in 1972.
- He became known publicly after the 2018 mass shooting at the Tree of Life synagogue in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.

## Ideology and Online Activity (Reported)
- Investigators and prosecutors described Bowers as motivated by antisemitic beliefs.
- Public reporting and court filings have described his use of social media to post antisemitic content prior to the attack.

## 2018 Tree of Life Synagogue Shooting
- **Date:** October 27, 2018
- **Location:** Tree of Life ("Or L'Simcha") synagogue, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
- **Outcome:** 11 people were killed; multiple others were injured.
- The event has been widely described as the deadliest antisemitic attack in U.S. history.

## Investigation, Charges, and Case Posture
- Bowers was arrested at the scene after an armed confrontation with law enforcement.
- He faced federal charges, including hate-crime-related counts, in addition to state-level proceedings reported in the media.

## Trial and Sentencing
- A federal jury convicted Bowers.
- He was sentenced to **death** in federal court (2023).
- As of the most recent updates in public sources, he has remained on death row while post-trial motions and appeals proceed.

## Impact and Significance
- The attack intensified U.S. public discussion around antisemitism, domestic extremism, and security for religious institutions.
- The case is frequently cited in discussions of federal hate-crime enforcement and mass-violence prevention.
