## Summary

Chen Shih-hsin won Taiwan's first-ever Olympic gold medal in taekwondo at the 2004 Athens Olympics.

## Key Details

- **Also known as**: Competed internationally under **Chinese Taipei**.
- **Olympic event**: Women’s **49 kg** (flyweight) taekwondo at the **2004 Summer Olympics (Athens)**.[^1]
- **Olympic date (gold medal match)**: **26 August 2004** (per Wikipedia).[^1]

## Why She Matters

- Chen is widely cited as **Taiwan’s first Olympic gold medalist**, making her a landmark figure in Taiwanese sporting history.[^1]
- Her victory helped elevate the public profile of **taekwondo** in Taiwan and is often referenced in discussions of Taiwan’s Olympic milestones.[^2]

## Competition Highlights (High-Level)

- **2004 Athens Olympics**: Won **gold** in women’s 49 kg taekwondo, securing Taiwan’s first Olympic gold.[^1][^2]
- **Regional & international circuit**: Participated in major competitions including the **Asian Games**, **East Asian Games**, and **World Taekwondo Championships** (as listed by Wikipedia).[^1]

## Recognition and Later Career

- **Ten Outstanding Young Persons (Taiwan)**: Recognized in **2001** (per Wikipedia).[^1]
- **Academic role**: Listed as an **associate professor** at the **University of Taipei** (per Wikipedia).[^1]

## Background Notes

- Wikipedia notes Chen has **Taiwanese Indigenous (Atayal) heritage** through her mother.[^1]
- **Birth data discrepancy**: This article’s frontmatter includes one birthdate; Wikipedia lists **16 November 1978** and **Taipei, Taiwan**.[^1]
