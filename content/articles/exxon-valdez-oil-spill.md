## Summary

Exxon Valdez oil tanker ran aground in Alaska's Prince William Sound, spilling 11 million gallons of crude oil. Captain was drunk; the spill devastated 1,300 miles of coastline.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

## What Happened

- The tanker was transporting Alaska North Slope crude when it left the shipping lane and struck Bligh Reef.
- Oil spread through Prince William Sound and into the Gulf of Alaska, impacting shorelines, wildlife habitat, and fisheries.

## Contributing Factors (High-Level)

- Navigation and watchstanding failures on the bridge (including human error).
- Fatigue and crew management issues often cited in later analyses of the incident.
- Limitations of spill containment in rough, remote conditions and delays in initial response.

## Environmental & Community Impact

- Widespread oiling of shorelines and intertidal zones; persistent residues remained in some protected beaches for years.
- Large wildlife losses were documented in the immediate aftermath (notably seabirds and marine mammals), alongside long-term ecosystem effects that were studied for decades.
- Subsistence and commercial fishing communities faced closures, lost income, and prolonged social disruption.

## Response & Cleanup (Overview)

- Mechanical recovery (skimmers/booms), shoreline washing, and labor-intensive manual removal were used.
- Chemical dispersants were considered and used in limited ways early on, amid concerns about effectiveness and environmental tradeoffs.
- Cleanup operations became one of the largest in U.S. history at the time, with significant logistical constraints.

## Legal & Policy Aftermath

- The spill became a major driver for OPA 90, including:
  - Stronger requirements for spill response plans and equipment readiness.
  - Expanded liability and compensation mechanisms for damages.
  - Phased moves toward double-hulled tanker requirements for certain operations in U.S. waters.

## Notes on Numbers

- Commonly cited spill volume is ~10.8–11 million US gallons; reported figures can vary by source and methodology.
