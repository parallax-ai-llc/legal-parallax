## Summary

Honduran indigenous and environmental activist murdered after opposing a hydroelectric dam on sacred Lenca land. Despite winning the Goldman Environmental Prize, she was assassinated by hitmen.

## Context and Background

- **Who she was:** Berta Cáceres was a prominent Lenca Indigenous leader and co-founder of COPINH (Civic Council of Popular and Indigenous Organizations of Honduras), known internationally for defending Indigenous land rights and the environment.
- **Project at the center of the dispute:** The **Agua Zarca** hydroelectric dam project on the **Gualcarque River** became a focal point of conflict. Cáceres and COPINH argued the project affected Lenca territory and sacred sites and raised concerns about consultation and consent.
- **Risk environment:** Honduras has been widely reported as a high-risk context for land, Indigenous, and environmental defenders, with recurrent threats and violence linked to resource conflicts.

## The Assassination (What Happened)

- Cáceres was killed by gunfire at her home in **La Esperanza** on **2 March 2016**.
- The killing quickly became an emblematic case in discussions about:
  - the safety of human rights and environmental defenders,
  - impunity and accountability in politically sensitive crimes,
  - corporate and state responsibilities in projects affecting Indigenous communities.

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- **Arrests and prosecutions:** Authorities arrested multiple suspects and pursued prosecutions that, over time, described an organized plot involving shooters and planners.
- **Court findings:** Honduran court decisions and subsequent reporting described coordinated roles (e.g., executors, intermediaries, and alleged organizers) rather than a single spontaneous act.
- **Broader accountability debates:** The case spurred sustained calls—domestically and internationally—for investigations that extend beyond the triggermen to include those who planned, financed, or benefited from the crime.

## Why the Case Matters

- **Signal case for environmental defense:** The assassination is frequently cited as a turning point in global awareness of violence against environmental and Indigenous rights defenders.
- **Corporate due diligence:** It is used in discussions of how investors and project developers should assess human-rights impacts, community consent, and security arrangements.
- **Legacy:** Cáceres’s work and death remain closely associated with ongoing advocacy for Indigenous rights, environmental protection, and justice in Honduras.

## Key Entities (Commonly Mentioned)

- **COPINH** — the organization Cáceres co-founded and represented.
- **Lenca communities** — Indigenous communities connected to the affected territory and river.
- **Agua Zarca / DESA** — the dam project and the company commonly referenced in reporting around the dispute.
