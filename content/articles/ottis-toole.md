## Summary
Ottis Toole was the criminal partner of Henry Lee Lucas. He confessed to the 1981 murder of Adam Walsh (son of America's Most Wanted host John Walsh). He died in prison of cirrhosis.

## Background
- Often described in records and reporting as having a chaotic childhood and long history of institutionalization.
- Frequently moved and lived transiently, which complicated investigations and record-keeping.

## Association with Henry Lee Lucas
- Toole and Lucas were widely reported as close criminal associates.
- Law-enforcement scrutiny intensified as Lucas produced extensive confessions; many were later questioned due to inconsistencies.

## Adam Walsh Confession (Controversy)
- Toole gave statements claiming responsibility for Adam Walsh’s 1981 abduction and murder.
- Investigators and later reviews noted contradictions and evidentiary gaps; the confession is often discussed as unreliable.
- The case influenced public awareness of missing children and helped shape later media and policing practices.

## Notability
- Known primarily for the Lucas association, high-profile confessions, and the broader debate over confession reliability.
- Cited in discussions of investigative pressure, false confessions, and the need for corroboration.
