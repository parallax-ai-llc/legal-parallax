## High-level overview (Jack Welch)

- **General Electric (GE):** Welch joined GE in 1960 and rose through management.
- **CEO tenure:** He served as **CEO of GE (1981–2001)** and later as chairman.
- **Reputation:** Nicknamed “Neutron Jack” early in his tenure due to aggressive restructuring and layoffs.

## Management ideas associated with Welch

- **Differentiation / forced ranking:** Popularized a performance-management approach that emphasized ranking employees and rewarding top performers.
- **“Fix, sell, or close”** (portfolio discipline): A strategy of exiting or restructuring underperforming businesses.
- **Operational excellence:** Welch’s GE is often discussed in relation to quality programs (including broad corporate interest in Six Sigma-era methods).

## Legacy and criticism (high level)

- Supporters credit Welch with improving GE’s competitiveness and shareholder returns during his era.
- Critics argue some practices (especially forced ranking and short-term financial targets) were widely copied and later questioned for cultural and long-run effects.
