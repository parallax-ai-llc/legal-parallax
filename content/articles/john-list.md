## Summary
John List murdered his mother, wife, and three children in their New Jersey mansion in 1971, then disappeared for 18 years. He was featured on America's Most Wanted and captured in 1989.

## Background
- List was raised in a Lutheran household and was known as orderly and private.
- He built a conventional résumé (military service and office work) that later contrasted with the crimes.

## 1971 Murders
- Victims: his mother, wife, and three children.
- Location: the family home in Westfield, New Jersey.
- Reports describe the killings as planned rather than impulsive.
- He remained missing after the deaths were discovered, triggering a large manhunt.

## Motives Cited in Accounts
- Financial pressure and fear of public humiliation are frequently cited in summaries of the case.
- List’s own writings and later reporting often emphasize a distorted religious rationale, including the idea of “saving” his family.

## Fugitive Years (1971–1989)
- List used a new identity and lived quietly for years while avoiding detection.
- He was able to blend in by taking ordinary jobs and maintaining low visibility.
- The case resurfaced nationally when it was profiled on television, including on *America’s Most Wanted*.

## Capture and Legal Outcome
- Following identification and arrest in 1989, List was extradited to New Jersey.
- He was tried, convicted of multiple counts of murder, and sentenced to life imprisonment.

## Why the Case Endures
- The long gap between the murders and capture is often highlighted as an example of how fugitives can evade detection through anonymity.
- The case is frequently referenced in discussions of family annihilators, financial stress, and the misuse of religious language to justify violence.
