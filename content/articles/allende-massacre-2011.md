## Summary

Los Zetas cartel massacred an estimated 300 people in the town of Allende, Coahuila, as revenge for a member's cooperation with the DEA. The Mexican government covered up the massacre.

## Background (High-Level)

- **Los Zetas** were among Mexico’s most violent organized-crime groups in the 2000s–2010s, known for territorial control through intimidation.
- **Coahuila** (a northern Mexican state bordering Texas) became a strategic corridor for trafficking routes and cartel competition.
- The episode is frequently discussed as a case study in how **mass disappearances** can occur during periods of high cartel influence and weak local accountability.

## What Happened (Overview)

- The attacks were widely reported as **retaliation** after cartel leaders believed local associates had cooperated with U.S. authorities.
- Armed groups reportedly:
  - targeted specific families and alleged collaborators,
  - carried out killings and abductions,
  - destroyed property (including homes/ranches) to erase traces and intimidate the community.
- Estimates of victims vary across sources; many victims are described as **disappeared** rather than formally identified.

## Aftermath and Impact

- Residents reported long-lasting effects including:
  - community displacement,
  - fear of reporting crimes,
  - unresolved missing-person cases.
- The incident is often cited in broader debates about:
  - Mexico’s missing-person crisis,
  - the capacity of municipal/state institutions to respond to organized crime,
  - transparency in casualty counts and investigative outcomes.

## Accountability and Investigations (General)

- Investigations have been described as **slow** and **fragmented**, with periodic announcements of arrests over subsequent years.
- Reporting has frequently noted gaps between:
  - local testimony and forensic recovery,
  - official statements and independent estimates of victim counts.

## Key Facts (Quick Reference)

- **Location:** Allende, Coahuila, Mexico
- **Timeframe:** March 2011 (commonly cited)
- **Perpetrator (widely reported):** Los Zetas
- **Nature:** Mass killing + mass disappearance + property destruction
- **Victim estimates:** Often reported in the **hundreds**, but exact totals vary by source and methodology
