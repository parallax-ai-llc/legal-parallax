## Summary

Jürgen Mossack is a German- and Panamanian-trained lawyer best known as the co-founder of Mossack Fonseca, the Panamanian law firm tied to the 2016 Panama Papers revelations. The leak catalyzed global investigations into offshore company formation and the use of shell companies for illicit finance, tax evasion, and secrecy.

## Offshore Services Context

Mossack Fonseca operated as an international provider of corporate services, helping clients incorporate entities across multiple jurisdictions. In general terms, offshore entities can be used for legitimate purposes (e.g., cross-border investment structuring), but secrecy and weak oversight can also enable:

- Concealment of beneficial ownership
- Tax evasion or aggressive tax avoidance
- Money laundering and sanctions evasion
- Fraud and bribery schemes

## Panama Papers (2016)

The Panama Papers were a large leak of internal documents from Mossack Fonseca that brought unprecedented visibility into the offshore finance ecosystem. Reporting on the leak described how the firm created and managed a very large number of shell companies worldwide, some later linked by investigators to wrongdoing.

## Legal and Public Aftermath

Public information sources describe a sequence of raids, arrests, and ongoing proceedings following the leak. The firm later announced its closure, and further court actions continued in subsequent years.

## Notes

- When writing about offshore finance, it is important to distinguish between *legal incorporation services* and *criminal misuse* by end users. Investigations commonly focus on intent, due diligence, and beneficial-ownership transparency.
