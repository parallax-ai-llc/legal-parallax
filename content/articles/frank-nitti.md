## Summary
Frank 'The Enforcer' Nitti was Al Capone's top enforcer and succeeded him as the Chicago Outfit's front boss. Facing a prison sentence, he shot himself near railroad tracks. He was heavily fictionalized in The Untouchables.

## Background
- **Origins**: Nitti was born in Italy and later immigrated to the United States, settling in Chicago.
- **Nickname**: He was often called **“The Enforcer,”** reflecting a reputation—both historical and popularized—for handling violence and discipline within the organization.

## Role in the Chicago Outfit (High Level)
- **Capone lieutenant**: Nitti is commonly described as one of Capone’s trusted lieutenants, involved in keeping the organization operating smoothly.
- **Operational focus**: Accounts typically portray him as a manager of day-to-day enforcement and coordination rather than a public celebrity in the way Capone became.
- **Front boss concept**: After Capone’s fall, the Outfit is often characterized as using a more insulated leadership structure; Nitti is frequently identified as a figurehead leader in that period.

## Crime Portfolio (Commonly Attributed)
These are the major categories of activity widely associated with the Outfit during Nitti’s rise and leadership era:
- Alcohol smuggling and distribution during Prohibition
- Gambling operations
- Extortion and “protection” rackets
- Labor and business influence operations (often discussed in connection with unions and strike leverage)

## Legal Pressure and Death
- **Law-enforcement environment**: By the late 1930s and early 1940s, federal investigations into racketeering, corruption, and interstate criminal enterprises intensified.
- **End of life**: Nitti’s death is widely reported as suicide near railroad tracks, occurring amid serious legal risk and the prospect of incarceration.

## Cultural Depictions
- Nitti appears in multiple books, documentaries, and dramatizations of the Capone era.
- Popular portrayals sometimes exaggerate or simplify Outfit leadership dynamics for narrative clarity.
- **The Untouchables** is a prominent example where characters and timelines are dramatized.

## Quick Facts
- **Associated organization**: Chicago Outfit
- **Era**: Prohibition and its aftermath
- **Public profile**: Less flamboyant than Capone, often portrayed as an organizational operator
