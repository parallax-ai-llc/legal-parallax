## Summary

See Abubakar Shekau. Boko Haram kidnapped 276 schoolgirls from Chibok in 2014.

## What happened (April 2014)

- **Location:** Government Girls Secondary School in **Chibok**, Borno State, northeastern Nigeria.
- **Date:** The abduction occurred overnight on **14–15 April 2014**.
- **Victims:** Approximately **276** female students were taken.
- **Group responsible:** **Boko Haram**, an Islamist insurgent group active in the region.

## Perpetrators and leadership context

- The kidnapping was widely attributed to Boko Haram under the leadership of **Abubakar Shekau** at the time.
- Boko Haram released propaganda videos referencing the abducted girls and issued demands in connection with prisoner exchange claims.

## Immediate aftermath

- The event drew intense domestic and international attention and became a symbol of the broader Boko Haram insurgency.
- Nigerian authorities faced criticism over **security conditions** in the region and the speed/effectiveness of the initial response.

## Public and international response

- The global social-media campaign **#BringBackOurGirls** amplified attention to the kidnapping and pressured officials to prioritize recovery efforts.
- Multiple countries offered assistance (including intelligence support, advisory teams, and surveillance capabilities) as the crisis became internationally salient.

## Releases and longer-term outcomes

- Some girls **escaped** in the days following the abduction.
- Over subsequent years, additional groups of abducted girls were **released or recovered** through a combination of rescue efforts and negotiations (often reported as involving intermediaries such as humanitarian organizations).
- Despite releases, reporting over the years has indicated that **some victims remained missing**, and the case continued to have political and humanitarian implications.

## Legacy

- The kidnapping influenced discussions about **school safety**, **civilian protection**, and counterinsurgency strategy in Nigeria.
- It remains one of the most widely cited Boko Haram attacks due to its scale, its targeting of students, and its long-running impact on families and communities.
