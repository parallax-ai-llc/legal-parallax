## Summary

Korean-American missionary detained in North Korea for two years.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2012 | Detained in North Korea while leading tourism/business activities connected to missionary work | 
| 2013 | Reported sentencing to 15 years of hard labor following a North Korean trial | 
| 2014 | Released after roughly two years in North Korea, following high-level U.S. diplomatic engagement |

## Detention in North Korea

- **Detention and charges:** Bae (Korean name: 배준호) was detained in North Korea in late 2012 and later tried and sentenced under North Korean law.[^1]
- **Sentence:** Public reporting summarized on Wikipedia describes a sentence of **15 years of hard labor**.[^1]
- **Health and conditions:** During detention, family and supporters raised concerns about his health and the conditions of imprisonment, which became part of the public discussion around his case.[^1]

## Release

- **Diplomatic context:** His release in 2014 was widely interpreted as the result of diplomatic engagement between the United States and North Korea, including a high-profile visit by a senior U.S. official to secure his return.[^1]
- **Aftermath:** After returning to the United States, Bae spoke publicly about his experience and recovery, and his case remained a frequently cited example in discussions of detainees in North Korea.[^1]

## Why He Is Notable

- **U.S.–North Korea relations:** The case is often referenced as an example of how detainee issues intersect with broader bilateral tensions and periodic diplomatic channels.
- **Travel risk signaling:** It is commonly cited in travel-risk narratives about the legal exposure of foreign nationals—especially those associated with religious activity—in North Korea.

## Additional Notes

- **Name variants:** Kenneth Bae is also widely referred to by his Korean name **Bae Jun-ho (배준호)** in English-language reporting.[^1]
- **Type of case:** He was a civilian foreign national detained and sentenced in North Korea, a category of cases that periodically becomes a focus of international diplomacy.[^1]
