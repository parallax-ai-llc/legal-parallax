## Summary
Peter Madsen was a Danish inventor who murdered Swedish journalist Kim Wall aboard his homemade submarine UC3 Nautilus in August 2017. He dismembered her body. Sentenced to life.

## Expanded Context
### Background
- Peter Madsen is a Danish inventor and entrepreneur associated with amateur/independent aerospace and submarine projects in Denmark.
- Before the 2017 homicide case, he had public visibility in Denmark for engineering hobbyist efforts and for participating in technology-related communities.

### 2017 UC3 Nautilus homicide (high-level)
- In August 2017, Swedish journalist **Kim Wall** boarded Madsen’s privately built submarine **UC3 Nautilus** for an interview/reporting trip.
- Wall did not return; the case quickly escalated into a major missing-person investigation.
- Authorities later recovered evidence indicating Wall was killed aboard the submarine and her body was dismembered and disposed of in the water.

### Investigation and legal outcome
- Danish police and prosecutors led the investigation, which included forensic recovery efforts and extensive public attention.
- In 2018, a Danish court convicted Madsen of murder and related offenses and sentenced him to **life imprisonment** (Denmark’s most severe sentence, generally indeterminate).
- The case remains one of Denmark’s most internationally reported criminal investigations of the 2010s.

### Notable subsequent events
- Madsen drew additional attention from the Danish prison system and media due to later incidents while incarcerated (including an attempted escape reported by Danish authorities).

- The murder of Kim Wall prompted widespread discussion about personal safety, journalism fieldwork risk, and the responsibilities of hosts/interview subjects.
- The case has been covered in documentaries, long-form journalism, and true-crime reporting internationally.
