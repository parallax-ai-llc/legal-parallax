## Summary

Hamas attacked Israel killing 1,200 people and taking 250 hostages. Israel's military response has killed over 40,000 Palestinians in Gaza, with the ICJ finding plausible genocide.

## Background and Context

- The war is rooted in the long-running Israeli–Palestinian conflict, including competing national movements and disputed governance and security arrangements in Israel/Palestine.
- Gaza has been governed by Hamas since 2007 following Palestinian political conflict and Hamas' takeover of the territory.
- Prior to October 2023, Gaza was subject to varying degrees of blockade and repeated rounds of Israel–Hamas fighting (notably 2008–09, 2012, 2014, 2021).

## Key Developments (high-level)

- **2023-10-07:** Hamas-led attack on southern Israel, including mass casualty attacks and hostage-taking.
- **October 2023 onward:** Israel begins large-scale military operations against Hamas in Gaza, including airstrikes and ground operations.
- **Hostage and detainee dynamics:** Negotiations and intermittent arrangements have included hostage releases and prisoner exchanges.
- **Regional spillover:** Tensions and clashes have periodically escalated along Israel's northern border and in other arenas in the region.

## Humanitarian and Infrastructure Impact

- Large-scale displacement occurred within Gaza, with many civilians moving multiple times as front lines and evacuation orders changed.
- Damage to housing, healthcare facilities, water and sanitation systems, and other critical infrastructure contributed to severe humanitarian conditions.
- Aid access has been constrained and politically contested; delivery levels have fluctuated over time based on border access, security conditions, and operational capacity.

## International Response and Diplomacy

- The war prompted sustained diplomatic activity involving the United States, Egypt, Qatar, the United Nations, and other states.
- International debates have focused on:
  - the legality and proportionality of military actions,
  - civilian protection and humanitarian access,
  - long-term governance and security arrangements for Gaza,
  - prospects for a broader Israeli–Palestinian political settlement.

## Legal Proceedings and Investigations

- The conflict has been the subject of international legal attention, including cases and proceedings involving the **International Court of Justice (ICJ)** and investigations by the **International Criminal Court (ICC)**.
- Multiple human rights organizations and UN-related bodies have documented allegations of violations of international humanitarian law by different parties.

## Legacy and Ongoing Relevance

- The war has had major effects on Israeli and Palestinian politics, regional security dynamics, and global public opinion.
- Longer-term questions commonly discussed include post-war governance in Gaza, reconstruction and funding, security guarantees, and the status of hostages and detainees.
