## Summary

Albanian drug lord who ran one of Europe's largest cannabis and cocaine trafficking networks.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2016 | Greek police issued an arrest warrant while Balili held a public post in Sarandë (Director of Road Transportation).[^2] |
| 2016–2018 | Reported to have remained at large; Albanian police did not arrest him at the time of the Greek warrant.[^2] |
| 2018 | Albanian authorities changed the law to prohibit extraditions (context cited in coverage of Balili’s case).[^2] |

## Background and Public Role

Balili is an Albanian figure widely described in regional media as an international drug trafficker, sometimes labeled “the Balkans’ Escobar”.[^2]

According to reporting, he worked for the Albanian government in Sarandë as **Director of Road Transportation** around the time a Greek arrest warrant was issued in 2016.[^2]

## Criminal Allegations and Charges (as Reported)

Coverage of his Albanian case has referenced the following charges:[^2]

- Drug trafficking
- Membership in a criminal group
- Lack of justification for assets
- Money laundering

## Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- Prosecutors reportedly sought a longer sentence (18 years) than the final term described in later reporting.[^2]
- Reporting states the first-instance sentence was reduced due to an **abbreviated trial** request, resulting in a lower final sentence.[^2]

## Extradition Context

Reporting notes that Balili was tried in Albania despite being wanted by Greek police, in a context where the two countries reportedly lacked an extradition agreement and Albanian law changes in 2018 were described as limiting extraditions.[^2]
