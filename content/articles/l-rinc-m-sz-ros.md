## Summary

Hungarian oligarch and childhood friend of Orbán who became one of Hungary's richest through state contracts.

## Background and Early Work

- Grew up in Felcsút and is widely reported to have been a childhood friend of Viktor Orbán.[^1]
- Began his career as a gas-fitter in the early 1990s, before expanding into a broader set of business interests over time.[^1]

## Public Office

- Served as mayor of Felcsút from 2011 to 2018.[^1]
- Elected representing Fidesz–KDNP; local development projects in Felcsút are frequently discussed in connection with this period.[^1]

## Business Expansion (Selected)

High-level examples frequently cited in public reporting include:[^1]

- **Public procurement and construction**: his companies have been reported as major recipients of public procurement revenue after 2010.[^1]
- **Holding companies / listed interests**: associated with Opimus (later Opus Global) in reporting about media, construction, and other holdings.[^1]
- **Media assets**: reported acquisitions including outlets such as Echo TV and other publishing/media interests (later transferred to KESMA, per reporting).[^1]
- **Energy and infrastructure**: reported interests include assets such as the Mátra Power Plant, and participation in large state-backed infrastructure projects (as described in public sources).[^1]
- **Banking**: reported acquisition of a majority stake in MKB Bank together with partners (per public reporting).[^1]

## Public Perception and Controversy

- Frequently described by media as an "oligarch" and as closely tied to Hungary's governing party and Viktor Orbán.[^1]
- Public debate often focuses on the speed of his wealth accumulation and the role of state and EU-funded projects and procurements in that growth.[^1]
- In a widely quoted remark, he acknowledged the importance of his relationship with Orbán, attributing his fortune to "God, luck, and Viktor Orbán" (as reported in public sources).[^1]
