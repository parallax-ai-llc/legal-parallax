## Summary

Former PM of Antigua accused of corruption, gun trafficking, and ties to organized crime.

## Career Timeline

## Positions and Roles

- Prime Minister of Antigua and Barbuda (1994–2004)
- Deputy Prime Minister; held portfolios including economic development, tourism, energy, and external affairs (various years)
- Chairman/leader within the Antigua Labour Party (ALP) across multiple decades

## Early Life and Education

- Born in New York City; later educated in Antigua.
- Attended the University of Michigan (graduated 1962).
- Trained as a lawyer in the United Kingdom and was called to the bar at Gray's Inn (1969).

## Athletics

- Competed as a long jumper.
- Won a bronze medal in the long jump at the 1959 Pan American Games.

## Political Context and Tenure

Bird emerged as a prominent figure within the Antigua Labour Party, later becoming prime minister after his father, Vere Bird, resigned. His administration is commonly discussed in the context of:

- The ALP's long-running dominance in Antiguan politics during the late 20th century.
- Efforts to expand tourism-led development and attract foreign investment.
- Regional diplomacy through participation in Eastern Caribbean institutions.

## Controversies (High-Level)

Bird's public career included repeated allegations and reporting related to corruption and governance issues, including claims concerning tourism partnerships and high-profile scandals. Accounts of these controversies vary by source and are typically summarized as allegations rather than proven findings.
