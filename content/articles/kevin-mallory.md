## Background

- Kevin Patrick Mallory is a former U.S. intelligence officer who previously worked for the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA).
- Public reporting describes him as having later worked in private-sector roles after leaving government service.

## Espionage Case (Overview)

- U.S. authorities alleged that Mallory attempted to provide U.S. national defense information to individuals he believed were acting on behalf of the Chinese government.
- The case is commonly summarized as an example of insider risk: a former cleared professional leveraging past access and credibility rather than stealing from an active position.

## Investigation and Evidence (High-level)

- Court reporting and press coverage highlight the use of communications, travel, and document-handling evidence to support the prosecution’s theory of intent.
- Open sources describe that classified or sensitive topics were involved, leading to charges tied to national defense information.

## Legal Proceedings and Outcome

- The matter proceeded in U.S. federal court.
- In 2019, he was convicted on charges relating to espionage / attempted espionage for China (as commonly summarized in open sources).

## Significance

- The Mallory case is frequently cited in counterintelligence discussions as a reminder that former officials can remain targets for recruitment.
- It also underscores the role of post-employment monitoring and security culture in reducing opportunities for illicit disclosures.
