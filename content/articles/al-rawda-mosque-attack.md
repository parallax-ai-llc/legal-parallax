## Summary

ISIS-linked terrorists killed 311 worshippers at a Sufi mosque in northern Sinai.

## Background

- **Location:** Al-Rawda mosque near Bir al-Abed, North Sinai, Egypt.
- **Religious context:** The mosque is associated with **Sufi** practice; Sufi communities have been targeted by extremist groups in the Sinai insurgency.
- **Security context:** The attack occurred amid the long-running insurgency in the **Sinai Peninsula**, involving militant factions and Egyptian security forces.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Date:** 24 November 2017.
- **Setting:** Friday prayers.
- **Method:** Attackers used firearms and explosives; reports describe coordinated shooting and an explosion near the mosque.
- **Scale:** 311 people were killed and many others were wounded, making it one of the deadliest attacks in modern Egyptian history.

## Perpetrators and Attribution

- The incident has been widely linked to militants aligned with the **Islamic State (ISIS)** operating in Sinai.
- Public reporting has described assailants arriving in vehicles and targeting people inside and around the mosque.

## Immediate Response

- Egyptian authorities conducted security operations in North Sinai following the attack.
- The attack drew broad condemnation domestically and internationally.

## Significance and Impact

- **Mass-casualty terrorism:** The death toll highlighted the capability of insurgent networks in Sinai to conduct large, coordinated attacks.
- **Targeting of civilians:** The victims were worshippers, underscoring the use of terror tactics against non-combatants.
- **Broader conflict:** The event is frequently cited in discussions of Egypt’s counterinsurgency campaign and the regional dynamics of ISIS-linked militancy.

## Key Facts (Quick Reference)

- **Event type:** Mass shooting / bombing at a place of worship.
- **Victims:** Primarily civilians attending prayer.
- **Fatalities:** 311.
- **Country/region:** Egypt, North Sinai.
