## Summary

American Revolutionary War general whose name became synonymous with treason after defecting to the British.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1780 | Defected to British Army |

## Background

- Born in Norwich, Connecticut, in 1741.
- Worked in commerce before the Revolutionary War and became involved in colonial resistance politics.

## Role in the American Revolutionary War

Arnold began the war as an energetic Patriot officer and was involved in several early campaigns. He gained a reputation for boldness and personal bravery, but also developed long-running disputes over rank, credit for victories, and finances.

Key wartime episodes often associated with Arnold include:

- The 1775 effort to seize Fort Ticonderoga and subsequent operations in the Lake Champlain corridor.
- The 1775 expedition toward Quebec, which ended with an unsuccessful assault on Quebec City.
- Naval actions on Lake Champlain in 1776 (including the Battle of Valcour Island), which helped delay British advances.
- His role at Saratoga in 1777, where he was wounded; the Saratoga campaign is widely remembered as a turning point in the war.

## Defection and the West Point Plot

By 1780, Arnold’s relationships with other officers and political authorities were badly strained. While in command of West Point, he entered into secret negotiations with the British.

- The best-known element of the plot involved British officer John André.
- The plan was uncovered in 1780; André was captured and later executed.
- Arnold escaped to British lines and accepted a commission in the British Army.

## Later Life

After defecting, Arnold served the British in a limited military capacity and later pursued business ventures.

- He participated in British operations and raids during 1781.
- After the war he lived primarily in Britain, with periods of commercial activity connected to British North America.
- He died in London in 1801.

## Historical Significance and Legacy

- In the United States, “Benedict Arnold” became a lasting byword for treason.
- Historians frequently note the contrast between his early Patriot service and his later defection, as well as the personal, political, and financial conflicts that shaped his trajectory.
