## Summary
Kenneth Noye was a British criminal involved in the Brink's-Mat gold laundering who murdered a police officer in 1985 (acquitted) and later stabbed Stephen Cameron in a road rage incident (convicted 2000).

## Key Facts
- Associated with the proceeds of the **Brink's-Mat robbery** (1983), one of the most notorious robberies in modern UK history.
- **Shot and killed** Detective Constable **John Fordham** during a confrontation in 1985; later **acquitted of murder**.
- **Convicted** for the 1996 killing of **Stephen Cameron** in a road rage dispute; sentenced to **life imprisonment**.
- Escaped from an open prison in 2008 and was **recaptured** shortly afterward.

## Background
- Noye became publicly known through investigations into the movement and laundering of Brink's-Mat gold after the 1983 armed robbery.
- His case is frequently referenced in discussions of how organized criminals convert stolen bullion into legitimate-appearing assets.
- Media coverage and later books/podcasts have treated the Brink's-Mat network as a major episode in 1980s UK organized crime.

## Notability and Legacy
- The Brink's-Mat aftermath is often used as a case study in:
  - Criminal finance and the laundering of high-value commodities (gold).
  - The long tail of major robberies, where prosecution can occur years later.
  - The risks faced by detectives investigating organized crime networks.
- Noye's cases (Fordham and Cameron) are frequently cited for how different legal outcomes can arise from different evidentiary records and contexts.
