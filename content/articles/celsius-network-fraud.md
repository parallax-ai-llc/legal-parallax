## Summary

Crypto lending platform that froze $4.7 billion in customer deposits before declaring bankruptcy.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2017 | Celsius Network launched as a crypto lending/borrowing platform (commonly reported launch period). |
| 2022-06 | Announced a pause on withdrawals, swaps, and transfers amid market stress. |
| 2022-07 | Filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in the U.S. (Southern District of New York). |
| 2023 | Founder/CEO Alex Mashinsky was arrested and charged by U.S. authorities (DOJ; civil complaints by SEC/CFTC). |

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Business model:** Celsius marketed high-yield “earn” products to retail customers, pooling deposited crypto and deploying it into lending, trading, and other yield strategies.
- **Liquidity mismatch:** Like other yield/lending platforms, Celsius faced a classic risk: customers can demand withdrawals quickly, while deployed assets may be illiquid, locked, or exposed to market volatility.
- **Market shock (2022):** Broad crypto market declines and failures in the ecosystem contributed to stress across lenders and leveraged participants.
- **Withdrawal freeze:** The withdrawal pause effectively trapped customer assets on the platform and signaled severe liquidity/solvency issues.

## Allegations & Core Issues

Public reporting and U.S. filings commonly describe allegations that Celsius and/or executives:

- **Misrepresented risk** around how customer deposits were deployed and how safe yield products were.
- **Overstated financial health** (e.g., liquidity and the sustainability of promised yields) during periods of stress.
- **Used customer assets** in ways customers did not fully understand or consent to, depending on product terms.

(Details vary by jurisdiction and filing; the key point is that alleged deception + risk-taking intersected with a bank-run dynamic.)

## Bankruptcy & Aftermath

- **Chapter 11 process:** The bankruptcy proceedings aimed to preserve value, unwind positions, and determine how remaining assets would be distributed.
- **Creditor impact:** Customers were typically treated as unsecured creditors for many “earn” products, which can materially affect recovery outcomes.
- **Regulatory ripple effects:** The collapse became a frequently cited example in debates about crypto lending regulation, marketing disclosures, and custody/ownership of deposited crypto.

## Why It Matters

- **Retail exposure:** Celsius was widely used by non-professional investors seeking yield on crypto holdings.
- **Systemic signaling:** Along with other 2022 failures, it highlighted how leverage, rehypothecation, and opaque balance sheets can amplify contagion.
- **Disclosure lesson:** Promised yield is not free; it implies counterparty risk, strategy risk, and (often) custody/ownership complexity.
