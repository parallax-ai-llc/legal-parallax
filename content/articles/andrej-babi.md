## Summary

Czech PM and billionaire charged with EU subsidy fraud in the Stork Nest Farm case.

| Year | Event |

## Background

- Born in 1954; Slovak-born Czech businessman and politician.
- Known for building a large agribusiness and chemicals conglomerate (Agrofert) before entering politics.

## Business and Political Roles

- Founder and long-time figure associated with **Agrofert**, a major Central European holding company.
- Served as **Minister of Finance** of the Czech Republic (2014–2017).
- Served as **Prime Minister** (2017–2021).

## Political Profile (high-level)

- Built a political movement (ANO 2011) positioned as anti-corruption/managerial reform in style.
- Frequently associated with pragmatic, populist campaigning and a focus on public services and fiscal themes.

## Notable Controversies (overview)

- **Conflict-of-interest allegations** related to the relationship between political power and business holdings.
- Scrutiny tied to EU subsidies and the governance/ownership structures of companies connected to him.

## The “Stork Nest” Case (Čapí hnízdo)

- A prominent case concerning alleged misuse of EU subsidy rules for a project commonly referred to as “Stork Nest.”
- The case has been widely reported and discussed within Czech politics and EU oversight contexts.
