## Context

- **Darfur** is a region in western Sudan with a long-running pattern of conflict involving state forces, allied militias, and rebel movements.
- The 2023 Sudan conflict between the **Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF)** and the **Rapid Support Forces (RSF)** created conditions for renewed large-scale violence in Darfur.

## Parties and Victims (High-Level)

- **Alleged principal perpetrator:** the RSF and aligned militia elements (as described in major reporting and reference summaries).
- **Primary affected communities:** civilians in Darfur, with reporting frequently emphasizing **ethnically targeted attacks** against non-Arab groups.
- **Other armed actors present:** local militias and armed groups; attribution and incident-level responsibility can vary by locality and time.

## Nature of Allegations

Commonly reported categories of abuses during the Darfur violence include:

- Mass killings and attacks on civilian neighborhoods
- Forced displacement and village destruction
- Sexual violence and other forms of gender-based violence
- Looting, property destruction, and obstruction of humanitarian access

## Geographic Focus

Reporting and summaries often emphasize parts of **West Darfur** (including areas around El Geneina) as well as other Darfur states where civilian communities were attacked or displaced.

## International Attention and Response (Overview)

- The violence in Darfur has been widely cited by international media and human-rights reporting as part of a broader **humanitarian crisis** in Sudan.
- International bodies and governments have periodically discussed the situation in terms of potential **war crimes** and **crimes against humanity**; specific determinations depend on evidence, investigations, and legal processes.

## Why It Matters

- The Darfur attacks are frequently described as a major driver of displacement and humanitarian need.
- The events are also discussed in the context of historical patterns of mass violence in Darfur and accountability efforts linked to earlier phases of the conflict.
