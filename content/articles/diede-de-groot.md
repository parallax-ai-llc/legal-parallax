## Summary

Diede de Groot became the first wheelchair tennis player to complete the Golden Grand Slam, winning all four majors and Paralympic gold in 2021.

## Background

- Diede de Groot is a Dutch professional wheelchair tennis player.
- She competes internationally on the ITF wheelchair tennis circuit.
- She is widely associated with the modern era of women’s wheelchair tennis due to an extended period of dominance at the top level.

## Notable Achievements (High-Level)

- **Golden Grand Slam (2021):** Won all four wheelchair tennis major singles titles in the same calendar year and added Paralympic gold.
- **World No. 1 (singles):** Recognized for holding the top ranking in women’s wheelchair singles during her peak years.
- **Major champion:** Known for winning major wheelchair tennis titles across multiple seasons, not limited to 2021.

## What “Golden Grand Slam” Means

In wheelchair tennis, the term is used (by analogy with able-bodied tennis) to describe winning:

1. The four major tournaments in the same calendar year, and
2. Paralympic gold in the same year.

De Groot’s 2021 season is frequently cited as a benchmark for sustained excellence across the full annual schedule.

## Career Notes

- Her achievements are often discussed in terms of consistency: repeatedly converting top-seeded status into titles over long stretches.
- She is commonly referenced in media and historical summaries as a defining champion of her generation.
