## Summary
Karim Lala was a Pathan don who ran the Bombay underworld from the 1940s-80s. He controlled the opium, gambling, and extortion rackets in central Mumbai. He claimed Dawood Ibrahim worked under him early on.

## Early Life and Background
- Often described as a **Pathan (Pashtun)** underworld figure who migrated to Bombay (Mumbai) and built influence among Pathan networks in the city.
- Common biographical profiles identify him as **Abdul Karim Sher Khan**, widely known by the alias **"Karim Lala"**.

## Underworld Role (High-Level)
- Became one of the prominent crime bosses in Mumbai during the mid-20th century, with influence associated with:
  - protection rackets and extortion
  - gambling operations
  - smuggling and narcotics-linked supply chains (frequently reported in secondary accounts)
- He is frequently mentioned alongside other well-known Mumbai figures of the era (e.g., **Haji Mastan** and **Varadarajan Mudaliar**) as part of a broader ecosystem that pre-dated the later consolidation of power by newer syndicates.

## Reputation and Historical Notes
- Frequently characterized as a **community-backed strongman** with a mix of coercion and patronage, a pattern common to underworld leaders in many port cities.
- Secondary reporting and popular histories often link his name to early underworld narratives involving younger figures who later became more prominent; such claims vary by source and should be treated cautiously.
