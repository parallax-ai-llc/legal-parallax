## Summary

French serial killer couple who kidnapped, raped, and murdered young women across France and Belgium.

## Background

- Michel Fourniret (born 1942) was a French serial killer; Monique Olivier (born 1949) was his wife and accomplice.[^1]
- The pair were active primarily in France and Belgium and are associated with the abduction and murder of multiple victims, many of them young women and girls.[^1]

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)

- Accounts describe a pattern of luring or kidnapping victims, sexual violence, and murder, with Olivier sometimes assisting in approaching or controlling victims.[^1]
- In reporting and court summaries, the couple has been linked to offenses spanning multiple jurisdictions, which complicated investigation and prosecution.[^1]

## Investigation and Arrest

- Fourniret was arrested in 2003 after the attempted abduction of a teenager in Belgium; this arrest led investigators to connect him to additional crimes and victims.[^1]
- Subsequent searches and interrogations expanded the scope of the case; Olivier was later prosecuted as an accomplice for her role in facilitating the crimes.[^1]

## Trial and Sentencing

- In 2008, Fourniret was convicted of multiple murders and received a life sentence; Olivier was also convicted and sentenced for complicity.[^1]
- Later proceedings in France continued to address additional cases attributed to Fourniret, including further convictions after 2008.[^1]

## Notes

- Public sources treat “Fourniret and Olivier” as a coupled case because the crimes and legal proceedings frequently reference Olivier’s role as an enabler or accomplice rather than as a principal offender.[^1]
- Exact victim counts and case attributions may vary across sources and over time as additional investigations and court decisions occur.[^1]
