## Summary

German serial poisoner who murdered three people with E605 (parathion) pesticide in West Germany in the early 1950s. She was convicted in 1954, sentenced to life imprisonment (multiple counts), and later released after serving decades in prison.

## Background

- Born Christa Ambros in Worms, Germany (sources commonly give 24 February 1923).[^1]
- Her family history is often described as unstable; her mother spent significant time institutionalized and her father struggled with alcoholism.[^1]
- Married Karl Franz Lehmann in 1944.[^1]

## Crimes and Modus Operandi

- **Poison used:** Parathion, an organophosphate pesticide marketed in Germany as **E605**.[^1]
- **Method:** Mixing E605 into food or drink (reported examples include milk, yogurt, and chocolate pralines).[^1]
- **Notable detail:** She reportedly tested poison dosage on a dachshund before poisoning her husband.[^1]

### Known victims (as commonly summarized)

- **Karl Franz Lehmann (husband)** — died 27 September 1952 after sudden violent convulsions; initially attributed to natural causes before later forensic findings.[^1]
- **Valentin Lehmann (father-in-law)** — died 14 October 1953; initially diagnosed as a heart attack.[^1]
- **Annie Hamann** — died 15 February 1954 after ingesting a poisoned praline; accounts state the intended target was Hamann's mother, Eva Ruh, but Hamann consumed the praline first.[^1]

## Investigation and Trial

- The 1954 death of Annie Hamann drew immediate suspicion after a family dog also died shortly after eating part of the same confection.[^1]
- Forensic work in Mainz identified E605 and later led to exhumations of earlier victims (reported as March 1954), which also showed signs consistent with parathion poisoning.[^1]
- Trial began in September 1954; she was convicted of three murders and received life imprisonment (reported as multiple life sentences).[^1]
- She was released after approximately 23 years in prison (reported release year: 1977) and subsequently lived under a new identity.[^1]
