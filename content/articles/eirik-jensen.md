## Summary

Norway's most senior police officer convicted of corruption, having helped smuggle tons of hashish into Norway.

## Context

Eirik Jensen was a senior figure in the Oslo Police Department prior to his arrest. Public reporting describes him as having worked for decades in Norwegian policing and as being associated with high-profile anti-organised-crime and anti-narcotics efforts before the corruption case.

## The Cappelen–Jensen affair (high-level)

The case is commonly described as involving Jensen and drug trafficker Gjermund Cappelen, with prosecutors alleging that a police officer provided assistance and protection that benefited a hashish trafficking operation.

Key publicly reported elements include:
- **Arrest and charges (2014):** arrested and charged with gross corruption and drug trafficking.
- **Trial outcomes (2017 → 2020):** convicted in district court and later convicted again in the court of appeal; the resulting sentence reported in mainstream sources is **21 years**, the maximum determinate sentence in Norway.
- **Incarceration:** reported as serving time in **Kongsvinger Prison**.

## Why the case mattered

In Norwegian public discourse, the Jensen case has been framed as a major breach of institutional trust: allegations and convictions involving a high-ranking police official interacting with organised drug trafficking. It is frequently referenced in discussions about:
- internal controls and oversight in law enforcement,
- vulnerabilities in long-running informer/handler relationships,
- the reputational and operational cost of corruption cases inside public safety institutions.

## Media and publications

Public reporting also notes that Jensen has been the subject of documentary coverage and that he has published books (including an autobiography and a crime novel) in the period surrounding the case.
