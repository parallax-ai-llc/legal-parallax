## Background and Early Life

- Born in Hong Kong on 13 October 1996.
- Became involved in civic and student activism while still in secondary school.

## Student Activism (Scholarism)

- Co-founded the student activist group **Scholarism** in 2011.
- Scholarism was best known for opposing Hong Kong’s proposed **Moral and National Education** curriculum.
- The campaign drew large public attention and is widely reported as contributing to the curriculum being shelved in 2012.

## Umbrella Movement (2014)

- Was an active participant in the 2014 pro-democracy protests commonly known as the **Umbrella Movement**.
- The movement centered on demands for democratic reforms and broader civil liberties in Hong Kong.

## Continued Organizing (2016–2019)

- Became associated with **Demosistō**, a pro-democracy political organization founded in 2016.
- Continued public advocacy on issues related to democratic governance and rights.

## Arrests and Imprisonment (High-Level)

- Faced multiple arrests and prosecutions connected to assemblies and protest-related activities across the 2010s and 2020s.
- Court outcomes and custody status have changed over time as different cases proceeded and appeals were decided.

## International Attention

- Frequently covered by international media and cited by human-rights organizations.
- Supporters often describe his legal cases as part of a wider crackdown on Hong Kong’s pro-democracy movement.
- Hong Kong and Chinese authorities have characterized prosecutions as enforcement of public-order and related laws.

## Why He Matters (Context)

- Commonly presented as a symbol of **youth-led** pro-democracy activism in Hong Kong.
- His public profile is often used to illustrate the changing political and legal environment in Hong Kong after 2014.
