## Summary
Robert Crimo III killed 7 people at a Fourth of July parade in Highland Park, Illinois on July 4, 2022. He fired from a rooftop. He was sentenced to life without parole.

## Context and Background
- Highland Park is a North Shore suburb of Chicago that held an annual Independence Day parade.
- Investigators described the attack as a planned rooftop shooting into a public crowd.

## The 2022 Highland Park Parade Shooting (High-Level)
- **Date/Time**: July 4, 2022, shortly after the parade began.
- **Location**: Downtown Highland Park, Illinois.
- **Method**: Fired from an elevated position into the parade route.
- **Casualties**: 7 killed; many others wounded.

## Investigation and Arrest
- Law enforcement identified Crimo as a suspect and issued a public alert.
- He was taken into custody later on July 4, 2022.

## Legal Proceedings and Sentence
- Prosecutors filed multiple counts including first-degree murder and attempted murder.
- In March 2025, Crimo entered guilty pleas to all charges.
- In April 2025, he received consecutive life sentences without the possibility of parole.

## Aftermath
- The shooting prompted renewed debate about public-safety planning for mass gatherings and firearms policy in Illinois and the U.S.
- The city and victims' families pursued memorials, vigils, and support efforts in the months following the attack.
