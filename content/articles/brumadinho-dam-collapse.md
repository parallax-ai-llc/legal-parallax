## Summary

Vale mining company's tailings dam collapsed in Brumadinho, Brazil, releasing 12 million cubic meters of toxic waste and killing 270 people. Executives were charged with homicide.

## Background and Context

- The collapse occurred at the Córrego do Feijão mine near Brumadinho in the state of Minas Gerais, Brazil.
- The structure was a tailings dam, used to store mining waste (tailings) generated by iron ore extraction and processing.
- The disaster renewed global attention on tailings-dam risk, particularly for upstream-construction dams, which have been widely debated in the mining industry and among regulators.

## Collapse and Immediate Effects

- The dam failed on 25 January 2019, releasing a large mudflow of tailings.
- The flow swept through parts of Vale's facilities and nearby areas, damaging buildings and infrastructure in its path.
- Downstream waterways in the Paraopeba River basin were affected, raising concerns about water quality, aquatic ecosystems, and contamination of river sediment.

## Human Toll and Emergency Response

- The disaster resulted in 270 deaths, making it one of Brazil's deadliest industrial disasters.
- Search, rescue, and recovery operations continued for months and involved extensive deployments of firefighters, civil defense teams, and technical specialists.
- The scale of the mudflow and contamination complicated recovery work and prolonged the identification of victims.

## Investigations, Accountability, and Legal Proceedings

- Brazilian authorities opened investigations into the causes of the failure and the role of corporate decision-making and safety oversight.
- Prosecutors filed criminal charges, including homicide-related allegations, against individuals connected to Vale and to firms involved in safety assessments.
- The case became a prominent example in debates about corporate accountability for industrial disasters, including the adequacy of audits, risk disclosure, and independent verification.

## Environmental, Regulatory, and Industry Impact

- The release of tailings triggered sustained environmental monitoring and remediation discussions in affected river systems and communities.
- The event intensified regulatory scrutiny in Brazil and contributed to changes in tailings-dam policies and decommissioning plans.
- Internationally, the disaster contributed to efforts to standardize best practices, including the Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management (published in 2020).

## Legacy

- Brumadinho is frequently discussed alongside other major tailings-dam failures (including the 2015 Mariana disaster) in discussions about systemic risk in mining.
- The incident influenced investor and public pressure on mining firms to improve transparency, emergency preparedness, and independent assurance practices.
