Greek military dictator who overthrew the existing junta and whose provocations led to the Turkish invasion of Cyprus.

## Context and Background

- **Dimitrios Ioannidis (1923–2010)** was a senior Greek Army officer associated with the **Greek military dictatorship (1967–1974)**.
- He is widely described as the **de facto leader** of Greece during the final phase of the dictatorship, after removing the earlier junta leadership.

## Role in the Greek Junta

- Ioannidis rose through the officer corps and became influential within the regime’s **security and coercive apparatus**.
- In late **1973**, he was a central figure in the internal coup that displaced the existing junta leadership and installed a new governing arrangement in which he exercised decisive behind-the-scenes control.

## Cyprus and the 1974 Crisis

- In **July 1974**, the Athens-backed coup against **President Makarios** in Cyprus helped trigger the subsequent **Turkish military intervention** on the island.
- The crisis contributed directly to the **collapse of the dictatorship** and Greece’s transition back to civilian government.

## Aftermath, Trial, and Later Life

- After the restoration of democracy in Greece, Ioannidis was **arrested and tried** in connection with junta-era crimes.
- He received **multiple sentences**, including a life sentence, and remained imprisoned for decades.
- He died in **2010**.

## Legacy

- Ioannidis is commonly cited as a key actor in the dictatorship’s final and most destabilizing period.
- Historical assessments emphasize his role in decisions that precipitated the **Cyprus catastrophe** and accelerated the regime’s end.
