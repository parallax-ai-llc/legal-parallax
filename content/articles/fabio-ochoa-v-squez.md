## Key Facts

- **Full name:** Fabio Ochoa Vásquez.
- **Family:** Youngest of the three Ochoa brothers (with Jorge Luis and Juan David), a prominent family within Medellín Cartel operations.[^1]
- **Known for:** Large-scale cocaine trafficking during the cartel era; later US prosecution following extradition.[^1]

## Context (Medellín Cartel)

- The Medellín Cartel was one of the most powerful cocaine-trafficking organizations of the late 20th century, centered in Colombia.
- The Ochoa family is frequently described as part of the cartel’s leadership and logistics/trafficking network rather than a single “kingpin” role.
- Public reporting commonly frames Fabio Ochoa as a high-ranking trafficker tied to the cartel’s broader distribution and export operations.[^1]

## US Prosecution and Imprisonment

- After being brought to the United States, Fabio Ochoa Vásquez was prosecuted in US federal court on drug-trafficking charges.[^1]
- **Sentence (2003):** 30 years in US prison (as noted in the timeline above).[^1]
- His case is often cited as an example of post-cartel-era extradition and long-term sentencing for alleged cartel figures.
