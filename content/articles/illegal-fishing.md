## Context and Definition

Illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing is an umbrella term used by governments and international bodies to describe fishing activities that violate applicable laws, evade reporting requirements, or exploit gaps in management and enforcement. In practice, IUU fishing can include operating without a valid license, fishing in closed areas or seasons, exceeding quotas, using prohibited gear, falsifying catch documentation, and transshipping catch to obscure origin.

## Common Tactics

- **Flags of convenience**: Registering vessels in jurisdictions with weaker oversight to reduce scrutiny.
- **Identity manipulation**: Changing vessel names, call signs, or using spoofed AIS signals to disguise operations.
- **At-sea transshipment**: Moving catch to refrigerated cargo vessels to extend time at sea and hide supply chains.
- **Labor exploitation**: Using abusive recruitment and working conditions, sometimes linked to forced labor, to reduce costs.

## Economic and Environmental Impacts

- **Stock depletion**: Sustained illegal harvest undermines rebuilding plans and can accelerate collapse of vulnerable populations.
- **Ecosystem damage**: Destructive gear and unregulated effort can increase bycatch and harm habitats.
- **Market distortion**: Illegal product can undercut compliant fishers, weakening incentives to follow regulations.
- **Government revenue loss**: Missed license fees and taxes, plus higher enforcement costs.

## Governance and Enforcement (High-Level)

Efforts to curb IUU fishing often combine surveillance, port controls, and trade measures:

- **Monitoring and surveillance**: Patrols, satellite monitoring, and vessel tracking to detect suspicious behavior.
- **Port State Measures**: Denying port entry or services to vessels suspected of IUU activity to prevent illegal catch from entering markets.
- **Catch documentation and traceability**: Paperwork and digital systems intended to track seafood from capture to sale.
- **Sanctions and blacklists**: Penalties for operators and vessel lists maintained by some regional fisheries management organizations.

## International Policy Milestones

- **2009**: The UN Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) adopted the *Agreement on Port State Measures* (PSMA), designed to prevent IUU-caught fish from entering ports and markets.
- **2016**: The PSMA entered into force, expanding the use of port-based controls as a global enforcement tool.
