## Identity and Names

- **Commonly known as**: Duch
- **Full name**: Kaing Guek Eav (also rendered in various transliterations)
- **Associated institution**: S-21 (Tuol Sleng) Security Prison, Phnom Penh

## Historical Role (High-Level)

Duch is most widely documented for his leadership role at **S-21**, a central detention and interrogation facility of the Khmer Rouge security apparatus during Democratic Kampuchea (1975–1979). Accounts summarized in major reference sources describe S-21 as a site where detainees were imprisoned, interrogated, and subsequently sent to execution sites.[^1]

## S-21 / Tuol Sleng (Context)

- S-21 is frequently cited in historical and legal records as one of the best-documented Khmer Rouge prisons due to surviving administrative materials.
- Duch’s role is typically characterized as **administrative and supervisory** over interrogation, record-keeping, and prison operations within a state security framework.[^1]

## Legal Proceedings (ECCC)

Duch was prosecuted by the **Extraordinary Chambers in the Courts of Cambodia (ECCC)**, a hybrid tribunal established with UN support to address senior Khmer Rouge crimes.

- Charges and convictions described in public summaries include **crimes against humanity** and **war crimes** connected to S-21 operations.[^1]
- Sentencing history in ECCC summaries includes an initial conviction and a later **appeal outcome resulting in a life sentence**.[^1]
