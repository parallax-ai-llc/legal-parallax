## Summary
Carl 'Coral' Watts, the 'Sunday Morning Slasher,' killed between 14 and 100 women across Michigan and Texas. He received immunity for many murders in a plea deal later overturned.

## Career Timeline

## Notability
- Known as the **"Sunday Morning Slasher"** due to a pattern of attacks occurring on Sunday mornings in the Detroit area.
- Confessed to numerous attacks; attribution and exact victim count remain disputed in public reporting.

## Modus Operandi (Reported)
- Targeted women in residential neighborhoods and near vehicles.
- Attacks frequently involved sudden, close-range violence and attempts to conceal or minimize evidence.
- Investigators linked cases through behavioral patterns and later statements.

## Legal Proceedings
- **Michigan**: A high-profile plea agreement granted immunity for many homicides in exchange for cooperation; later appellate decisions limited or overturned aspects of that arrangement, enabling further prosecution.
- **Texas**: Prosecuted and convicted for a Houston-area murder; sentenced to death.

## Impact and Discussion
- Frequently cited in discussions of:
  - the risks and ethics of broad immunity deals in serial crime investigations
  - multi-jurisdiction linkage challenges (cases spanning cities and states)
  - the difficulty of confirming victim totals when confessions exceed corroborated evidence
