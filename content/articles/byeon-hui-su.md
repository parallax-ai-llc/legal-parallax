## Background

- Byeon Hui-su (변희수) was a South Korean soldier who became widely known after publicly transitioning while serving in the Republic of Korea Army.[^1]
- She is often cited in discussions about LGBTQ rights, transgender health care, and military policy in South Korea.[^1]

## Military Service and Transition

- Byeon served as a non-commissioned officer (NCO) in the South Korean military.[^1]
- After undergoing gender-affirming surgery, she sought to continue her service as a woman.[^1]
- Public attention focused on questions of legal gender recognition, medical fitness standards, and anti-discrimination protections.[^1]

## Discharge and Aftermath

- The military dismissed Byeon following a determination that she was not fit for service under existing regulations.[^1]
- The case drew domestic and international media coverage and became a focal point for advocacy groups and public debate.[^1]

## Death and Legacy

- Byeon was found dead in March 2021, after her dismissal from the military.[^1]
- Her death prompted renewed calls for stronger protections against discrimination and for clearer policies regarding transgender service members.[^1]
