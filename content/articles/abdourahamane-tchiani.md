## Summary

Head of Niger's presidential guard who led the 2023 coup against President Bazoum. His seizure of power was condemned internationally and led to ECOWAS sanctions.

## Background

- Abdourahamane Tchiani (also spelled **Abdourahmane** or **Omar** Tchiani in some reports) is a Nigerien military officer best known for commanding the **Presidential Guard**.
- He rose to national prominence in July 2023 after the guard detained President **Mohamed Bazoum** and announced a change of government.

## 2023 Coup and Rise to Power

- **26 July 2023**: The presidential guard detained President Bazoum in Niamey and declared the suspension of the constitution.
- A military body calling itself the **National Council for the Safeguard of the Homeland (CNSP)** announced it had taken power; Tchiani was presented publicly as the coup's leader.
- The coup triggered immediate diplomatic pressure and threats of intervention from **ECOWAS**, as well as economic and border-related measures by neighboring states.

## Domestic and International Response

- The coup was widely condemned by foreign governments and regional organizations; ECOWAS imposed sanctions and demanded the restoration of constitutional order.
- The crisis reshaped Niger's external relationships, including security and diplomatic engagements with partners that had been supporting counterterrorism operations in the Sahel.
