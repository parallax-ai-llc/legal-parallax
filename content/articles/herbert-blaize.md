## Summary

Grenadian PM who presided over the controversial post-invasion trials after the 1983 US intervention.

## Background and Early Life

- Born in Beausejour on the island of Carriacou (part of Grenada).
- Worked for many years in Aruba at the Lago Oil and Transport Company’s refinery before becoming a long-term figure in Grenadian politics.

## Political Career (Overview)

- In 1953, founded the Grenada National Party (GNP), positioning it as a principal rival to Eric Matthew Gairy’s Grenada United Labour Party (GULP).
- Elected to the legislature in 1957.
- Held ministerial responsibility for Trade and Production during his early parliamentary career.

## Chief Minister and Premier

- Served as Grenada’s first Chief Minister (1960–1961), also carrying the Finance portfolio.
- Returned as Chief Minister (1962–1967) after Gairy was dismissed, during a period when Grenada was still a British Crown Colony.
- In 1967, briefly became the first Premier when Grenada moved into associated-state status, gaining greater internal self-government.

## Opposition Leadership and 1970s Realignment

- Served as Leader of the Opposition in the House of Representatives (1974–1976).
- For the 1976 election, his center-right National Party cooperated electorally with the left-wing New Jewel Movement led by Maurice Bishop.
- The relationship between Blaize and Bishop ended by 1979, when Bishop seized power; Blaize withdrew from active politics and returned to Carriacou.

## Prime Minister (1984–1989)

- After the 1983 coups and the U.S.-led intervention, Blaize returned to front-line politics.
- In the 1984 election, multiple center-right parties merged to form the New National Party, which won 14 of 15 seats.
- Served as Prime Minister from 4 December 1984 until his death in 1989.
- Also held a wide set of cabinet portfolios simultaneously, including Home Affairs, Security, Information, Finance, Trade, Planning, Industrial Development, and responsibility for Carriacou and Petite Martinique affairs.
- His administration advocated close economic and security ties with the United States and encouraged overseas investment.

## Death and Legacy

- Died on 19 December 1989 in St. George’s, Grenada, while still in office.
- Long-serving representative for Carriacou and Petite Martinique (in parliament from 1957 until 1989).
