## Role in the Germanwings Flight 9525 investigation

Brice Robin is noted in coverage of the Germanwings Flight 9525 crash as the **government prosecutor based in Marseille** who communicated early investigative updates to the public.

Key points associated with his public role include:

- **Confirmation of recorder recovery:** He announced that the **flight data recorder** (FDR) had been found and was usable despite fire damage.[^2]
- **Comments reported from early recorder analysis:** He was quoted in summaries of the investigation describing aspects of the cockpit voice recording and the sequence of events during the flight’s final minutes (as reported by investigators and prosecutors at the time).[^2]

## Context: Germanwings Flight 9525 (high-level)

Germanwings Flight 9525 was a scheduled passenger flight from **Barcelona (Spain) to Düsseldorf (Germany)**.

- **Date:** 24 March 2015
- **Location:** French Alps (near Prads-Haute-Bléone)
- **Fatalities:** 150 (no survivors)
- **Investigations:** Led by France’s BEA with participation from German and other authorities

Public reporting of the investigation concluded the crash was **deliberately caused by the first officer**, Andreas Lubitz.[^2]
