## Additional Context

### Background

- Igor Vsevolodovich Girkin (also known by the nom de guerre **"Strelkov"**) is a Russian former Federal Security Service (FSB) officer and was a prominent militant commander in the early stages of the Russia–Ukraine conflict in 2014.
- He became internationally known for his role in the separatist movement in eastern Ukraine and for statements and interviews given during the 2014 fighting.

### Role in the Donbas War (2014)

- In 2014, Girkin emerged as a key military figure among pro-Russian separatists in **Sloviansk** and later **Donetsk**, and he was often described as helping to organize or lead armed units.
- Public reporting commonly associates him with the creation, command, or leadership of forces linked to the self-proclaimed **Donetsk People's Republic (DPR)** during that period.

### MH17 case (Malaysia Airlines Flight 17)

- Flight MH17 was shot down over eastern Ukraine on **17 July 2014**, killing all **298** people on board.
- A Dutch court convicted Girkin (in absentia) in **2022** for his role in the downing, alongside other defendants; Girkin has denied involvement.

### Legal and political status

- International investigations and sanctions regimes have listed Girkin in connection with the Donbas conflict and MH17-related proceedings.
- After 2014, he remained active in Russian nationalist circles and commentary, periodically criticizing aspects of Russia's policy and military leadership (as reported publicly).

### Names and aliases

- Common transliterations: **Igor Girkin** / **Igor Ghirkin**; patronymic: **Vsevolodovich**.
- Frequently referred to in media by the callsign **Strelkov** ("Shooter").
