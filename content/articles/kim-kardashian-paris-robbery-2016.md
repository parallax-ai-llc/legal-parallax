## Summary

Armed men dressed as police robbed Kim Kardashian at gunpoint in a Paris luxury hotel, stealing $10 million in jewelry. Five elderly men were convicted in the sophisticated heist.

## Background and Context

- The incident occurred during Paris Fashion Week in early October 2016, when Kardashian was in France for fashion-related appearances.
- High-value jewelry was reportedly brought to Paris for events and public appearances, which increased risk and visibility.
- The robbery became widely covered internationally because it involved a globally recognized celebrity and an unusually high stated value of stolen items.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The attackers were reported to have posed as police officers to gain access to the building.
- Kardashian was confronted in her room, threatened with a firearm, and restrained.
- Jewelry was taken and the perpetrators left the hotel; the event was treated as a targeted armed robbery.

## Investigation (High-Level)

- French authorities opened an investigation shortly after the incident, with emphasis on CCTV footage, witness accounts, and mobility patterns in and around the hotel.
- Media reporting commonly described the group as experienced criminals; some coverage used the nickname "grandpa robbers" to describe older suspects.
- In subsequent months, French police reported arrests connected to the case, and the investigation continued through charging and pre-trial proceedings.
