## Background

- **Name:** Sung Kook "Bill" Hwang.
- **Known for:** Founder of Archegos Capital Management (a private family office).
- **Education (reported):** Carnegie Mellon University (undergraduate) and Columbia University (MBA).[^1]
- **Early career (reported):** Worked under Julian Robertson at Tiger Management before launching his own fund.[^1]

## Tiger Asia and prior regulatory issues

- Hwang previously ran **Tiger Asia Management**, a hedge fund.
- In 2012, Tiger Asia and Hwang **pleaded guilty** to a U.S. insider-trading-related case and paid penalties; after that, Hwang shifted to operating via a family office structure.[^1]

## Archegos: strategy and collapse (high level)

- Archegos built **large, concentrated positions** in a small set of public companies.
- It used **equity derivatives** (commonly described as total return swaps) that could provide exposure with limited public disclosure compared with direct share ownership.[^1]
- In March 2021, adverse price moves and **margin calls** led brokers to unwind positions, triggering a rapid sell-off.
- The unwind produced **multi-bank losses** and became a key case study in prime-broker risk controls.[^1]

## Legal proceedings (high level)

- U.S. authorities later charged Hwang in connection with the Archegos collapse.
- A federal jury later **convicted** Hwang on fraud-related charges (per widely reported coverage summarized on Wikipedia).[^1]

## Why this matters

- The Archegos episode is frequently cited for:
  - **Opacity risk:** large synthetic exposures can be harder for markets and counterparties to see in real time.
  - **Concentration risk:** highly concentrated books can amplify volatility when forced selling begins.
  - **Counterparty coordination risk:** multiple prime brokers exiting simultaneously can accelerate a crash.
