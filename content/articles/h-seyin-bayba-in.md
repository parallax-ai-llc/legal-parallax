## Summary

Kurdish-Turkish drug lord convicted in the Netherlands of running a major European heroin network.

## Additional Context

### Background

- Wikipedia reports that Hüseyin Baybaşin was born on **25 December 1956** in **Lice, Diyarbakır Province, Turkey** and is described as a **Kurdish** drug trafficker and crime boss.[^1]
- He is commonly described as a leading figure associated with the **Baybaşin family**.[^1]

### Reported Criminal Activity (High Level)

- Sources describe his organization as being involved in **large-scale heroin trafficking in Europe**, particularly during the **1990s**.[^1]
- Wikipedia notes that Baybaşin became widely known in Turkish and European media after the **1992 “Kısmetim-1” incident**, which was linked in reporting to alleged narcotics smuggling.[^1]

### Arrest and Trial

- Wikipedia states he was arrested in the **Netherlands** on **27 March 1998** in **Lieshout** during a joint operation (reported as code-named **“Black Tulip”**).[^1]
- He was **convicted in the Netherlands** and, according to Wikipedia, was **sentenced to life imprisonment in 2002**.[^1]

### Imprisonment and Public References

- Wikipedia lists his imprisonment as continuing in the Netherlands (reported location: **Nieuw Vosseveld**).[^1]
- European press has referred to him with nicknames such as **“Europe’s Pablo Escobar”** (as reported by Wikipedia).[^1]

### Related People / Entities

- Wikipedia lists close relatives including **Abdullah Baybaşin** and **Mehmet Baybaşin** (brothers), and references the broader **Baybaşin family** topic.[^1]
