## Overview

- Hezbollah ("Party of God") is a Lebanon-based Shiite Islamist political party and armed organization founded in the early 1980s.
- It operates as both a parliamentary political actor in Lebanon and an armed group with a significant security apparatus.
- The organization is widely described as having close ties to Iran, including ideological alignment and long-term material support.

## Origins and Development

- Emerged during the Lebanese Civil War era, amid the 1982 Israeli invasion of Lebanon and the broader regional context.
- Built networks that combined armed resistance, community organizing, and social-service provision in parts of Lebanon.

## Ideology and Structure (high level)

- Commonly characterized as Shiite Islamist with an emphasis on "resistance" narratives.
- Maintains a leadership hierarchy and a parallel set of military/security units distinct from its public political representation.
- Public messaging frequently frames activities in terms of defense of Lebanon and deterrence against Israel.

## Political Role in Lebanon

- Participates in Lebanese elections and has held seats in parliament, as well as cabinet participation through allied blocs.
- Maintains alliances with other Lebanese political parties and factions; these coalitions have shifted over time.

## Armed Activity and Regional Involvement

- Historically associated with attacks and insurgent activity in Lebanon and cross-border conflict with Israel.
- In the 2010s, became notably involved in the Syrian civil war alongside forces aligned with the Syrian government.

## Designations and Legal Status

- Designated as a terrorist organization by several governments; others distinguish between its armed wing and political wing or do not list it.
- These designations affect sanctions, financial restrictions, and diplomatic engagement.
