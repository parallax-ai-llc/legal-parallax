## Summary

Donovan Bailey won the 100m gold at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics in a world record 9.84 seconds.

## Notes and Context

### Background

- Born in Manchester Parish, Jamaica; later immigrated to Canada as a child and represented Canada internationally.
- Entered elite sprinting relatively late compared to many peers, developing into a world-class 100m specialist in the early-to-mid 1990s.

### What He Is Known For

- **1996 Olympic 100m champion** and former **100m world-record holder** (9.84).
- Widely described as a sprinter with exceptional **top-end speed**; sources note his 1996 Olympic final included one of the fastest measured peak speeds at the time.
- Recognized as a landmark figure in Canadian sprinting, including being cited as the first Canadian to legally run sub-10 seconds in the 100m.

### Legacy

- A central figure in Canada’s sprinting era of the 1990s, with major medals in both the 100m and the 4×100m relay.
- Inductions into major Canadian sports halls of fame reflect both his individual achievements and his role in Canada’s Olympic relay success.
