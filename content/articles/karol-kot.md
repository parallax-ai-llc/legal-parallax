## Summary

Polish teenage serial killer known as 'the Vampire of Kraków' who murdered elderly women.

## Background

- Kot was born in 1946 and grew up in Kraków, Poland.
- Contemporary accounts describe early fascination with violence and knives, and frequent visits to hospitals to observe injuries.
- He was still a teenager during the period associated with his attacks, which drew intense public attention.

## Crimes (high-level)

- He attacked victims in public spaces in Kraków, using a knife.
- Reported targets included elderly women; at least one child was also attacked.
- The nickname "Vampire of Kraków" was used by press and public to describe the fear surrounding the stabbings.

## Investigation and Arrest

- Police investigation focused on patterns of knife attacks in the city and witness descriptions.
- Kot was identified and arrested in 1967 after investigative leads connected him to the series of assaults.

## Trial and Sentencing

- He was tried and convicted for multiple murders and attempted murders.
- A death sentence was imposed; he was executed in 1968.

## Historical Notes

- The case is frequently cited in discussions of juvenile/young offenders committing serial violence.
- Media framing and public panic in Kraków became part of the lasting narrative around the case.
