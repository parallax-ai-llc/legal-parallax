## Summary
David Koresh was the leader of the Branch Davidians cult at Waco, Texas. The 1993 ATF/FBI siege ended in a fire that killed 76 people including Koresh and 25 children.

## Early Life and Background
- Born Vernon Wayne Howell in Houston, Texas, and raised primarily by his mother.
- Took the name **David Koresh** in the late 1980s, referencing biblical figures (King David and Cyrus/Koresh).

## Rise Within the Branch Davidians
- The Branch Davidians were an offshoot movement associated with Seventh-day Adventist traditions and apocalyptic interpretation.
- Koresh joined the group in the early 1980s and became a prominent teacher within its community near Waco, Texas.
- After internal conflict and leadership disputes, he consolidated control of the community at Mount Carmel.

## Teachings and Control
- Known for intensive scriptural study, with a focus on the Book of Revelation and end-times expectations.
- Reported to have claimed unique prophetic authority and exercised strong influence over followers’ daily life.
- Multiple accounts and later investigations described allegations of coerced “spiritual marriages” and sexual abuse, including minors.

## The 1993 Waco Siege (High-Level)
- **Feb 28, 1993:** ATF attempted to execute search and arrest warrants at the Mount Carmel Center; a gunfight erupted, causing deaths on both sides.
- Following the raid, the FBI took over the standoff and initiated negotiations that lasted for weeks.
- **Apr 19, 1993:** The standoff ended after an FBI tactical operation; a fire consumed the compound.
- 76 people died in the fire, including Koresh; fatalities included many children.

## Aftermath and Public Impact
- The siege prompted prolonged debate about the use of force, negotiation strategy, and federal law-enforcement tactics.
- Congressional hearings and multiple investigations examined decision-making during the standoff.
- The event became a major reference point in discussions of religious movements, government authority, and militia/extremist narratives in the United States.
