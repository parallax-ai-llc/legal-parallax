## Background

- **Full name:** Demetrius Edward Flenory (often referred to as “Big Meech”).
- **Family / associates:** Co-founded the Black Mafia Family (BMF) with his brother Terry Lee Flenory (often referred to as “Southwest T”).
- **Origin:** Commonly associated with Detroit, Michigan, with BMF later building a major presence in Atlanta, Georgia.

## Black Mafia Family (BMF) — High-Level Overview

- BMF has been described by U.S. law enforcement as a large-scale **drug trafficking and money laundering** organization.
- During its peak years, the organization was linked to distribution networks spanning multiple U.S. states.
- BMF also operated a public-facing music and entertainment imprint/branding (“BMF Entertainment”), which contributed to its cultural visibility.

## Legal Case (Public Record Summary)

- In the mid-2000s, federal authorities prosecuted leadership figures connected to BMF.
- **2005:** The organization’s leadership was targeted in a major federal case.
- **2008:** Demetrius Flenory was sentenced in U.S. federal court to a **30-year prison term** in connection with drug trafficking and money laundering offenses.

## Cultural Impact

- The “BMF” brand became widely referenced in hip-hop culture, contributing to a long-running public fascination with the organization.
- Flenory’s story has been adapted and popularized through media portrayals, including dramatized television treatment.
