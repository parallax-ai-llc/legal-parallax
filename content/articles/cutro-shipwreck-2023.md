## Summary

Migrant boat broke apart off Cutro, Calabria, killing at least 94 people including 35 children. Italian authorities were criticized for delays in launching rescue operations.

## Context and Background

- The incident occurred on the Ionian Sea coast of Calabria, near the town of Cutro (province of Crotone), in southern Italy.[^1]
- The vessel was carrying migrants and asylum seekers attempting the sea crossing to Europe; such crossings have long been a major route into Italy via the central Mediterranean corridor.[^1]

## What Happened (High-Level)

- In late February 2023, the migrant boat encountered rough sea conditions and broke apart near shore off Cutro, leading to a mass-casualty shipwreck.[^1]
- Reported fatalities were at least 94 people, including 35 children; survivors and victims’ bodies were recovered in the days that followed.[^1]

## Immediate Response

- Rescue and recovery efforts involved Italian authorities and local responders; the incident became a focal point for scrutiny of how distress reports and sightings are handled in Italian waters.[^1]
- The event prompted public mourning and high-profile political attention, including visits to the area and statements by national leaders.[^1]

## Aftermath and Debate

- The shipwreck led to strong criticism and debate about the timeliness and adequacy of rescue operations, and about broader Italian and EU migration policies.[^1]
- It also fed into discussions about the balance between border enforcement, search-and-rescue obligations, and humanitarian protections for people attempting irregular sea crossings.[^1]
