## Summary

Romanian serial killer who murdered and mutilated four women in Bucharest in the communist era.

## Context

- Rîmaru committed a series of late-night attacks in Bucharest during 1970–1971, in what contemporary sources describe as a period of public fear and intensive police activity.[^1]
- The crimes occurred under Romania’s communist-era policing and media environment, where official information was limited and rumors circulated widely.[^1]

## Crimes (high-level)

- Victims: four women were killed; additional women were attacked and survived, according to accounts summarized by Wikipedia.[^1]
- The attacks were associated with severe violence and mutilation.[^1]
- Reports describe elements such as biting and the use of a blunt object in some assaults (as summarized in secondary reporting referenced by Wikipedia).[^1]

## Investigation and Arrest

- The case prompted a large-scale investigation and heightened patrols/stakeouts in the city.[^1]
- Rîmaru was ultimately identified and arrested; public descriptions emphasize the investigative focus on physical evidence and linking attacks by pattern.[^1]

## Trial and Execution

- He was tried and convicted for serial murder and related assaults.[^1]
- He was executed in 1971.[^1]

## Legacy and Notes

- The case remains one of Romania’s most widely cited serial murder investigations of the period.[^1]
- Later retellings often frame the episode as a landmark example of a high-profile manhunt in Bucharest.[^1]
