## Background

- John Numbi (John Numbi Tambo Banza) is a Congolese former military officer and police chief.
- He has been described as a close associate/retainer of then-president Joseph Kabila.
- He is reported to have been born in 1962 in the Haut-Lomami area (formerly part of the Katanga region).

## Roles and positions (high level)

The following items summarize major roles commonly attributed to him in public biographies:

- Senior roles in the Congolese Air Force (including commander/chief of staff in the early 2000s).
- Inspector General of the Congolese National Police (in office until January 2010).
- Inspector General of the Armed Forces of the Democratic Republic of the Congo (FARDC), reportedly from 2018 to 2020.

## Context: the Chebeya–Bazanaa case (2010)

- Floribert Chebeya Bahizire, a prominent Congolese human-rights defender, was found dead in 2010.
- His colleague Fidèle Bazana (often referenced in coverage of the case) disappeared the same year.
- Numbi has been widely reported as having been suspected/accused in connection with the events; public summaries often describe the allegation as an order or responsibility at a senior level.
- Reporting on this case frequently emphasizes that multiple security officials were implicated, investigated, or tried, and that the matter became politically sensitive.
