## Background

Horie Takafumi (堀江 貴文) is known in Japan as an early-era internet entrepreneur and high-profile corporate executive associated with the rapid expansion of the Livedoor group in the early 2000s.

## Livedoor and Business Activities

High-level themes commonly noted in overviews of his business period include:

- Aggressive growth via acquisitions and new ventures during Japan’s early web boom
- Heavy media attention around nontraditional management style and public communications
- Attempts to expand Livedoor’s influence beyond pure internet services into broader corporate and media assets

## Securities Fraud Case (Livedoor)

Public summaries of the case emphasize:

- Investigations and allegations related to accounting and disclosure practices at Livedoor
- A major criminal prosecution that became one of Japan’s best-known corporate-scandal stories of the period
- A conviction for securities-related offenses (see reference)

## Post-Conviction Public Profile

After the Livedoor case, he remained visible in Japanese public life, frequently appearing in media and online as a commentator and business figure.

## Why He Is Notable

- Became a symbol—both positive and negative—of Japan’s early 2000s “internet entrepreneur” era
- His prosecution and conviction were widely discussed in debates about corporate governance, disclosure, and enforcement
- Continued public presence kept the scandal in the news cycle for years beyond the initial events
