## Summary

Mauritanian anti-slavery activist repeatedly imprisoned for challenging Mauritania's continued practice of slavery. An estimated 43,000 people remain enslaved in the country.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2012 | Arrested for burning slavery-supportive texts |

| 2013 | One of the recipients of the United Nations Prize in the Field of Human Rights |
| 2014 | Arrested and later convicted in a case linked to anti-slavery activism; the case drew international attention from human-rights organizations |
| 2019 | Ran for President of Mauritania (anti-slavery platform featured prominently in his campaign messaging) |

## Background and Context

- Mauritania has long faced scrutiny over the persistence of hereditary slavery and slavery-like practices, particularly affecting Haratin (often described as descendants of enslaved people) and other marginalized communities.
- The country **formally abolished slavery in 1981**, later **criminalized slavery in 2007**, and **strengthened penalties and enforcement mechanisms in 2015**; activists and NGOs have argued that implementation and victim protection have lagged behind the law.

## Advocacy Focus (high-level)

- **Public campaigning:** speeches, demonstrations, and high-visibility actions aimed at challenging social and religious justifications used to defend slavery.
- **Legal and political pressure:** pushing for stronger prosecutions, support for survivors, and recognition that slavery can persist through inherited status and coercion.
- **International attention:** engaging global media and human-rights institutions to raise the cost of repression and increase scrutiny of state practices.

## Why He Matters

- His repeated arrests and prosecutions are frequently cited by observers as an example of how anti-slavery activism can be treated as a political-security issue rather than a human-rights cause.
- His prominence helped keep Mauritania’s slavery problem on the international agenda and contributed to broader debate on enforcement of anti-slavery laws.

## Related Topics

- **Haratin**: a social group in Mauritania often discussed in the context of historic slavery and contemporary discrimination.
- **Abolition in Mauritania**: legal abolition (1981) vs. criminalization and enforcement (2007 onward).
