## Summary

Hong Kong media tycoon and pro-democracy activist imprisoned under the national security law.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2024 | Convicted under national security law |

## Background

- Born in mainland China and later moved to Hong Kong as a child.
- Built a business career in manufacturing before entering publishing and mass-market media.

## Companies and Media Work

- Founder of **Giordano International** (apparel retail) and later **Next Digital** (media group).[^1]
- Best known internationally for founding the pro-democracy tabloid **Apple Daily**, which became one of Hong Kong’s highest-circulation papers prior to its closure after national security-era investigations and arrests.[^1]

## Political Advocacy

- Became a prominent public supporter of Hong Kong’s pro-democracy movement and a frequent critic of Beijing’s tightening control over the territory.[^1]
- His prominence made him a symbolic figure for debates about press freedom, civil liberties, and the reach of Hong Kong’s post-2020 national security framework.[^1]

## Legal Proceedings and Detention (High Level)

- Faced multiple arrests and charges in the years following the 2019–2020 protest period, including allegations connected to public assembly laws and national security-related accusations.[^1]
- Remained one of the highest-profile detainees tied to national security-era prosecutions; specific trial scheduling and outcomes have changed over time and should be checked against current reporting.[^1]

## Significance

- Often cited as a test case for the boundaries of free expression and independent media in Hong Kong after major legal and political changes.
- Discussed by international governments and rights organizations as emblematic of broader trends affecting journalists, publishers, and civil society actors in the territory.
