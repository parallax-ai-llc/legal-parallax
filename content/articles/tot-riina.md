## Background
- **Full name**: Salvatore "Totò" Riina.
- Associated with the **Sicilian Mafia (Cosa Nostra)** and became identified in media and judicial accounts as one of its most powerful leaders.
- His rise is commonly discussed alongside internal Cosa Nostra factional conflicts and the consolidation of authority by the Corleonesi faction.

## Role in Cosa Nostra
- Often described as a **boss of bosses** (capo dei capi) during a period of heightened violence.
- Linked in public reporting and court records to strategic decisions within the organization, including enforcement, intimidation, and internal discipline.
- Noted for a leadership style characterized by **centralized control** and willingness to authorize extreme measures.

## Major Themes (High-Level)
- **Factional struggle**: Accounts frequently frame his ascent within broader conflict among Palermo-area clans and Corleonesi rivals.
- **State response**: His era overlaps with an intensified Italian state crackdown, including major investigations, prosecutions, and anti-mafia institutions.
- **Public violence**: Widely associated with the phase in which attacks targeted both rivals and state representatives.

## Impact and Legacy
- Became a symbol of **organized-crime violence** in late-20th-century Italy.
- Frequently referenced in discussions of:
  - the evolution of anti-mafia prosecution strategy,
  - witness cooperation programs (pentiti),
  - and institutional reforms following high-profile killings.
