## Summary
Giuseppe Setola was a Camorra hitman responsible for a wave of murders in Caserta Province in 2008, killing 18 people. He was a fugitive on Italy's most wanted list. Captured in 2009.

## Background
- Setola was linked to the **Casalesi clan**, a powerful Camorra organization operating in Campania (notably the Caserta area).
- Italian reporting and court proceedings described him as an **enforcer/hitman** involved in intimidation, extortion, and targeted killings connected to clan control.

## 2008 Violence in Caserta Province
- In 2008, authorities attributed to Setola participation in a **series of murders** tied to clan disputes and the policing of local territory.
- The killings drew national attention because they targeted both alleged rivals and civilians, escalating public pressure for an intensive manhunt.

## Castel Volturno Massacre (September 2008)
- Setola was identified by investigators as a key participant in the **Castel Volturno shootings**, where six African migrants were killed.
- The episode became a symbol of the Casalesi clan’s violence and helped catalyze broader anti-mafia efforts and public demonstrations.

## Manhunt and Capture
- After being placed on Italy’s **most wanted** lists, Setola remained a fugitive for months.
- He was **arrested in 2009** in the Campania region following investigative leads and surveillance.

## Trial and Sentencing
- Italian courts convicted Setola in connection with multiple homicides associated with the 2008 wave of violence.
- He received **life imprisonment** (ergastolo) in decisions reported in Italian media and referenced in law-enforcement summaries.

## Legacy and Significance
- The Setola case is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - the operational reach of the **Casalesi clan**,
  - the role of contract-style killings in Camorra governance,
  - and Italy’s coordinated prosecutorial strategy against organized crime in Campania.
