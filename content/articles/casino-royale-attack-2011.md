## Summary

Los Zetas cartel members set fire to the Casino Royale in Monterrey, killing 52 people. The attack was retaliation for the casino refusing to pay extortion.

## Location and Context

- **City:** Monterrey, Nuevo León, Mexico.
- **Venue:** Casino Royale, a commercial gambling venue open to the public.
- **Broader setting:** The attack is widely discussed in the context of Mexico’s cartel violence in the early 2010s, including extortion and intimidation of local businesses.[^1]

## Attack Overview (High-Level)

- **Date:** 2011-08-25.
- **Method:** A group of assailants set a fire inside the casino (commonly reported as an arson attack involving accelerants), leading to a fast-spreading blaze and heavy smoke.
- **Casualties:** 52 people were reported killed; additional people were reported injured.[^1]

## Reported Motive and Attribution

- The incident has been attributed in reporting to **Los Zetas**.
- A commonly cited motive is **retaliation and/or pressure tied to extortion demands**, including allegations that the business refused to pay.

## Aftermath and Public Response

- The attack prompted national and international attention due to the high civilian death toll.
- Authorities publicly emphasized investigations and security measures aimed at organized crime activity in the region.

## Why It Matters

- Often cited as one of the **deadliest single attacks on civilians** associated with cartel violence in Mexico’s drug war era.
- Became a reference point in debates about public safety, protection rackets (extortion), and the vulnerability of civilian venues.
