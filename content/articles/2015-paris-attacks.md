## Summary

Coordinated ISIS attacks at six locations across Paris killed 130 people. Bataclan concert venue was the deadliest site with 90 killed; France's worst atrocity since WWII.

## Background and Context

- The attacks occurred amid a broader wave of jihadist violence in Europe linked to the Islamic State (ISIS), including networks that had operated in Syria and Iraq.
- France had been part of the U.S.-led coalition conducting air strikes against ISIS targets in Iraq and Syria.

## Attack Overview (High-Level)

- **Date:** 13 November 2015
- **Type:** Coordinated shootings and suicide bombings
- **Primary locations:**
  - Bataclan theatre (concert venue)
  - Restaurants and cafés in central/eastern Paris
  - Stade de France (near Saint-Denis)
- **Fatalities:** 130 total; the Bataclan attack accounted for the majority of civilian deaths.

## Suspected/Attributed Perpetrators

- ISIS claimed responsibility.
- Several attackers were linked to extremist networks operating across borders in Western Europe.

## Immediate Response

- French authorities launched a large-scale security operation in Paris and the surrounding region.
- France declared a state of emergency and implemented expanded police powers and security measures.

## Investigation and Follow-on Events

- Investigations focused on identifying accomplices, logistics, and travel routes used by the attackers.
- The attacks influenced subsequent counterterrorism policy debates in France and across the European Union.

## Impact and Significance

- Widely regarded as one of the deadliest attacks on French soil in modern history.
- Accelerated changes in public security posture, event security, and intelligence coordination.
- Became a reference point in European discussions on radicalization, foreign fighter returnees, and cross-border policing.
