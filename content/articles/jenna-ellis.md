## Background

- Ellis is an American attorney and conservative commentator.
- She has been associated with Republican politics and media, including commentary roles on conservative outlets.
- Public reporting describes her as having served as a legal adviser/spokesperson for Donald Trump’s 2020 campaign as election litigation and public messaging intensified.

## Role in 2020 Election Disinformation Claims

- After the 2020 U.S. presidential election, Ellis participated in public efforts to promote claims of widespread election fraud.
- Those claims were widely rejected by courts and election officials; multiple recounts and audits did not substantiate allegations of outcome-changing fraud in the key jurisdictions at issue.
- Her public-facing role included media appearances and participation in organized messaging alongside other Trump allies.

## Legal and Professional Consequences (High-Level)

- Ellis later acknowledged that some of the public claims she helped promote were false or misleading.
- In related proceedings, she entered a guilty plea in the Georgia election-interference case (Fulton County) to aiding and abetting false statements and writings.
- The plea agreement and subsequent reporting indicated cooperation obligations, including providing truthful information and testimony if required.
