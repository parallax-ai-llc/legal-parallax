## Additional Context

### Why he is historically notable

- Guzmán became one of the most internationally recognized leaders associated with Mexico’s large-scale drug trafficking networks, particularly the Sinaloa Cartel.
- His repeated escapes from high-security prisons became major political and law-enforcement scandals, prompting public debate about corruption, state capacity, and prison security.
- His eventual extradition, trial, and conviction in the United States made his case a landmark example of cross-border organized-crime prosecution.

### Public narrative vs. documented record

- Popular portrayals sometimes frame him as a “folk” figure; however, mainstream reporting and official accounts emphasize cartel violence, coercion, and the destabilizing effects of narcotics trafficking.
- Many details in media coverage vary by source; the most consistently documented milestones are arrests, escapes, recaptures, extradition, and the U.S. verdict/sentencing.

### Commonly cited themes in coverage

- **Transnational logistics:** movement of drugs and money across multiple countries.
- **Institutional stress tests:** challenges for policing, courts, and prisons.
- **Symbolic impact:** his name became shorthand for cartel power, state corruption allegations, and the “war on drugs” era.
