## Summary

Military police stormed São Paulo's Carandiru prison, killing 111 inmates. Officers were accused of executing prisoners in their cells. Colonel Ubiratan Guimarães was convicted then acquitted.

## Background

- **Location:** Casa de Detenção de São Paulo (Carandiru), a large pre-trial detention complex in São Paulo, Brazil.
- **Context:** The facility was widely reported as **overcrowded** and marked by **poor living conditions** and tension between inmates and authorities.
- **Immediate trigger:** A **prison uprising/riot** in one of the pavilions led the state to deploy police to regain control.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Date:** 2 October 1992.
- **Actors:** São Paulo state **Military Police** entered the prison to end the disturbance.
- **Outcome:** **111 inmates were killed** during the operation.
- **Allegations:** Survivors, lawyers, and human-rights groups alleged that some prisoners were **shot after surrendering** or were **executed in cells**, rather than killed in crossfire.

## Aftermath

- The event became one of Brazil’s most notorious incidents of **state violence inside a prison**.
- It intensified domestic and international scrutiny of:
  - **Prison overcrowding and governance**
  - **Use of lethal force** by security forces
  - **Accountability** for mass-casualty operations

## Legal Proceedings (Overview)

- Multiple investigations and court actions followed over the years.
- **Colonel Ubiratan Guimarães** (a commanding officer) was convicted in one major proceeding and later had the conviction overturned on appeal (as reflected in common summaries).
- Subsequent trials addressed responsibility of additional officers; outcomes and sentencing details varied across cases and appeals.

## Legacy and Cultural Impact

- The massacre remains a touchstone in Brazilian debates over **public security** versus **human rights**.
- It has been referenced in journalism, documentaries, and cultural works about:
  - Conditions in Brazil’s carceral system
  - Policing and militarization
  - Institutional accountability
