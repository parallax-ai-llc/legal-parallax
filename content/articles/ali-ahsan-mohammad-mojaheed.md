## Background

- Ali Ahsan Mohammad Mojaheed (often spelled “Mojahid/Mujaheed” in English-language reporting) was a Bangladeshi Islamist political figure associated with Jamaat-e-Islami.
- He was active in national politics after Bangladesh’s independence, and later served in government during the 2000s.

## Political roles and public positions

- Jamaat-e-Islami Bangladesh: held senior roles within the party over time (commonly described as a top leader in contemporary coverage).
- Government service: served as a cabinet minister in Bangladesh (frequently cited as “Minister of Social Welfare” during the early-to-mid 2000s, within a BNP-led coalition government).

## 1971 war and allegations

- The charges against him related to Bangladesh’s 1971 Liberation War.
- Prosecutors alleged involvement in crimes such as killings and other abuses committed during the conflict.
- The case was prosecuted decades later under Bangladesh’s domestic war-crimes tribunal framework.

## Trial, conviction, and execution

- He was tried at the International Crimes Tribunal (Bangladesh), a domestic court established to hear 1971-related cases.
- He was convicted of crimes against humanity.
- Appeals were pursued through Bangladesh’s legal system; the conviction and sentence were ultimately upheld.
- He was executed by hanging in 2015.

- Reporting about Mojaheed typically focuses on (1) his senior Jamaat-e-Islami position, (2) the tribunal proceedings, and (3) the 2015 execution.
