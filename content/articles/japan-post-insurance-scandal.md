## Summary

Japan Post Insurance employees fraudulently manipulated 183,000 insurance policies, canceling old policies and signing up customers for more expensive ones without consent.

## What the scandal involved

- **Policy “churning” / switching**: employees encouraged or executed cancellations of existing policies and enrolled customers into new policies that were **more expensive** or otherwise less favorable.
- **Non-consensual changes**: the core issue reported was that a large number of customers were switched **without informed consent**.
- **Customer harm mechanisms (typical for this pattern)**:
  - higher premiums and added fees
  - loss of accumulated benefits from older contracts
  - temporary gaps in coverage during cancellation/re-enrollment windows

## Why it mattered

- **Scale and trust**: Japan Post is a household brand, so the incident raised broader concerns about consumer trust in mass-market financial products.
- **Incentives and governance**: the scandal is frequently cited as a case where sales incentives and weak controls can drive systematic mis-selling.

## Reported response (high level)

- **Internal review and remediation**: investigations, customer outreach, and remediation programs are typical components of a response to this kind of discovery.
- **Regulatory scrutiny**: large-scale mis-selling in insurance generally triggers intensified oversight and demands for stronger compliance processes.
- **Accountability**: corporate governance responses commonly include disciplinary action and leadership accountability measures.

## Lessons for compliance programs

- **Suitability & consent checks**: require explicit, recorded customer confirmation for cancellation and replacement sales.
- **Replacement-sale guardrails**: cooling-off periods, second-person approval, and automated flags for repeat cancellations.
- **Audit sampling**: routine audits on branches/agents with abnormal cancellation-to-new-sales ratios.
- **Customer communications**: clear, plain-language explanation of premium changes, surrender impacts, and coverage continuity.
- **Whistleblower channels**: safe reporting and protections to surface problems before they scale.
