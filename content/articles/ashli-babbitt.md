## Summary

Air Force veteran shot dead by Capitol police while attempting to breach the Speaker's Lobby on January 6.

Babbitt became one of the most widely reported fatalities connected to the January 6, 2021 attack on the U.S. Capitol. Accounts typically focus on the moments outside the Speaker's Lobby, where a Capitol Police officer fired a single shot as rioters attempted to climb through a broken window in a barricaded doorway.

## Background

- Born in the United States in 1985.
- Served in the U.S. Air Force and later had service connected to the Air National Guard, according to widely cited biographical reporting.
- Lived for periods in California and the Washington, D.C. area.

## January 6, 2021 (Capitol attack)

Key high-level facts commonly reported:

- Babbitt entered the U.S. Capitol during the breach.
- She moved with a crowd toward the Speaker's Lobby area.
- As the crowd pressed against a barricaded doorway, glass in the door/window was broken.
- While attempting to climb through the opening, she was shot by a Capitol Police officer.
- She later died from her injuries on the same day.

## Investigations and official actions

- The shooting was reviewed by federal authorities, and the officer was not charged.
- Internal reviews by relevant agencies were also reported in major coverage, with conclusions generally describing the shooting as justified under the circumstances.

## Public discourse and political significance

- The incident became a focal point in broader debates about January 6, law enforcement use of force, and political polarization in the United States.
- Babbitt has been portrayed in sharply divergent ways in public narratives, ranging from a participant in an unlawful breach to a political martyr.
