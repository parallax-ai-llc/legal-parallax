## Summary
Rocco Morabito was a leader of the 'Ndrangheta involved in cocaine trafficking from South America. Known as 'the cocaine king of Milan,' he was a fugitive for 23 years before capture in Brazil in 2021.

## Notability
- Described by Italian investigators and press as a high-level cocaine trafficker linked to the 'Ndrangheta.
- Nicknamed the "cocaine king of Milan" for alleged role in trafficking and laundering proceeds through business and financial channels.

## Background and Network
- Origin: Locride/Africo area of Calabria, a region historically associated with 'Ndrangheta clans.
- Reported clan links: Morabito 'ndrina (a 'Ndrangheta family group).
- Nickname: **'u tamunga** (reported in Italian media), tied to the vehicle he was known to drive (a DKW Munga).

## Fugitive Period (1994–2017)
- Allegations centered on coordinating cocaine shipments from South America to Italy and managing proceeds.
- Lived for years outside Italy using false documents; reporting frequently places him in Brazil and later Uruguay.
- In Uruguay, he was reported to have lived a low-profile life in the Punta del Este area and worked in legitimate-appearing businesses (e.g., agriculture/real estate) while evading capture.

## Arrest, Escape, and Re-Arrest
- **2017 (Uruguay):** Arrest followed investigative leads tied to family identity details; authorities reported seizing multiple phones and documents at the time.
- **2019 (Uruguay):** Escaped from Montevideo's central penitentiary with other inmates via the roof, prompting an international manhunt.
- **2021 (Brazil):** Captured again and returned to custody; subsequent proceedings centered on extradition and enforcement of prior convictions.
