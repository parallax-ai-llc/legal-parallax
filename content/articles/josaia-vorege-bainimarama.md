## Summary

Fiji's military dictator who ruled for 16 years after his 2006 coup before being imprisoned for corruption.

## Additional Context

### Military background

- Career officer in the Republic of Fiji Military Forces (RFMF) and later its commander.[^1]
- Became a prominent national figure during periods of political instability that involved the military and civilian government.[^1]

### 2006 coup and rule

- Led a military coup in December 2006, removing the elected government and establishing a military-backed administration.[^1]
- Served as Prime Minister for an extended period afterward (often characterized by critics as authoritarian rule), and was a central figure in Fiji’s politics for roughly the next decade and a half.[^1]

### Return to elections and later political career

- Oversaw a transition to elections and remained a leading political figure through subsequent electoral cycles.[^1]
- Continued in national politics until losing power after the 2022 election and the formation of a new government.[^1]

### Legal issues

- In 2023, was convicted and sentenced to prison for perverting the course of justice (sometimes described as “perverting justice”).[^1][^2]
