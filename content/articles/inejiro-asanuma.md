## Background

- Inejiro Asanuma (浅沼 稲次郎) was a prominent postwar Japanese socialist politician.
- He became best known internationally as the leader of the Japan Socialist Party (JSP) in the late 1950s.

## Political Role and Positions

- Served as a member of Japan's House of Representatives (National Diet).
- As JSP chairman, he was associated with a left-wing, anti-U.S.-security-treaty stance during a period of intense political polarization.
- He was noted for advocating closer relations with the People's Republic of China, a position that attracted both support and fierce opposition in Cold War-era Japan.

## Assassination (1960)

- On 1960-10-12, Asanuma was stabbed with a short sword during a televised political event at Tokyo's Hibiya Hall.
- The attacker was Otoya Yamaguchi, a teenage ultranationalist.
- The killing was captured in widely circulated photographs and broadcast footage, becoming one of the most iconic images of political violence in modern Japan.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The assassination intensified debates over political violence and public security in Japan.
- A press photograph of the moment of attack by Yasushi Nagao won major international awards (including the Pulitzer Prize for Photography).
- The event is frequently cited in discussions of Japan's 1960 protest era and the risks faced by high-profile political figures.
