## Summary

Binance pleaded guilty to money laundering violations in a $4.3 billion settlement with US regulators.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2023 | $4.3 billion settlement |
| 2023 | Binance agreed to pay a multi-agency penalty package totaling roughly $4.3B, and to implement major compliance remediation and monitoring requirements. |
| 2023 | Changpeng Zhao (CZ) pleaded guilty to a related US criminal charge and stepped down as CEO; Richard Teng was announced as CEO. |

## What the Settlement Covered (High Level)

- **Agencies involved (reported in public announcements):** US Department of Justice (DOJ), the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN), and the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), with separate actions/settlements discussed by other regulators such as the CFTC.
- **Core allegations:** Failures to maintain an effective anti-money-laundering (AML) program and controls required under the Bank Secrecy Act, and compliance failures related to sanctions screening and related controls (as described by US agencies).
- **Resolution shape:** A guilty plea by Binance plus large monetary penalties and a forward-looking compliance remediation program.

## Key Terms and Obligations (Commonly Highlighted)

- **Monetary penalties:** A combined payment package described as approximately **$4.3 billion** across multiple US authorities.
- **Compliance remediation:** Commitments to strengthen AML/KYC controls, transaction monitoring, governance, and reporting.
- **Independent oversight:** Public communications referenced forms of monitoring/independent review designed to verify remediation progress over time.

## Why It Mattered

- **Scale:** The penalty size and breadth of agencies made it one of the most prominent enforcement actions involving a global crypto exchange.
- **Precedent:** It underscored that US AML/sanctions compliance expectations can apply to globally distributed crypto businesses when they have US touchpoints.
- **Industry impact:** The case became a reference point in discussions about licensing, compliance staffing, banking access, and the operational cost of regulatory alignment in crypto.

## Notes

- This article focuses on the **publicly described** high-level settlement outcomes; the detailed legal and compliance terms are specified in agency filings and agreements.
