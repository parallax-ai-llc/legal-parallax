## Summary

Polish painter and nationalist who assassinated Poland's first president, Gabriel Narutowicz, on December 16, 1922, just five days after his inauguration. He opposed Narutowicz's election by ethnic minority votes.

## Additional Background

Eligiusz Niewiadomski was a Polish painter and political nationalist who is primarily known today for assassinating **Gabriel Narutowicz**, the first president of the Second Polish Republic, in December 1922. Open-source summaries describe the killing as occurring shortly after Narutowicz’s election and inauguration, during a period of intense political polarization.[^1]

## Political Context in Early 1920s Poland (High-Level)

Public overviews commonly frame the assassination within:

- The instability and factional competition of the newly independent Polish state.
- Controversies over Narutowicz’s election, including claims that minority-party support influenced the result.
- A broader climate of inflammatory rhetoric and street-level agitation.[^1]

## The Assassination (High-Level)

Wikipedia summaries typically emphasize:

- **Date and setting**: Narutowicz was shot on December 16, 1922, at an art exhibition in Warsaw.[^1]
- **Perpetrator**: Niewiadomski, who had a background in the arts and nationalist politics.
- **Immediate impact**: The killing became a national shock and a defining political trauma for the young republic.[^1]

## Trial and Execution (Context)

Open-source accounts describe a relatively rapid legal process, followed by execution by firing squad in early 1923. Public summaries often cite this sequence as reflecting the state’s desire to reaffirm authority and deter further political violence.[^1]
