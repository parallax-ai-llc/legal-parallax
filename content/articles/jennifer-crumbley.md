## Background and Public Profile

- Jennifer Crumbley is known primarily through the criminal case arising from the 2021 Oxford High School shooting in Oxford Township, Michigan.
- She is the mother of the shooter and, together with the shooter’s father, became a central figure in a high-profile debate about parental responsibility and firearm access.

## Oxford High School Shooting Context (2021)

- The Oxford High School shooting occurred on November 30, 2021.
- After the shooting, prosecutors examined the parents’ actions before the event, including issues related to a firearm purchased for the shooter and responses to warning signs reported at school.

## Criminal Case Overview

- Prosecutors charged Jennifer Crumbley with **involuntary manslaughter** in connection with the deaths caused by the shooting.
- The case drew national attention because criminal charges against parents in a school shooting are uncommon, and the prosecution emphasized alleged negligence rather than direct participation.

## Trial and Verdict

- In 2024, a Michigan jury convicted Jennifer Crumbley of involuntary manslaughter.
- The conviction is widely reported as a precedent-setting outcome in the United States, discussed in the context of how the criminal law may apply to parental decisions involving firearms and supervision.

## Sentencing and Aftermath

- Sentencing occurred after the 2024 conviction (see the linked reference for details and subsequent legal developments).
- The case continued to be covered in relation to appeals, civil litigation, and broader policy conversations about school safety and gun regulation.

## Significance

- The conviction has been cited in commentary about:
  - the threshold for criminal negligence when parents are warned about a child’s behavior,
  - responsibilities tied to firearm storage and access,
  - how schools and families respond to reports of concerning behavior.
