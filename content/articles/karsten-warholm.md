## Summary

Karsten Warholm set the 400m hurdles WR of 45.94s at 2021 Tokyo in one of the greatest Olympic races ever.

## Early life and background

- Born in Norway (Ulsteinvik, Møre og Romsdal) in 1996.
- Developed as an all-around youth athlete before specializing in the 400m hurdles.
- Broke through in European age-group competitions and quickly transitioned to senior-level success.

## Athletic profile

- Primary event: **400 metres hurdles** (one lap with 10 hurdles).
- Known for combining elite flat-400 speed with aggressive hurdling rhythm.
- Competes primarily on the international track circuit and in championship meets.

## Major achievements (high level)

- **Olympic champion (2021, Tokyo)** in the 400m hurdles.
- **World champion** in the 400m hurdles (2019; later regained the title in subsequent championships).
- **Multiple-time European champion** in the 400m hurdles.
- Set one of the sport’s most famous modern records: **45.94s** in the Olympic final at Tokyo 2021.

## Notable races and rivalry context

- The men’s 400m hurdles peaked in the 2019–2021 period with historically fast times.
- Warholm’s Tokyo 2021 final is widely cited because multiple athletes ran near-record performances in the same race.

## Records and benchmarks

- **400m hurdles world record:** 45.94s (set at the 2021 Olympic Games).
- The 45-second barrier in the 400m hurdles is exceptionally rare; Warholm’s record is considered a landmark performance for the event.

## Style, strengths, and impact

- Emphasizes speed endurance and maintaining cadence between hurdles under fatigue.
- Helped redefine expectations for what is possible in the 400m hurdles era through record-setting performances.
