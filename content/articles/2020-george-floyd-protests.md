## Summary

The murder of George Floyd by Minneapolis police officer Derek Chauvin sparked the largest protest movement in US history. Over 25 people died and $2 billion in property damage occurred.

## Background and Trigger

- **Triggering incident (May 25, 2020):** George Floyd, a Black man, was killed during an arrest in Minneapolis, Minnesota, after officer Derek Chauvin knelt on his neck for several minutes. The event was recorded by bystanders and widely circulated.
- **Broader context:** The protests connected Floyd’s killing to long-running concerns in the United States over police use of force, racial discrimination, and accountability mechanisms.

## Scale and Geography

- The protest wave spread rapidly across the United States and became international, with demonstrations reported in **thousands of U.S. localities** and in many countries.
- Analysts and media outlets frequently described it as one of the largest mass protest movements in modern U.S. history.

## Notable Developments (High Level)

- **Late May–June 2020:** Large demonstrations occurred in major U.S. cities; some jurisdictions implemented curfews and deployed National Guard units.
- **Policing debates:** Public attention intensified around crowd-control tactics, the use of less-lethal munitions, and the legal standards governing use of force.
- **COVID-19 era conditions:** Many protests occurred during the COVID-19 pandemic, shaping public-health messaging and operational decisions.

## Legal and Policy Aftermath

- **Criminal case:** Derek Chauvin was convicted in Minnesota state court (2021) for Floyd’s murder-related charges and later pleaded guilty in federal court to violating Floyd’s civil rights (as well as to a separate civil-rights case).
- **Police reform proposals:** The protests accelerated reform efforts in many cities and states (e.g., changes to use-of-force policies, limits on chokeholds, and new reporting requirements), though proposals varied widely and outcomes differed by jurisdiction.
- **Federal-level debate:** U.S. federal legislation proposals (often grouped under “police reform” bills) advanced through parts of the legislative process but faced negotiation and political constraints.

## Cultural and Institutional Impact

- **Corporate and institutional responses:** Many organizations issued public statements and reviewed diversity, equity, and inclusion policies; some re-evaluated symbolism and naming practices.
- **Public discourse:** Terms such as “systemic racism,” “police accountability,” and “defund the police” became central in mainstream debate, though their interpretations and policy implications differed across groups.

## Controversies and Violence

- The protest period included both **peaceful demonstrations** and episodes of **property damage, arson, and clashes** between protesters and police in some areas.
- Public and media attention focused on questions of protest policing, the role of opportunistic criminal activity, misinformation, and the balance between public order and civil liberties.
