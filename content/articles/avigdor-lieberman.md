## Overview

Avigdor Lieberman is an Israeli politician who has led the secular nationalist party **Yisrael Beiteinu**. Over multiple decades in politics, he has held senior cabinet roles including **Foreign Affairs**, **Defense**, and **Finance**, and has been a recurring power-broker in coalition negotiations in Israel's parliamentary system.[^1]

## Early Life and Background

- Born in 1958 in the Moldavian SSR (then part of the Soviet Union) and immigrated to Israel in 1978.[^1]
- Became active in Israeli politics and party organization work before entering the Knesset.[^1]

## Political Profile (High-Level)

Commonly cited themes in Lieberman's political career include:

- Advocacy for a strong security posture and hawkish positions in national security debates.[^1]
- Focus on representing the interests of immigrants from the former Soviet Union and promoting secular civil reforms (for example, issues related to religion and state).[^1]
- Participation in and influence over coalition formation, with periods both inside government and in opposition.[^1]
