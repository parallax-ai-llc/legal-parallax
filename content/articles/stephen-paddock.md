## Summary
Stephen Paddock killed 60 people and wounded 411 in a mass shooting from the Mandalay Bay hotel in Las Vegas on October 1, 2017. It is the deadliest mass shooting in US history. His motive remains unclear.

## Background
- Paddock was a U.S. citizen who lived in multiple states over his lifetime, including Nevada.
- Public reporting and law-enforcement summaries describe him as financially comfortable, with significant time spent gambling (particularly slot machines).[^2]

## 2017 Las Vegas Shooting (High-Level Overview)
- **Date/Location:** October 1, 2017; Mandalay Bay Resort and Casino, Las Vegas, Nevada.
- **Target:** Attendees of the Route 91 Harvest music festival on the Las Vegas Strip.
- **Method:** Fired from an upper-floor hotel suite into the concert crowd; used multiple firearms brought into the room over prior days.[^2]
- **Casualties:** 60 people killed; hundreds injured (counts vary by definition/reporting).[^2]

## Investigation and Findings
- **Law enforcement response:** LVMPD led the criminal investigation, with federal support.
- **Evidence recovery:** Investigators documented firearms, ammunition, and materials in the hotel room and vehicle(s), and reconstructed the timeline of preparation and the shooting.[^2]
- **No clear motive established:** Official summaries stated a definitive motive could not be determined based on available evidence.[^2][^3]

## Motive and Speculation (What is Known vs. Unknown)
- **Known:** Planning and logistics indicated deliberate preparation.
- **Unknown:** A verified ideological or personal motive has not been conclusively identified in official reporting.[^2][^3]

## Aftermath and Legacy
- The attack intensified U.S. debate over firearms policy and public-event security.
- The shooting led to civil litigation and settlements involving affected parties, and changes in venue/hotel security practices (e.g., monitoring for unusual room activity and baggage patterns).[^4]
