## Expanded Biography

### Early Life and Training

- Ágnes Keleti was born in Budapest, Hungary, and trained as a gymnast in the pre–World War II era.
- She developed into one of Hungary’s leading gymnasts before her international career was interrupted by the war.

### World War II and Survival

- As a Hungarian Jew during World War II, she faced persecution under Nazi-aligned policies.
- She survived the Holocaust period in hiding, and members of her family were affected by wartime violence.

### Olympic Achievements

- Keleti became one of the most decorated female gymnasts of her era, winning a total of **10 Olympic medals**.
- Her Olympic medals include **5 gold**, **3 silver**, and **2 bronze**, earned across the **1952** and **1956** Summer Olympics.
- She is widely recognized for excellence on apparatus events such as floor exercise and uneven bars, as well as for team contributions.

### Later Life and Coaching

- After the 1956 Olympics, she settled in Israel, where she remained influential in the development of gymnastics.
- She worked in coaching and instruction, contributing to the sport beyond her competitive years.

### Legacy

- Keleti is often cited among the greatest Hungarian Olympians and among the most accomplished gymnasts in Olympic history.
- Her life story is frequently referenced as a combination of elite athletic achievement and wartime survival.
