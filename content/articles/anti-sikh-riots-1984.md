## Summary

Following Indira Gandhi's assassination, organized mobs killed an estimated 3,000-8,000 Sikhs in Delhi and other cities. Congress Party leaders were accused of orchestrating the violence.

## Context

- **Immediate trigger:** The violence followed the assassination of Prime Minister **Indira Gandhi** on **31 October 1984** by two of her Sikh bodyguards.
- **Wider background:** In the years leading up to 1984, tensions escalated around Sikh political demands in Punjab and a broader security crackdown, culminating in **Operation Blue Star** (June 1984) at the Golden Temple complex.

## Pattern of Violence (High-Level)

- Reports describe **organized attacks** in parts of Delhi and other cities, including arson, lynchings, and destruction of Sikh-owned property.
- Many accounts emphasize the **speed** and **scale** of violence in the first several days of November 1984.
- Estimates of deaths vary by source; widely cited figures include **thousands killed**, with Delhi among the hardest-hit areas.

## Allegations and Accountability

- Survivors and journalists have alleged involvement or facilitation by **local political figures** and failures by segments of the administration and police to prevent or halt attacks.
- Over subsequent decades, India established multiple **commissions of inquiry** and investigative efforts to examine responsibility and institutional failures.
- Legal proceedings and compensation/rehabilitation measures have been pursued unevenly, with continuing debate over adequacy and justice.

## Impact

- The events profoundly affected **Sikh communities** in India and the diaspora, shaping political mobilization, memory, and demands for accountability.
- The riots are frequently cited in discussions of **communal violence**, state capacity, and the rule of law in India.
