## Biography (from the cited Wikipedia source)

- **Born:** 1673 in Szokolya (Kingdom of Hungary).[^1]
- **Died:** 6 August 1757 in Dresden.[^1]
- **Background:** Son of a Reformed pastor; the family was poor, and he was reportedly supported by a German staff officer (named Dölfer) who promised him an education.[^1]

### Education and early training

- Studied in **Lüneburg** and later **Hamburg**.[^1]
- Apprenticed/studied with **Andreas Scheits** in Hanover and **Nicolas de Largillière** in Paris.[^1]
- Completed further study in the **Netherlands**.[^1]

### Court painter appointments and patrons

- Worked in Berlin as a court painter for **Frederick William I of Prussia** (1703–1707).[^1]
- Entered the service of **Francis II Rákóczi** in 1707; traveled with him to Poland in 1711, then remained there.[^1]
- Commissioned to paint **Augustus II the Strong** and became a court painter in 1717.[^1]
- Later painted portraits connected to the Habsburg court, including **Charles VI** and his daughters **Maria Theresa** and **Maria Anna** (as described in the cited source).[^1]

### Hungary period and later years

- Returned to Hungary (1724–1731), painting notable sitters including members of the **Podmanitzky family** and **Pál Ráday** (per the cited source).[^1]
- Lived in **Dresden** and **Leipzig** after 1731.[^1]
- Again served as a court painter (1736) for **Augustus III of Poland**.[^1]

## Themes, style, and significance (high-level)

- Primarily known for **Baroque portraiture**, often associated with courtly and aristocratic patrons.[^1]
- His career illustrates the mobility of early modern artists across **Hungary, Germany, France, the Netherlands, and Poland/Saxony**.[^1]
- The cited source notes that he resigned from a court position in 1753 due to **unpaid salary**, and that his final years were marked by financial hardship.[^1]

## End of life (as described in the cited source)

- Resigned in 1753 over unpaid salary.[^1]
- Spent money on **alchemy** during his final years (described as a period "mania" in the source).[^1]
- Died impoverished in Dresden and was buried with help from friends.[^1]
