Gopalasamudram Narayana Ramachandran (G. N. Ramachandran) was an Indian biophysicist best known for pioneering work on the structure of proteins and polypeptides. He is widely associated with the **Ramachandran plot**, a foundational tool in structural biology used to visualize and validate the sterically allowed backbone conformations (φ/ψ dihedral angles) of amino-acid residues in protein structures.

## Key Contributions

- **Ramachandran plot (protein backbone conformations):** A graphical representation of the sterically allowed regions of the φ (phi) and ψ (psi) torsion angles of amino-acid residues in polypeptide chains. It became a standard diagnostic for assessing the plausibility and quality of experimentally determined protein structures.
- **Collagen triple-helix modeling:** Early theoretical and diffraction-informed work proposing a triple-helical architecture for collagen, helping establish a structural framework for a major biological macromolecule.
- **Bridging physics and biology:** Trained in crystallography and X-ray methods, he applied physical reasoning (notably steric constraints and geometry) to macromolecular structure problems at a time when high-resolution protein structures were still scarce.

## Why the Ramachandran Plot Matters

- It translates atomic-level **steric clashes** into an interpretable map of allowed conformational space.
- It provides a practical "sanity check" for protein models: most residues in well-resolved structures cluster in favored regions corresponding to common secondary-structure motifs (e.g., α-helices and β-sheets).
- It remains central in modern structural biology workflows (crystallography, NMR, and cryo-EM model validation) because backbone geometry is a universal constraint across proteins.

## Legacy

Ramachandran is remembered as a leading figure in establishing structural biology in India and as a scientist whose geometric/steric approach produced tools that outlived the limitations of the experimental data of his era.
