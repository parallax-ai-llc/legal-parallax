## Summary

South African serial killer who poisoned two husbands for insurance and her son with strychnine.

## Identity and Background

- Full name commonly given as **Daisy Louisa de Melker** (née **Hancorn-Smith**).
- Born **1 June 1886** in **Seven Fountains**, then part of the Cape Colony.
- Worked as a **nurse**, which influenced public perceptions of her access to and knowledge of poisons.

## Crimes (Accusations vs. Conviction)

- She was **accused** of poisoning **two husbands** with **strychnine**, allegedly connected to insurance payouts.
- She was **convicted** of **murdering her only surviving son**.
- Accounts commonly state her son's death involved **arsenic** poisoning (while the alleged spousal poisonings are often described as involving strychnine).
- Because the allegations and the conviction differ, many summaries compress the story; for accuracy, it is helpful to separate what was *alleged* from what was *proven in court*.

## Legal Outcome

- Apprehended in **April 1932**.
- Sentenced to **death**.
- Executed by **hanging** on **30 December 1932**.
- Execution took place at **Pretoria Central Prison** (Pretoria), then in the Union of South Africa.

## Historical Significance

- Frequently cited as the **second woman** executed by hanging in South African criminal history.
- Her case remains notable in discussions of:
  - early 20th-century sensational crime reporting,
  - the role of insurance motives in poisoning narratives,
  - and how “medical” occupations were portrayed in public moral panics.

## Quick Facts

- Country: South Africa
- Period associated with the allegations: **1920s–early 1930s**
- Victims commonly attributed in popular retellings: **3** (two husbands + son), though court findings emphasize the **single murder conviction**.
