## Background (High Level)

- Angelo Izzo is widely described in Italian media as one of the perpetrators involved in the **Circeo massacre** (1975), a crime that became a highly publicized case in Italy.
- His criminal notoriety is associated with **sexual violence**, **homicide**, and later **recidivism** after a period of imprisonment.

## Circeo Massacre (1975)

- The Circeo massacre refers to the abduction and brutal assault of two young women by a group of men connected to a wealthy Roman social circle.
- Izzo is commonly listed as one of the principal perpetrators in accounts of the case.
- The case is often cited in discussions of:
  - gendered violence in Italy,
  - class privilege and impunity narratives of the era,
  - changes in public awareness and media coverage of violent crime.

## Investigation and Conviction

- Following the 1975 crime, Izzo was **arrested and convicted** (as reported in mainstream summaries of the case).
- He received a **lengthy prison sentence** and became a recognizable figure in Italian true-crime coverage.

## Release / Semi-Release and Later Offenses

- Izzo later obtained forms of **release** or **semi-liberty** from prison (often summarized as work release / temporary leave depending on the source).
- He was later implicated in additional killings in the mid-2000s, frequently summarized as the **2004 murders of two women**.
- These later crimes are a key reason the case remains referenced in debates about:
  - prisoner release policies,
  - risk assessment and supervision,
  - the management of violent offenders.

## Public Impact and Legacy

- In Italian public discourse, Izzo is often mentioned as an example of a high-profile offender associated with:
  - a landmark crime that shaped national attention to violence against women,
  - long-term media fascination with notorious cases,
  - controversy over judicial and correctional decisions.
