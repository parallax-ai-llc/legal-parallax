## Summary

Gang rivals set fire to a prison block in Altamira, Brazil, killing 62 inmates. Many were decapitated and 16 were burned alive in one of Brazil's deadliest prison massacres.

## Background

- The incident occurred in a prison complex in Altamira, Pará state, in northern Brazil.
- Brazilian prisons have periodically faced severe overcrowding, resource constraints, and gang control inside facilities.
- The massacre was widely reported as linked to conflict between organized criminal factions operating in and beyond the prison system.

## What Happened

- A violent confrontation broke out among rival inmate groups.
- A prison block was set on fire during the attack.
- Reports described extreme brutality, including decapitations and deaths by burning.
- The death toll was reported at 62 people, making it one of Brazil’s deadliest prison massacres.

## Immediate Aftermath

- Authorities reported that the disturbance was contained after security forces intervened.
- Victim identification and forensic work followed, given the scale of casualties and the condition of bodies.
- The episode prompted scrutiny of prison security measures and the ability of the state to control internal gang violence.

## Broader Context

- Brazil has experienced multiple large-scale prison riots and massacres in recent decades, often tied to disputes between criminal factions.
- Such events have fueled public debate over prison governance, inmate transfers, intelligence gathering inside facilities, and the separation of rival groups.
