## Background

- Florence Rey is a French figure primarily known for her role in a fatal 1994 Paris shooting involving police.
- Contemporary reporting and later summaries often describe the episode as tied to an anarchist/militant milieu; public accounts vary on the precise ideological framing.[^2]

## 1994 Paris shooting (high-level)

- The incident is commonly summarized as a confrontation that escalated into a shootout, resulting in multiple deaths, including police officers.[^2]
- Rey was arrested and later prosecuted for her involvement in the events.[^2]

## Legal outcome

- Rey was tried in France and convicted in relation to the killings connected to the 1994 shootout.[^2]
- She received a long prison sentence; later accounts note her eventual release after serving a substantial term.[^2]

## Aftermath and legacy

- The case remains notable in France due to the number of victims and the killing of police officers.
- In retrospectives, the event is often cited in discussions about political violence and the security climate in France during the 1990s.[^2]
