## Summary
Thomas Hamilton carried out the Dunblane school massacre in Scotland on March 13, 1996, killing 16 children and their teacher before killing himself. It led to a near-total ban on handguns in the UK.

## Background
- Thomas Hamilton was a Scottish man whose actions culminated in the Dunblane massacre in 1996.
- Multiple accounts describe a pattern of social isolation and ongoing disputes with youth organizations and parents prior to the attack.
- Official inquiries after the event examined both his history and institutional interactions in the years leading up to March 1996.

## Dunblane Massacre (1996)
- On 13 March 1996, Hamilton attacked Dunblane Primary School in Dunblane, Scotland.
- The victims included 16 children and one teacher; Hamilton then killed himself.
- The incident is widely regarded as one of the most consequential mass shootings in modern UK history because of the scale of the tragedy and the policy response.

## Aftermath and Impact
- The Dunblane massacre triggered a sustained public campaign for tighter gun control in the United Kingdom.
- Legislative changes that followed (notably in 1997) sharply restricted handgun ownership and are often cited as a defining shift in UK firearms regulation.
- The event remains a reference point in UK debates about public safety, firearms licensing, and the responsibilities of institutions that receive complaints.
