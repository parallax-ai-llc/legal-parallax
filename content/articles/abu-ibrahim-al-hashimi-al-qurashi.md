## Summary

ISIS leader who blew himself up during a US Special Forces raid in Syria.

## Background and Identity

- Also known as **Amir Muhammad Sa'id Abdal-Rahman al-Mawla**.
- Reported to be from **Tal Afar, Iraq**, and to have had religious training and/or roles within ISIS governance structures.
- Western and Iraqi sources have described him as a **key organizational figure** rather than a public-facing propagandist.

## Role in ISIS

- After the death of Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi (2019), ISIS announced him as the group’s new **“caliph”**.
- Under his leadership, ISIS continued to function as a **decentralized insurgency**, relying on regional affiliates and clandestine networks.
- Reporting has linked him to internal ISIS **security/oversight** functions, including disciplinary structures.

## Leadership Period (2019–2022)

- Maintained a comparatively **low public profile**, with limited verified imagery and fewer direct audio/video statements.
- Faced sustained counterterrorism pressure: targeted raids, leadership decapitation efforts, and disruption of financing and logistics.
- The group continued periodic attacks in Iraq and Syria, while ISIS “provinces” outside the core maintained varying levels of activity.

## Death (2022)

- Died during a **U.S. special operations raid** in Idlib Province, Syria (Atmeh area).
- U.S. officials stated he **detonated an explosive device**, killing himself and others in the building.
- The operation was publicly announced by the White House shortly after the raid.

## Why He Matters (Historical Context)

- Illustrates ISIS’s ability to **regenerate leadership** after major battlefield losses.
- His tenure is often cited as part of ISIS’s shift from territorial control toward **insurgent persistence**.
- The circumstances of his death reinforced the pattern of ISIS leaders dying during high-risk capture attempts.
