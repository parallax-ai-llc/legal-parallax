## Summary

An arsonist set fire to a Daegu subway train killing 192 people. The fire spread to a second train whose doors were locked by the driver's order. Ventilation systems failed catastrophically.

## Context

- The Daegu subway fire occurred on **18 February 2003** in Daegu, South Korea, during the morning commute.[^1]
- The incident is widely regarded as one of South Korea’s deadliest peacetime disasters and led to significant scrutiny of emergency response and rail safety procedures.[^1]

## What Happened (High-Level)

- A passenger set a fire on a train stopped at **Jungangno Station**; the fire and smoke rapidly filled the station environment.[^1]
- A second train entered or stopped at the same station and was also affected by the fire and smoke conditions.[^1]
- High heat and heavy smoke reduced visibility, complicated evacuation, and contributed to fatalities primarily through smoke inhalation.[^1]

## Contributing Factors Discussed in Reporting

- **Passenger egress and door control:** reports note that door and operational decisions affected evacuation conditions for at least one train.[^1]
- **Smoke management:** ventilation and smoke control were insufficient to prevent rapid smoke spread through the station and trains.[^1]
- **Materials and fire load:** interior materials and the presence of combustible items increased the speed and intensity of the fire.[^1]

## Immediate Aftermath

- The disaster resulted in mass casualties and a large-scale emergency response involving firefighters, police, and medical services.[^1]
- Survivors and families demanded clearer accountability for operational decisions and safety readiness.[^1]

## Safety Impact and Legacy

- The incident became a reference point for improving **subway fire safety**, including evacuation procedures, emergency communication, and smoke control planning.[^1]
- It is frequently cited in discussions of public-transport risk management: how human decisions, equipment limits, and infrastructure design interact during a fast-moving fire event.[^1]

## Key Facts

- **Location:** Jungangno Station, Daegu, South Korea.[^1]
- **Date:** 2003-02-18.[^1]
- **Fatalities:** 192.[^1]
- **Type:** Arson-triggered transit fire with secondary operational and infrastructure failures contributing to the severity.[^1]
