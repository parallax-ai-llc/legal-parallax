## Summary

Al-Qaeda commander killed by US drone strike in Pakistan.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2008 | Killed by US drone |

## Background

- **Full name (as commonly reported):** Abu Laith al-Libi.
- **Nationality:** Libyan.
- **Affiliation:** Al-Qaeda.
- **Known for:** Senior operational role and public-facing messaging on behalf of Al-Qaeda in the mid-2000s.

## Role in Al-Qaeda

Open-source reporting commonly describes al-Libi as:

- A **field commander** operating in the Afghanistan–Pakistan theater.
- An **operational planner and trainer** tied to militant networks in Pakistan’s tribal areas.
- A **spokesperson-like figure**, with statements and interviews attributed to him in jihadist media.

## Activity and Public Statements

- He was frequently linked to **threat messaging** directed at Western countries during the Iraq War period.
- He was also associated with **calls for attacks** and commentary on ongoing conflicts, a pattern typical of senior Al-Qaeda cadres who mixed operational and propaganda roles.

## Death

- **Date:** 29 January 2008.
- **Location:** Pakistan (widely reported to be in the Federally Administered Tribal Areas region).
- **Cause:** Killed in a **U.S. drone strike**, according to multiple media reports and later confirmations.

## Why He Matters

- His death is often cited as part of the broader U.S. campaign of **targeted strikes** against Al-Qaeda leadership in Pakistan.
- Analysts and journalists have used cases like al-Libi’s to discuss:
  - The **disruption of leadership networks**.
  - The tradeoffs between **counterterrorism effectiveness** and **sovereignty/civilian-risk concerns** surrounding drone warfare.
