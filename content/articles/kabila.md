## Presidency (high-level)

- **Security and conflict:** The DRC faced persistent armed-group violence, especially in the eastern provinces, throughout Kabilas time in power.
- **State capacity and governance:** International observers frequently characterized the period as marked by weak institutions, corruption risks, and uneven rule of law.
- **Economy and resources:** The DRCs vast mineral sector (notably cobalt, copper, gold, and coltan) remained central to the political economy, and was repeatedly linked to patronage networks and allegations of illicit enrichment.

## Term-limits controversy

- The DRC constitution limits presidents to two terms.
- Kabilas continued rule after the expected end of his mandate became a major focal point for opposition parties, civil society groups, and foreign governments.
- A negotiated political process (including agreements aimed at organizing elections) shaped the late-stage transition.

## Corruption and wealth allegations

- Kabila has been **accused** by journalists and investigators of involvement in networks that benefited from state-linked business deals and resource concessions.
- These claims are typically framed as allegations; adjudicated outcomes vary by forum and are often politically contested.
