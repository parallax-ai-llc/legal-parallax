## Summary
Diego León Montoya Sánchez, known as 'Don Diego,' was the head of the Norte del Valle Cartel. He was once on the FBI's Ten Most Wanted list. Captured in 2007 and extradited to the US.

## Background
- Diego León Montoya Sánchez is a Colombian narcotrafficker associated with the **Norte del Valle Cartel**, a major cocaine-trafficking organization that operated after the fragmentation of larger Medellín and Cali-era structures.
- He became widely known by the nickname **“Don Diego”** and was identified by U.S. authorities as a high-value fugitive during the mid-2000s.

## Role in the Norte del Valle Cartel
- Public reporting and law-enforcement accounts describe Montoya as a **senior leader** who helped coordinate large-scale cocaine shipments out of Colombia.
- His organization relied on **networks of producers, transporters, and corrupt intermediaries**, rather than a single vertically integrated “company.”
- In this period, cartel power was often maintained through **alliances and violent competition** with rival trafficking groups.

## International Pursuit
- Montoya appeared on the **FBI Ten Most Wanted Fugitives** list, reflecting U.S. prioritization of narcotics trafficking cases tied to transnational distribution.
- Rewards and inter-agency cooperation (Colombian authorities working with U.S. counterparts) were used to pressure trafficking leadership.

## Capture and Extradition
- He was **captured in Colombia in 2007** and later **extradited to the United States**, where federal courts have jurisdiction over drug trafficking conspiracies tied to U.S. markets.
- Extradition in this era was a central Colombian strategy to reduce the influence of major traffickers and weaken their ability to command operations from prison.

## Legal Outcomes (High-Level)
- In U.S. proceedings, cases against major traffickers commonly involve **conspiracy charges**, **drug-quantity allegations**, and extensive evidence from cooperating witnesses and investigative surveillance.
- Montoya remains **imprisoned** (as reflected in commonly cited summaries), though specific sentence details should be verified against court records and reputable reporting.

- Extradition policy was controversial domestically, but it materially altered incentives for cartel leaders by increasing the likelihood of long U.S. sentences.
