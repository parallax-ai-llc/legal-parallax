## Overview

Boko Haram is an Islamist militant organization that originated in northeastern Nigeria and became one of the most lethal insurgencies in Africa. The group is associated with widespread violence against civilians and state targets, including mass-casualty bombings, raids on towns and villages, and kidnappings.

## Origins and Ideology (High-Level)

- **Name and messaging**: The nickname “Boko Haram” is often translated as opposing “Western education,” though the group’s ideology is broader, advocating for a hardline interpretation of Islam and rejection of the Nigerian state.
- **Stated goals (as reported)**: Establish governance based on their interpretation of Islamic law and undermine secular institutions.
- **Tactics**: Guerrilla warfare, suicide bombings, targeted assassinations, and mass abductions; the insurgency has also included attacks on schools, markets, religious sites, and security facilities.

## Areas of Operation

- **Primary**: Borno State and neighboring areas in northeastern Nigeria.
- **Regional spillover**: Cameroon, Chad, and Niger—especially around the Lake Chad Basin—through cross-border raids and attacks.

## Leadership and Factions

- **Mohammed Yusuf**: Early leader whose death in 2009 is widely seen as a turning point.
- **Abubakar Shekau**: Longtime leader associated with extreme violence and public propaganda; later tied to a separate faction after the ISWAP split.
- **ISWAP**: A splinter aligned with ISIS that has been reported to focus more on military and state targets relative to some other factions, though it has also been responsible for violence against civilians.

## Notable Incidents (Examples)

- **2011 UN Abuja bombing**: Demonstrated the group’s ability to strike high-profile international targets.
- **2014 Chibok schoolgirls kidnapping**: Became a defining event highlighting systematic kidnappings and forced captivity.

## International Designation and Response

- Many governments and international bodies classify Boko Haram (and associated factions) as a terrorist organization.
- Nigeria and regional partners have pursued military operations and multinational coordination; humanitarian and stabilization efforts have also been central due to displacement and civilian harm.

## Notes on This Entry

This article is intentionally brief and high-level. Some sources treat Boko Haram and ISWAP as related but distinct entities after factional splits; summaries often reference Shekau due to his prominence.
