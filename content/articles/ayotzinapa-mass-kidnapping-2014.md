## Summary

43 student teachers from Ayotzinapa were forcibly disappeared by police and cartel members in Iguala, Guerrero. The case became Mexico's most notorious human rights scandal.

## Context

- **Location:** Iguala, Guerrero, Mexico.
- **Victims:** 43 students from the Rural Teachers' College of Ayotzinapa (normalistas) who were traveling to participate in protests/commemorations.
- **Core allegation:** Collusion among local officials, municipal police, and organized crime groups, followed by obstruction of justice.

## Key Events (High Level)

- **26–27 Sep 2014:** Students were attacked and detained in/around Iguala; the 43 were forcibly disappeared.
- **2014–2015:** Federal authorities announced an early official narrative (the so‑called “historical truth”), which was later challenged by independent investigators and victims’ families.
- **2015–2018:** International and independent reviews highlighted inconsistencies, evidence-handling problems, and credible indications of torture and coerced confessions.
- **2020s:** New investigative steps and arrest warrants continued; the case remained unresolved and politically consequential.

## Investigations & Findings

- **Family-led advocacy:** Families of the disappeared sustained domestic and international pressure for an independent investigation.
- **Independent review:** The Interdisciplinary Group of Independent Experts (GIEI), supported through the Inter-American human rights system, reviewed evidence and publicly questioned key elements of the initial government narrative.
- **Human rights concerns:** Reports in the broader case record have included allegations of torture, irregular detentions, and mishandling of forensic and chain-of-custody processes.

## Impact

- **Public trust:** The case became a symbol of impunity, corruption, and the depth of Mexico’s forced-disappearance crisis.
- **Protests:** Large demonstrations occurred across Mexico and internationally, keeping the case in the public eye.
- **Policy discourse:** The case intensified calls for reforms in policing, prosecutorial independence, forensic capacity, and protections for victims’ families and witnesses.

## Why It Matters

- **Accountability test:** The Ayotzinapa case is widely treated as a benchmark for whether institutions can investigate state-linked violence.
- **Forced disappearance:** It is frequently cited in global human rights discussions as emblematic of the regional and national scale of disappearances.
