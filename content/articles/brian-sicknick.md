## Summary

Capitol Police officer who died after being attacked during the January 6 insurrection.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2021 | Died after January 6 attack |

## Background

- Brian David Sicknick was an officer with the United States Capitol Police (USCP).[^1]
- He served in the U.S. National Guard prior to joining the USCP.[^1]

## January 6, 2021 attack and death

- On January 6, 2021, Sicknick was on duty during the attack on the U.S. Capitol.[^1]
- Reports described him being assaulted in the crowd and exposed to chemical irritants while responding to the violence.[^1]
- He collapsed after returning to his office and was later taken to a hospital, where he died on January 7, 2021.[^1]

## Investigation and medical findings

- The Office of the Chief Medical Examiner of the District of Columbia determined that Sicknick died of natural causes (stroke).[^1]
- Public reporting around the incident evolved over time as investigations and official findings were released.[^1]

## Honors and legacy

- Sicknick lay in honor in the U.S. Capitol Rotunda in February 2021, an honor granted to a small number of individuals.[^1]
- He has been cited in discussions of January 6 accountability and of risks faced by law-enforcement officers during civil unrest.[^1]
