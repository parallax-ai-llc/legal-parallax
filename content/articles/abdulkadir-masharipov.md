## Background and Identity

- Abdulkadir Masharipov is an Uzbek national identified by Turkish authorities as the perpetrator of the attack at the Reina nightclub in Istanbul.
- Reporting has described him as connected to the Islamic State (IS/ISIS) militant organization.
- Public accounts indicate he traveled through multiple countries and used false identities and safe houses while evading capture.

## 2017 Reina Nightclub Attack (Istanbul)

- Date: 2017-01-01 (New Year’s Day, shortly after midnight local time).
- Location: Reina nightclub, Ortaköy neighborhood, Istanbul, Turkey.
- Outcome: 39 people were killed and many others were injured.
- The attack was widely reported as one of the deadliest terrorist incidents in Turkey in that period.

### Key reported details (high level)

- The assailant entered the venue and opened fire on patrons.
- The incident triggered a large-scale security response and a multi-week manhunt.
- Turkish officials attributed the attack to ISIS, and ISIS-linked channels later claimed responsibility.

## Manhunt, Arrest, and Legal Proceedings

- Turkish police conducted raids and detentions during the investigation, including alleged logistical supporters.
- Masharipov was captured in Istanbul in January 2017 after authorities traced him to an apartment.
- He was prosecuted in Turkey; court proceedings focused on terrorism-related charges and the events at Reina.

## Aftermath and Significance

- The attack intensified domestic and international attention on ISIS-linked networks operating in or transiting through Turkey.
- It contributed to heightened security measures around major public venues and holiday periods in Istanbul.
- The case remains notable in discussions of counterterrorism operations, cross-border militant mobility, and urban-target attacks.
