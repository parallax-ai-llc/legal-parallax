## Summary
Mohamed Merah killed seven people in three separate attacks in Toulouse and Montauban, France in March 2012, targeting soldiers and a Jewish school. He was killed by police after a 32-hour siege.

## Background
- Born in Toulouse, France, to a family of Algerian origin.
- Raised in the Toulouse area; reported history of petty crime and multiple encounters with the justice system.
- Traveled to the Middle East and South Asia in the years before the 2012 attacks; French authorities later examined these trips for possible radicalization and contact networks.

## March 2012 Attacks (High-Level)
- **Targets**: French military personnel and a Jewish school.
- **Locations**: Montauban and Toulouse (southwestern France).
- **Victims**: Seven people were killed across the three attacks, including children and adults.
- **Impact**: The attacks triggered a major manhunt and heightened national security measures.

## Investigation and Siege
- French police identified Merah as a suspect and tracked him to an apartment in Toulouse.
- A prolonged standoff followed (widely reported as roughly 30+ hours).
- Specialized police units negotiated with him before launching an assault.
- Merah was killed during the final raid; the case became a major point of public debate around counterterrorism preparedness.

## Political and Social Aftermath
- The attacks occurred during the 2012 French presidential campaign and influenced national discussion on security, policing, and extremism.
- France held public memorials; Jewish communities and military institutions were directly affected.
- Subsequent proceedings and investigations examined any enabling networks, potential failures to detect threat escalation earlier, and the handling of returned travelers.
