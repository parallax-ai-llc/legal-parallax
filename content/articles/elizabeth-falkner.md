Falkner is best known as an American chef, pastry chef, and restaurateur associated with the San Francisco food scene and with competitive cooking television. Her public profile comes primarily from restaurant entrepreneurship (not industrial production) and media appearances.

## Background and Education

- Born in **San Francisco, California (1966)**; raised in Southern California.
- Earned a **BFA from the San Francisco Art Institute (1989)**.
- Entered professional kitchens through entry-level work (including dishwashing) and moved into fine-dining pastry roles in the 1990s.

## Culinary Career (High-Level)

- Built a reputation in **pastry and dessert** work before expanding into full-service restaurant concepts.
- Worked in or alongside notable San Francisco fine-dining kitchens (including *Masa's* and *Rubicon*) early in her career.
- Known for blending **California** sensibilities with **Italian** and globally influenced flavors, with an emphasis on technique and presentation.

## Restaurants and Entrepreneurship

- **Citizen Cake (San Francisco, 1997–2011):** dessert café concept that became her signature early venture and a recognizable brand in the city.
- **Orson (San Francisco, opened 2008):** a larger, more capital-intensive restaurant project co-owned with a partner; operated into the early 2010s.
- Later projects included restaurant leadership roles in **Brooklyn** and **New York City** (early-to-mid 2010s).

## Television and Media

Falkner has appeared as both a competitor and judge on cooking competition shows, including:

- *The Next Iron Chef: Super Chefs* (competitor)
- *Tournament of Champions* (competitor)
- Association with the *Top Chef* franchise (alum)
