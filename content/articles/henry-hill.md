## Summary
Henry Hill was a Lucchese crime family associate whose life was the basis for the film Goodfellas. He participated in the Lufthansa heist and entered witness protection. He died of natural causes.

## Criminal Activity (High-Level)
- **Role in organized crime:** Hill was widely described as an *associate*—someone who worked with a crime family’s crews without being formally inducted as a “made” member.
- **Typical revenue streams:** Accounts of his activities include theft, fencing stolen goods, extortion-related intimidation, and drug trafficking involvement.
- **Notoriety:** His later fame came less from rank and more from the combination of a major heist association and his decision to cooperate with law enforcement.

## Cooperation and Witness Protection
- **Trigger for cooperation:** After being arrested on serious charges in 1980, Hill agreed to provide information to federal authorities.
- **Impact:** His cooperation contributed to prosecutions and convictions of various figures connected to his crew and broader organized-crime networks.
- **WITSEC life:** While in witness protection, Hill lived under new identities, but his profile repeatedly resurfaced due to media coverage and his own public statements.

## Media and Cultural Legacy
- **Primary source narrative:** Hill’s story was popularized in Nicholas Pileggi’s *Wiseguy*, which framed his life as a case study in everyday organized crime operations.
- **Film depiction:** The 1990 film *Goodfellas* (directed by Martin Scorsese) drew heavily from the book and used Hill’s arc to depict the allure and consequences of mob life.
- **Public perception:** Hill became an emblematic example of the “mobster-turned-informant” narrative—celebrity created by testimony, memoir, and screen adaptation.

## Notes and Caveats
- **Disputed details:** As with many organized-crime biographies, specific claims (who did what, exact roles in particular crimes) can vary across court records, journalistic accounts, and memoir-style retellings.
- **Scope:** This article summarizes broadly reported facts and does not attempt to adjudicate contested allegations beyond what is commonly documented.
