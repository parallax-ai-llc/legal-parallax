## Summary

Israel's military campaign in Gaza following October 7 has killed over 40,000 Palestinians. The ICJ ordered Israel to prevent acts of genocide; accusations of collective punishment.

## Background

- The war followed the **7 October 2023** Hamas-led attack on Israel and Israel's subsequent declaration of war.
- Fighting has taken place primarily in the **Gaza Strip**, with cross-border exchanges of fire also involving other actors in the region.

## Key Developments (high level)

- **Ground operations** and air strikes expanded after the initial phase of the campaign.
- Israeli operations have focused on dismantling Hamas' military and governing capabilities; Hamas and other groups have continued rocket fire and insurgent-style attacks.
- Periodic **humanitarian pauses** and negotiated releases of hostages and detainees have occurred at various points.

## Humanitarian Impact

- The conflict has produced large-scale **civilian casualties**, extensive destruction of housing and infrastructure, and major displacement.
- Access to **food, water, medical care, and fuel** has been repeatedly constrained, contributing to a severe humanitarian emergency.

## International Response and Legal Proceedings

- The conflict has been the subject of extensive UN debate and diplomatic efforts focused on ceasefire proposals, humanitarian access, and protection of civilians.
- In 2024, the **International Court of Justice (ICJ)** issued provisional measures in a case brought by South Africa alleging violations of the Genocide Convention; the case remains ongoing.
- The **International Criminal Court (ICC)** has also been a focal point of international discussion regarding accountability for alleged crimes.

## Terminology and Disputes

- Reporting and political statements frequently dispute how to characterize specific incidents (e.g., proportionality, distinction, and intent), and casualty figures may vary by source.
- The war is also referred to as part of the broader **Israel–Hamas war**.
