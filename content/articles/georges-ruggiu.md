## Background

- Born in Belgium (1957) and later worked in media and political circles connected to Rwanda.
- Known for joining a foreign network of supporters aligned with Rwanda’s Hutu Power movement in the early 1990s.

## Role in RTLM (Rwanda)

Radio Télévision Libre des Mille Collines (RTLM) was a private radio station that became a key propaganda outlet during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi.

- Ruggiu became one of RTLM’s broadcasters and delivered commentary in French.
- His broadcasts are widely described as encouraging hostility and violence, contributing to an atmosphere that facilitated mass atrocities.
- RTLM programming is frequently cited in international criminal law discussions as an example of mass media used for incitement.

## Legal Proceedings and Conviction

- Ruggiu was arrested and transferred to the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR).
- He pleaded guilty (2000) to incitement to commit genocide.
- ICTR cases involving media figures helped clarify how speech acts can meet legal thresholds for direct and public incitement.

## Historical Significance

- Often discussed alongside other “media cases” from the ICTR as part of the broader record on propaganda, hate speech, and atrocity mobilization.
- His case is used in scholarship and journalism to illustrate how foreign participants could play roles in Rwanda’s information ecosystem.
