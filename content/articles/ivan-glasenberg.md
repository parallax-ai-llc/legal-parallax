## Summary

Former Glencore CEO under whose leadership the company engaged in systematic bribery across three continents.

## Additional Context

### Background

- Born in South Africa (1957) and later associated with Switzerland through his long tenure at Glencore.
- Trained as an accountant and moved into commodity trading early in his career.

### Role at Glencore

- Joined Glencore (then part of Marc Rich & Co’s trading business) in the 1980s and rose through trading and management roles.
- Became Chief Executive Officer in the early 2000s and led the company through major growth, including the expansion of its mining and metals footprint.
- Oversaw Glencore’s period as a publicly listed company following its 2011 IPO.

### Governance and Compliance Issues (Company-Level)

- In 2022, Glencore admitted to and resolved charges with authorities related to bribery and corruption schemes in multiple countries during periods that included his leadership tenure.
- Public reporting around these resolutions emphasized weaknesses in internal controls, third-party due diligence, and oversight in certain high-risk jurisdictions.

### Why He Appears in “Historical Parallax”

- The core historical signal is not a single incident but an organizational pattern: global commodity and extractive operations operating across jurisdictions where enforcement, transparency, and political risk vary widely.
- His tenure is often discussed in the context of how incentive structures, aggressive deal-making, and weak controls can create systemic exposure to corruption risk.

## Quick Facts

- Primary domain: Global commodities trading and mining.
- Known for: Long-term stewardship of one of the world’s largest commodity groups and the reputational/legal risk profile that accompanied parts of its operations.
