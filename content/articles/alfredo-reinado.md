## Context and Significance

Alfredo Alves Reinado was an East Timorese (Timor-Leste) soldier who became a central figure in the country’s post-independence security crisis. He is most commonly referenced in connection with the 2006 unrest and the 2008 attacks targeting senior political leaders.

## Background

- Served in the Timor-Leste security forces (often described as military police).
- Rose to international attention during a period of institutional fragility in the newly independent state.
- Was associated in reporting with a faction of armed dissidents following the 2006 crisis.

## Role in the 2006 Crisis (high-level)

The 2006 Timor-Leste crisis involved splits within the security forces, political contestation, and communal violence. Reinado became one of the better-known dissident figures during and after this period, frequently cited in connection with armed incidents and disputes involving state security institutions.

## 2008 Attacks and Death

In February 2008, armed attacks were carried out against:

- President José Ramos-Horta (who was seriously wounded), and
- Prime Minister Xanana Gusmão (who survived).

Reinado was killed during the attacks and is widely described as being among the principal attackers.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The 2008 attacks led to an intensive security response and renewed attention to demobilization and reconciliation.
- Reinado’s name remains tied to debates about accountability, command-and-control in young security institutions, and the long shadow of the 2006 crisis.
