## Background

- Often identified as **Mustafa Faraj Muhammad al-Azibi** (also rendered as *Mustafa Faraj Mohammed al-Azibi*), with the kunya **Abu Faraj al-Libi**.
- Reported to have been a Libyan national and an experienced facilitator within al-Qaeda’s transnational network.
- Public reporting on early life and formal education is limited; most biographical details come from intelligence, court filings, and secondary reporting summarized in open sources.

## Role in al-Qaeda

- Described in open-source reporting as a senior al-Qaeda operative involved in **operational planning and coordination**.
- After the 2003 capture of **Khalid Sheikh Mohammed (KSM)**, Abu Faraj al-Libi was widely reported to have assumed a larger role in **external operations** and communications.
- Frequently characterized by officials and journalists as being among al-Qaeda’s senior leadership tier during the early-to-mid 2000s.

## Capture and Detention

- **Captured:** May 2005 in Pakistan (reported in the Mardan area).
- The arrest was publicly framed as a major counterterrorism success, with some statements at the time describing him as al-Qaeda’s “number three.”
- He was held in U.S. custody as a **high-value detainee** and later reported to have been transferred into the military detention system.
- Public record indicates he was among detainees moved to **Guantánamo Bay** as part of the 2006 transfer of high-value detainees.

## Why He Matters (High-Level)

- Illustrates the post-9/11 period in which al-Qaeda relied on geographically distributed facilitators and couriers to maintain operational continuity.
- His case is often cited in discussions about:
  - the leadership succession that followed major captures in 2002–2003,
  - the CIA “black site” detention era,
  - the transition of high-value detainees into Guantánamo Bay.

## Additional Notes

- **Death:** Often listed as unknown in open sources.
- **Name variants:** Transliteration differences are common; multiple spellings appear across public documents and media reports.
