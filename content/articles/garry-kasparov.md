## Notable Achievements

- **World Champion**: Widely recognized as a dominant world champion of the late 20th century, holding the top title in the classical lineage for many years.[^1]
- **Long-term world #1**: Spent extensive time as the highest-rated player in the world across multiple rating lists.[^1]
- **Opening preparation**: Known for deep preparation and for pushing the theory of many sharp openings in elite play.[^1]

## Playing Style (High-Level)

- **Dynamic and tactical**: Often sought initiative and complex positions that rewarded calculation and activity.
- **Preparation + calculation**: Combined intensive opening work with strong middlegame tactics.
- **Endgame competence**: While famed for attack, he also converted advantages and defended difficult endings at top level.

## Deep Blue and Computer Chess

Kasparov's matches with IBM's Deep Blue in 1996 and 1997 became a public symbol of the accelerating strength of chess engines and computer-assisted analysis.[^1]

## Post-Chess Work and Public Life

- **Political activism**: Became an outspoken critic of the Russian government and a prominent political activist.[^1]
- **Writing and commentary**: Authored books and essays on chess, history, and politics; also worked as a commentator and public speaker.

## Legacy

- Frequently cited among the greatest chess players in history.
- Influential in modern professional preparation culture, where databases, engines, and rigorous opening research are central.
