## Summary

Colombian cartels build semi-submersible and fully submersible vessels to transport tonnes of cocaine. 'Narco submarines' can carry $100 million in drugs and are nearly undetectable.

## Background and Context

Drug-sub trafficking refers to the use of semi-submersible or fully submersible vessels—often called "narco-subs"—to move illicit drugs by sea while reducing the chance of detection. These craft are typically designed for a single voyage and may be scuttled or abandoned after delivery.

## Vessel Types

- **Low-profile vessels (LPVs):** Ride very low in the water to reduce radar and visual signature.
- **Semi-submersibles:** Operate with most of the hull below the surface while retaining a small conning structure above water.
- **Fully submersibles:** Capable of running completely underwater for at least part of a transit; these are rarer and more complex.

## Construction and Operations

- **Build sites:** Often assembled in remote coastal or riverine areas where oversight is limited.
- **Materials:** Commonly use fiberglass, wood, and improvised components; the priority is stealth and payload, not long-term durability.
- **Crew and navigation:** Crews are small. Navigation can rely on GPS and pre-planned routes, with emphasis on avoiding shipping lanes and patrol patterns.
- **Logistics:** Craft may rendezvous with support boats for fuel, provisions, or to transfer cargo.

## Detection and Interdiction (High-Level)

Authorities typically focus on:

- **Maritime patrol and surveillance:** Visual spotting, radar, and coordinated patrols.
- **Signals and human intelligence:** Disrupting networks that plan routes, supply components, or recruit crews.
- **Boarding and seizure:** Interdiction at sea is challenging because vessels are designed to minimize time exposed on the surface.

## Why This Method Persists

- **Risk distribution:** Using disposable vessels can reduce the operational impact of a single seizure.
- **Stealth advantages:** Low profile and unconventional construction can complicate detection.
- **High value cargo:** Even small shipments can be financially significant, incentivizing repeated attempts.

## Notes

This topic is generally discussed in the context of cocaine trafficking routes in the Americas, but the underlying principle—using low-signature maritime craft to move high-value contraband—can apply to other regions and illicit supply chains.
