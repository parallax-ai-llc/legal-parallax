## Summary

Ousted president of Kyrgyzstan who fled after a violent revolution, convicted in absentia of mass killings.

## Additional Context

- Kurmanbek Bakiyev served as a leading opposition figure before becoming a central figure in Kyrgyz politics after the 2005 "Tulip Revolution."
- He became acting president and later won the presidency following the departure of President Askar Akayev.

## Presidency (High-Level)

- His administration was widely associated with consolidation of power and allegations of corruption.
- Political opponents and independent media outlets reported pressure and intimidation during his time in office.
- Key state positions and economic influence were often described as concentrated among close allies and family members.

## 2010 Uprising and Aftermath

- In April 2010, protests escalated in Bishkek and other cities amid public anger over governance and rising utility prices.
- Security forces opened fire during unrest; Bakiyev was later held responsible by Kyrgyz authorities for the deaths.
- After losing control, he fled Kyrgyzstan and subsequently lived in exile.

## Legal and Political Legacy

- Kyrgyz courts convicted Bakiyev in absentia for crimes connected to the 2010 events (per widely cited reporting).
- His ouster marked a major constitutional and political shift in Kyrgyzstan, with subsequent governments attempting reforms and prosecutions tied to the previous era.
