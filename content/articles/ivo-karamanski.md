## Background

- Known in Bulgarian media as a reputed mob figure during the turbulent post-communist years.
- Also known by the nickname **"The Godfather"** (and the phrase *"Capo di tutti Capi"* is associated with his public image).[^1]

## Early life and sport

- Born in **Sofia** (People's Republic of Bulgaria).[^1]
- Wikipedia notes a difficult childhood, including the death of his mother when he was four.[^1]
- In **1981**, he became a **national and Balkan rowing champion**.[^1]

## Business activity (as reported)

Wikipedia attributes to him a mix of criminal reputation and business activity:

- Founded the insurance company **"Korona-ins"**.[^1]
- Co-owned **around 20** other small companies (per Wikipedia).[^1]

## Criminal reputation and public portrayal

- Described as a "colorful underworld character" in accounts of the Bulgarian Mafia.[^1]
- Reported characterization includes the line that he was a killer "who never even shot a gun" (quoted in Wikipedia's summary of sources).[^1]
- A notable quote associated with him in Wikipedia: "The good boys go to heaven, the bad ones wherever they want (like) to".[^1]
- Wikipedia also states he was reportedly an informant for the communist-era State Security.[^1]

## Death

- Wikipedia states that Karamanski and his bodyguard were **gunned down in 1998**, in what police called a **drunken quarrel**.[^1]
- Wikipedia lists the death date as **December 20, 1998** and the place as **Sofia, Bulgaria**.[^1]
