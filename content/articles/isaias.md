## Summary

Isaias Afwerki (born 1946) is the longtime political leader of Eritrea and has served as the country's president since independence in 1993. He was a leading figure in the Eritrean independence movement and the Eritrean People's Liberation Front (EPLF), and after independence became the central decision-maker in Eritrea's one-party state.

## Overview

- **Role:** President of Eritrea (since 1993) and former secretary-general/chairman in the EPLF/PFDJ political structures.
- **Political system:** Eritrea is widely described as a one-party state with limited political pluralism and no national elections held since independence.
- **International profile:** Known for a strong emphasis on sovereignty and security, and for contentious relations with some neighbors and international bodies.

## Domestic Governance (high-level)

- **Executive dominance:** The presidency has been the primary locus of executive authority in Eritrea since independence.
- **National service:** Eritrea operates a national service system that has been heavily debated internationally, including concerns about duration and labor conditions.
- **Political rights:** International reporting frequently characterizes Eritrea as having severe restrictions on political opposition, independent media, and civil liberties.

## Foreign Relations (high-level)

- **Ethiopia:** Relationship has oscillated between war (late 1990s) and rapprochement (notably in 2018).
- **Horn of Africa:** Eritrea has played roles in regional security and diplomacy, and has at times faced sanctions and diplomatic isolation.
