## Summary

Italian PhD student tortured and killed by Egyptian security services in Cairo. His body showed signs of extensive torture over days; Egyptian authorities obstructed the investigation.

## Background

- **Victim**: Giulio Regeni, an Italian PhD student at the University of Cambridge.
- **Research context**: Regeni was in Egypt conducting academic research related to independent trade unions and labor issues.
- **Disappearance**: He went missing in Cairo in late January 2016.

## Key Events

- **2016-01-25**: Regeni disappeared in Cairo on the fifth anniversary of the Egyptian revolution.
- **Early February 2016**: His body was found on the outskirts of Cairo.
- **Forensic findings (reported)**: Publicly reported descriptions noted extensive injuries consistent with prolonged torture.

## Investigation and Attribution (High-Level)

- **Suspected perpetrators**: Italian prosecutors and investigators have alleged involvement by members of Egypt's security apparatus.
- **Cooperation issues**: The case became known for prolonged disputes over evidence-sharing and judicial cooperation between Italy and Egypt.
- **Competing narratives**: Egyptian authorities provided various explanations over time; Italian authorities publicly disputed key claims.

## Legal Proceedings

- **Italian judicial actions**: Italian prosecutors pursued proceedings in Italy focused on named suspects from Egyptian security services.
- **Evidentiary challenges**: Proceedings and investigative steps have faced obstacles related to access to witnesses, documentation, and cross-border cooperation.

## Diplomatic and Political Aftermath

- **Italy–Egypt relations**: The case significantly strained diplomatic relations, including debates over ambassadorial representation and broader cooperation.
- **Public advocacy**: Regeni's family and civil-society groups kept sustained public attention on accountability and transparency.

## Why the Case Matters

- **Academic freedom and researcher safety**: The case is widely cited in discussions of risks faced by researchers working under authoritarian security environments.
- **Rule-of-law and accountability**: It became a prominent international example of the difficulty of investigating alleged state-linked abuses.
