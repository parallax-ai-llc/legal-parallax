## Summary

Key surviving member of the ISIS cell that carried out the November 2015 Paris attacks.

| Year | Event |

## Background and Identity

- The individual commonly referenced in reporting on the November 2015 Paris attacks is **Salah Abdeslam** (often written with given name first). Some datasets invert the order as "Abdeslam Salah"; the linked Wikipedia page uses the more common ordering and biographical details.
- Born in **Brussels, Belgium**, and associated with the **Molenbeek** area that produced multiple members of the 2015 attack network.

## Role in the November 2015 Paris Attacks

- Considered a key surviving participant from the coordinated attack cell.
- After the attacks, he became one of Europe’s most wanted fugitives for several months.

## Arrest and Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- **March 2016:** Arrested in Belgium after a major manhunt.
- **2018:** Convicted in Belgium in connection with a 2016 police shootout and sentenced to a multi-year prison term.
- **2022:** In France, convicted for his role in the 2015 attacks and sentenced to **life imprisonment** (with restrictions on parole).

## Additional Timeline Notes

- 2016–2022: Extradition and pre-trial detention followed by a large multi-defendant trial in France concerning the 2015 attacks.
