## Expanded Context

This article title indicates a *duplicate* entry in the source corpus rather than an independent biography. In practice, the primary subject associated with the name is **Warren Buffett** (born 1930), an American investor best known for leading Berkshire Hathaway and for popularizing a long-term, fundamentals-driven approach to buying businesses and public equities.

At a high level, Buffett’s public profile is shaped by three themes:

- **Value investing tradition:** an emphasis on purchasing assets for less than their assessed intrinsic value, influenced by Benjamin Graham’s investing framework.
- **Operating-company ownership:** a strategy of acquiring and holding controlling stakes in durable businesses, often allowing existing management to continue day-to-day operations.
- **Capital allocation discipline:** using insurance “float,” retained earnings, and opportunistic deployment of cash to compound returns over decades.
