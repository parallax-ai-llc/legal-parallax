## Expanded Context

### Role and Affiliation

- Identified in public reporting as a leader of the Florida chapter of the Oath Keepers, a far-right, anti-government militia organization.
- Discussed coordination/partnership with other extremist groups (e.g., Proud Boys and Three Percenters) in communications cited in coverage of January 6-related investigations.

### January 6 and Investigation (High-Level)

- Entered the U.S. Capitol during the January 6, 2021 attack.
- Described in reporting as participating in and/or helping lead the Oath Keepers' coordinated "stack" formation movement inside the building.
- Arrested in February 2021 in connection with January 6-related charges.

### Trial, Conviction, and Sentencing

- Found guilty in late 2022 of seditious conspiracy and related obstruction/conspiracy offenses tied to January 6.
- Sentenced in 2023 to a multi-year federal prison term (reported as 12 years), followed by a period of supervised release.

### Post-Sentencing Developments

- Public sources report later clemency/commutation actions affecting some January 6 defendants, including Meggs.
- Separate reporting notes court-imposed restrictions in 2025 limiting entry into Washington, D.C. and/or Capitol grounds without court permission.
