## Background

- **Full name (Vietnamese):** Bùi Tín (often rendered as “Bui Tin” in English).[^1]
- **Born:** 29 December 1927 near Hanoi; educated in Huế (per Wikipedia).[^1]
- **Service:** People’s Army of Vietnam (North Vietnam), eventually a **colonel**, with reported service years **1945–1990**.[^1]

## Notable Wartime / Postwar Details

- **Military + journalism roles:** He is commonly described as combining military service with work connected to Party/state media, including later senior editorial work at *Nhân Dân* (the Communist Party’s official newspaper).[^1]
- **1975 surrender narrative (disputed):** Western reporting has often credited him with accepting the surrender of South Vietnam’s leadership at the Fall of Saigon, but Wikipedia notes this attribution has been disputed (including by Tín himself), with the act attributed to another officer in some accounts.[^1]

## Exile and Dissident Activity

- **Departure:** In **1990** he left Vietnam and settled in **Paris**, describing disillusionment with postwar corruption and Vietnam’s political system.[^1]
- **POW/MIA testimony:** In **1991** he appeared before the U.S. Senate Select Committee on POW/MIA Affairs and stated he believed there were **no American prisoners alive in Vietnam** at that time; the appearance drew attention due to symbolic reconciliation moments with U.S. figures such as John McCain (as described by Wikipedia’s summary and citations).[^1]

## Works (commonly cited)

- *Following Ho Chi Minh: The Memoirs of a North Vietnamese Colonel* (memoir; commonly associated with Bùi Tín in English-language references).[^1]

## Quick Facts

- **Died:** 11 August 2018 in Montreuil (Paris area), France (per Wikipedia).[^1]
