## Summary

Osama bin Laden's son-in-law and al-Qaeda spokesman convicted of conspiracy in New York.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2014 | Convicted of conspiracy in US court |

## Background and Profile

- Abu Ghaith (often spelled **Sulaiman Abu Ghaith**) was a Kuwaiti national who became publicly associated with **al-Qaeda** in the early 2000s.[^1]
- He was widely described as a **spokesman** and public face for the group, appearing in propaganda statements and videos after the September 11 attacks.[^1]

## Role in al-Qaeda

- Reported responsibilities included delivering public messages intended to recruit supporters and threaten or intimidate perceived adversaries.[^1]
- His family connection to Osama bin Laden (as a **son-in-law**) amplified his visibility within al-Qaeda’s media ecosystem.[^1]

## Legal Case (United States)

High-level, publicly reported case milestones:[^1]

- **Capture / transfer:** He was taken into U.S. custody and brought to New York for prosecution.[^1]
- **Charges:** The case centered on **conspiracy-related terrorism offenses**, rather than direct participation in the September 11 operation itself.[^1]
- **Trial and conviction:** A federal jury in New York found him guilty of conspiracy counts connected to material support and related terrorism conspiracies.[^1]

## Significance

- The prosecution was frequently cited as an example of using **U.S. federal courts** to try high-profile alleged al-Qaeda figures captured abroad.[^1]
- The case also highlighted al-Qaeda’s use of **media spokespeople** as part of its organizational strategy, alongside operational leadership.[^1]

## Additional Notes

- Many public summaries of Abu Ghaith focus on his **public communications role** and the legal outcome in U.S. court proceedings.[^1]
- Some sources also describe him as a prominent figure in al-Qaeda messaging during the early post-9/11 period.[^1]
