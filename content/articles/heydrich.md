## Roles and Responsibilities (high-level)

- **SS and security leadership:** Senior SS official who helped build and run the Nazi security and intelligence system.
- **RSHA chief:** Led the RSHA, the central office combining security police and intelligence functions in the Third Reich.
- **Occupation authority:** As Deputy Reich Protector, exercised coercive control in Bohemia and Moravia, including repression of resistance and labor exploitation policies.

## Historical Significance

Heydrich is widely regarded by historians as a key organizer and administrator of Nazi state repression and mass violence.
At a high level, his influence is most often discussed in three areas:

1. **Institution-building:** Consolidation of overlapping security agencies into a powerful, centralized apparatus.
2. **Political repression:** Expansion of surveillance, arrests, intimidation, and suppression of opponents.
3. **Genocidal policy coordination:** Participation in senior-level coordination related to the Holocaust, including the Wannsee Conference.

## Assassination and Aftermath

- **Operation Anthropoid (1942):** Heydrich was attacked in Prague by Czechoslovak agents trained and supported by the Allies.
- **Death:** He died from complications following the attack.
- **Reprisals:** The Nazi regime carried out severe retaliatory measures in the region after the assassination.
