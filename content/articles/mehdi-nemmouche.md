## Summary
Mehdi Nemmouche carried out the 2014 Jewish Museum of Belgium shooting in Brussels, killing four people. He was a French-Algerian ISIS fighter who had returned from Syria. Sentenced to life.

## Background
- Nemmouche is a French national of Algerian descent; public reporting describes a history of delinquency and incarceration prior to his involvement in jihadist networks.
- Belgian and French cases associated him with travel to Syria during the period when ISIS was expanding and attracting foreign fighters.

## Jewish Museum of Belgium shooting (2014)
- Location: Jewish Museum of Belgium, Brussels.
- Outcome: Four people were killed.
- Modus operandi: The attacker used a firearm attack at the museum.
- Significance: The case became one of the early high-profile prosecutions in Europe involving returnees from the Syria conflict.

## Investigation and arrest
- Nemmouche was arrested in France (Marseille) shortly after the Brussels attack, after traveling by bus.
- Investigators and prosecutors alleged that weapons and items linking him to the attack were found in his possession.

## Trials and sentencing
- Belgium: Convicted for the Brussels museum attack and sentenced to life imprisonment.
- France: Convicted for involvement in ISIS-related hostage-taking of journalists in Syria; also received a life sentence.
