## Summary

American spy who sold satellite intelligence to the Soviet Union, inspiring *The Falcon and the Snowman*.

## Key Facts

- Worked as a security-related employee/contractor in the U.S. aerospace/defense ecosystem (commonly reported as TRW), which gave him access to sensitive information.
- Passed U.S. satellite reconnaissance and intelligence-related materials to Soviet intelligence during the Cold War.
- His case became widely known because it involved a close associate (often identified as Andrew Daulton Lee) and was later adapted into a popular book and film.

## Espionage Overview

Boyce is publicly described as having exploited his position to obtain and transmit classified information. Open-source reporting commonly frames his motivations as a mix of personal ideology, disillusionment, and the opportunity created by lax controls relative to his access.

At a high level, the activity involved:

- Obtaining sensitive documents related to U.S. intelligence collection (especially satellite reconnaissance).
- Transferring material to Soviet handlers through meetings and/or intermediaries.
- Triggering a counterintelligence investigation once anomalies and suspicions were detected.

## Investigation, Arrest, and Aftermath

Public accounts describe an investigation that culminated in arrest and prosecution for espionage offenses. Following conviction, Boyce received a lengthy sentence. Later reporting also notes additional legal consequences connected to post-conviction conduct.

## Cultural Impact

- The story is closely associated with the book *The Falcon and the Snowman* (Robert Lindsey).
- It was adapted into the 1985 film of the same name, contributing to the enduring public awareness of the case.
