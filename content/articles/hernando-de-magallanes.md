## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1505 | Sailed to India as part of the Portuguese Estado da Índia expeditions (early service in the Indian Ocean). |
| 1511 | Participated in the Portuguese capture of Malacca, a major hub in Southeast Asian maritime trade. |
| 1517 | Broke with the Portuguese court and moved to Spain, seeking royal backing for a westward route to the Spice Islands. |
| 1518 | Received a Spanish capitulación (royal contract) and began organizing an expedition intended to reach the Moluccas by sailing west. |
| 1519 | Departed Spain with five ships; crossed the Atlantic and wintered in Patagonia amid serious command tensions and a mutiny. |
| 1520 | Navigated the strait later named the Strait of Magellan, enabling passage from the Atlantic to the Pacific. |
| 1521 | Reached the Philippines; became involved in local politics and was killed at the Battle of Mactan. |
| 1522 | Although Magallanes did not survive, one ship (Victoria) returned to Spain under Juan Sebastián Elcano, completing the first circumnavigation. |

## Why He Matters (High-Level)

- Led the expedition that found a practical sea passage between the Atlantic and Pacific via the southern tip of South America.
- Initiated the voyage that became the **first circumnavigation of the Earth** (completed after his death).
- Demonstrated, through sustained oceanic navigation, the global connectedness of the Atlantic and Pacific trade routes.

## Commonly Cited Contributions / Associations

- **Strait of Magellan**: the navigational route linking two oceans, named for the expedition’s passage.
- **"Magellanic"** as a descriptor: used in geography and astronomy (e.g., Magellanic Clouds) as a later honorific association.
