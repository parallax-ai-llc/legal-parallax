## Background

- Cesar Altieri Sayoc is an American man born in 1962.
- He lived in Florida around the time of the 2018 mail-bombing case.[^1]
- Reporting and court filings described his political views as strongly supportive of Donald Trump; the nickname “MAGA Bomber” came from this media framing.[^1]

## 2018 Mail Bombing Case (High-Level)

- In October 2018, a series of improvised explosive devices (IEDs) were sent via U.S. mail to multiple recipients.[^1]
- Targets included prominent Democratic politicians and public figures, as well as media organizations.[^1]
- Authorities reported that the devices did not detonate as intended, but were treated as serious threats and potential explosives.[^1]

## Investigation and Arrest

- Federal investigators traced evidence from packages and other leads to identify a suspect.[^1]
- Sayoc was arrested in late October 2018 in Florida in connection with the mailed devices.[^1]

## Charges and Sentencing

- Sayoc pleaded guilty in 2019 in federal court to charges related to mailing the devices.[^1]
- He received a lengthy federal prison sentence (the Wikipedia summary reports a 20-year sentence).[^1]
