## Summary

Wealthy Nigerian banker who reported his own son to authorities before the underwear bomb plot.

## Additional Context

## Background

- Abdul Mutallab is described in public reporting as a wealthy Nigerian banker and businessman.
- He became widely known internationally in connection with the 2009 attempted bombing of Northwest Airlines Flight 253, because he alerted authorities about concerns regarding his son, Umar Farouk Abdulmutallab.

## Relationship to the 2009 Flight 253 Plot

- According to widely cited accounts, he contacted U.S. officials at the U.S. Embassy in Abuja (Nigeria) to warn that his son had become radicalized and could pose a threat.
- His warning contributed to the son being placed into U.S. counterterrorism systems (often described as a watchlisting process), though the son was not ultimately prevented from boarding the flight.
- The incident is frequently referenced in discussions about the strengths and gaps of pre-attack intelligence sharing, watchlisting, and airline security screening.

## Why This Case Is Notable

- It is notable because a parent proactively reporting a family member is relatively rare in public terrorism case narratives.
- The episode is often cited as an example of community-initiated reporting and as a case where human-source warning did not fully translate into operational interdiction.

## Quick Facts (High-Level)

- **Country/region:** Nigeria (reporting origin); case impacts primarily discussed in U.S./international aviation-security context.
- **Public role:** Banker/business figure; known mainly through his connection to the Flight 253 attempted attack and his pre-incident warning.
- **Commonly associated event:** 2009 warning to authorities about his son prior to the attempted “underwear bomb” attack.
