## Summary

Michael Ryan killed 16 people and wounded 15 in a shooting rampage through Hungerford, Berkshire. Led to the UK's Firearms Amendment Act banning semi-automatic weapons.

## Background

- The attack occurred in the English market town of Hungerford in Berkshire.
- The perpetrator, Michael Robert Ryan, was a 27-year-old local resident.
- The event became one of the defining mass-shooting incidents in modern UK history.

## What Happened (High Level)

- The violence unfolded over several locations in and around Hungerford.
- Victims were shot in the town and nearby areas as the perpetrator moved between sites.
- The incident ended after an armed police response and a final standoff.

## Weapons and Modus Operandi

- Reports commonly describe the use of multiple firearms during the rampage.
- The case is closely associated with subsequent UK restrictions on certain types of semi-automatic firearms.
- The perpetrator also used additional equipment and ammunition consistent with a sustained attack.

## Casualties

- **Killed:** 16 people (excluding the perpetrator).
- **Wounded:** 15 people.
- Casualty figures are widely cited in contemporary reporting and later summaries.

## Law Enforcement Response

- Police conducted an armed containment operation as the situation developed.
- The final phase involved isolating the perpetrator and negotiating/controlling the perimeter.
- The incident concluded with the perpetrator's death.

## Aftermath and Policy Impact

- The massacre helped catalyze major UK firearms reforms.
- It is frequently referenced as a key precursor to the **Firearms (Amendment) Act 1988**, which tightened controls and prohibited certain semi-automatic rifles.
- Public debate following the attack focused on firearm access, licensing, and the availability of high-capacity weapons.
