## Additional Context

### Background

- Nizeyimana was a soldier in the Rwandan Armed Forces (FAR) during the period leading up to and including the 1994 genocide.
- Some publicly available biographical sources list his birth date as **5 October 1963** (Gisenyi prefecture), which differs from the placeholder birth date in this stub.

### Role in Butare (1994)

- During the genocide, he served as **second-in-command** (after Tharcisse Muvunyi) of the **École des sous-officiers (ESO)** in Butare.
- He was found responsible for **ordering killings**, including the execution of **Queen Dowager Rosalie Gicanda**, a prominent early victim in Butare.

### ICTR Indictment and Arrest

- The ICTR issued an indictment (27 Nov 2000) alleging responsibility for:
  - genocide (or, alternatively, complicity in genocide)
  - direct and public incitement to commit genocide
  - crimes against humanity (including murder)
- He was arrested in **Kampala, Uganda (6 Oct 2009)** while reportedly traveling on false documents.
- A **U.S. State Department reward** program had offered up to **US$5 million** for information leading to his arrest or conviction.

### Trial Outcome

- ICTR trial proceedings resulted in a conviction for genocide-related crimes.
- On appeal (29 Sep 2014), the ICTR **confirmed the conviction** and **reduced the sentence** from **life imprisonment to 35 years**.
