## Summary

Moroccan who attacked passengers on a Thalys train before being subdued by American servicemen.

## Background (high-level)

- El Khazzani is a Moroccan national reported to have lived in Europe prior to the 2015 attack.[^1]
- Contemporary reporting and later court proceedings described him as having traveled in Europe in the period leading up to the incident.[^1]

## 2015 Thalys train attack (overview)

- Date: 21 August 2015 (widely reported as the day of the incident).[^1]
- Location: A Thalys high-speed train traveling from Amsterdam toward Paris.[^1]
- Description: El Khazzani boarded the train and opened fire and/or attempted to attack passengers before being confronted and subdued.[^1]
- Intervention: He was restrained by passengers, including off-duty U.S. servicemen who were later publicly credited with stopping the attack.[^1]

## Aftermath and legal proceedings

- El Khazzani was arrested after the incident and faced prosecution in France in connection with the attack.[^1]
- The case became a high-profile example of passenger intervention preventing mass casualties on public transport.[^1]

## Significance

- The Thalys attack is frequently cited in discussions of rail security in Europe and the role of bystander/passenger response during active-violence incidents.[^1]
- The event influenced public awareness and policy debates about screening and security practices on cross-border rail lines.[^1]
