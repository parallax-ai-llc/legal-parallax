## Background

- Antonio Fuentes Trillanes IV (born 6 March 1971) is a Filipino politician and former Navy officer who became prominent after leading a 2003 military mutiny and later serving as a senator of the Philippines.
- He is commonly associated with reformist movements within the Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP) during the early 2000s.

## Military career

- Served in the Philippine Navy and was linked with the "Magdalo" group of junior officers.
- Rose to national attention in 2003 as one of the leaders of the Oakwood Mutiny, an attempted uprising by military personnel alleging corruption and governance failures.

## Political career

- Entered electoral politics while detained; he was elected to the Philippine Senate in the late 2000s and later won re-election.
- As a senator, he was known for investigations and public criticism involving government officials and issues such as corruption and security policy.
