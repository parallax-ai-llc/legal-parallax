## Quick Facts (Jeff Bezos)

- Founder of **Amazon** (founded 1994; began as an online bookstore and expanded into broad e-commerce and cloud computing).
- Founder of **Blue Origin** (spaceflight company founded in 2000).
- Purchased **The Washington Post** in 2013 (through Nash Holdings).
- Served as Amazon CEO from 1994 until stepping down in 2021; became Executive Chair.

## Career Highlights

### Amazon

- Built Amazon from a startup into a global company spanning retail, logistics, devices, and subscription services.
- Oversaw the growth of **Amazon Web Services (AWS)**, which became a major cloud computing platform.

### Blue Origin

- Founded with the goal of lowering the cost of space access and enabling long-term human presence in space.
- Blue Origin developed suborbital flight systems and orbital launch vehicles.

### Media and Other Investments

- Acquired The Washington Post, a major U.S. newspaper, in 2013.
- Led **Bezos Expeditions**, a personal investment vehicle involved in technology and other sectors.

## Philanthropy and Public Initiatives

- Launched the **Bezos Earth Fund** (climate-focused philanthropy).
- Announced the **Day 1 Families Fund** and other charitable commitments focused on homelessness and early childhood education.

## Commonly Cited Themes (Leadership / Strategy)

- Customer obsession and long-term orientation.
- Willingness to invest ahead of demand (notably in infrastructure and cloud).
- Emphasis on experimentation and scalable operational processes.
