## Summary
Terry Lynn Nichols was convicted as a co-conspirator in the 1995 Oklahoma City bombing. He helped McVeigh plan and build the bomb. Sentenced to 161 consecutive life terms.

## Background
- Born in 1955 in the United States.
- Served in the U.S. Army and later had intermittent work and financial instability.
- Became closely associated with Timothy McVeigh in the early 1990s, sharing anti-government and militia-adjacent interests documented in court records.

## Role in the Oklahoma City Bombing (1995)
- Prosecutors and courts found Nichols assisted McVeigh in preparing the attack, including logistics related to materials used in the truck bomb.
- The bombing targeted the Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building in Oklahoma City, killing 168 people and injuring hundreds.

## Investigation, Trial, and Convictions
- Nichols was arrested shortly after the bombing during the broader investigation into McVeigh and any accomplices.
- He was convicted in U.S. federal court for conspiracy and for providing material support to the bombing plot.
- In a separate Oklahoma state prosecution, he was convicted on additional charges related to the deaths caused by the attack.

## Sentencing
- Federal sentence: 161 consecutive life terms (effectively life imprisonment without release).
- State sentence: additional life terms imposed by Oklahoma, reinforcing permanent incarceration.
