## Summary

The acquittal of police officers who beat Rodney King triggered riots that killed 63 people and caused $1 billion in damage. The worst civil unrest in the US since the Civil War.

## Context and Background

- **Underlying tensions**: Longstanding friction in Los Angeles over policing practices, socioeconomic inequality, and racial discrimination shaped the environment in which unrest could rapidly escalate.
- **Catalyst incident**: The March 1991 beating of **Rodney King** by Los Angeles Police Department (LAPD) officers was recorded on video and widely broadcast, becoming a focal point for debates about police accountability.

## Immediate Trigger

- **Verdict**: On **April 29, 1992**, a jury in Simi Valley acquitted (or failed to convict) the involved officers on key charges, which many residents viewed as a denial of justice.
- **Initial outbreak**: Protests and confrontations began soon after the verdict announcement, with violence spreading across multiple parts of the city.

## Key Events (High-Level)

- **Duration**: The most intense period lasted roughly **six days** (late April to early May 1992).
- **Geography**: Unrest concentrated in areas including **South Central Los Angeles** (now often referred to as South Los Angeles) and spread to other neighborhoods.
- **Common features**: Arson, looting, assaults, and clashes with law enforcement; disruptions to transportation and commerce; widespread property damage.

## Government and Security Response

- **Local response**: LAPD and other local agencies attempted to contain the unrest, with major challenges in mobility, coordination, and scale.
- **State and federal involvement**: The response expanded to include **California National Guard** deployments and additional federal support as conditions deteriorated.

## Human and Economic Impact

- **Casualties**: The riots resulted in **63 deaths** (as reflected in the article’s summary and timeline).
- **Injuries and arrests**: Thousands were injured and thousands were arrested during the period of civil unrest.
- **Property losses**: Damage estimates are commonly cited around **$1 billion**, with many businesses destroyed or heavily damaged.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- **Public debate**: The events intensified national discussions about policing, racial justice, economic inequality, and the role of the justice system.
- **Policy focus**: Subsequent years saw renewed attention to police oversight mechanisms, community policing concepts, and broader reforms—often uneven and contested.
- **Cultural memory**: The 1992 unrest remains a major reference point in U.S. urban history, frequently compared to later episodes of civil disorder.
