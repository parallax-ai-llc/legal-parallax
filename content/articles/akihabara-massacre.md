## Summary

Tomohiro Katō drove a truck into a crowd in Tokyo's Akihabara district and then stabbed pedestrians, killing 7 and injuring 10. He was executed in 2022.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Date/Place:** 8 June 2008, Akihabara (Chiyoda, Tokyo), a busy electronics/shopping district.
- **Sequence:** A rented truck was driven into pedestrians, followed by a **knife attack** on people in the street.
- **Victims:** **7 people killed** and **10 injured** (as commonly reported in major summaries).

## Perpetrator

- **Name:** Tomohiro Katō (加藤 智大).
- **Age at time:** 25.
- **Background (reported):** Employment and social instability were cited in reporting; he left messages online prior to the attack.

## Investigation and Legal Outcome

- Katō was arrested at the scene and later prosecuted for multiple murders.
- **Sentence:** Death penalty.
- **Execution:** Reported in Japan in **2022**.

## Broader Impact / Aftermath

- The attack intensified scrutiny of **public safety** in crowded urban spaces and of threats posted online.
- It is frequently referenced in discussions of **Japan’s gun laws vs. knife violence**, and the limits of prevention in sudden lone-actor attacks.
