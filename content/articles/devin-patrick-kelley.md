## Summary
Devin Kelley killed 26 people at the First Baptist Church in Sutherland Springs, Texas on November 5, 2017. He was shot by a civilian and killed himself during a car chase.

## Background
- Kelley is identified by law enforcement as the perpetrator of the **Sutherland Springs church shooting** (Texas, 2017).
- The attack is frequently cited in discussions about:
  - firearm purchase background-check reporting gaps,
  - domestic-violence convictions and firearm prohibitions,
  - civilian intervention during active-shooter incidents.

## Early Life
- Public reporting describes him as born in **1991** and raised in the United States.
- Biographical details in public sources are limited compared to the extensive coverage of the 2017 attack.

## Military Service and Discharge
- Served in the **U.S. Air Force**.
- He was later **court-martialed** and received a punitive discharge.
- Reporting commonly notes that records tied to the conviction were not properly transmitted to the federal background-check system at the time.

## Domestic Violence History
- Public accounts describe convictions and allegations involving **domestic violence**.
- These records are central to analyses of how the shooter was able to purchase firearms despite legal prohibitions.

## 2017 Sutherland Springs Attack (High-Level)
- Date: **2017-11-05**.
- Location: **First Baptist Church**, Sutherland Springs, Texas.
- Casualties: **26 killed**; additional people were injured.
- Kelley fled the scene and died after being pursued.

## Immediate Response and Aftermath
- A civilian engaged the shooter, and Kelley was wounded during the encounter.
- Kelley died during or after a vehicle chase (widely reported as a self-inflicted gunshot).
- The event prompted national-level scrutiny of military-to-civilian record reporting and the implementation of improvements.
