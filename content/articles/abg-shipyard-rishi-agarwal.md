## Context: ABG Shipyard case (high-level)

- ABG Shipyard Ltd. was an Indian shipbuilding and ship-repair company.
- The ABG Shipyard fraud case is widely reported as one of India's largest alleged bank frauds by value.
- Allegations in public reporting describe a pattern of borrowing from a consortium of banks and subsequent diversion/misuse of funds.

## Allegations commonly described in reporting

- Large working-capital and term-loan facilities were raised from multiple lenders.
- Funds were alleged to be:
  - diverted to other group companies or purposes not aligned with loan terms
  - used to repay earlier borrowings ("evergreening")
  - routed through multiple entities, complicating traceability
- Financial stress at the company escalated into defaults and loan-classification actions by lenders.

## Law-enforcement involvement (typical sequence)

- Banking fraud cases of this scale in India commonly involve:
  - bank audits and forensic reviews
  - complaints/referrals leading to registration of a criminal case
  - parallel financial-investigation actions focused on tracing proceeds
- Public accounts of the ABG Shipyard matter describe multi-agency investigation steps and charges.

## Why the case matters

- It is frequently cited in discussions about:
  - credit risk management and lender due diligence
  - forensic accounting and detection of fund diversion
  - governance and transparency in large, debt-financed industrial projects
