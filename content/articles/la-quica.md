## Background

- Known publicly by the alias **"La Quica"**; widely reported legal name: **Dandeny(s) Muñoz Mosquera**.[^1]
- Described in reporting and court summaries as an enforcer/sicario associated with the **Medellín Cartel** during the late 1980s–early 1990s.[^1]
- **Data-quality note:** open sources do not always agree on biographical details (including birth date and exact spellings); this profile keeps the existing header values and adds corroborating context here.[^1]

## Notability

- Most associated in U.S. public records with the **1989 Avianca Flight 203 bombing**, a major mass-casualty attack that killed 110 people.[^1]
- Reported to be serving **multiple life sentences** in U.S. federal custody for terrorism-related offenses tied to that case.[^1]

## Legal Case (High-Level)

- Prosecuted in U.S. federal court under statutes covering attacks on civil aviation and terrorism-related crimes when U.S. jurisdictional links applied (e.g., U.S. victims).[^1]
- Public sources summarize the outcome as **convictions** and **long-term imprisonment** in the U.S. federal system.[^1]

## Additional Context (Careful / High-Level)

- Open-source accounts characterize him as a cartel operative operating during the period of escalated violence in Colombia (targeting state institutions, rivals, and civilians).[^1]
- Because secondary sources sometimes mix allegations, investigative claims, and court-proven facts, treat attribution carefully and prioritize court records where available.[^1]
