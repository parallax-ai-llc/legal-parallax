## Summary
Faisal Shahzad attempted to detonate a car bomb in Times Square, New York on May 1, 2010. The bomb failed. He was arrested at JFK Airport while trying to flee. Sentenced to life.

## Background
- Shahzad was born in Pakistan and later became a naturalized U.S. citizen.
- Reporting on his case commonly notes that he lived in the United States for several years prior to the 2010 attack attempt.

## Times Square Car Bomb Attempt (2010)
- Date/location: May 1, 2010, Times Square, New York City.
- Method: a vehicle-borne improvised explosive device (VBIED) placed in a parked SUV.
- Outcome: the device did not detonate as intended; the attempt caused evacuation and a major law-enforcement response.

## Investigation, Arrest, and Legal Outcome
- Shahzad was identified and arrested after the failed attempt; widely reported accounts state he was detained at John F. Kennedy International Airport while attempting to leave the U.S.
- He faced federal terrorism-related charges in U.S. court and ultimately received a life sentence.

## Why the Case Matters
- The incident became a reference point for post-9/11 domestic counterterrorism, including rapid incident response in crowded public spaces.
- It also contributed to public discussion of radicalization pathways and international militant networks.
