## Summary

ISIS militants massacred between 1,566 and 1,700 Shia Iraqi Air Force cadets at Camp Speicher near Tikrit. One of the largest mass killings committed by ISIS.

## Background and Context

- The massacre occurred during ISIS’s rapid territorial gains in Iraq in mid‑2014, including the capture of Mosul and the collapse of local security positions in parts of Salah ad‑Din province.
- Camp Speicher (near Tikrit) was associated with Iraqi security training facilities; many victims were unarmed trainees or cadets at the time they were captured.

## Location

- Camp Speicher is located near Tikrit, close to the Tigris River.
- Reports and later investigations tied killings to multiple execution sites in and around Tikrit, including areas where mass graves were later documented.

## Victims

- Victims were primarily Shia Iraqi Air Force cadets and trainees.
- The commonly cited death toll range (roughly 1,566–1,700) reflects differing counts in official statements and later documentation.

## Perpetrators and Methods

- ISIS militants carried out the capture, detention, and mass execution of the cadets.
- The massacre became widely known through ISIS propaganda, including photographs and videos used for intimidation and recruitment.

## Aftermath

- Families sought information about missing cadets for months and years, with public protests and demands for government action.
- Iraqi authorities and local actors later reported the discovery of mass graves and undertook recovery and identification efforts.

## Investigations and Accountability

- The massacre was cited in Iraqi domestic prosecutions and in international reporting on ISIS crimes.
- Identification of victims relied on forensic work where possible, including the documentation of grave sites and collection of evidence.

## Legacy

- The Camp Speicher massacre is frequently referenced as one of ISIS’s largest single mass killings in Iraq and a symbol of sectarian violence during the 2014 conflict.
