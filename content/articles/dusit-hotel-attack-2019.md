## Summary

Al-Shabaab militants attacked the DusitD2 hotel complex in Nairobi killing 21 people. The attack included a suicide bomber and armed gunmen who engaged in a 20-hour siege.

## Background

- The DusitD2 complex is part of the larger 14 Riverside development in Nairobi, which includes hotel, offices, and dining areas.
- The attack occurred amid a broader regional security context in which Al-Shabaab has targeted Kenyan interests in response to Kenya’s military involvement in Somalia.

## Attack Overview

- **Date:** 15 January 2019
- **Location:** DusitD2 hotel complex (14 Riverside), Nairobi, Kenya
- **Tactics reported:** a suicide bombing followed by an armed assault and hostage-taking inside the complex.
- **Duration:** the incident developed into an extended siege that lasted into the following day.

### Sequence (high-level)

- An explosion was reported near the complex, followed by gunmen entering and firing inside the premises.
- Civilians and staff sheltered in place, fled the area, or were evacuated as security forces established a perimeter.

## Casualties and Impact

- **Fatalities:** widely reported as **21** victims (excluding attackers), with additional injuries reported.
- The attack disrupted businesses in the area and drew national attention to urban counter-terror preparedness.

## Response and Resolution

- Kenyan security forces (including specialized units) conducted clearing operations and room-by-room searches.
- The situation was declared under control after the attackers were neutralized and the site was secured.

## Attribution

- Al-Shabaab claimed responsibility for the attack in public statements.

## Aftermath

- The incident contributed to renewed discussion of security screening, emergency response procedures, and private-sector preparedness for complex attacks.
- Memorials and public statements honored victims and first responders; investigations sought to reconstruct planning and facilitation networks.
