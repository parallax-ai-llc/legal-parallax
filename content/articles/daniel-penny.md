## Summary

Former Marine acquitted of manslaughter for choking Jordan Neely to death on a New York subway.

## Background

- Daniel Penny is a U.S. Marine Corps veteran.
- He became widely known in 2023–2024 due to a New York City subway death involving Jordan Neely.

## 2023 New York City subway incident (overview)

- Date: May 2023.
- Location: A New York City subway train.
- Outcome: Jordan Neely died after Penny restrained him with a chokehold.
- The event was recorded on video and drew extensive public attention.

## Legal proceedings (high level)

- Prosecutors charged Penny in connection with Neely’s death.
- The case became a prominent public debate about public safety, self-defense, and the use of force.
- In 2024, Penny was acquitted of manslaughter (as summarized in many contemporary reports).

## Public discussion and significance

- The case was frequently discussed in the context of:
  - Mental health and homelessness in New York City.
  - Bystander intervention and “good Samaritan” narratives.
  - Standards for criminal liability when restraining someone in a public setting.
  - Media framing and polarization around high-profile trials.

## Key terms

- **Chokehold**: A restraint that compresses the neck; depending on method and duration, it can be medically dangerous.
- **Manslaughter**: A criminal homicide charge generally involving unlawful killing without the intent required for murder (definitions vary by jurisdiction).
