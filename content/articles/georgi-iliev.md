## Summary

Bulgarian crime boss and wrestler who controlled organized crime before being assassinated.

## Background

- Georgi Iliev was a Bulgarian organized-crime figure who also had a public profile linked to sport and business.
- English-language sources most commonly describe him as a crime boss; Bulgarian sources and reporting often place him in the post-communist “wrestler” (борци) milieu that rose during the 1990s.

## Notability

Iliev is primarily notable for:

- His role in Bulgarian organized crime during the post-1989 transition period.
- His association with the VIS group (often written as VIS/ВИС), a well-known brand of 1990s-era Bulgarian security and insurance rackets.
- His assassination in 2005, which became a widely reported contract-killing case.

## Public profile and activities

High-level points reported in mainstream summaries include:

- **Sport:** He is frequently described as a wrestler (or part of the “wrestler” subculture tied to security companies in the 1990s).
- **Business front:** Like many contemporaries in the region, organized-crime influence was commonly reported to overlap with nominal business entities (e.g., security firms, entertainment, hospitality).
- **Organized-crime control:** He is described as having controlled or influenced criminal networks before his death.

## Assassination (2005)

- **Date:** 25 August 2005.
- **Circumstances:** Reported as being shot and killed at a beachside location in Bulgaria.
- **Impact:** His killing was treated as a major underworld event and is often cited when discussing contract killings and organized-crime violence in Bulgaria in the 2000s.

## Context: post-communist organized crime in Bulgaria

For readers unfamiliar with the era, common features of Bulgarian organized crime in the 1990s–2000s (as described in general reporting) include:

- Rapid privatization and weak institutions creating space for protection rackets.
- Security companies and “insurance” schemes acting as organized-crime cover.
- Public, high-profile violence (including assassinations) connected to disputes over territory, revenue streams, and influence.
