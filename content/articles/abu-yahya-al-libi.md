## Background

- **Birth name**: Hassan Qaid (also rendered as Hasan Qa'id).[^1]
- **Origin**: Libyan national who became a prominent ideologue and operational figure within al-Qaeda.
- **Reputation**: Known for public-facing religious messaging and propaganda, often released via jihadist media channels.

## Key Events (Expanded)

- **Captured (early 2000s)**: Detained after the U.S.-led invasion of Afghanistan and held at the U.S. detention facility at **Bagram Air Base** in Afghanistan.[^1]
- **Escape (2005)**: One of several prisoners who escaped from Bagram and later re-emerged in militant networks.[^1]
- **Rise in al-Qaeda**: Over subsequent years, became a senior figure frequently described as a **deputy leader** and a major voice for al-Qaeda’s strategic messaging.[^1][^2]

## Significance

- **Ideological role**: Helped articulate al-Qaeda’s religious-ideological framing for recruitment and morale.
- **Operational influence**: Frequently cited in reporting as part of al-Qaeda’s upper leadership, bridging propaganda with strategic direction.
- **Counterterrorism relevance**: His death was presented by U.S. officials as a meaningful disruption to al-Qaeda’s leadership cadre.[^2]
