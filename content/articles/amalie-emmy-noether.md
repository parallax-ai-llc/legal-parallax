## Why She Matters

Emmy Noether is widely regarded as one of the most influential mathematicians of the 20th century. Her work reshaped **abstract algebra** and provided a foundational principle connecting **symmetry and conservation laws** in physics.

## Key Contributions (High-Level)

- **Noether's theorem (1918):** In modern physics, continuous symmetries of an action correspond to conservation laws (e.g., time-translation symmetry ↔ energy conservation). This became essential in classical mechanics, relativity, and quantum field theory.
- **Noetherian rings/conditions:** In algebra, “Noetherian” captures a finiteness condition (ascending chain condition) that makes many classification and proof techniques possible.
- **Structural approach to algebra:** Helped move algebra from computational invariant theory toward the axiomatic, structural style that became standard in the 20th century.

## Context and Constraints

Noether’s early career unfolded under legal and institutional restrictions on women in German universities. For years she lectured under the names of male colleagues or without pay before eventually securing the right to teach and later a professorial title.

## Notes

This page uses her full name (Amalie Emmy Noether). In many historical and mathematical references she appears simply as **Emmy Noether**.
