## Market Manipulation (2000–2001)

Ketan Parekh became widely associated with a set of heavily traded shares sometimes referred to in Indian financial media as the **"K-10"** stocks. The general pattern described in public accounts is that coordinated buying, concentrated positions, and aggressive financing were used to push prices up, followed by a sharp reversal when liquidity dried up and leverage was forced to unwind.[^1]

Key elements commonly cited in summaries of the episode include:

- **Concentration in a small basket of stocks** (especially technology / "new economy" names during the dot-com era).
- **Use of leverage and funding lines** to sustain buying pressure.
- **Circular trading / synchronized activity** (described in secondary sources as a way to create the appearance of demand and momentum).
- **Contagion via broker and banking exposures**, where credit stress can transmit into broader market selling.

## Why It Mattered

The 2001 Indian market crash is often discussed as a case study in how:

- speculative narratives can amplify price moves,
- leverage can convert a price correction into a liquidation spiral, and
- concentrated manipulation risk can propagate into systemic concerns when intermediaries are exposed.

## Regulatory / Legal Aftermath (High Level)

Public summaries note that Indian regulators (notably **SEBI**) took actions that included restrictions on market access and penalties in connection with manipulation findings.[^1] Many retrospective write-ups also mention broader governance and risk-control discussions across brokers, exchanges, and parts of the financial system following the episode.

## Key Terms

- **Market manipulation:** Trading or related conduct intended to distort prices, volume, or price discovery.
- **Pump-and-dump (conceptual):** A pattern where prices are promoted or pushed up and then sold into, leaving later buyers with losses.
- **Leverage / margin funding:** Borrowed money used for trading, which increases both upside and downside and can force selling when prices fall.
