## Summary

Private security guards stole $282 million from the Dar Es Salaam bank in Baghdad, the largest bank robbery in history. The money was never fully recovered.

## Context

- **Location:** Baghdad, Iraq.
- **Target:** Dar Es Salaam Investment Bank (commonly referenced as “Dar Es Salaam bank”).
- **Period:** 2007, during a time of severe insecurity and institutional strain in post‑invasion Iraq.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The robbery was carried out by **private security guards assigned to protect the bank**, rather than by an external gang.
- Reports describe the theft as involving **large cash holdings** held by the bank at the time.
- The total widely cited amount is **about $282 million**, often described as the largest bank robbery on record.

## Immediate Aftermath

- The stolen funds were **not fully recovered**.
- The case became notable internationally because it highlighted how **insider access and weak oversight** can enable extremely large thefts.

## Why It Matters

- **Insider threat:** The incident is frequently cited as an example of how trusted personnel can exploit access.
- **Security environment:** The scale of the loss is often linked to the broader instability and governance challenges in Iraq in 2007.
- **Historical comparison:** It is commonly compared against other major robberies, but stands out due to the **magnitude** and the **inside-job** narrative.

## Key Facts (Commonly Cited)

- **Approximate amount:** $282 million.
- **Nature:** Bank robbery / theft from a financial institution.
- **Perpetrators (reported):** Private security guards.
- **Outcome:** Not fully recovered; details and accountability have remained limited in many summaries.
