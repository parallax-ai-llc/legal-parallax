## What Happened (High-Level)

Genesis Global Capital (often referred to as “Genesis Lending”) was a crypto lending business that paused withdrawals and new loan originations during the 2022 industry downturn and subsequently entered Chapter 11 bankruptcy in early 2023. The collapse is commonly described as part of a broader contagion cycle in crypto credit markets, where losses at large borrowers and counterparties impaired lenders’ balance sheets and liquidity.

## Key Contributing Factors

- **Counterparty credit risk**: Losses tied to distressed borrowers/counterparties reduced the value of Genesis’s loan book and/or recoverability of assets.
- **Liquidity mismatch**: A lender can face a run-like dynamic when short-term liabilities (customer redemption demands) collide with illiquid or impaired assets.
- **Market-wide deleveraging**: Falling crypto prices and reduced risk appetite made refinancing and asset sales more difficult.
- **Contagion effects**: Failures and insolvencies across major crypto firms increased uncertainty, tightened credit, and accelerated withdrawals.

## Stakeholders & Exposure (At a Glance)

- **Creditors**: Public reporting around the bankruptcy indicated billions of dollars in claims from a range of institutional and programmatic counterparties.
- **DCG ecosystem links**: Genesis was associated with the Digital Currency Group (DCG) corporate umbrella, which became a focal point in discussions about intercompany obligations and restructuring options.
- **Retail-facing programs**: Some yield/earn products in the market routed customer assets into institutional lenders; when liquidity halted, retail users were indirectly impacted through those programs.

## Aftermath

- **Bankruptcy and restructuring**: Chapter 11 proceedings aimed to stabilize the estate, assess claims, and propose a plan for creditor recoveries.
- **Regulatory and legal scrutiny**: The episode contributed to increased attention on crypto lending disclosures, risk controls, and the marketing of yield products.
- **Industry lessons**: The failure reinforced the importance of transparency, conservative collateral and margin practices, and clear segregation/limitations on rehypothecation where applicable.
- **Creditor negotiations**: Restructuring processes typically involve creditor committees, claim reconciliation, and plan negotiations to determine recovery mechanics.
