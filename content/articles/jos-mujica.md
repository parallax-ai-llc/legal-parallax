## Context & Notes

- **Classification:** The frontmatter currently lists the occupation as "Terrorist". Mujica was a member of the Tupamaros guerrilla movement, but he is more commonly described in mainstream sources as a **Uruguayan politician** and **former guerrilla** rather than categorized as a criminal figure in contemporary reference works.[^1]
- **Why he is discussed:** Mujica is frequently cited in political and ethics discussions for his public positions on consumerism, inequality, and democratic norms, as well as for choosing to live relatively modestly while in office.[^1]

## Key Themes (High-Level)

- **Austerity & symbolism:** His personal lifestyle was widely treated as a political statement—emphasizing restraint, simplicity, and distance from elite privilege.[^1]
- **Democratic legitimacy after conflict:** Mujica’s trajectory (armed struggle → imprisonment → electoral politics) is often referenced in debates about how societies reintegrate former insurgents and repair political legitimacy after authoritarian periods.[^1]
- **Pragmatism in office:** Commentary about his presidency commonly highlights a pragmatic governing style despite radical origins, including coalition politics and institutional continuity.[^1]
