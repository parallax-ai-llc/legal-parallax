## Summary
Woo Bum-kon was a South Korean police officer who killed 56 people in a massacre in Uiryeong County on April 26-27, 1982. It was the deadliest peacetime mass shooting by a single person at the time.

## Key Facts
- **Location:** Uiryeong County, South Gyeongsang Province, South Korea
- **Dates:** April 26–27, 1982
- **Perpetrator:** Woo Bum-kon (then a South Korean police officer)
- **Fatalities:** 56 people killed (widely reported figure)
- **Historical note:** Often cited as the deadliest peacetime mass shooting by a single perpetrator at the time.

## Background
Public accounts describe Woo Bum-kon as having served as a police officer prior to the 1982 incident. Coverage typically focuses on the scale of the attack and the fact that he had access to police weapons.

## 1982 Uiryeong Incident (High-Level)
- The incident unfolded across multiple locations in Uiryeong County over the course of roughly one night.
- Reports commonly note that Woo used **police-issued firearms and ammunition**, and that the violence continued as he moved between villages.
- Many summaries emphasize the **speed and surprise** of the attacks, and the difficulty of stopping him in the early phase.

## Aftermath and Impact
- The event became a reference point in discussions of **mass violence**, **police armories**, and **institutional safeguards**.
- In later years, it is frequently included in comparative lists of large-scale shootings worldwide, particularly in historical overviews.
