## Additional Context

- **Political role:** Joseph Lambert (born 5 February 1961 in Jacmel, Haiti) is a Haitian politician who has served multiple terms as **President of the Haitian Senate**, including 2006–2008, 2018–2019, and 2021–2023.
- **2021 presidential dispute:** After the assassination of President **Jovenel Moïse** in July 2021, a group of Haitian senators nominated Lambert as a proposed interim president. Reporting at the time described this as **disputed** amid competing claims of authority and limited constitutional guidance.
- **Sanctions (2022):** On 4 November 2022, the **U.S. Treasury (OFAC)** and the **U.S. Department of State** announced sanctions/designations related to allegations of involvement in the global illicit drug trade, corruption, and gross human rights violations. Canada announced coordinated sanctions the same day under its Haiti-related regulations.
- **Security incident (2023):** Media reports stated Lambert was **injured** in an armed attack in Port-au-Prince in January 2023.
