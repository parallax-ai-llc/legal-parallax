## Identity & Names

The label **"Golden State Killer"** is a media and investigative umbrella term used to describe a single offender responsible for multiple crime series across California.
The offender was identified as **Joseph James DeAngelo** (born 1945), and many sources now consolidate coverage under his name.

## Overview of Crimes (High-Level)

Commonly cited components of the consolidated case include:
- **Burglary and stalking** patterns that escalated over time.
- **Sexual assaults** attributed to the "East Area Rapist" series.
- **Homicides** attributed to the "Original Night Stalker" series.
- A broad geographic spread across Northern and Southern California over more than a decade.

## Investigation & Identification

Key investigation themes frequently noted in reputable summaries:
- **Linkage of separate case series** through behavioral patterns and forensic evidence.
- **DNA evidence** collected from crime scenes and preserved across decades.
- **Investigative genetic genealogy** (familial matching using public genetic databases) that helped narrow suspects.
- Traditional police work (surveillance, records checks, and interviews) culminating in the 2018 arrest.

## Prosecution & Outcome

In 2020, DeAngelo **pleaded guilty** and provided **admissions** related to additional charges.
He received **multiple life sentences without the possibility of parole** in California state court.
