## Background and Role

- Fausto Isidro Meza Flores is commonly identified in public reporting as a Mexican drug trafficker known by the alias **"El Chapo Isidro"**.
- He has been described as a leader of **Los Mazatlecos** and as a high-ranking figure associated with the **Beltrán Leyva Cartel**.[^1]
- Public sources describe him as having worked with or under senior cartel figures prior to later leadership roles.[^1]

## Publicly Reported Activities (High Level)

- Reported involvement includes trafficking of multiple narcotics (commonly cited: methamphetamine, cocaine, heroin, and marijuana) with cross-border smuggling routes.[^1]
- Reporting also links him to violent inter-cartel conflict in northern Mexico, particularly in periods of heightened clashes around 2010.[^1]
- Sources describe his organization as operating in parts of **Sinaloa** and neighboring regions, though specific territorial control and relationships can vary by year and by source.[^1]

## Wanted / Legal Status (Public Record)

- Open-source reporting notes that he has been listed by U.S. authorities as a high-profile fugitive in recent years (including placement on major wanted lists).[^1]
