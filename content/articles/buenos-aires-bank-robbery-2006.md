## Summary

Gang used a toy gun and a fake pizza delivery to rob the Banco Río in Acassuso, Argentina of $19 million from 143 safe deposit boxes. They escaped through a tunnel to a motorboat.

## What Happened (Expanded)

- The robbery targeted **safe deposit boxes** at the Banco Río branch in Acassuso (Buenos Aires Province).
- The group used **deception** at the start (including a **fake pizza delivery**) to help initiate entry and control the scene.
- A **toy gun** was used as part of the intimidation tactic while executing the theft.
- The theft involved emptying **143** safe deposit boxes and taking an estimated **$19 million** in valuables.

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)

- **Social engineering / misdirection:** a benign pretext (pizza delivery) helped reduce initial suspicion.
- **Controlled access:** the group sought to reach the vault and deposit boxes rather than focusing on teller cash.
- **Time management:** the operation relied on buying time long enough to complete removal from many boxes.
- **Prepared escape route:** the perpetrators exited through a **tunnel**, enabling separation from the bank’s main exits.
- **Getaway by water:** the escape was reported to end at a **motorboat**, reducing traceability via roads.

## Why This Case Is Notable

- It combines **low-tech physical infrastructure** (a tunnel) with **simple psychological tactics** (a delivery disguise).
- The primary target was **customer-held assets** (deposit boxes), which can be harder to inventory and value quickly.
- The escape plan was designed to **avoid predictable choke points** (front doors, nearby streets, immediate perimeter).

## Investigation Considerations (General)

- Incidents of this type typically lead investigators to focus on:
  - **Access to construction know-how** and time needed to prepare a tunnel.
  - **Local reconnaissance** (branch layout, security routines, and surrounding terrain).
  - **Logistics** for moving and fencing mixed valuables from many deposit boxes.
