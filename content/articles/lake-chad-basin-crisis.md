## Summary

Boko Haram and ISWAP operations across northeastern Nigeria, Niger, Chad, and Cameroon have killed 350,000 and displaced 3 million. One of the world's worst humanitarian crises.

## Background

The Lake Chad Basin spans parts of northeastern Nigeria (including Borno State), southeastern Niger, western Chad, and northern Cameroon. The region combines borderland trade routes, rural communities, and difficult-to-govern terrain that has shaped how armed groups move, recruit, and hide.

## Main Actors

- **Boko Haram**: Islamist insurgent movement originating in northeastern Nigeria, associated with mass-casualty attacks and kidnappings.
- **ISWAP (Islamic State West Africa Province)**: A major Boko Haram splinter faction that aligned with the Islamic State; often described as operating with its own leadership and strategies.
- **State forces**: Nigerian, Nigerien, Chadian, and Cameroonian security forces, including joint and cross-border operations.
- **Civilians and self-defense groups**: Communities affected by raids and coercion; some areas have organized local defense initiatives.

## Conflict Dynamics (High-Level)

- **Cross-border mobility** is central: armed groups exploit borders, lake islands, and remote corridors to evade pressure.
- **Civilian targeting** has included village raids, abductions, extortion, and intimidation aimed at controlling territory and resources.
- **Humanitarian access constraints** arise from insecurity, road risks, and restrictions around military operations.
- **Environmental stress** (including water variability in the Lake Chad ecosystem) can compound livelihood pressures, though it is not the sole driver of violence.

## Humanitarian Impact

- Large-scale **internal displacement** and refugee flows have affected Nigeria and neighboring states.
- Disruption to **agriculture, fishing, and trade** has contributed to food insecurity.
- Many communities face a cycle of **insecurity → displacement → reduced livelihoods → heightened vulnerability**.

## Regional and International Response

- The **Multinational Joint Task Force (MNJTF)** coordinates regional security efforts among Lake Chad Basin states.
- International organizations and NGOs support **emergency relief**, protection services, and stabilization programs where access permits.
- Policy debates commonly focus on balancing **security operations** with **civilian protection**, accountability, and long-term governance reforms.

## Geography Notes

- The basin includes **wetlands, islands, and seasonal waterways**, which can change navigability and surveillance conditions.
- The surrounding Sahelian zones connect the crisis to wider instability patterns in West and Central Africa.

## Open Questions / Ongoing Issues

- How durable are gains from military pressure without sustained governance, services, and economic recovery?
- What approaches most effectively reduce recruitment incentives and protect civilians in remote areas?
- How should reintegration, amnesty, and justice mechanisms be designed for defectors and affected communities?
