## Background

- Rakhimov is an Uzbek businessman and underworld figure frequently described in international reporting as a prominent organized-crime leader from the post-Soviet era.
- Public profiles commonly place his early influence in Tashkent and connect him to networks involved in commodities, logistics, and informal protection arrangements that emerged during the 1990s.

## Allegations and Sanctions

- In 2012, the United States Department of the Treasury (Office of Foreign Assets Control) designated Rakhimov under the Foreign Narcotics Kingpin Designation Act, describing him as a major heroin trafficker and an organizer with influence over transnational criminal activity.[^2]
- Such designations generally restrict dealings with designated persons under U.S. jurisdiction and can complicate banking, travel, and international business relationships.
- Rakhimov has disputed wrongdoing in public reporting; summaries of his case often note that allegations, designations, and legal findings can differ by country and forum.

## International Boxing Association (AIBA/IBA)

- Rakhimov became a visible figure in amateur boxing governance and was elected president of the International Boxing Association (commonly known as AIBA at the time, later IBA).[^3]
- Reporting around his election highlighted concerns from sporting bodies and national federations about governance, reputational risk, and the impact of sanctions allegations on Olympic-affiliated sport.
- He later stepped aside from the presidency amid controversy, while IBA/AIBA continued to face broader questions about governance and Olympic recognition.
