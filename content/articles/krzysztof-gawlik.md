## Additional Details

### Identity and Alias

- Commonly referred to as **Krzysztof Gawlik**.
- Wikipedia describes him as a Polish serial killer nicknamed **"Skorpion"** ("Scorpion").[^1]
- Wikipedia lists his birth year as **1965** and his birthplace as **Świerczyna (Pleszew County), Poland**.[^1]

### Background (as reported in public sources)

- He reportedly grew up in a family where his father worked as a miner and where domestic violence and alcohol abuse were present.[^1]
- After finishing school, he is described as having worked at the **Julia coal mine in Wałbrzych** until it closed (reported as 1996).[^1]
- He later attempted to run a business that reportedly went bankrupt.[^1]

### Financial and Criminal Context

- Wikipedia reports that he accumulated significant debt (reported as **over 90,000 zł** by 1999).[^1]
- He was reportedly arrested in **June 2000** on suspicion of robbery and released on bail in **January 2001** (with bail paid by his wife, per Wikipedia).[^1]

### Crimes (high-level)

- Wikipedia attributes **five victims** to Gawlik, with the cited crime period focused on **February–March 2001**.[^1]
- Public reporting describes several victims as being contacted through advertisements (e.g., escort-related ads or other listings).[^1]

### Modus Operandi / Signature

- Wikipedia states that he used a **Škorpion-type silenced machine gun**, which contributed to the "Skorpion" nickname.[^1]
- Accounts describe victims being shot multiple times, including a characteristic execution-style shot to the back of the head.[^1]
- Some reports also mention additional violence such as stabbing and throat cutting in at least one case.[^1]

### Selected Victim Details Mentioned by Wikipedia

- A 17-year-old prostitute (identified by Wikipedia as **Sylwia L.**) was reported as a victim in **Poznań** in early February 2001.[^1]
- Wikipedia also describes the killing of a Ukrainian woman (identified as **Lesia H.**) and a man associated with her (identified as **Tomasz S.**) in **Wrocław** a few days after the first murder.[^1]
- Wikipedia indicates two additional victims in March 2001 (described as a married couple in Wrocław), bringing the reported total to five victims.[^1]

### Trial, Sentence, and Incarceration

- Wikipedia lists the conviction as **murder** and the criminal penalty as **life imprisonment**.[^1]
- Wikipedia also notes that he **attempted to escape prison twice**, and was caught during both attempts.[^1]
