## Summary

Kurdish-Turkish drug lord and one of the largest heroin traffickers in Europe, killed in an extrajudicial assassination.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1994 | Assassinated by suspected state agents |

## Background

- Born in Lice (Diyarbakır Province), Turkey (per Wikipedia).
- Described as Kurdish; his mother is reported as an Armenian from the Lice district.

## Activities (high-level)

- Wikipedia describes Cantürk as a drug trafficker and arms trafficker.
- Reported to have financed or partnered with smugglers beginning in the mid-1970s.
- Reported involvement includes arms trafficking (late 1970s) and smuggling of high-value goods (early 1980s).
- As of the early 1980s, Wikipedia reports he controlled portions of the heroin market in Diyarbakır and related overseas sales.

## Notable incidents & allegations

- Wikipedia reports an arrest (1984) by a military court in Ankara on allegations including membership in the PKK and another Kurdish organization.
- Wikipedia reports alleged drug-transport and partnership claims tied to the *Kısmetim-1* incident (late 1992).
- Some reported claims connect him to political violence and state–criminal nexus allegations later discussed in the context of the Susurluk/Ergenekon-era reporting; these remain contested and should be treated as allegations rather than settled fact.

## Death

- Wikipedia reports he was abducted on 14 Jan 1994 by assailants dressed like police and that his body was found the next day near Sapanca, Turkey.
