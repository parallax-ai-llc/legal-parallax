## Summary
Ismael 'El Mayo' Zambada was a co-leader of the Sinaloa Cartel and one of the longest-serving drug lords in Mexican history. He was arrested in the US in 2024 after apparently being tricked into flying there.

## Background
- **Full name**: Ismael Zambada García.
- **Common aliases**: "El Mayo" (a long-running nickname in reporting and law-enforcement coverage).
- **Known for**: Maintaining influence within the Sinaloa Cartel for decades while generally avoiding the public profile associated with other cartel leaders.

## Role in the Sinaloa Cartel (high level)
- Zambada has been widely described by Mexican and U.S. authorities as a senior leader within the Sinaloa Cartel.
- He was frequently characterized as a *co-leader* alongside other prominent figures tied to the organization.
- Unlike some cartel figures who cultivated media attention, he was commonly portrayed as operating through trusted intermediaries and longstanding relationships.

## Public and law-enforcement visibility
- For many years, public photographs and verified appearances were comparatively limited relative to other major cartel leaders.
- His longevity in organized crime coverage is often attributed to a combination of operational security, layered networks, and disciplined communications.

## Arrest and detention (2024)
- In 2024, U.S. authorities announced his arrest after he arrived in the United States.
- Reporting around the event described the trip as the result of deception or manipulation, with accounts suggesting he was "tricked" into traveling.
- Following the arrest, he was held in U.S. custody, and the case became a major development in U.S.–Mexico counternarcotics efforts.

## Why he mattered (context)
- Zambada is frequently cited as an example of a "long-game" cartel leadership model: less public, more networked, and resilient across multiple enforcement waves.
- His capture was treated as symbolically significant because it targeted a figure associated with continuity rather than short-lived succession battles.
