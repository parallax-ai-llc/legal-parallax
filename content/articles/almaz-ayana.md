## Expanded Profile

### Why she matters

- Ayana is best known for transforming the modern standard in the women’s 10,000 metres.
- Her 2016 Olympic final in Rio de Janeiro is frequently cited as one of the fastest, deepest 10,000m races ever run.
- The scale of her 2016 improvement over the long-standing record (set in 1993) made the performance historically notable.

### Key achievements (high level)

- **Olympic champion (2016)** in the **10,000m**, setting a **world record of 29:17.45**.
- **Olympic medalist (2016)** in the **5,000m**.
- Regular competitor on the international circuit for Ethiopia in track and road events.

### Notable performances

- **10,000m:** 29:17.45 (Rio 2016, world record at the time).
- **5,000m:** set a **world record in 2016** (later surpassed).

### Style and strengths

- Typically described as a high-endurance runner with the ability to sustain a long, fast surge.
- Most effective in championship settings when the pace becomes hard early and stays hard.

### Legacy and context

- Ethiopia has a deep tradition in women’s distance running; Ayana’s 2016 peak sits alongside other landmark Ethiopian Olympic and world performances.
- Her Rio 2016 record became a modern reference point for the event, used in comparisons of subsequent championship finals and record attempts.
