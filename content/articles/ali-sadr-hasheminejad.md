## Legal case (U.S. sanctions)

Public reporting describes Ali Sadr Hashemi Nejad (also known as Ali Sadr) as facing U.S. federal charges tied to alleged sanctions violations connected to a Venezuelan housing-related construction project from 2010.[^1][^2]

Later coverage notes that the case became highly contested and that, after extended proceedings, the charges were dismissed **with prejudice** due to findings described as prosecutorial misconduct—meaning the government could not refile the same charges.[^3]

### Why this matters

- **Sanctions enforcement:** The case is frequently cited in discussions of how sanctions cases are investigated and prosecuted.
- **Disclosure obligations:** The dismissal is also referenced in commentary about evidence disclosure (Brady) and prosecutor conduct in complex financial cases.

## Glossary

- **Sanctions evasion:** Efforts to bypass legal restrictions on trade, finance, or transactions imposed by a government.
- **Dismissed with prejudice:** A dismissal that bars the case from being filed again on the same claim.
- **Prosecutorial misconduct:** Improper conduct by prosecutors, which can include failures to disclose required evidence.
