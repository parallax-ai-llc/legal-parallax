## Summary

Boko Haram kidnapping of 110 girls from a school in Dapchi, Yobe State.

## Background

- Dapchi is a town in **Yobe State**, northeastern Nigeria, an area affected by a long-running insurgency and repeated attacks on civilians.
- The kidnapping occurred in the broader context of mass-abduction tactics used by insurgent groups in the region to intimidate communities and extract leverage.

## What Happened

- The abducted students were taken from the **Government Girls Science and Technical College** in Dapchi.
- The incident is commonly described as a **mass kidnapping** attributed to **Boko Haram** (with reporting also discussing factions and splinter groups operating in the same insurgency ecosystem).

## Immediate Aftermath

- The abduction triggered widespread concern in Nigeria and internationally, recalling earlier mass kidnappings such as the **Chibok schoolgirls** case.
- Nigerian authorities faced pressure to locate and return the students and to improve security around schools in vulnerable regions.

## Outcomes (High-Level)

- Reporting on the case describes **large-scale releases/returns** of many of the kidnapped girls in the weeks following the abduction.
- One student, **Leah Sharibu**, became a prominent symbol of the incident in public discourse because she was reported as **not released** with others.

## Public Response and Significance

- The kidnapping intensified debates about:
  - school safety and the protection of children in conflict zones,
  - government capacity and counterinsurgency strategy,
  - negotiation, ransom, and prisoner-exchange dynamics in hostage situations.
- The event is frequently cited in discussions of Nigeria’s broader challenge of **kidnapping-for-leverage** and attacks on education.
