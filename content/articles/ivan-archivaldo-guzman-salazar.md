## Summary
Iván Archivaldo Guzmán Salazar is a son of El Chapo and a leader of the Los Chapitos faction of the Sinaloa Cartel, heavily involved in fentanyl trafficking. Arrested in 2024.

## Background
- Iván Archivaldo Guzmán Salazar is one of the best-known sons of Joaquín "El Chapo" Guzmán.
- In open-source reporting, he is most often grouped with the "Los Chapitos" ("Little Chapos") leadership circle, alongside other Guzmán family members.
- Coverage frequently emphasizes a transition from the earlier Sinaloa Cartel leadership era to a more factional, networked structure after El Chapo’s removal from Mexico.

## Role in the Sinaloa Cartel (Los Chapitos)
- Los Chapitos is commonly described as a faction within the broader Sinaloa Cartel ecosystem rather than a fully separate cartel.
- Reporting often attributes to this faction:
  - coordination of trafficking routes and logistics (Mexico → U.S. markets)
  - control or influence over local security/enforcement networks
  - involvement in synthetic-drug production chains, particularly fentanyl precursors and pill-press distribution

## Law Enforcement Status (High-Level)
- Multiple governments have pursued investigations into the broader Sinaloa Cartel and Los Chapitos over many years.
- Public reporting on arrest/custody status can be inconsistent across outlets and time; readers typically verify current status via:
  - official government announcements
  - sanctions lists
  - reputable court filings

## Why He Matters (Context)
- Los Chapitos is frequently cited in policy and media narratives because fentanyl production and trafficking became a major public-health and political issue in North America.
- In many analyses, the faction’s prominence is tied to:
  - the industrialization of synthetic opioid supply chains
  - the evolution of cartel governance into smaller, semi-autonomous groups
