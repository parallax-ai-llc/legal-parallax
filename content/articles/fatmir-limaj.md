## Background

- Fatmir Limaj (born 4 February 1971) is a Kosovo Albanian politician and former commander in the Kosovo Liberation Army (KLA).
- He became a prominent public figure during and after the Kosovo War, later transitioning into formal politics in post-war Kosovo.

## Role in the Kosovo War (high-level)

- During the 1998–1999 conflict, Limaj was associated with the KLA, an armed group that fought Serbian/Yugoslav forces.
- In international reporting and later legal proceedings, his name is most often linked to allegations regarding the treatment of detainees during the war.

## ICTY proceedings (context)

- Limaj was indicted by the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) in connection with alleged abuses at the Llapushnik (Lapushnik) detention facility.
- The case became notable both for its focus on command responsibility and for broader challenges the tribunal faced in war-crimes prosecutions, including witness cooperation and intimidation concerns (as discussed in public coverage of multiple ICTY-era cases).
- He was acquitted in the tribunal’s first-instance judgment in 2005 in the case commonly referred to as the Llapushnik/Lapushnik camp case.

## Political career (overview)

- After the war, Limaj entered politics and has been associated with major Kosovo political parties and later splinter/alternative movements.
- He has held senior government roles, including ministerial responsibilities related to transport/infrastructure (portfolio names have varied across different cabinets).
- In Kosovo’s domestic political landscape, he has been a recurring figure in coalition negotiations and party leadership dynamics.

## Public profile and controversy

- Supporters have portrayed him as a wartime leader who helped build post-war institutions.
- Critics have continued to associate him with wartime allegations and with concerns about governance and accountability in Kosovo’s young institutions.
