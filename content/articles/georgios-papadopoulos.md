## Summary

Greek military dictator who led the 1967 coup and ruled during the Greek junta.

## Career Timeline

## Key Context

- **Historical setting**: Papadopoulos is primarily associated with the Greek military dictatorship (1967–1974), a period marked by the suspension of normal parliamentary politics and heavy control over public life.
- **Role**: He is widely described as one of the central organizers and early leaders of the 1967 seizure of power and the initial phase of junta governance.

## Governance (High-Level)

- **Political control**: The junta period is commonly characterized by restrictions on political parties, civil liberties, and opposition activity.
- **Security apparatus**: Accounts of the era frequently emphasize the use of policing and intelligence services to monitor and suppress dissent.
- **Censorship**: Contemporary reporting and later histories note censorship and limitations on press and expression as a defining feature of the regime.

## 1973 "Liberalization" and Internal Overthrow

- In 1973, Papadopoulos attempted a controlled transition that included declaring a republic and adopting a new political structure.
- The effort did not stabilize the regime; he was removed later in 1973 by an internal coup led by Dimitrios Ioannidis, shifting power to a harder-line leadership.

## Post-Junta Trial and Later Life

- Following the 1974 restoration of democracy (often referred to as the *Metapolitefsi*), Papadopoulos was prosecuted for his role in the dictatorship.
- He was convicted and spent the remainder of his life largely out of public power, remaining a prominent symbol of the junta period in Greek political memory.

## Legacy

- **In Greece**: Papadopoulos remains a polarizing historical figure, primarily remembered for authoritarian rule and the interruption of democratic governance.
- **In scholarship**: He is typically discussed within broader analyses of Cold War-era military interventions in politics and the institutional dynamics of authoritarian regimes.
