## Background

- Haitian political and paramilitary figure who became prominent during periods of instability.
- Known internationally for his role in the 2004 armed rebellion that contributed to the ouster of President Jean-Bertrand Aristide.

## 2004 Rebellion and Political Aftermath

- Philippe was one of the leading commanders associated with insurgent forces that advanced on Port-au-Prince in early 2004.
- The crisis ended with Aristide leaving Haiti; subsequent interim governance and international involvement shaped the country’s security and political landscape.

## Criminal Allegations and U.S. Case

- Over multiple years, Philippe was publicly accused by U.S. authorities of involvement in drug-trafficking networks and of providing protection for cocaine shipments moving through Haiti.
- In the United States, he ultimately pleaded guilty to a money-laundering-related conspiracy charge connected to proceeds from cocaine trafficking (per U.S. court reporting and major news coverage).

## Later Electoral Politics

- Philippe later participated in formal politics and won elected office in Haiti (commonly reported as a senate seat win in the mid-2010s).
- His election campaign and victory were politically polarizing, in part due to longstanding allegations and his 2004 role.

- Illustrates the overlap between armed political movements, weak state institutions, and transnational illicit finance in Haiti’s modern history.
- Became a symbol in debates over accountability: celebrated by some as an anti-Aristide figure and condemned by others as a destabilizing actor tied to organized crime.
