## Summary

Child support -- the ongoing financial contribution by a non-custodial parent toward the cost of raising their child -- is a legal obligation recognized across all modern legal systems, though the methods of calculation, enforcement mechanisms, and institutional frameworks differ substantially. South Korea governs child support (양육비) through Articles 837 and 843 of the Civil Act (민법), with the amount determined by agreement between the parties or by the family court based on the child's needs and parents' financial capacity; chronic non-payment has been a persistent problem, leading to the establishment of the Child Support Agency (양육비이행관리원) in 2015 and significant enforcement reforms including the 2020 amendment permitting driver's license suspension and passport restriction for delinquent obligors, as well as the 2021 introduction of a temporary child support advance system (양육비 긴급지원). The United States operates the most extensive child support enforcement system in the world through Title IV-D of the Social Security Act, administered by the federal Office of Child Support Services and state child support agencies, employing standardized guideline-based calculation (income shares model, percentage of income model, or Melson formula depending on the state) and powerful enforcement tools including income withholding, tax refund interception, license suspension, credit reporting, and federal criminal prosecution under the Deadbeat Parents Punishment Act (18 U.S.C. Section 228); approximately $34 billion in child support is collected annually, though total arrears exceed $113 billion. Germany calculates child support (Kindesunterhalt) according to the Düsseldorf Table (Düsseldorfer Tabelle), a non-binding but universally applied guideline updated annually by the Higher Regional Court of Düsseldorf, based on the obligor's net income and the child's age, with enforcement through the family court system and a state advance payment system (Unterhaltsvorschuss) that provides support when the obligor fails to pay, substantially expanded in 2017 to cover children up to age 18. At the European level, EU Regulation 4/2009 on maintenance obligations provides uniform rules for jurisdiction, applicable law, recognition, and enforcement of maintenance decisions across member states, the Hague Convention on the International Recovery of Child Support (2007) facilitates cross-border collection, and the ECHR has recognized under Article 8 that states have positive obligations to ensure the effective enforcement of child support orders.

## South Korea

### Legal Framework

Child support obligations in South Korea arise from the parental duty of upbringing (양육의무) under Articles 837 and 913 of the Civil Act (민법). Article 837 requires parents divorcing to agree on custody and support arrangements, with the family court determining these matters absent agreement. Article 843 extends these provisions to judicial divorce.

### Calculation

Unlike the formulaic approaches used in the United States and Germany, Korean courts exercise broad discretion in determining child support amounts, considering:

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Child's needs | Age, health, education costs, standard of living |
| Parents' income | Both parents' earning capacity and actual income |
| Parents' assets | Property and financial resources |
| Living standards | Standard of living during the marriage |
| Number of children | Total number of children requiring support |

The Seoul Family Court publishes advisory child support calculation guidelines (양육비 산정기준표), most recently updated in 2023, which suggest ranges based on parental income and child age. While not binding, these guidelines are widely followed by family courts nationwide.

### Child Support Agency (양육비이행관리원)

The Act on the Facilitation of Child Support Payment (양육비 이행확보 및 지원에 관한 법률) established the Child Support Agency in March 2015 under the Ministry of Gender Equality and Family. The Agency provides:

- Mediation and negotiation between parents
- Legal assistance for child support claims
- Monitoring of child support payment compliance
- Referral for enforcement proceedings

### 2020-2021 Enforcement Reforms

Chronic non-payment of child support prompted significant legislative reforms:

**2020 Amendment:** The Act was amended to permit:
- Suspension of the obligor's driver's license (운전면허 정지)
- Restriction of the obligor's passport issuance or validity (여권 발급 제한)
- Disclosure of the obligor's name and photograph (명단공개) for delinquent obligors exceeding a threshold amount

**2021 Emergency Support:** A temporary child support advance system (양육비 긴급지원제도) was introduced to provide emergency financial support to custodial parents when the obligor fails to pay, with the government subrogating the claim against the obligor. Initial coverage was limited to 200,000 KRW per month per child, subsequently increased.

### Enforcement Statistics

As of 2024, the Child Support Agency reported that approximately 60 percent of non-custodial parents were delinquent in their child support obligations, with total arrears exceeding 1.7 trillion KRW. The enforcement reforms have increased collection rates, but compliance remains significantly lower than in the United States or Germany.

## United States

### Legal Framework

The federal child support system operates under Title IV-D of the Social Security Act (42 U.S.C. Sections 651-669b), creating a federal-state partnership. The federal Office of Child Support Services (formerly OCSE) establishes policy and provides funding; state child support agencies operate the program at the state level. Every state is required to have child support guidelines that make the calculation of support a rebuttable presumption.

### Calculation Models

| Model | Used By | Method |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Income Shares | Approximately 40 states | Based on both parents' combined income; applies percentage for number of children; proportional share allocated |
| Percentage of Income | Approximately 7 states (including Wisconsin, Texas) | Fixed percentage of the non-custodial parent's income |
| Melson Formula | 3 states (Delaware, Hawaii, Montana) | Modified income shares with self-support reserve and standard of living adjustment |

### Enforcement Tools

| Tool | Legal Basis | Effect |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Income withholding | 42 U.S.C. Section 666(b) | Mandatory for all orders since 1994 |
| Tax refund intercept | 42 U.S.C. Section 664 | Federal and state tax refunds offset against arrears |
| Credit reporting | 42 U.S.C. Section 666(a)(7) | Arrears reported to consumer credit agencies |
| License suspension | 42 U.S.C. Section 666(a)(16) | Driver's, professional, and recreational licenses |
| Passport denial | 42 U.S.C. Section 652(k) | Denial for arrears exceeding $2,500 |
| Federal criminal prosecution | 18 U.S.C. Section 228 | Willful failure to pay for child in another state: up to 2 years |
| Contempt of court | State law | Civil or criminal contempt; imprisonment possible |

### Turner v. Rogers (2011)

In Turner v. Rogers, 564 U.S. 431 (2011), the Supreme Court held that the Due Process Clause does not automatically require appointed counsel for indigent defendants in civil contempt proceedings for child support non-payment. However, the Court required alternative procedural safeguards, including notice that ability to pay is a critical issue, a form to report financial information, and an express judicial finding of ability to pay.

### Collection Statistics

The federal child support program collected approximately $34.8 billion in fiscal year 2023, serving approximately 13.2 million children. However, total arrears exceeded $113 billion, reflecting a longstanding gap between ordered and collected amounts. The cost-effectiveness ratio (collections per dollar of expenditure) has improved from approximately $4.68 in 2008 to approximately $5.72 in 2023.

## Germany

### Legal Framework

The child support obligation (Kindesunterhalt) is governed by Sections 1601-1615 BGB. Under Section 1601, lineal relatives are obligated to maintain each other, with the parental obligation taking priority. Section 1612a establishes the minimum support amount (Mindestunterhalt), tied to the child tax exemption (Kinderfreibetrag).

### Düsseldorf Table (Düsseldorfer Tabelle)

The Düsseldorf Table, published annually by the Higher Regional Court of Düsseldorf (Oberlandesgericht Düsseldorf) since 1962, is the most widely used guideline for calculating child support in Germany. While not legally binding, it is applied by virtually all family courts as a de facto standard.

The Table classifies obligor net income into 15 brackets and child age into four categories:

| Income Group (net monthly, EUR) | Age 0-5 | Age 6-11 | Age 12-17 | Age 18+ |
|-------------------------------|---------|----------|-----------|---------|
| Up to 2,100 | 480 | 551 | 645 | 689 |
| 2,101-2,500 | 504 | 579 | 678 | 724 |
| 2,501-2,900 | 528 | 607 | 710 | 758 |
| (higher brackets continue) | ... | ... | ... | ... |

*(2025 figures; updated annually)*

The self-support reserve (Selbstbehalt) protects the obligor's own subsistence: 1,450 EUR/month for employed obligors and 1,200 EUR/month for non-employed obligors (2025 figures).

### Advance Maintenance Payments (Unterhaltsvorschuss)

The Unterhaltsvorschussgesetz (UVG, Advance Maintenance Act) provides state-funded maintenance to children whose non-custodial parent fails to pay. Major reform in 2017 expanded the program:

| Feature | Before 2017 | After 2017 |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Age limit | Under 12 | Under 18 |
| Duration limit | 72 months maximum | No duration limit |
| Amount | Based on Mindestunterhalt minus child benefit | Same formula |

The state recovers the advance payments from the delinquent obligor through subrogation. In 2023, approximately 780,000 children received Unterhaltsvorschuss, at a total cost of approximately 2.3 billion EUR.

### Criminal Enforcement

Section 170 StGB (Verletzung der Unterhaltspflicht -- violation of maintenance obligation) criminalizes the willful failure to provide maintenance for a person to whom one is legally obligated. The offense carries a penalty of imprisonment up to three years or a fine. Prosecution is typically initiated only after civil enforcement measures have proven unsuccessful.

### Duration of Obligation

Unlike many jurisdictions that terminate child support at the age of majority, German law requires support until the child completes their first vocational training or first degree (erste Ausbildung). This can extend support obligations well into the child's twenties for university students.

## ECHR & EU

### EU Maintenance Regulation (No 4/2009)

Council Regulation (EC) No 4/2009 on jurisdiction, applicable law, recognition and enforcement of decisions relating to maintenance obligations provides a comprehensive framework for cross-border maintenance cases within the EU.

**Jurisdiction:** The regulation establishes jurisdiction based on the defendant's habitual residence, the creditor's habitual residence, or the court with jurisdiction over status proceedings (e.g., divorce) (Article 3).

**Applicable Law:** For member states bound by the 2007 Hague Protocol, the law of the creditor's habitual residence generally applies (Article 3 of the Protocol). Parties may also agree on the applicable law in certain circumstances.

**Recognition and Enforcement:** Decisions from member states bound by the Hague Protocol are recognized and enforceable in other such states without any declaration of enforceability (exequatur abolition) (Articles 17-22).

### Hague Convention on Child Support (2007)

The Hague Convention on the International Recovery of Child Support and Other Forms of Family Maintenance, concluded on November 23, 2007, establishes a worldwide system for cross-border child support enforcement. The EU ratified it in 2014 (Decision 2014/218/EU). Key features include:

- Central Authority cooperation for transmission and processing of applications
- Free legal assistance for child support applications
- Effective recognition and enforcement of maintenance decisions
- Direct enforcement mechanisms

### ECHR Jurisprudence

**Airey v. Ireland (1979):** The Court established that Article 6 requires effective access to family court proceedings, including legal aid where necessary, which encompasses child support claims.

**Iosub Caras v. Romania (Application No. 7198/04, July 27, 2006):** The Court emphasized the positive obligation of states under Article 8 to ensure that child maintenance orders are effectively enforced, finding that unreasonable delay in enforcement violated the applicant's rights.

**Malec v. Poland (Application No. 28623/12, June 28, 2022):** The Court found a violation of Article 1 of Protocol No. 1 (protection of property) where the state failed to effectively enforce a child support judgment, treating the maintenance obligation as a property right of the child.

### EU Directive on Transparent and Predictable Working Conditions

EU Directive 2019/1152 requires member states to ensure that employers provide information about social security contributions, which indirectly supports child support enforcement by improving income transparency.

## Comparative Analysis

### Calculation Approaches

The three jurisdictions employ fundamentally different calculation philosophies. The United States uses formulaic guidelines creating a rebuttable presumption, prioritizing consistency and predictability. Germany uses the Düsseldorf Table, which functions as a non-binding but universally followed guideline. South Korea relies primarily on judicial discretion, supplemented by advisory guidelines. The formulaic approaches tend to produce more consistent outcomes but may fail to account for individual circumstances; discretionary approaches offer flexibility but create unpredictability.

### Enforcement Effectiveness

The United States has the most aggressive enforcement arsenal, including mandatory income withholding, tax intercept, and federal criminal prosecution. Germany's Unterhaltsvorschuss system is the most child-protective, ensuring that children receive support regardless of the obligor's payment behavior. South Korea's enforcement infrastructure is the newest and still developing, with significant compliance gaps. The ECHR has established that states have positive obligations to ensure effective enforcement, but has not prescribed specific mechanisms.

### State Advance Systems

Germany's Unterhaltsvorschuss represents the most comprehensive approach to protecting children from the consequences of non-payment, covering children up to 18 with no duration limit. South Korea's emergency support system (2021) is more limited in scope and amount. The United States has no federal advance system, though some states operate limited "child support assurance" programs. The variation reflects different welfare state philosophies regarding the appropriate balance between individual parental responsibility and collective support.

### Duration

Germany's requirement that support continue through first vocational training extends obligations beyond those in the United States (typically 18-21) and South Korea (generally until majority). This reflects Germany's emphasis on educational attainment and the recognition that true economic independence often requires completion of post-secondary education or training.
