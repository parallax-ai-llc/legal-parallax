## Summary

Cooling-off periods -- statutory rights allowing consumers to withdraw from contracts within a specified timeframe after conclusion without penalty or justification -- represent a fundamental consumer protection mechanism designed to counteract the psychological pressures of aggressive sales techniques, information asymmetries in complex transactions, and the impulsive nature of distance and doorstep purchasing. South Korea provides robust withdrawal rights (청약철회) through the Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce (전자상거래 등에서의 소비자보호에 관한 법률) granting a 7-day cooling-off period for online and distance purchases, the Door-to-Door Sales Act (방문판매 등에 관한 법률) providing a 14-day cooling-off period for door-to-door and multi-level marketing sales, and the Insurance Business Act (보험업법) granting a 15-day withdrawal period for insurance contracts; these periods are supplemented by the Framework Act on Consumers (소비자기본법) establishing general withdrawal principles and the Korean Consumer Agency's dispute mediation system. The United States provides a more limited and fragmented cooling-off framework, with the FTC's Cooling-Off Rule (16 C.F.R. Part 429) granting a 3-business-day cancellation right for door-to-door sales of $25 or more, the Truth in Lending Act (TILA) providing a 3-day right of rescission for certain home-secured loans, and various state laws providing additional cooling-off periods for specific transaction types; notably, the United States does not provide a general federal cooling-off period for online purchases. Germany implements cooling-off rights primarily through the BGB's provisions on Widerrufsrecht (right of withdrawal) for distance contracts (Fernabsatzverträge, Sections 312c-312h) and off-premises contracts (Außergeschäftsraumverträge), transposing the EU Consumer Rights Directive's 14-day withdrawal period into German law; the German implementation is notable for its strict requirements regarding the Widerrufsbelehrung (withdrawal instruction) that the trader must provide, with failure to properly inform the consumer resulting in an extended withdrawal period of up to 12 months and 14 days. At the European level, the Consumer Rights Directive 2011/83/EU establishes a harmonized 14-day withdrawal period for distance and off-premises contracts across all EU member states, the European Court of Justice has interpreted withdrawal rights expansively in cases including Messner v. Firma Stefan Krüger (C-489/07, 2009) and Walbusch Walter Busch (C-430/17, 2019), and the Digital Content Directive (EU) 2019/770 extends withdrawal rights to contracts for digital content and services.

## South Korea

### Legal Framework

South Korea provides among the most generous cooling-off protections in Asia, with multiple statutes addressing different transaction types.

### E-Commerce Act (전자상거래 등에서의 소비자보호에 관한 법률)

**Article 17 -- Right of Withdrawal (청약철회):**
- **Duration:** 7 days from receipt of the goods or from the date the contract document was made available (whichever is later)
- **Scope:** Online purchases, catalog sales, TV home shopping, and other distance transactions
- **No-Fault Withdrawal:** The consumer may withdraw without providing any reason
- **Exceptions:** (1) Goods damaged or consumed by the consumer's fault; (2) goods whose value has substantially decreased making resale difficult; (3) customized or made-to-order goods; (4) digital content that has been used (unless a trial sample was not provided); (5) periodicals and newspapers

**Extended Withdrawal Period:** If the trader fails to provide proper withdrawal information or if delivery is late, the withdrawal period is extended to 3 months from receipt.

### Door-to-Door Sales Act (방문판매 등에 관한 법률)

**Article 8 -- Withdrawal Right:**
- **Duration:** 14 days from the conclusion of the contract or from receipt of the goods (whichever is later)
- **Scope:** Door-to-door sales (방문판매), telephone solicitation sales (전화권유판매), and multi-level marketing sales (다단계판매)
- **Enhanced Protection:** For multi-level marketing transactions, the cooling-off period is 14 days, and for purchases of 20,000 KRW or more, the consumer has 3 months from receipt to request a refund if the product is defective or does not match the contract description.

### Insurance Contracts

**Insurance Business Act (보험업법) Article 102-5:**
- **Duration:** 15 days from receipt of the insurance policy document
- **Scope:** All types of insurance contracts
- **Full Premium Refund:** Insurer must refund all premiums paid within 3 business days of receiving the withdrawal notice

### Installment Credit

**Installment Transactions Act (할부거래에 관한 법률) Article 8:**
- **Duration:** 7 days from receipt of the contract document
- **Scope:** Installment purchase agreements
- **Effect:** Cancellation of the installment agreement and return of goods; seller must refund payments within 3 business days

### Implementation and Enforcement

The Korean Consumer Agency (한국소비자원) handles disputes arising from cooling-off period claims. In 2024, withdrawal-related disputes constituted approximately 25% of all consumer complaints handled by the Agency. The KFTC has authority to impose corrective orders and fines on businesses that obstruct consumer withdrawal rights.

### Platform Economy Challenges

The rise of platform-based commerce has created new challenges for cooling-off period enforcement. The KFTC has issued guidelines clarifying that platform operators have secondary responsibility for facilitating consumer withdrawal rights and that delivery apps, social media commerce (소셜커머스), and live commerce (라이브커머스) transactions are all subject to the E-Commerce Act's withdrawal provisions.

## United States

### Legal Framework

The United States provides a more limited and fragmented cooling-off framework compared to the other jurisdictions, with no general federal right of withdrawal for online purchases.

### FTC Cooling-Off Rule (16 C.F.R. Part 429)

- **Duration:** 3 business days
- **Scope:** Door-to-door sales of $25 or more at the buyer's home, workplace, or temporary seller locations (e.g., hotel rooms, convention centers)
- **Requirements:** Seller must inform buyer of the right to cancel, provide two copies of a cancellation form, and provide a receipt with the cancellation information
- **Exclusions:** Sales under $25; sales at seller's permanent place of business; sales of real estate, insurance, or securities; emergency home repair sales requested by the buyer

**2023 Rule Update:** The FTC modernized the rule in 2023 to (1) extend coverage to sales conducted via video calls and other remote methods when the seller is not at a fixed business location, and (2) require sellers to provide cancellation forms electronically where the sale was conducted electronically.

### Truth in Lending Act (TILA) Right of Rescission

**15 U.S.C. §1635; Regulation Z (12 C.F.R. §226.23):**
- **Duration:** 3 business days
- **Scope:** Credit transactions in which a security interest is taken in the consumer's principal dwelling (e.g., home equity loans, refinancing)
- **Exclusions:** First mortgage to purchase a dwelling; credit extended by a state agency
- **Extended Right:** If the lender fails to provide proper rescission disclosures, the right of rescission extends to 3 years from consummation
- **Effect:** Upon rescission, the security interest in the dwelling is void, and the creditor must return all money and property given by the consumer within 20 days

### State Cooling-Off Laws

State laws supplement federal protections with additional cooling-off periods:

| Type | Typical Duration | States |
|------|-----------------|--------|
| Health club memberships | 3-10 days | California, New York, Illinois, others |
| Timeshares | 3-15 days | All 50 states (varies) |
| Home improvement contracts | 3 days | California, New York, others |
| Hearing aid purchases | 30 days (trial period) | Most states |
| Funeral pre-arrangements | 30 days | Several states |

### Absence of Online Purchase Cooling-Off Period

The United States is notable for not providing a general federal cooling-off period for online purchases. American e-commerce consumer protection relies primarily on:
- Individual retailer return policies (Amazon's 30-day return policy, etc.)
- Credit card chargeback rights under the Fair Credit Billing Act (60-day dispute period for billing errors)
- State consumer protection statutes prohibiting deceptive practices
- FTC enforcement against deceptive online practices

### Click-to-Cancel Rule (2024)

The FTC's "Click-to-Cancel" rule, finalized in October 2024, requires that businesses make it as easy to cancel recurring subscriptions and memberships as it was to sign up. While not a traditional cooling-off period, this rule addresses the related problem of businesses making cancellation unreasonably difficult.

## Germany

### Legal Framework

Germany provides comprehensive cooling-off rights through its implementation of the EU Consumer Rights Directive, codified in the BGB.

### BGB Widerrufsrecht (Right of Withdrawal)

**Section 312g BGB -- Right of Withdrawal for Distance and Off-Premises Contracts:**
- **Duration:** 14 days
- **Commencement:** For goods: from the day the consumer or a third party designated by the consumer (other than the carrier) takes physical possession. For services: from the day of contract conclusion. For digital content: from the day of contract conclusion.
- **Exercise:** The consumer must inform the trader of the decision to withdraw by unequivocal statement (letter, email, or model withdrawal form)
- **No Reason Required:** The consumer need not give any reason for withdrawal

### Widerrufsbelehrung (Withdrawal Instruction)

One of the most distinctive features of German withdrawal law is the strict requirement for the Widerrufsbelehrung -- a formal written instruction that the trader must provide to the consumer regarding the right of withdrawal:

**Required Content (Article 246a EGBGB):**
- Conditions, time limit, and procedure for exercising the right of withdrawal
- Model withdrawal form
- Information about consequences of withdrawal (return of goods, cost allocation)
- Information about any obligation to pay for services received during the withdrawal period

**Consequence of Failure:** If the trader fails to provide proper withdrawal instructions, the withdrawal period does not begin to run and is extended by up to 12 months beyond the original 14-day period (Section 356(3) BGB, implementing CRD Article 10). This creates a maximum withdrawal period of 12 months and 14 days.

### Exceptions (Section 312g(2) BGB)

The right of withdrawal does not apply to contracts for:
1. Goods made to the consumer's specifications or clearly personalized
2. Sealed goods unsuitable for return due to health or hygiene reasons, if unsealed
3. Goods that have been inseparably mixed with other items after delivery
4. Alcoholic beverages with price agreed at contract conclusion but not deliverable before 30 days
5. Sealed audio/video recordings or computer software if unsealed
6. Newspapers, periodicals, and magazines (except subscription contracts)
7. Accommodation (other than residential), transport, car rental, catering, or leisure services with a specific date or period
8. Digital content if the consumer expressly consented to performance beginning and acknowledged loss of withdrawal right

### Consumer Credit Withdrawal

**Section 495 BGB:** Consumers have a 14-day right of withdrawal for consumer credit agreements, starting from the date the consumer receives the complete contractual information. This implements the Consumer Credit Directive's withdrawal requirement and applies to all consumer loans, including vehicle financing and personal loans.

### Insurance Contract Withdrawal

**Section 8 VVG (Versicherungsvertragsgesetz):** Insurance policyholders have a 14-day withdrawal right (30 days for life insurance) from receipt of the policy document and all required pre-contractual information.

### BGH Jurisprudence

The BGH has decided numerous cases on withdrawal rights:

**BGH, VIII ZR 249/14 (2016):** The buyer of a mattress who removed its protective packaging could still exercise the right of withdrawal, as the hygiene exception (Section 312g(2)(3) BGB) requires that the product be "sealed" and that the seal be relevant to health or hygiene -- a regular mattress packaging does not constitute such a seal.

**BGH, I ZR 7/16 (2017):** An email sent by the consumer stating "I want to return the product" constitutes a sufficiently unequivocal declaration of withdrawal, even without using the word "Widerruf" (withdrawal).

## ECHR & EU

### Consumer Rights Directive 2011/83/EU

The CRD is the cornerstone of EU withdrawal rights law, establishing maximum harmonization for distance and off-premises contracts.

**Article 9 -- Right of Withdrawal:**
- 14-day withdrawal period for distance and off-premises contracts
- No reason required
- Member states may not extend or reduce the period (maximum harmonization)

**Article 10 -- Failure to Inform:**
- If the trader fails to inform the consumer about the right of withdrawal, the withdrawal period expires 12 months after the end of the initial 14-day period

**Article 13 -- Trader's Obligations in the Event of Withdrawal:**
- Refund all payments received, including delivery costs, within 14 days of being informed of the withdrawal decision
- Refund must be made using the same payment method as the original transaction
- Trader may withhold refund until goods are returned or evidence of dispatch is provided

**Article 14 -- Consumer's Obligations:**
- Consumer must return goods within 14 days of informing the trader of withdrawal
- Consumer bears the direct cost of returning goods (unless the trader agreed to bear the cost or failed to inform the consumer)
- Consumer is liable for diminished value of goods resulting from handling beyond what is necessary to establish their nature, characteristics, and functioning

### CJEU Jurisprudence

**Messner v. Firma Stefan Krüger (C-489/07, 2009):** The Court held that a national rule requiring the consumer to pay compensation for use of goods during the withdrawal period was incompatible with the (then applicable) Distance Selling Directive, as it would deter consumers from exercising their withdrawal rights.

**Walbusch Walter Busch (C-430/17, 2019):** The Court addressed the information requirements for limited-space communications (such as marketing letters), holding that traders must always provide information about the right of withdrawal but may provide the model withdrawal form through other means if space is limited.

**Content Services (C-49/11, 2012):** The Court held that making pre-contractual information available only via a hyperlink on a website does not satisfy the requirement to provide information "on a durable medium."

**Verbraucherzentrale Bundesverband v. Amazon EU (C-649/17, 2019):** The Court held that a "callback" button on a website that does not directly connect the consumer to the trader does not satisfy the requirement for "rapid contact" under the CRD.

### Digital Content Directive (EU) 2019/770

The Directive extends withdrawal-related consumer protections to contracts for the supply of digital content and digital services. Where the consumer withdraws from a digital content contract, the trader must refrain from using any content provided by the consumer, and the consumer is entitled to retrieve their data.

### Omnibus Directive (EU) 2019/2161

The Omnibus Directive amended the CRD to address:
- **Online marketplaces:** Consumers must be informed whether the third-party seller is a trader or non-trader, as withdrawal rights apply only to trader-consumer contracts
- **Digital Services:** Extended withdrawal rights to contracts where the consumer provides personal data as "payment" for digital services (e.g., free social media services in exchange for data)

## Comparative Analysis

### Duration and Scope

The EU/German 14-day withdrawal period for distance and off-premises contracts is significantly more generous than the U.S. FTC's 3-business-day rule and South Korea's 7-day e-commerce period. However, South Korea's 14-day period for door-to-door sales matches the EU standard. The most striking gap is the absence of any U.S. federal cooling-off period for online purchases, leaving millions of consumers dependent on retailer goodwill and credit card dispute mechanisms.

### Information Duties and Consequences

Germany/EU imposes the most stringent information duties through the Widerrufsbelehrung requirement, with the powerful sanction of extended withdrawal periods (up to 12 months + 14 days) for non-compliance. South Korea extends the period to 3 months for failure to provide information. The U.S. FTC rule requires cancellation form provision but does not extend the cooling-off period for information failures (though TILA extends the rescission period to 3 years).

### Cost Allocation

The allocation of return shipping costs varies. The EU CRD places return costs on the consumer (unless the trader agreed otherwise or failed to inform). South Korea generally places return costs on the seller for non-defective goods in online purchases. The U.S. has no uniform federal rule on return costs. Cost allocation affects the practical exercise of withdrawal rights, as consumers may be deterred from withdrawing if they must bear substantial return shipping costs.

### Digital Content and Services

The EU is the most advanced jurisdiction in extending withdrawal rights to digital content and services, including the novel concept of contracts where personal data constitutes the consideration. Neither the U.S. nor South Korea has comparably comprehensive legislation for digital content withdrawal rights.
