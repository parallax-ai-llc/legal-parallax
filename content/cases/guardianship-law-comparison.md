## Summary

Guardianship -- the legal arrangement by which a court appoints an individual or entity to make decisions on behalf of a person who lacks the capacity to manage their own affairs, whether due to minority, mental disability, cognitive decline, or other incapacity -- has undergone fundamental reform across jurisdictions in recent decades, driven by the disability rights movement, the aging of populations, and the recognition that traditional plenary guardianship often unnecessarily restricts the autonomy and dignity of persons under protection. South Korea comprehensively reformed its guardianship system through the Act on the Revision of the Guardianship System (성년후견제도 개정), effective July 1, 2013, which replaced the rigid quasi-incompetency (한정치산) and incompetency (금치산) declarations with a flexible three-tier adult guardianship system comprising adult guardianship (성년후견), limited guardianship (한정후견), and specific guardianship (특정후견), emphasizing respect for the ward's residual capacity and preferences under revised Civil Act (민법) Articles 9-14-3; the reform was influenced by the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) and sought to move from a substituted decision-making model to one incorporating supported decision-making elements. The United States addresses guardianship through state law, with the Uniform Guardianship, Conservatorship, and Other Protective Arrangements Act (UGCOPAA, 2017) providing a modern model framework emphasizing the least restrictive alternative and supported decision-making; the highly publicized conservatorship of Britney Spears, terminated in November 2021, catalyzed a national reform movement resulting in the bipartisan federal FREED Act proposals and numerous state legislative reforms. Germany replaced its guardianship system (Vormundschaft/Gebrechlichkeitspflegschaft) in 1992 with the Betreuungsgesetz (Custodianship Act), creating the Betreuung (custodianship) system under BGB Sections 1896-1908k, and comprehensively reformed it again through the Act to Reform Custodianship Law (Gesetz zur Reform des Vormundschafts- und Betreuungsrechts), effective January 1, 2023, which strengthened the supported decision-making model, codified the principle that Betreuung does not alter legal capacity, and introduced enhanced quality standards for professional custodians. The European framework includes the CRPD (2006), which in Article 12 requires recognition of legal capacity on an equal basis for persons with disabilities, the Council of Europe Recommendation CM/Rec(2009)11 on continuing powers of attorney and advance directives, the ECHR's Article 8 jurisprudence on guardianship as an interference with private life requiring procedural safeguards, and the Hague Convention on the International Protection of Adults (2000).

## South Korea

### Legal Framework

South Korea's guardianship reform, effective July 1, 2013, replaced the pre-existing system of quasi-incompetency (한정치산) and incompetency (금치산) declarations, which had remained essentially unchanged since the original 1958 Civil Act and were modeled on the Japanese Civil Code.

### Three-Tier System

| Type | Civil Act | Capacity Effect | Scope |
|------|-----------|----------------|-------|
| Adult Guardianship (성년후견) | Art. 9 | Significant restriction on legal capacity | Comprehensive; for persons who continuously lack capacity |
| Limited Guardianship (한정후견) | Art. 12 | Limited restriction; court specifies | Partial; for persons with partially diminished capacity |
| Specific Guardianship (특정후견) | Art. 14-2 | No restriction on capacity | Task-specific; temporary |

### Adult Guardianship (성년후견)

**Article 9:** The family court may order an adjudication of adult guardianship for a person who is continuously in a state of lacking the mental capacity to manage their affairs due to disease, disability, old age, or other causes. The adult guardian (성년후견인) exercises substitute decision-making authority over the ward's legal acts.

**Article 10:** Legal acts performed by a person under adult guardianship are voidable, except for acts of daily life (일상생활에 관한 행위) which remain valid.

### Limited Guardianship (한정후견)

**Article 12:** The family court may order limited guardianship for a person whose mental capacity is partially insufficient. The court specifies which legal acts require the limited guardian's (한정후견인) consent, preserving the ward's autonomous decision-making in all other areas.

### Specific Guardianship (특정후견)

**Article 14-2:** The family court may appoint a specific guardian (특정후견인) to assist a person who temporarily needs support for specific matters. This is the most autonomy-preserving option and most closely aligns with the supported decision-making model advocated by the CRPD.

### Implementation Challenges

Despite the reform's progressive design, implementation has faced challenges:

- **Awareness:** Public understanding of the new system remains limited, particularly in rural areas.
- **Professional guardians:** The pool of qualified professional guardians is insufficient, particularly for persons without family support.
- **Cost:** Court proceedings and professional guardian fees create barriers to access.
- **Cultural factors:** Confucian cultural norms emphasizing family authority sometimes conflict with the individualized autonomy principles underlying the reform.

As of 2024, approximately 45,000 persons were under some form of adult guardianship in South Korea, a figure that is expected to increase rapidly with population aging.

## United States

### Legal Framework

Guardianship in the United States is governed by state law, with the Uniform Law Commission providing model legislation. The UGCOPAA (2017) replaced the earlier Uniform Guardianship and Protective Proceedings Act (1997).

### Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Guardian | Person appointed to make personal/healthcare decisions |
| Conservator | Person appointed to manage financial affairs |
| Guardian ad litem | Person appointed to represent interests in litigation |
| Ward | Person subject to guardianship |
| Protected person | Modern terminology preferred by UGCOPAA |

Note: California and several other states use "conservator" for both personal and financial authority (California Probate Code Sections 1800-1898).

### UGCOPAA (2017) Key Principles

The UGCOPAA emphasizes:

- **Least restrictive alternative:** The court must consider less restrictive alternatives, including supported decision-making, before appointing a guardian (Section 301).
- **Limited guardianship preferred:** Courts should limit the guardian's authority to only those areas where the individual lacks capacity (Section 311).
- **Person-centered planning:** The guardian must take into account the wishes and preferences of the protected person.
- **Supported decision-making:** Section 102 defines and recognizes supported decision-making arrangements.
- **Periodic review:** The court must review the guardianship at least annually.

### The Britney Spears Effect

The conservatorship of Britney Spears (2008-2021) brought unprecedented public attention to guardianship reform:

- **Establishment:** In February 2008, a Los Angeles court established a conservatorship over Spears's person and estate.
- **#FreeBritney Movement:** Beginning around 2019, public advocacy questioned the conservatorship's necessity and the conservator's decisions.
- **Termination:** On November 12, 2021, Judge Brenda Penny terminated the conservatorship.
- **Legislative Impact:** The case prompted the introduction of the federal FREE (Freedom and Right to Emancipate from Exploitation) Act, bipartisan legislation to improve guardianship protections, and numerous state reforms enhancing procedural safeguards.

### State Reform Trends

| Reform | States Adopting |
|--------|----------------|
| Supported decision-making statutes | 20+ states (as of 2025) |
| Enhanced due process protections | Majority of states |
| Professional guardian certification | Growing number of states |
| Right to counsel in guardianship proceedings | Expanding (UGCOPAA recommends) |
| Guardianship monitoring/oversight agencies | Approximately 15 states |

## Germany

### Legal Framework

Germany pioneered modern guardianship reform with the Betreuungsgesetz of 1992, replacing the centuries-old concepts of Entmündigung (incapacitation), Vormundschaft (guardianship), and Gebrechlichkeitspflegschaft (infirmity curatorship) with the unified Betreuung (custodianship) system.

### 2023 Reform (Gesetz zur Reform des Betreuungsrechts)

The comprehensive reform, effective January 1, 2023, restructured BGB Sections 1814-1881 and introduced fundamental changes:

**Principle of Support Before Representation (Unterstützung vor Vertretung):**

**BGB Section 1814(3):** A Betreuer (custodian) may be appointed only if and to the extent that the custodianship is necessary. Other assistance, particularly supported decision-making, social services, and powers of attorney, must be considered as alternatives.

**Preservation of Legal Capacity:** The 2023 reform codifies explicitly that the appointment of a Betreuer does not restrict the legal capacity of the Betreute (person under custodianship). The Betreuer acts alongside the person, not in replacement of them.

**Wishes of the Betreute:** BGB Section 1821 (reformed) strengthens the obligation of the Betreuer to ascertain and follow the wishes of the Betreute. The Betreuer may deviate from the expressed wishes only when following them would cause considerable danger to the person or their property and the person cannot appreciate this danger due to their condition.

### Types and Scope

| Element | Pre-2023 | Post-2023 |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Legal capacity effect | Betreuung does not restrict capacity (but Einwilligungsvorbehalt possible) | Same; reinforced |
| Einwilligungsvorbehalt (consent reservation) | Available for property matters | Retained; strengthened review requirements |
| Duration | Maximum 7 years; court review | Same; enhanced review |
| Professional qualifications | Minimal | Registration requirement; continuing education; personal suitability |
| Role of Betreuungsvereine | Supportive | Enhanced coordinating role |

### Betreuungsvereine (Custodianship Associations)

Betreuungsvereine are recognized associations that recruit, train, and support volunteer custodians; provide professional custodians; and advise persons under custodianship and their families. The 2023 reform strengthened their role, requiring them to meet enhanced quality standards and establishing a coordinating function between courts, custodians, and social services.

### Advance Directive and Power of Attorney

The Betreuung system operates in conjunction with:

- **Vorsorgevollmacht (Precautionary Power of Attorney):** A person may grant a power of attorney for future incapacity, avoiding the need for court-appointed custodianship (BGB Section 1814(3)(1)).
- **Patientenverfügung (Advance Healthcare Directive):** Written instructions regarding medical treatment in the event of decisional incapacity (BGB Section 1827, formerly 1901a).
- **Betreuungsverfügung (Custodianship Directive):** Written preferences regarding who should be appointed as custodian and how custodianship should be conducted.

## ECHR & EU

### UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (2006)

**Article 12 (Equal Recognition Before the Law):**

- Paragraph 2: Persons with disabilities enjoy legal capacity on an equal basis with others in all aspects of life.
- Paragraph 3: States shall take appropriate measures to provide access to support in exercising legal capacity.
- Paragraph 4: Safeguards must be proportionate, respect rights, will and preferences, be free of conflict of interest, and be subject to regular review.

The CRPD Committee's General Comment No. 1 (2014) interprets Article 12 as requiring the abolition of substituted decision-making regimes in favor of supported decision-making. This interpretation has been controversial, as most legal systems retain some form of substituted decision-making for persons who truly lack decisional capacity.

### ECHR Jurisprudence

**Shtukaturov v. Russia (Application No. 44009/05, March 27, 2008):** The Court found violations of Articles 6 and 8 where a person was declared legally incapable without being heard, without notification, and without periodic review. The judgment established that deprivation of legal capacity constitutes a serious interference with private life requiring robust procedural safeguards.

**Stanev v. Bulgaria (Application No. 36760/06, Grand Chamber, January 17, 2012):** The Court found violations of Articles 3, 5, and 6 in the placement of a person under partial guardianship in a social care institution without his consent and without access to a court to challenge the placement.

**A.N. v. Lithuania (Application No. 17280/08, March 31, 2016):** The Court found a violation of Article 6 where the applicant's full legal incapacitation was based solely on a psychiatric diagnosis without an individualized assessment of capacity.

### Council of Europe Recommendation CM/Rec(2009)11

This Recommendation establishes principles for continuing powers of attorney and advance directives, including:

- The primary importance of self-determination and autonomy
- The principle that measures of protection should be proportionate and tailored
- The promotion of alternatives to judicial measures of protection
- Minimum content and safeguards for powers of attorney

### Hague Convention on International Protection of Adults (2000)

The Convention, in force since 2009, addresses cross-border adult protection by establishing:

- Jurisdiction based on habitual residence of the adult (Article 5)
- Recognition and enforcement of protection measures across contracting states
- Cooperation between Central Authorities
- Applicable law rules for protection measures

As of 2026, 14 states are parties, including Germany, France, the UK, and Switzerland.

## Comparative Analysis

### Substituted vs. Supported Decision-Making

The fundamental axis of reform across all jurisdictions is the shift from substituted to supported decision-making. Germany's 2023 reform most explicitly codifies this shift through the "support before representation" principle. The United States' UGCOPAA promotes supported decision-making but leaves implementation to states. South Korea's three-tier system incorporates supported elements in specific guardianship but retains full substituted decision-making in adult guardianship. The CRPD Committee's position that all substituted decision-making should be abolished remains aspirational and has not been fully implemented by any major jurisdiction.

### Capacity Preservation

Germany's post-2023 system is the most protective of legal capacity, explicitly providing that custodianship does not affect the person's legal capacity. South Korea's adult guardianship does restrict capacity. U.S. systems vary, with plenary guardianship stripping capacity but limited guardianship preserving it in unaffected areas. The ECHR requires that any restriction on capacity be proportionate and necessary.

### Monitoring and Oversight

All jurisdictions require judicial oversight but differ in intensity. Germany's Betreuungsverein system provides an institutional layer of support and monitoring beyond the court. U.S. monitoring varies dramatically by state, with some requiring annual reports and court review while others have minimal oversight. South Korea's monitoring system is developing but faces resource constraints. The ECHR requires periodic review of guardianship arrangements to prevent indefinite deprivation of autonomy.

### Population Aging

All jurisdictions face increasing demand for guardianship services due to population aging. South Korea, with one of the world's fastest-aging populations, faces particular pressure. Germany's experience with the Betreuung system since 1992 provides the longest track record of modern guardianship administration. The United States' fragmented state-by-state approach creates significant variation in the quality and availability of guardianship services.
