## Summary

Excise tax -- a levy imposed on the manufacture, sale, or consumption of specific goods, often referred to as "sin taxes" when targeting products deemed harmful to health or the environment -- serves dual purposes of revenue generation and behavioral modification, with jurisdictions increasingly using excise policy as a public health instrument alongside its traditional fiscal function. South Korea imposes excise taxes under the Individual Consumption Tax Act (개별소비세법) and the Liquor Tax Act (주세법), with particularly significant levies on alcohol, tobacco, and motor vehicles, supplemented by the Transportation, Energy, and Environment Tax (교통·에너지·환경세) on petroleum products that generates approximately 10 trillion KRW annually and is earmarked for transportation infrastructure and environmental programs. The United States imposes federal excise taxes under the Internal Revenue Code, Subtitle D (26 U.S.C. Sections 4001-5000), and Subtitle E (alcohol, tobacco, and firearms), administered by the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) and the IRS, with rates that are generally lower than peer nations -- particularly the federal cigarette tax at $1.01 per pack compared to an average of $3.50-5.00 in other OECD countries -- though state-level excise taxes add substantial additional burdens varying from $0.17 (Missouri) to $5.35 (Connecticut) per pack of cigarettes. Germany imposes excise duties within the EU harmonized framework, with the Energy Tax Act (Energiesteuergesetz), Tobacco Tax Act (Tabaksteuergesetz), Spirits Monopoly Act (Branntweinmonopolgesetz, replaced by the Alkoholsteuergesetz in 2018), and Beer Tax Act (Biersteuergesetz) implementing EU minimum rates established by Council Directive 2011/64/EU (tobacco), Directive 2003/96/EC (energy), and Directive 92/83/EEC (alcohol), with Germany generally applying rates above EU minimums. At the EU level, harmonization of excise duties on tobacco, alcohol, and energy products represents one of the most developed areas of EU tax coordination, while the ECHR has upheld excise taxation as a legitimate exercise of state fiscal authority under Protocol 1, Article 1.

## South Korea

### Legal Framework

South Korea's excise tax system comprises several distinct levies administered by different agencies. The Individual Consumption Tax (개별소비세) under the Individual Consumption Tax Act (개별소비세법, Act No. 19931) applies to luxury goods, automobiles, petroleum products, and certain entertainment venues. The Liquor Tax (주세) under the Liquor Tax Act (주세법) applies to all alcoholic beverages. Tobacco-related levies are imposed through multiple statutes including the Tobacco Business Act (담배사업법), the National Health Promotion Act (국민건강증진법), and the Local Tax Act (지방세법).

### Tobacco Taxation

South Korea's tobacco tax structure is notable for its multi-layered approach. Following the major 2015 tax increase that raised the retail price of a standard pack of cigarettes from 2,500 KRW to 4,500 KRW, the combined tax components are:

| Component | Amount per Pack |
|-----------|----------------|
| Individual consumption tax (개별소비세) | 841 KRW |
| National health promotion contribution (건강증진부담금) | 841 KRW |
| Local education tax (지방교육세) | 443 KRW |
| Tobacco consumption tax (담배소비세, local) | 1,007 KRW |
| Waste charge (폐기물부담금) | 24.4 KRW |
| VAT (부가가치세) | 409 KRW |
| **Total tax burden** | **~3,565 KRW (~79% of retail price)** |

The 2015 increase was the first major tobacco tax adjustment since 2004. Smoking prevalence among adult males declined from 43.7 percent in 2014 to 36.7 percent in 2016, though the causal relationship is debated. The health promotion contribution is earmarked for the National Health Promotion Fund.

### Alcohol Taxation

The Liquor Tax Act imposes ad valorem rates on the manufacturer's or importer's release price:

| Beverage Type | Tax Rate |
|--------------|----------|
| Spirits (소주, whisky) | 72% |
| Beer (맥주) | 830.3 KRW/liter (volume-based since 2020) |
| Wine | 30% |
| Traditional liquor (전통주) | 5% (preferential) |
| Makgeolli (rice wine) | 5% |

A significant 2020 reform converted beer taxation from ad valorem to a specific (volume-based) rate, primarily to eliminate the competitive disadvantage faced by domestic craft brewers relative to imported beer, which was previously taxed on a lower import price base.

### Transportation, Energy, and Environment Tax

The Transportation, Energy, and Environment Tax (교통·에너지·환경세) under its dedicated statute applies to petroleum products and is South Korea's primary fuel excise:

| Product | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Gasoline | 529 KRW/liter |
| Diesel | 375 KRW/liter |
| LPG (butane for vehicles) | 160 KRW/kg |

The tax was originally enacted as a temporary measure in 1994 but has been repeatedly extended. Revenue is earmarked: 80 percent for transportation infrastructure (roads, railways) and 20 percent for environmental programs and regional development. An additional Education Tax (교육세) at 15 percent of the fuel excise is levied.

## United States

### Legal Framework

Federal excise taxes are imposed under the Internal Revenue Code, primarily Subtitle D (miscellaneous excise taxes) and Subtitle E (alcohol, tobacco, and certain other excise taxes). The Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB), within the Department of the Treasury, administers alcohol and tobacco excise taxes. The IRS administers other federal excise taxes. State-level excise taxes add a substantial additional layer.

### Tobacco Excise

The federal cigarette tax is $1.01 per pack of 20 cigarettes, last increased by the Children's Health Insurance Program Reauthorization Act of 2009 (CHIPRA). State rates vary enormously:

| State | Rate per Pack | Combined Federal + State |
|-------|-------------|------------------------|
| Missouri | $0.17 | $1.18 |
| Virginia | $0.60 | $1.61 |
| Texas | $1.41 | $2.42 |
| California | $2.87 | $3.88 |
| New York | $5.35 | $6.36 |
| Connecticut | $4.35 | $5.36 |
| District of Columbia | $5.03 | $6.04 |

The Federal Cigarette Labeling and Advertising Act (15 U.S.C. § 1331 et seq.) preempts state regulation of cigarette advertising but does not preempt state taxation. The Prevent All Cigarette Trafficking Act (PACT Act, 2009) addresses online sales and interstate cigarette trafficking to prevent excise tax evasion.

### Alcohol Excise

Federal alcohol excise rates under 26 U.S.C. Subtitle E:

| Product | Federal Rate |
|---------|-------------|
| Distilled spirits | $13.50/proof gallon |
| Beer | $18.00/barrel (31 gallons); reduced rates for small brewers |
| Wine (14% or less) | $1.07/gallon |
| Wine (14-21%) | $1.57/gallon |
| Wine (21-24%) | $3.15/gallon |

The Craft Beverage Modernization and Tax Reform Act (2017, made permanent in 2020) significantly reduced rates for small producers: $3.50/proof gallon on first 100,000 proof gallons for distillers producing under 22.23 million proof gallons; $3.50/barrel on first 60,000 barrels for brewers producing under 2 million barrels.

### Federal Motor Fuel Excise

The federal gasoline tax is $0.184 per gallon ($0.244 for diesel), unchanged since 1993. Revenue is directed to the Highway Trust Fund under 26 U.S.C. § 9503. The failure to adjust for inflation has resulted in a declining real value, contributing to chronic underfunding of transportation infrastructure. Multiple proposals to index the tax to inflation or replace it with a vehicle-miles-traveled (VMT) tax have failed to gain legislative traction.

State gasoline taxes range from approximately $0.09 (Alaska) to $0.67 (California, including cap-and-trade charges). Several states have adopted variable-rate mechanisms tied to wholesale price or inflation indices.

### No Federal Sugar or SSB Tax

The United States has no federal sugar-sweetened beverage (SSB) tax. Local jurisdictions including Philadelphia (1.5 cents/ounce, 2017), Berkeley (1 cent/ounce, 2015), Seattle (1.75 cents/ounce, 2018), and Boulder (2 cents/ounce, 2017) have enacted SSB taxes. Several states have preemptively prohibited local SSB taxes, including Arizona, Michigan, and Washington state.

### Landmark Cases

**National Federation of Independent Business v. Sebelius, 567 U.S. 519 (2012):** The Supreme Court upheld the ACA individual mandate as a valid exercise of the taxing power, characterizing the shared responsibility payment as an excise tax, while rejecting Commerce Clause authority.

**Department of Revenue of Montana v. Kurth Ranch, 511 U.S. 767 (1994):** The Court held that Montana's "dangerous drug tax" -- essentially an excise tax on illegal drugs imposed after criminal prosecution -- violated the Double Jeopardy Clause.

## Germany

### Legal Framework

Germany's excise tax regime operates within the EU harmonized framework established by Council Directive (EU) 2020/262 (general arrangements for excise duty), supplemented by product-specific directives. Domestic implementation occurs through the Energiesteuergesetz (EnergieStG), Tabaksteuergesetz (TabStG), Alkoholsteuergesetz (AlkStG, effective 2018), Biersteuergesetz (BierStG), Schaumwein- und Zwischenerzeugnissteuergesetz (sparkling wine and intermediate products), and Kaffeesteuergesetz (coffee tax -- unique to Germany among major EU members).

### Tobacco Taxation

Germany's tobacco excise under the Tabaksteuergesetz implements Directive 2011/64/EU:

| Product | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Cigarettes | EUR 118.78/1000 cigarettes + 21.69% of retail price |
| Fine-cut tobacco | EUR 60.82/kg + 13.13% of retail price |
| Cigars | EUR 14/1000 cigars + 1.47% of retail price |
| Heated tobacco products | EUR 15/kg tobacco content (introduced 2022) |

The effective tax burden on a standard pack of cigarettes (20 cigarettes, retail ~EUR 8.00) is approximately EUR 5.40, representing approximately 67.5 percent of the retail price. Germany progressively increased tobacco tax rates through the Tabaksteuermodernisierungsgesetz (2021), implementing annual rate increases through 2026.

### Energy Taxation

The Energiesteuergesetz implements Directive 2003/96/EC:

| Product | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Gasoline (unleaded) | EUR 0.6545/liter |
| Diesel | EUR 0.4704/liter |
| Natural gas (motor fuel) | EUR 0.0180/kWh |
| Light heating oil | EUR 0.0614/liter |
| Heavy heating oil | EUR 0.0250/kg |
| LPG (motor fuel) | EUR 0.409/kg |

Germany temporarily reduced energy tax rates on motor fuels during summer 2022 (the "Tankrabatt") as part of the federal government's energy price relief package in response to the post-Ukraine-invasion price surge, cutting gasoline tax by approximately EUR 0.30/liter and diesel by approximately EUR 0.14/liter for three months.

### CO2 Pricing (BEHG)

The Brennstoffemissionshandelsgesetz (BEHG, 2019) introduced a national emissions trading system for heating and transport fuels not covered by the EU ETS. The CO2 price escalation:

| Year | CO2 Price per Tonne |
|------|-------------------|
| 2021 | EUR 25 |
| 2022 | EUR 30 |
| 2023 | EUR 30 |
| 2024 | EUR 45 |
| 2025 | EUR 55 |
| 2026 | Market-based (corridor EUR 55-65) |

This effectively functions as an additional excise on carbon content of fuels, adding approximately EUR 0.11/liter to gasoline and EUR 0.12/liter to diesel at the 2024 price level.

### Alcohol Taxation

| Product | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Spirits | EUR 13.03/liter pure alcohol |
| Alcopops surcharge | EUR 5,550/hectoliter pure alcohol |
| Beer | EUR 0.787/degree Plato per hectoliter |
| Sparkling wine | EUR 136/hectoliter |
| Wine | EUR 0 (no excise on still wine) |
| Coffee | EUR 2.19/kg (roasted); EUR 4.78/kg (instant) |

Germany is notable for imposing no excise duty on still wine, as permitted under EU Directive 92/83/EEC (which sets the minimum rate for wine at EUR 0). The coffee tax (Kaffeesteuer), a historical relic dating to Frederick the Great's era, is unique among major EU members and generates approximately EUR 1 billion annually.

### Sugar-Sweetened Beverage Tax

Germany has not enacted a sugar-sweetened beverage tax, despite advocacy from public health organizations. The federal government has instead pursued a voluntary industry agreement (Nationale Reduktionsstrategie, 2018) targeting sugar content reduction. Several other EU member states have implemented SSB taxes: France (2012, reformed 2018), United Kingdom (Soft Drinks Industry Levy, 2018), Ireland (Sugar Sweetened Drinks Tax, 2018), and Portugal (2017).

## ECHR & EU

### EU Harmonization Framework

The EU has achieved significant harmonization of excise duties through several key directives:

**Council Directive (EU) 2020/262 (General Arrangements):** Establishes the common framework for excise duty on alcohol, tobacco, and energy products, including the system of duty suspension during movement between member states using the Excise Movement and Control System (EMCS).

**Council Directive 2011/64/EU (Manufactured Tobacco):** Sets minimum rates and structure requirements for tobacco taxation. Minimum excise yield must be at least EUR 90 per 1,000 cigarettes, with total taxation (excise + VAT) representing at least 60 percent of the weighted average retail selling price.

**Council Directive 2003/96/EC (Energy Products):** Establishes minimum rates for energy products used as motor or heating fuels. A proposed revision (COM/2021/563) would restructure rates based on energy content and environmental performance, eliminating favorable treatment of fossil fuels.

**Council Directive 92/83/EEC (Alcohol):** Harmonizes the structures of excise duties on alcohol and alcoholic beverages, with minimum rates set by Council Directive 92/84/EEC. Amended in 2020 to update provisions for small independent producers and denatured alcohol.

### EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)

Regulation (EU) 2023/956 established the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism, applying from October 2023 (transitional reporting phase) with full financial obligations from January 2026. CBAM effectively functions as an excise-type levy on the embedded carbon content of imported goods (initially covering cement, iron and steel, aluminum, fertilizers, electricity, and hydrogen), calculated by reference to the EU ETS carbon price. This represents a novel form of border adjustment designed to prevent carbon leakage.

### ECHR Jurisprudence on Excise Taxation

**Ferrazzini v. Italy (Application No. 44759/98, 2001):** The Grand Chamber held that tax disputes, including excise tax matters, do not concern "civil rights and obligations" under Article 6 ECHR, meaning the fair trial guarantees of Article 6 do not apply to substantive tax assessment disputes (though they apply to penalties constituting "criminal charges").

**Bulves AD v. Bulgaria (Application No. 3991/03, 2009):** While focused on VAT rather than excise, this case established that denying a taxpayer's input tax credit due to a supplier's failure to comply with tax obligations violates Protocol 1, Article 1. The principle has been applied analogously to excise duty refund situations.

**Impar Ltd v. Lithuania (Application No. 13102/04, 2010):** The Court examined excise duty penalties and found that disproportionate penalties for excise irregularities could violate the right to property, requiring a balance between the public interest in tax collection and the burden imposed on the taxpayer.

## Comparative Analysis

### Revenue and Behavioral Policy Tension

All three jurisdictions navigate the inherent tension between maximizing excise revenue and achieving behavioral change. Higher tax rates that successfully reduce consumption of harmful products necessarily reduce the tax base. South Korea's 2015 tobacco tax increase produced initial revenue gains but declining collections as smoking rates fell. The U.S. federal fuel tax, frozen since 1993, has eroded as a revenue source but has also failed to achieve environmental objectives due to its declining real value.

### Tobacco Tax Effectiveness

Among the three jurisdictions, South Korea's 2015 tobacco tax increase provides the clearest natural experiment in excise effectiveness. The near-doubling of cigarette prices produced measurable reductions in smoking prevalence, particularly among younger adults and lower-income groups. Germany's graduated increase strategy under the Tabaksteuermodernisierungsgesetz takes a more gradual approach. The fragmented U.S. system, with vast interstate rate differentials, generates significant cross-border purchasing and smuggling, undermining both revenue and public health objectives.

### Fuel Taxation and Environmental Policy

Germany imposes the highest effective fuel taxes among the three jurisdictions, particularly when the CO2 pricing under BEHG is included, with total gasoline taxation approaching EUR 0.90/liter (approximately $3.60/gallon). South Korea's fuel excise is moderate by OECD standards. The United States has the lowest fuel taxation, with the combined federal and average state gasoline tax of approximately $0.50/gallon representing a fraction of European levels. This disparity reflects fundamentally different political economies of transportation, urban planning, and climate policy.

### Sugar and Health-Related Excise

None of the three primary jurisdictions has enacted a national sugar-sweetened beverage tax, though several EU member states (France, UK, Ireland) and U.S. localities (Philadelphia, Berkeley, Seattle) have done so. The emerging evidence suggests that SSB taxes reduce consumption and incentivize product reformulation, as demonstrated by the UK's tiered Soft Drinks Industry Levy, which prompted manufacturers to reduce sugar content even before the tax took effect.

### Excise Tax Earmarking

South Korea's extensive earmarking of fuel tax revenue for transportation and environmental purposes stands in contrast to Germany's general revenue approach and the U.S. hybrid model (federal fuel tax earmarked for the Highway Trust Fund but most other excise revenue going to general funds). Earmarking provides fiscal certainty for infrastructure investment but reduces budgetary flexibility.
