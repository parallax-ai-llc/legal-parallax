## Summary

Vaccination mandates (백신 의무접종) -- legal requirements compelling individuals to receive vaccinations as a condition of school enrollment, employment, access to public spaces, or as a general civic obligation -- represent one of the most contested intersections of public health authority, individual autonomy, and constitutional rights. South Korea's vaccination framework operates through the Infectious Disease Control and Prevention Act (감염병의 예방 및 관리에 관한 법률) and the National Immunization Program (국가예방접종사업), which provides free vaccination for children and designated adult populations through the Korea Disease Control and Prevention Agency (질병관리청, KDCA); while South Korea does not impose criminal penalties for vaccine refusal, school enrollment requires completion of the National Immunization Schedule, and the COVID-19 pandemic triggered the most extensive vaccination campaign in Korean history, with a vaccine pass system (방역패스) implemented and subsequently discontinued. The United States has the longest legal history of vaccination mandates, with the Supreme Court's Jacobson v. Massachusetts (197 U.S. 11, 1905) establishing the foundational principle that states may exercise police power to enforce compulsory vaccination, upheld in the school context by Zurich v. King (260 U.S. 174, 1922); all 50 states require vaccination for school enrollment, though exemptions vary dramatically -- all states permit medical exemptions, 44 states permit religious exemptions, and 15 states permit philosophical exemptions; the COVID-19 era generated landmark Supreme Court decisions including Biden v. Missouri (2022) upholding CMS healthcare worker mandates and NFIB v. OSHA (2022) blocking the OSHA employer mandate. Germany enacted the Masernschutzgesetz (Measles Protection Act, effective March 2020), requiring proof of measles vaccination or immunity for children attending schools and childcare facilities and for certain healthcare and community facility workers, with the Bundesverfassungsgericht upholding the mandate in its 2022 decision (1 BvR 469/20); the COVID-19 vaccination mandate for healthcare workers (einrichtungsbezogene Impfpflicht) was enacted in December 2021 and expired in December 2022. At the European level, the European Court of Human Rights issued its landmark judgment in Vavřička and Others v. Czech Republic (Grand Chamber, 2021), holding by 16 votes to 1 that compulsory childhood vaccination does not violate Article 8 of the Convention, establishing the most authoritative international human rights standard on vaccination mandates.

## South Korea

### Legal Framework

South Korea's vaccination framework emphasizes universal access and encouragement over punitive mandates, though school entry requirements create de facto compulsion for childhood vaccines.

**Infectious Disease Control and Prevention Act (감염병의 예방 및 관리에 관한 법률):** This Act provides the legal basis for national immunization programs, disease surveillance, and epidemic response. Article 24 authorizes the state to recommend and provide vaccinations, while Article 31 empowers local governments to mandate vaccinations during epidemic emergencies.

**National Immunization Program (국가예방접종사업):** Administered by the KDCA (질병관리청, Korea Disease Control and Prevention Agency), the program provides free vaccinations for 17 diseases on the National Immunization Schedule for children and designated adult populations. Vaccines are administered at public health centers and contracted medical institutions.

### School Entry Requirements

Under Article 31 of the School Health Act (학교보건법), schools must verify completion of required vaccinations upon enrollment. Children who have not completed the National Immunization Schedule are required to complete it; however, the enforcement mechanism is administrative documentation rather than denial of enrollment. In practice, Korea achieves vaccination rates exceeding 97 percent for most childhood vaccines through a combination of school requirements, free provision, and cultural attitudes favoring vaccination.

### COVID-19 Vaccination Campaign

South Korea's COVID-19 response included one of the most rapid and comprehensive vaccination campaigns globally:

- **Vaccine Pass (방역패스):** Implemented from November 2021, the vaccine pass system required proof of vaccination or negative test for access to restaurants, cafes, movie theaters, gyms, and other multipurpose facilities. The system was challenged in court, with the Constitutional Court initially declining to suspend it but the government voluntarily discontinuing the pass system in April 2022 as case severity declined.
- **Vaccination Rates:** By 2024, approximately 87 percent of the total population had received at least two doses, and approximately 65 percent had received booster doses.
- **Vaccine Injury Compensation:** The Act on Prevention of Infectious Diseases provides a government compensation system (피해보상) for injuries caused by government-recommended vaccinations.

## United States

### Legal Framework

The United States has the longest and most extensively litigated history of vaccination mandates.

**Jacobson v. Massachusetts (197 U.S. 11, 1905):** The Supreme Court upheld a Cambridge, Massachusetts ordinance requiring smallpox vaccination, rejecting individual liberty challenges. Justice Harlan wrote: "The liberty secured by the Constitution does not import an absolute right in each person to be, at all times and in all circumstances, wholly freed from restraint." The decision established that states may exercise police power to compel vaccination when the community's health is at risk.

**Zurich v. King (260 U.S. 174, 1922):** The Court upheld a school vaccination requirement in San Antonio, Texas, citing Jacobson and affirming the state's authority to condition school attendance on vaccination.

### School Vaccination Requirements

All 50 states require vaccinations for school enrollment, but exemption policies vary dramatically:

| Exemption Type | Number of States | Key States |
|---------------|-----------------|------------|
| Medical only | 5 | California, New York, West Virginia, Mississippi, Maine |
| Medical + religious | 30 | Most states |
| Medical + religious + philosophical | 15 | Including Texas, Ohio, Michigan |

**California SB 277 (2015) and SB 276 (2019):** California eliminated non-medical exemptions following the 2014-2015 Disneyland measles outbreak, and subsequently tightened medical exemption procedures to prevent abuse. These laws survived legal challenges and have been associated with increased vaccination rates.

### COVID-19 Era Mandates

**Biden v. Missouri (595 U.S. ___, 2022):** The Supreme Court (6-3) upheld CMS's interim final rule requiring COVID-19 vaccination for healthcare workers at facilities participating in Medicare and Medicaid, finding it within the Secretary's authority to impose conditions of participation to protect patient health and safety.

**NFIB v. OSHA (595 U.S. ___, 2022):** The Supreme Court (6-3) stayed OSHA's emergency temporary standard requiring employers with 100+ employees to ensure workers were vaccinated or tested weekly. The majority held that OSHA lacked authority to impose such a broad mandate, as COVID-19 was not an occupational hazard unique to the workplace.

### Vaccine Injury Compensation

The National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act (NCVIA, 1986) established the National Vaccine Injury Compensation Program (VICP), a no-fault administrative system for compensating vaccine injuries. The Countermeasures Injury Compensation Program (CICP) covers COVID-19 vaccines under the PREP Act declaration.

## Germany

### Legal Framework

Germany's vaccination mandate landscape reflects the country's strong tradition of individual rights tempered by community health obligations.

**Masernschutzgesetz (Measles Protection Act, effective March 1, 2020):** Following measles outbreaks and declining vaccination rates, the Bundestag enacted the first disease-specific vaccination mandate in post-war German history. The Act requires:

- **Children:** Proof of measles vaccination (or immunity or medical contraindication) for enrollment in kindergartens, schools, and other community facilities (Gemeinschaftseinrichtungen).
- **Workers:** Proof of measles vaccination for workers in childcare facilities, schools, medical facilities, and refugee accommodation centers.
- **Documentation:** Proof through vaccination records, medical attestation of immunity, or medical contraindication certificate.
- **Penalties:** Non-compliant individuals face fines of up to 2,500 EUR. Children without proof may be excluded from daycare (but not from compulsory schooling, as the constitutional right to education prevails).

### Bundesverfassungsgericht Decision (2022)

**BVerfG, Order of August 21, 2022 (1 BvR 469/20, 1 BvR 472/20, 1 BvR 1295/20, 1 BvR 1454/20):** The Federal Constitutional Court upheld the measles vaccination mandate, finding it compatible with the Basic Law. Key holdings:

- **Article 2(2) (Physical Integrity):** The mandate interferes with the right to physical integrity, but this interference is justified by the legitimate aim of protecting public health and the health of vulnerable persons who cannot be vaccinated.
- **Article 6(2) (Parental Rights):** While parents have the right to make health decisions for their children, this right is limited by the state's duty to protect children's welfare (Kindeswohl) and public health.
- **Proportionality:** The mandate is proportionate because measles is a serious and highly contagious disease, vaccination is safe and effective, medical exemptions protect those who cannot be vaccinated, and the enforcement mechanism (fines, daycare exclusion) is moderate.

### COVID-19 Vaccination Mandates

**Facility-Based Mandate (einrichtungsbezogene Impfpflicht):** Enacted in December 2021, this mandate required healthcare and care facility workers to provide proof of COVID-19 vaccination, recovery, or medical contraindication by March 15, 2022. Non-compliant workers could be prohibited from entering their workplace. The mandate expired on December 31, 2022.

**Failed General Mandate:** In April 2022, the Bundestag voted on multiple proposals for a general COVID-19 vaccination mandate for adults (initially proposed for persons over 60, with a later extension to all adults). All proposals were defeated, reflecting deep political divisions and public resistance to compulsory COVID-19 vaccination.

### Infektionsschutzgesetz (IfSG, Infection Protection Act)

The IfSG provides the general framework for infectious disease prevention, including authorization for vaccination recommendations by the Standing Committee on Vaccination (Ständige Impfkommission, STIKO) at the Robert Koch Institute (RKI). STIKO recommendations form the basis for statutory health insurance coverage of recommended vaccinations and inform public health policy, though STIKO recommendations are advisory, not mandatory.

## ECHR & EU

### Vavřička and Others v. Czech Republic (Grand Chamber, 2021)

**Applications Nos. 47621/13 and 5 others, Grand Chamber Judgment of April 8, 2021:** The most significant international human rights ruling on vaccination mandates. The Grand Chamber (16-1) held that the Czech Republic's mandatory childhood vaccination program did not violate Article 8 (respect for private life) of the Convention.

**Key Findings:**

- **Interference with Article 8:** Compulsory vaccination constitutes an interference with the right to respect for private life, specifically physical integrity.
- **Legitimate Aim:** The mandate pursues the legitimate aims of protecting health (both individual children and the community through herd immunity) and the rights of others (particularly immunocompromised persons who cannot be vaccinated).
- **Margin of Appreciation:** States enjoy a wide margin of appreciation in health policy matters. The Court noted that a general consensus exists among Council of Europe member states that vaccination is one of the most successful public health interventions, though specific mandates vary.
- **Proportionality:** The Czech system was proportionate because: it targeted nine well-established diseases (not novel vaccines); medical exemptions and "conscience objections" were available; the consequence of non-compliance was exclusion from preschool (not criminal penalties); and the vaccination schedule was based on scientific evidence and medical consensus.

**Dissent:** Judge Wojtyczek's sole dissent argued that the majority's analysis insufficiently addressed the specificity of individual vaccine risks and the implications of accepting state authority to impose medical procedures on healthy individuals.

### EU Health Policy

The EU has no harmonized vaccination mandate framework. Vaccination policy is a national competence under Article 168 TFEU. However, the EU coordinates through:

- **European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control (ECDC):** Provides surveillance data, risk assessments, and guidance on vaccination.
- **Council Recommendation on Strengthening Cooperation against Vaccine-Preventable Diseases (2018):** Encourages member states to strengthen immunization programs, improve surveillance, and address vaccine hesitancy.
- **EU Digital COVID Certificate (Regulation (EU) 2021/953):** Facilitated free movement during the pandemic by providing standardized proof of vaccination, test, or recovery, applicable from July 2021 to June 2023.

### European Court of Human Rights -- Additional Jurisprudence

**Solomakhin v. Ukraine (Application No. 24429/03, Judgment of March 15, 2012):** The Court found no Article 8 violation where an individual was vaccinated against diphtheria and tetanus without explicit consent during a medical examination. The Court held that vaccination serves the legitimate aim of protecting health and may be proportionate even absent specific consent in appropriate circumstances.

## Comparative Analysis

### Mandate Models

Jurisdictions employ different mandate models. South Korea uses school documentation requirements without criminal penalties (soft mandate). The United States requires vaccination for school enrollment with varying exemption breadth. Germany enacted disease-specific mandates (measles) with fines. The ECHR Vavřička framework validates compulsory vaccination as a proportionate interference with Article 8 rights, providing wide latitude to states.

### Exemption Policies

The breadth of exemptions is a critical policy variable. States with broad philosophical exemptions (15 US states) tend to have lower vaccination rates and more frequent outbreaks. California's elimination of non-medical exemptions was associated with significant increases in vaccination coverage. Germany permits only medical exemptions for the measles mandate. The ECHR has validated both limited-exemption systems (Vavřička) and indicated that "conscience objections" may be part of a proportionate system.

### COVID-19 Lessons

The COVID-19 pandemic tested vaccination mandate frameworks globally. South Korea achieved high voluntary compliance with moderate access-restriction measures. The US Supreme Court's divergent rulings (upholding healthcare worker mandates, blocking employer mandates) demonstrated the importance of statutory authority and sector-specificity. Germany's failed general mandate vote showed the political limits of compulsory vaccination even during a pandemic.

### Compensation Systems

All jurisdictions maintain some form of vaccine injury compensation. The US VICP provides the most established system, with a dedicated no-fault administrative process and a vaccine injury table providing presumptive compensation for specified injuries. South Korea and Germany provide government compensation for injuries from recommended/mandated vaccines. Compensation systems are essential complements to mandate programs, addressing the fairness concern that individuals bearing risks for community benefit should be compensated.
