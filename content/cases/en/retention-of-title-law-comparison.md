## Summary

Retention of title (also known as reservation of title or conditional sale) is a contractual arrangement whereby a seller retains ownership of goods sold until the buyer fulfills specified conditions, typically full payment of the purchase price. This device is of fundamental importance in commercial law, providing the unpaid seller with proprietary protection superior to that of a mere unsecured creditor, particularly in the buyer's insolvency. South Korea recognizes retention of title under the Korean Civil Code (민법) Article 591 (소유권유보, ownership reservation) and the Installment Transactions Act (할부거래에 관한 법률), with the Korean Supreme Court developing jurisprudence on the scope, effects, and insolvency treatment of ownership reservation clauses, though extended and enlarged forms of retention of title are less developed than in Germany. The United States treats retention of title as creating a security interest under UCC Article 9 (specifically § 2-401(1) and § 1-201(b)(35)), stripping the seller of proprietary claims and subjecting the retention to the filing and priority rules of Article 9 as a purchase-money security interest (PMSI); the American functional approach fundamentally recharacterizes what other legal systems treat as retained ownership into a security device. Germany provides the most sophisticated and expansive retention of title regime, rooted in BGB § 449 (Eigentumsvorbehalt), with extensive case law and commercial practice developing the simple retention of title (einfacher Eigentumsvorbehalt), extended retention of title (verlängerter Eigentumsvorbehalt) covering resale proceeds, enlarged retention of title (erweiterter Eigentumsvorbehalt or Kontokorrentvorbehalt) securing all claims between the parties, and the expanded retention of title covering processed goods (Verarbeitungsklausel); German retention of title survives insolvency under the Insolvenzordnung (InsO) § 47 as a right of segregation (Aussonderungsrecht). The EU/ECHR framework addresses retention of title through the Late Payment Directive 2011/7/EU (Article 9), which requires member states to recognize simple retention of title, the DCFR Book IX provisions on retention of ownership devices, and the EU Insolvency Regulation 2015/848, which protects reservation of title rights in cross-border insolvency proceedings under Article 10.

## South Korea

### Legal Framework

South Korea's retention of title law is grounded in the Korean Civil Code and supplemented by consumer protection legislation for installment sales.

**Civil Code (민법) Article 591 (소유권유보):** Article 591 provides the basic framework for retention of title in the context of installment sales. Where it is agreed that ownership of goods sold shall not pass to the buyer until full payment of the price, and the buyer has paid more than half of the price, the seller may not rescind the contract on the ground of non-payment of the remainder unless the seller has demanded payment and the buyer has failed to pay within a reasonable period (the "more than half payment" protection).

**Installment Transactions Act (할부거래에 관한 법률):** This Act regulates consumer installment purchases, providing additional protections for buyers. The seller who retains title must clearly disclose the retention of title clause. The Act restricts the seller's right to repossess goods where the buyer has paid a substantial portion of the price, balancing seller security against buyer protection.

### Judicial Development

The Korean Supreme Court has developed the scope and effects of retention of title through several significant decisions:

- **Supreme Court Decision 97Da26637 (1999):** Held that a retention of title clause is effective between the parties and against third parties if the clause is part of a valid sales contract. The seller retains full ownership until the condition (payment) is fulfilled.
- **Supreme Court Decision 2003Da30463 (2005):** Addressed the conflict between a retention of title seller and a bona fide purchaser from the buyer. The Court held that a buyer in possession of goods subject to retention of title may transfer ownership to a bona fide third party under the Civil Code's bona fide acquisition rules (Article 249), extinguishing the seller's retained title.
- **Supreme Court Decision 2010Da49752 (2012):** Held that where the buyer resells goods subject to retention of title before full payment, the seller's retained title is extinguished by the resale to a bona fide purchaser, and the seller has only a personal claim against the buyer for the unpaid price.

### Extended and Enlarged Retention of Title

Korean law has not developed extended or enlarged retention of title to the same degree as German law. The Korean Supreme Court has not authoritatively recognized a verlängerter Eigentumsvorbehalt equivalent (assignment of resale proceeds to the original seller) or a Kontokorrentvorbehalt (retention securing all claims between the parties). In practice, Korean suppliers seeking extended protection rely on the 2010 Act on Security over Movable Property to register a security interest in the buyer's inventory or receivables.

### Insolvency Treatment

Under the Debtor Rehabilitation and Bankruptcy Act (채무자 회생 및 파산에 관한 법률), the seller's retained title is generally respected. In rehabilitation proceedings, the seller may assert an ownership claim to undelivered or unpaid goods. However, the rehabilitation court may order continued use of the goods subject to retention of title if necessary for the debtor's rehabilitation, subject to providing adequate protection to the seller.

## United States

### Legal Framework

The United States takes a fundamentally different approach to retention of title, recharacterizing it as a security interest under UCC Article 9 rather than recognizing it as retained ownership.

**UCC § 2-401(1):** Any retention or reservation by the seller of the title (property) in goods shipped or delivered to the buyer is limited in effect to a reservation of a security interest. This provision, together with the definition of "security interest" in § 1-201(b)(35), means that a seller who purports to retain title to goods delivered to the buyer is deemed to have created an Article 9 security interest, not retained ownership.

**Consequences of Recharacterization:** The seller's retained interest is a purchase-money security interest (PMSI) in the goods. The seller must perfect this interest by filing a financing statement under Article 9 to maintain priority against the buyer's other creditors. If the seller fails to file, the interest is unperfected and subordinate to the rights of a lien creditor (including a bankruptcy trustee under 11 U.S.C. § 544(a)).

### PMSI Treatment

**PMSI Super-Priority (§ 9-324):** If the seller perfects the PMSI within 20 days after the buyer receives possession of the goods (for goods other than inventory), the PMSI has priority over a conflicting security interest in the same goods, even if the conflicting interest was filed first. For inventory, the PMSI holder must file before the buyer receives the inventory and notify existing secured parties.

**Proceeds (§ 9-315):** A security interest continues in identifiable proceeds of the collateral. The seller with a perfected PMSI has a security interest in cash proceeds, accounts, or other identifiable proceeds from the buyer's disposition of the goods.

### Enforcement and Insolvency

**Self-Help Repossession (§ 9-609):** After default (typically non-payment), the seller may repossess the goods without judicial process, provided this can be done without breach of the peace. The seller must then dispose of the goods in a commercially reasonable manner (§ 9-610) or propose to retain them in satisfaction of the debt (§ 9-620).

**Bankruptcy Treatment:** In bankruptcy, the seller's PMSI is treated as a secured claim under 11 U.S.C. § 506(a). The automatic stay (§ 362) prevents the seller from repossessing the goods. The seller is entitled to adequate protection of its interest. Under Chapter 11 reorganization, the debtor may retain the goods subject to the PMSI if the reorganization plan provides for payment of the secured claim.

### Comparison with Proprietary Approach

The American recharacterization approach has been criticized for eliminating the seller's proprietary protection and subjecting retention of title to the complexities of Article 9 perfection and priority rules. However, proponents argue that the functional approach promotes transparency (through filing), prevents secret liens, and provides a single coherent priority framework for all security interests in personal property.

| Feature | Proprietary (Korean/German) Approach | Functional (American) Approach |
|---------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Legal nature | Retained ownership | Security interest |
| Formality | No registration required | Filing required for perfection |
| Priority basis | Property right; segregation in insolvency | First-to-file-or-perfect; PMSI super-priority |
| Third-party effect | Effective without publicity (subject to bona fide acquisition) | Requires filing for effect against third parties |
| Insolvency | Segregation right (Aussonderung) | Secured creditor subject to automatic stay |

## Germany

### Legal Framework

Germany has the most developed and sophisticated retention of title regime among the studied jurisdictions, with extensive statutory, judicial, and contractual elaboration.

**BGB § 449 (Eigentumsvorbehalt):** The basic provision states that where a seller has reserved ownership of a movable thing until payment of the purchase price, it is presumed in case of doubt that the transfer of ownership is subject to the condition precedent (aufschiebende Bedingung) of full payment, and that the seller is entitled to rescind the contract if the buyer defaults on payment. The retention requires no special formality -- it is effective by mere agreement between the parties.

**Anwartschaftsrecht (Expectancy Right):** The buyer under retention of title acquires an Anwartschaftsrecht -- a legally recognized expectancy right that is itself a property right. The Anwartschaftsrecht is transferable (BGH, BGHZ 28, 16), pledgeable, and subject to attachment by the buyer's creditors. The Anwartschaftsrecht automatically ripens into full ownership upon payment of the last installment.

### Forms of Retention of Title

**Einfacher Eigentumsvorbehalt (Simple Retention of Title):** Title is retained until full payment of the purchase price for the specific goods. This is the basic form recognized by BGB § 449.

**Verlängerter Eigentumsvorbehalt (Extended Retention of Title):** The seller permits the buyer to resell the goods in the ordinary course of business, and the buyer assigns in advance (Vorausabtretung) to the seller the claims arising from such resale. Upon resale, the seller's retained title is extinguished (the sub-buyer acquires ownership), but the seller acquires the buyer's claim against the sub-buyer. This device is essential for goods intended for resale:

- **BGH, Urteil vom 30.10.1961 -- VIII ZR 172/60 (BGHZ 36, 56):** Recognized the validity of extended retention of title and the advance assignment of resale proceeds.
- **BGH, Urteil vom 09.11.1978 -- VII ZR 54/77:** Addressed the conflict between extended retention of title and Globalzession (bulk assignment of receivables to a financing bank), developing the principle that extended retention of title claims are excluded from the Globalzession.

**Erweiterter Eigentumsvorbehalt (Enlarged/Current Account Retention of Title, Kontokorrentvorbehalt):** Title is retained until all claims of the seller against the buyer arising from the business relationship are satisfied, not just the price of the specific goods. This secures a running account between the parties:

- **BGH, Urteil vom 09.07.1975 -- VIII ZR 89/74:** Upheld the validity of Kontokorrentvorbehalt in general terms, but the BGH has placed limits on excessive enlargement.
- **Limits on Enlargement:** A retention of title clause that secures claims of affiliated companies (Konzernvorbehalt) or claims unrelated to the commercial relationship may be invalid as an unreasonable restraint on the buyer's property (Übersicherung, over-collateralization). The BGH has applied § 307 BGB (control of unfair standard terms) to strike down excessively broad enlargement clauses.

**Verarbeitungsklausel (Processing Clause):** Where goods subject to retention of title are processed or combined with other materials by the buyer, BGB § 950 would normally transfer ownership of the new product to the manufacturer (buyer). The Verarbeitungsklausel provides that the seller is deemed the manufacturer for purposes of § 950, or that the seller acquires co-ownership of the processed product in proportion to the value of the supplied goods:

- **BGH, Urteil vom 03.03.1956 -- IV ZR 334/55 (BGHZ 20, 159):** Recognized the validity of the Verarbeitungsklausel, holding that the parties may contractually derogate from the statutory rule that the processor acquires ownership.

### Insolvency Treatment

**InsO § 47 (Aussonderungsrecht):** In insolvency proceedings, a person who has a right in rem or a personal right to an object that does not belong to the insolvency estate may demand segregation (Aussonderung) of the object from the estate. The retention of title seller has an Aussonderungsrecht for goods still in the buyer's possession and unpaid for.

**InsO § 107 (Executory Contracts):** If the sales contract is executory (neither side has fully performed) at the time of insolvency, the insolvency administrator may elect to perform or reject the contract. If the administrator elects to perform (by paying the price), the seller must deliver the goods.

**Extended Retention in Insolvency:** The advance assignment of resale proceeds (verlängerter Eigentumsvorbehalt) creates an Absonderungsrecht (right of separate satisfaction) rather than an Aussonderungsrecht, because the receivable from resale is part of the insolvency estate. The seller has a preferential right to satisfaction from the proceeds, but does not have the right to segregate the receivable entirely from the estate.

### Practical Significance

Retention of title clauses (Eigentumsvorbehaltsklauseln) are ubiquitous in German business-to-business transactions. The Allgemeine Geschäftsbedingungen (AGB, general terms and conditions) of virtually every German manufacturer and wholesaler contain detailed retention of title provisions. Conflicts between competing retention of title claims (of multiple suppliers) and the buyer's secured lender (who holds a Globalzession) are among the most litigated issues in German commercial law.

## ECHR & EU

### Late Payment Directive 2011/7/EU

The Late Payment Directive, which replaced the earlier Directive 2000/35/EC, addresses the problem of late payment in commercial transactions. Article 9 specifically requires member states to provide that a seller retains title to goods until they are fully paid for, where a retention of title clause has been expressly agreed before delivery. This is the most direct EU harmonization measure on retention of title:

- **Scope:** Applies to commercial transactions between undertakings, and between undertakings and public authorities.
- **Requirements:** The retention of title clause must be expressly agreed before delivery. The Directive does not prescribe specific formalities.
- **Limitation:** Article 9 applies only to simple retention of title. Extended, enlarged, and processing clause variants are not covered by the Directive and remain subject to divergent national laws.

### EU Insolvency Regulation 2015/848

Article 10 of the Insolvency Regulation provides crucial cross-border protection for retention of title:

**Article 10(1):** The opening of insolvency proceedings against a buyer does not affect the seller's rights based on a reservation of title where at the time of the opening of proceedings the asset subject to the reservation of title is situated in a member state other than the state of the opening.

**Article 10(2):** The opening of insolvency proceedings against a seller, after delivery of the asset, does not constitute a ground for rescinding or terminating the sale and does not prevent the buyer from acquiring ownership where at the time of the opening of proceedings the asset is situated in a member state other than the state of the opening.

This provision ensures that retention of title rights are not defeated by the mere opening of insolvency proceedings in another member state, applying the lex rei sitae principle to determine the effectiveness of the reservation.

### DCFR Book IX: Retention of Ownership Devices

The DCFR Book IX treats retention of ownership as a form of proprietary security, subjecting it to the same general framework as other security interests. This approach:

- **Registration (IX.-3:107):** Requires retention of title to be registered to be effective against third parties other than the buyer. This would fundamentally change German practice, where no registration is required.
- **Functional Equivalence (IX.-1:103):** Treats retention of title, financial leasing, and conditional sale as functionally equivalent proprietary security devices, all governed by the same rules for creation, publicity, priority, and enforcement.
- **Extended Retention (IX.-2:305):** Recognizes extended retention of title covering proceeds from resale and products of processing.

### Cross-Border Challenges

The cross-border effectiveness of retention of title within the EU remains problematic despite the Insolvency Regulation. Key issues include:

| Issue | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Applicable law | Rome I Regulation Art. 14 governs the contractual relationship, but property law aspects are governed by lex rei sitae (not harmonized) |
| Recognition of extended RoT | An extended retention of title valid under German law may not be recognized in a member state that does not recognize advance assignment of future receivables |
| Insolvency priority | The ranking of retention of title in insolvency varies by member state, even under the Insolvency Regulation |
| Processed goods | Whether the seller retains co-ownership of processed goods depends on the property law of the state where processing occurs |

### CJEU Case Law

- **CJEU, Lutz v. Bäuerle (Case C-557/13, 2015):** Addressed the interplay between retention of title and the EU Insolvency Regulation, holding that the effects of insolvency proceedings on a pending lawship concerning an asset belonging to the insolvency estate are governed by the law of the member state of the opening of proceedings.
- **CJEU, Senior Home (Case C-195/15, 2016):** Clarified the application of Article 10 in cross-border retention of title scenarios, affirming the protection of seller's rights where the goods are located in a different member state.

## Comparative Analysis

### Proprietary vs. Functional Approach

The most fundamental divergence is between the proprietary approach (Germany, South Korea) that treats retention of title as retained ownership and the functional approach (United States) that recharacterizes it as a security interest. The German proprietary approach provides the seller with stronger protection in insolvency (Aussonderungsrecht -- right to reclaim the goods entirely, rather than merely having a secured claim). The American approach subordinates the seller's interest to the transparency requirements of the filing system, potentially exposing sellers who fail to file to loss of priority.

### Sophistication of Extended Forms

Germany stands alone in the sophistication and commercial importance of extended and enlarged retention of title. The verlängerter Eigentumsvorbehalt (extended retention covering resale proceeds) and erweiterter Eigentumsvorbehalt (enlarged retention securing all claims) are commercially ubiquitous and deeply embedded in German business practice. Neither South Korea nor the United States has developed comparable proprietary devices, though the American PMSI in proceeds and future advance clauses achieve some similar practical results through the Article 9 framework.

### Insolvency Protection

In insolvency, the German seller with simple retention of title enjoys the strongest protection (Aussonderungsrecht under InsO § 47), reclaiming the goods entirely rather than competing with other creditors. The American seller has a secured claim subject to the automatic stay and the Chapter 11 reorganization process. The South Korean seller has ownership-based protection, but the rehabilitation court may order continued use of the goods. The EU Insolvency Regulation protects cross-border retention of title through Article 10, but the substantive strength of the protection depends on the applicable national law.

### Bona Fide Acquisition

All three national systems recognize that a bona fide purchaser from the buyer may acquire ownership free of the seller's retained title. In Germany, this is achieved through §§ 932ff. BGB. In South Korea, through Civil Code Article 249. In the United States, through UCC § 9-320(a) (buyer in ordinary course of business). The practical difference is that the German buyer's Anwartschaftsrecht is itself a recognized property right that can be transferred and pledged, creating additional complexity in the priority hierarchy.
