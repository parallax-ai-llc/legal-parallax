## Summary

Customs duties -- taxes levied on goods as they cross national borders -- represent one of the oldest forms of government revenue and remain a critical instrument of trade policy, industrial protection, and international economic relations, governed by an increasingly complex web of multilateral rules under the World Trade Organization, bilateral and regional free trade agreements, and domestic customs legislation. South Korea administers customs duties under the Customs Act (관세법) and the Tariff Schedule Act (관세율표), with the Korea Customs Service (관세청) collecting duties based on a Most-Favored-Nation (MFN) applied tariff averaging approximately 13.6 percent (WTO simple average, 2024), while maintaining an extensive network of 21 free trade agreements covering 59 countries including the Korea-EU FTA (2011), KORUS FTA (2012), and the Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership (RCEP, 2022). The United States imposes customs duties under the Tariff Act of 1930 (Smoot-Hawley) as amended and codified in Title 19 of the U.S. Code, administered by U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) under the Department of Homeland Security, with the Harmonized Tariff Schedule of the United States (HTSUS) establishing MFN rates averaging approximately 3.4 percent, though recent Section 301 tariffs on Chinese goods (up to 25 percent) and Section 232 tariffs on steel (25 percent) and aluminum (10 percent) have significantly increased the effective rate. Germany, as an EU member state, operates within the EU's common customs framework under the Union Customs Code (Regulation 952/2013), with the Common External Tariff (CET) and Combined Nomenclature administered collectively, MFN rates averaging approximately 5.1 percent, and customs revenue constituting one of the EU's "own resources" with member states retaining 25 percent as collection costs. The ECHR has addressed customs enforcement measures under Protocol 1, Article 1, while WTO dispute settlement and the EU's extensive network of preferential trade agreements form the supranational framework governing tariff policy.

## South Korea

### Legal Framework

South Korea's customs regime is governed by the Customs Act (관세법, Act No. 19929), the Tariff Schedule Act (관세율표), and numerous subordinate regulations. The Korea Customs Service (관세청), an agency under the Ministry of Economy and Finance, administers customs collection, enforcement, and border control. South Korea's tariff schedule follows the Harmonized System maintained by the World Customs Organization, expanded to a 10-digit national code (HSK).

### Tariff Structure

South Korea maintains relatively high tariffs by OECD standards, particularly for agricultural products:

| Sector | Simple Average MFN Rate |
|--------|------------------------|
| Agricultural products | ~56.7% |
| Fish and fish products | ~16.6% |
| Industrial products | ~6.8% |
| Textiles and clothing | ~10.3% |
| Overall simple average | ~13.6% |

Tariff-rate quotas (TRQs) apply to sensitive agricultural products including rice, barley, soybeans, and red peppers. Rice remains among the most protected products, with an in-quota tariff of 5 percent on limited quantities and an out-of-quota rate of 513 percent following the transition from quantitative restrictions under the WTO Agreement on Agriculture.

### Free Trade Agreements

South Korea has pursued an aggressive FTA strategy:

| Agreement | Entry into Force | Key Features |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Korea-Chile FTA | 2004 | Korea's first FTA; phased tariff elimination |
| Korea-ASEAN FTA | 2007 | Covers 10 ASEAN members; normal and sensitive tracks |
| Korea-EU FTA | 2011 | Comprehensive; immediate elimination on 98.7% of tariff lines within 5 years |
| KORUS FTA (Korea-US) | 2012 | Immediate duty-free on 80% of US agricultural exports |
| Korea-China FTA | 2015 | Phased; 90% of tariff lines within 20 years |
| RCEP | 2022 | 15 Asia-Pacific countries; modest tariff reductions |
| Korea-UK FTA | 2021 | Continuity agreement post-Brexit |

Rules of origin under each FTA vary but generally require substantial transformation or minimum regional value content (typically 35-55 percent) for goods to qualify for preferential tariff treatment.

### Trade Remedies

South Korea actively employs trade remedy instruments. The Korea Trade Commission (무역위원회) investigates anti-dumping, countervailing duty, and safeguard cases. Notable actions include anti-dumping duties on Chinese stainless steel products, Japanese chemical imports, and various steel products. The Customs Act Articles 51-67 provide the domestic legal basis, implementing the WTO Anti-Dumping Agreement and SCM Agreement.

### Authorized Economic Operator (AEO)

Korea's AEO program under Customs Act Article 255-2 provides expedited customs clearance, reduced inspections, and mutual recognition agreements with 22 countries' customs authorities. AEO-certified companies receive priority treatment at the border, with clearance times reduced by up to 80 percent.

## United States

### Legal Framework

U.S. customs law is rooted in the Tariff Act of 1930 (Smoot-Hawley Act), codified primarily in Title 19 of the U.S. Code. U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP), within the Department of Homeland Security, is the primary enforcement agency. The U.S. International Trade Commission (USITC) maintains the Harmonized Tariff Schedule of the United States (HTSUS). Trade policy is coordinated by the Office of the United States Trade Representative (USTR).

### Tariff Structure and Recent Developments

While the U.S. MFN applied tariff averages approximately 3.4 percent, several executive actions have significantly altered the effective tariff landscape:

| Action | Legal Authority | Products Affected | Rate |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|------|
| Section 301 tariffs (China) | Trade Act of 1974, § 301 | Lists 1-4: ~$370 billion of Chinese goods | 7.5-25% |
| Section 232 tariffs (steel) | Trade Expansion Act of 1962, § 232 | Steel imports (most countries) | 25% |
| Section 232 tariffs (aluminum) | Trade Expansion Act of 1962, § 232 | Aluminum imports (most countries) | 10% |
| Section 201 tariffs (solar) | Trade Act of 1974, § 201 | Solar cells and modules | 14.75% (2024) |

The legality of Section 301 tariffs on Chinese goods was challenged in United States Court of International Trade in HMTX Industries v. United States (2023), where the court questioned the procedural adequacy of the USTR's notice-and-comment process for the List 3 and List 4 tariff expansions.

### Customs Valuation

The United States implements the WTO Customs Valuation Agreement through 19 U.S.C. § 1401a. Transaction value (the price actually paid or payable) is the primary valuation method. Six alternative methods apply sequentially when transaction value cannot be determined: (1) transaction value of identical merchandise, (2) transaction value of similar merchandise, (3) deductive value, (4) computed value, (5) deductive/computed in reverse order, and (6) fallback method.

### Free Trade Agreements and Preference Programs

| Agreement/Program | Partners | Key Feature |
|-------------------|----------|-------------|
| USMCA (2020) | Canada, Mexico | Replaced NAFTA; auto rules of origin at 75% RVC |
| CAFTA-DR | Central America, DR | Phased tariff elimination |
| US-Korea FTA (KORUS) | South Korea | Full implementation by 2027 |
| US-Australia FTA | Australia | Immediate duty-free on 99% of tariff lines |
| GSP (expired/renewed) | Developing countries | Duty-free on eligible products (lapsed 2020) |
| AGOA | Sub-Saharan Africa | Duty-free for qualifying African countries |

### Anti-Dumping and Countervailing Duties

The Department of Commerce's International Trade Administration (ITA) determines dumping margins and subsidy rates. The USITC determines material injury. The United States is the most active user of anti-dumping duties globally, with over 400 active anti-dumping orders as of 2024. The annual administrative review process under 19 U.S.C. § 1675 adjusts duty rates based on updated pricing and cost data.

### Landmark Cases

**United States v. Mead Corp., 533 U.S. 218 (2001):** The Supreme Court held that CBP's tariff classification rulings do not receive Chevron deference but are entitled to Skidmore respect, establishing the standard of judicial review for customs classification decisions.

**Algoma Steel Corp. v. United States, 865 F.2d 240 (Fed. Cir. 1989):** The Federal Circuit established the substantial evidence standard for reviewing ITC injury determinations in anti-dumping cases.

**Nippon Steel Corp. v. United States, 337 F.3d 1373 (Fed. Cir. 2003):** Clarified the standard for reviewing Commerce Department dumping margin calculations, requiring substantial evidence support.

## Germany

### Legal Framework

As an EU member state, Germany does not maintain an independent customs tariff. The EU's common commercial policy under Article 207 TFEU places exclusive competence for customs matters with the European Union. The Union Customs Code (UCC, Regulation 952/2013), which replaced the Community Customs Code (Regulation 2913/92) on May 1, 2016, provides the comprehensive legal framework. Germany's domestic customs administration (Zoll) falls under the Federal Ministry of Finance and operates 43 principal customs offices (Hauptzollämter).

### EU Common External Tariff

The Combined Nomenclature (CN), based on the HS with EU-specific subdivisions at the 8-digit level, and the integrated tariff (TARIC) at the 10-digit level, establish the applicable duty rates. The EU's average MFN applied tariff is approximately 5.1 percent, with significant variation:

| Sector | EU MFN Average |
|--------|---------------|
| Agricultural products | ~10.1% |
| Dairy products | ~32.3% |
| Sugar and confectionery | ~23.6% |
| Industrial products | ~4.2% |
| Textiles and clothing | ~6.5% |
| Motor vehicles | 10% (cars); 22% (trucks) |
| Overall simple average | ~5.1% |

### EU Preferential Trade Agreements

The EU maintains over 40 preferential trade agreements:

| Agreement | Key Partners | Status |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| EU-South Korea FTA | South Korea | In force since 2011 |
| CETA | Canada | Provisionally applied since 2017 |
| EU-Japan EPA | Japan | In force since 2019 |
| EU-Vietnam FTA | Vietnam | In force since 2020 |
| EU-UK TCA | United Kingdom | In force since 2021 |
| EU-Mercosur | Brazil, Argentina, etc. | Agreed in principle 2024 |
| EU-New Zealand FTA | New Zealand | In force since 2024 |
| GSP/GSP+/EBA | Developing/LDC countries | Regulation 978/2012 |

### Rules of Origin

EU preferential rules of origin are governed by Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2015/2446 (UCC Delegated Act). Rules vary by product but typically require either a change in tariff classification, sufficient working or processing, or a minimum value-added threshold. The Pan-Euro-Mediterranean (PEM) Convention on rules of origin allows diagonal cumulation of origin among 25 participating Mediterranean and European countries.

### Anti-Dumping and Trade Remedies

EU trade remedy investigations are conducted by the European Commission under Regulation (EU) 2016/1036 (anti-dumping) and Regulation (EU) 2016/1037 (anti-subsidy). The EU maintains approximately 150 active anti-dumping measures. The 2017 amendment to the anti-dumping basic regulation introduced a new methodology for calculating normal value in cases involving "significant distortions" in the exporting country, effectively addressing China's non-market economy status without explicitly maintaining the "analogue country" method.

### Germany-Specific Enforcement

The German customs administration (Zollverwaltung) plays a significant enforcement role beyond tariff collection, including combating customs fraud, enforcing import restrictions (e.g., dual-use goods under Regulation 2021/821), intellectual property border measures, and the enforcement of sanctions. The Zollfahndungsdienst (Customs Investigation Service) and Zollkriminalamt (Customs Criminal Office) handle complex fraud cases.

## ECHR & EU

### WTO Framework

The WTO framework establishes the multilateral rules governing customs duties:

**GATT 1994, Article II (Schedules of Concessions):** Each WTO member binds its tariff rates in its schedule. Applied rates may be below bound rates but cannot exceed them without compensation or renegotiation under Article XXVIII.

**WTO Agreement on Customs Valuation:** Establishes the transaction value methodology as the primary basis for customs valuation, with five alternative methods. The Agreement requires transparency, uniformity, and right of appeal in valuation decisions.

**WTO Agreement on Rules of Origin:** Mandates a harmonization work program (still uncompleted after 30 years) and establishes disciplines for non-preferential rules of origin.

**Trade Facilitation Agreement (TFA, 2017):** Requires WTO members to streamline customs procedures, enhance transparency, and reduce border delays. Implementation is supported by capacity-building provisions for developing countries.

### WTO Dispute Settlement

Major customs-related WTO disputes:

- **EC -- Customs Classification of Frozen Boneless Chicken Cuts (DS269/286, 2005):** Addressed the boundary between salted and frozen chicken for tariff classification purposes, establishing important principles on HS classification.
- **China -- Measures Related to the Exportation of Various Raw Materials (DS394/395/398, 2012):** The Appellate Body ruled that China could not justify export duties on raw materials under Article XX exceptions.
- **United States -- Certain Measures on Steel and Aluminium Products (DS544 et al.):** Multiple WTO members challenged U.S. Section 232 tariffs. Panels found violations of GATT Articles I and II, rejecting the U.S. invocation of the national security exception under Article XXI.

### ECHR and Customs Enforcement

**Agosi v. United Kingdom (Application No. 9118/80, 1986):** The Court held that the seizure and forfeiture of smuggled gold coins did not violate Protocol 1, Article 1, as the interference was in accordance with law and proportionate to the legitimate aim of controlling imports.

**Air Canada v. United Kingdom (Application No. 18465/91, 1995):** Customs seizure of an aircraft used for drug smuggling was held proportionate, even though the airline was not directly involved in the criminal activity.

**Jussila v. Finland (Application No. 73053/01, 2006):** The Grand Chamber held that customs surcharges constituting a "criminal charge" under Article 6 required fair trial protections, though the nature of customs proceedings allowed for certain procedural modifications.

## Comparative Analysis

### Tariff Level Philosophy

South Korea maintains the highest tariff levels among the three jurisdictions, particularly for agricultural products (56.7 percent average), reflecting a deliberate policy of protecting domestic farming and food security. The United States has historically maintained low MFN tariffs but has significantly increased effective rates through unilateral executive actions (Section 301, Section 232). The EU occupies a middle ground with moderate MFN rates but maintains significant agricultural protection through the Common Agricultural Policy and associated tariff structures.

### Trade Agreement Strategy

South Korea has pursued the most aggressive bilateral FTA strategy among the three, with 21 agreements covering 59 countries. The EU's approach combines bilateral agreements with a comprehensive GSP system for developing countries. The United States has been more selective, with only 14 FTAs, and has shown increasing skepticism toward multilateral and bilateral trade liberalization since 2017.

### Trade Remedy Usage

The United States is the most prolific user of anti-dumping and countervailing duties, with over 400 active orders. The EU maintains approximately 150 measures. South Korea uses trade remedies more sparingly but has increased activity in recent years. All three jurisdictions face WTO challenges to their trade remedy practices.

### Customs Administration and Modernization

All three jurisdictions have invested heavily in customs modernization, including electronic filing, risk-based targeting, and authorized economic operator programs. The EU's UCC represents the most comprehensive harmonized framework, creating uniform procedures across 27 member states. South Korea's customs clearance system is widely regarded as among the most efficient globally, with average clearance times of 1.5 hours for AEO-certified importers.

### National Security Tariffs

The U.S. use of Section 232 tariffs on national security grounds represents a significant departure from the post-war consensus on trade liberalization. Neither South Korea nor the EU has invoked national security exceptions for tariff measures on a comparable scale, though the EU has imposed safeguard measures on steel imports in response to trade diversion caused by U.S. Section 232 tariffs.
