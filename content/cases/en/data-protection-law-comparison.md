## Summary

Data protection law -- the regulatory framework governing the collection, processing, storage, and transfer of personal information -- has become one of the most consequential areas of modern law, shaping the global digital economy, international trade, law enforcement cooperation, and individual rights in an era of ubiquitous data collection by governments and private enterprises. South Korea enacted the Personal Information Protection Act (개인정보 보호법, PIPA) in 2011, creating one of Asia's most comprehensive data protection frameworks, significantly amended in 2020 to establish the Personal Information Protection Commission (개인정보보호위원회, PIPC) as an independent supervisory authority, and further revised in 2023 to introduce provisions for automated decision-making, data portability, and enhanced cross-border transfer mechanisms aligned with the EU adequacy framework -- Korea received an EU adequacy decision on December 17, 2021 under GDPR Article 45. The United States lacks a comprehensive federal data protection statute, instead relying on a sectoral patchwork including the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) for health data, the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act for financial data, the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) for children's data, and the FTC's enforcement authority under Section 5, supplemented by state laws led by the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA, as amended by CPRA) and comprehensive laws in over 15 additional states. Germany applies the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, Regulation 2016/679) directly, supplemented by the Bundesdatenschutzgesetz (BDSG) exercising national-level options, with the data protection supervisory authorities of the 16 Länder plus the Federal Commissioner for Data Protection (Bundesbeauftragte für den Datenschutz und die Informationsfreiheit, BfDI) forming a robust enforcement infrastructure. The GDPR itself, effective May 25, 2018, has established the global gold standard for data protection, with extraterritorial scope reaching any entity processing EU residents' data, enforcement through fines of up to EUR 20 million or 4 percent of global annual turnover, and fundamental rights grounding in the Charter of Fundamental Rights Articles 7 (private life) and 8 (data protection), while the ECHR has developed extensive data protection jurisprudence under Article 8 of the Convention.

## South Korea

### Legal Framework

South Korea's data protection framework is anchored by the Personal Information Protection Act (개인정보 보호법, PIPA, Act No. 19234), supplemented by sector-specific laws including the Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection (정보통신망 이용촉진 및 정보보호 등에 관한 법률) for telecommunications and online service providers, and the Credit Information Use and Protection Act (신용정보의 이용 및 보호에 관한 법률) for financial data.

### 2023 PIPA Amendments

The 2023 amendments (effective September 2023) introduced significant new provisions:

| New Provision | Detail |
|--------------|--------|
| Automated decision-making | Right to explanation and opt-out of automated decisions with significant impact |
| Data portability | Right to receive personal data in structured, machine-readable format |
| Cross-border transfer | Streamlined transfer mechanisms; adequacy recognition procedure |
| Pseudonymization framework | Enhanced rules for processing pseudonymized data for research and statistics |
| Children's data | Strengthened consent requirements for processing children's personal information |

### PIPC Enforcement

The Personal Information Protection Commission (개인정보보호위원회), established as an independent central administrative agency under the Prime Minister's Office in 2020, has enforcement powers including:

- Corrective orders (시정명령)
- Suspension of processing
- Administrative fines up to 5 billion KRW or 3 percent of related revenue
- Criminal referral for serious violations

Notable enforcement actions include fines against major technology companies for inadequate consent mechanisms and unauthorized data sharing.

### EU Adequacy Decision

On December 17, 2021, the European Commission adopted an adequacy decision for South Korea under GDPR Article 45, recognizing that Korea provides an adequate level of protection for personal data. This decision facilitates EU-Korea data flows without additional safeguards. The adequacy finding was based on PIPA's comprehensive framework, PIPC's independence, and supplementary safeguards provided by Korean authorities.

## United States

### Legal Framework

The United States lacks a comprehensive federal data protection law. Instead, privacy is protected through a patchwork of federal sectoral statutes, state laws, and FTC enforcement:

### Federal Sectoral Laws

| Statute | Sector | Key Provisions |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| HIPAA (1996) | Health | Privacy Rule for protected health information; Security Rule; breach notification |
| GLBA (1999) | Financial | Privacy notices; opt-out rights; safeguards rule |
| COPPA (1998) | Children (under 13) | Parental consent for data collection; FTC enforcement |
| FERPA (1974) | Education | Student record privacy; parental rights |
| ECPA (1986) | Electronic communications | Wiretap Act; Stored Communications Act; Pen Register Act |
| FCRA (1970) | Credit reporting | Accuracy; disclosure; dispute rights |

### State Comprehensive Privacy Laws

Following California's CCPA (2018) and CPRA amendment (2020), comprehensive state privacy laws have proliferated:

| State | Law | Effective Date |
|-------|-----|---------------|
| California | CCPA/CPRA | January 2020 / January 2023 |
| Virginia | VCDPA | January 2023 |
| Colorado | CPA | July 2023 |
| Connecticut | CTDPA | July 2023 |
| Utah | UCPA | December 2023 |
| Texas | TDPSA | July 2024 |
| Oregon | OCPA | July 2024 |
| Montana, Iowa, Indiana, Tennessee, etc. | Various | 2024-2025 |

As of early 2026, over 15 states have enacted comprehensive privacy laws, creating a complex compliance landscape for businesses operating nationally.

### American Privacy Rights Act (APRA)

The most promising federal comprehensive privacy bill, the American Privacy Rights Act, advanced through committee in 2024 but was not enacted. Key features included: national data minimization standard, individual rights (access, correction, deletion, portability), preemption of state laws (with exceptions), and a private right of action.

### FTC Enforcement

The Federal Trade Commission exercises de facto data protection authority under Section 5 of the FTC Act (unfair or deceptive practices). Major enforcement actions include $5 billion fine against Facebook/Meta (2019) and significant actions against Google, Amazon, and other technology companies. The FTC has proposed a comprehensive privacy rulemaking (Advanced Notice of Proposed Rulemaking, 2022).

## Germany

### Legal Framework

Germany applies the GDPR directly as an EU regulation, supplemented by the Bundesdatenschutzgesetz (BDSG), which exercises national options permitted under the GDPR (e.g., employee data processing, video surveillance, DPA procedures). Germany was a pioneer in data protection law, having enacted the world's first data protection statute in Hesse (Hessisches Datenschutzgesetz) in 1970 and the first federal data protection law (Bundesdatenschutzgesetz) in 1977.

### Supervisory Authority Structure

Germany has a unique multi-tiered supervisory authority structure:

| Authority | Jurisdiction |
|-----------|-------------|
| BfDI (Bundesbeauftragte für den Datenschutz) | Federal public bodies; telecommunications; postal services |
| 16 Länder DPAs | Private sector and state public bodies within each Land |
| DSK (Datenschutzkonferenz) | Coordination body for all German DPAs |

### Employee Data Protection

Section 26 BDSG provides specific rules for employee data processing (Beschäftigtendatenschutz), permitting processing necessary for the employment relationship, including recruitment, performance, and termination. Germany's works councils (Betriebsräte) have co-determination rights regarding employee monitoring under the Betriebsverfassungsgesetz.

### Significant Enforcement

German DPAs have imposed substantial GDPR fines:

- **H&M (Hamburg DPA, 2020):** EUR 35.3 million for systematic surveillance of employees
- **1&1 Telecom (BfDI, 2019):** EUR 9.55 million for inadequate authentication procedures
- **Deutsche Wohnen (Berlin DPA, 2019/2021):** EUR 14.5 million for excessive data retention (partially overturned)

### Schrems Litigation Impact

The CJEU decisions in Schrems I (C-362/14, 2015) and Schrems II (C-311/18, 2020), both originating from complaints to the Irish DPA about Facebook's transfers of EU data to the United States, fundamentally reshaped transatlantic data flows. Schrems II invalidated the EU-U.S. Privacy Shield framework, finding that U.S. surveillance law (particularly Section 702 FISA and E.O. 12333) did not provide adequate protection. This led to the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework (adopted July 2023), based on Executive Order 14086 establishing a Data Protection Review Court.

## ECHR & EU

### GDPR (Regulation 2016/679)

The GDPR, effective May 25, 2018, is the world's most influential data protection law:

**Principles (Article 5):** Lawfulness, fairness, transparency; purpose limitation; data minimization; accuracy; storage limitation; integrity and confidentiality; accountability.

**Legal Bases (Article 6):** Consent; contractual necessity; legal obligation; vital interests; public task; legitimate interest.

**Data Subject Rights:** Access (Art. 15), rectification (Art. 16), erasure/right to be forgotten (Art. 17), restriction (Art. 18), data portability (Art. 20), objection (Art. 21), automated decision-making (Art. 22).

**Cross-Border Transfers (Chapter V):** Adequacy decisions; Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs); Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs); derogations.

### ECHR Article 8 and Data Protection

The ECHR has developed extensive data protection jurisprudence:

**S. and Marper v. United Kingdom (Grand Chamber, 2008):** The Court found that the blanket and indiscriminate retention of DNA profiles and fingerprints of persons not convicted of any offense violated Article 8. The decision established that the systematic collection and storage of personal data engages Article 8 regardless of whether the data is subsequently used.

**Big Brother Watch and Others v. United Kingdom (Grand Chamber, 2021):** The Court found violations of Articles 8 and 10 in the UK's bulk interception regime (GCHQ/NSA programs revealed by Edward Snowden), establishing detailed requirements for the authorization, supervision, and safeguards of bulk surveillance programs.

**Barbulescu v. Romania (Grand Chamber, 2017):** The Court found a violation of Article 8 where an employer monitored an employee's personal communications without adequate notice, establishing that Article 8 protects the privacy of communications in the workplace.

### EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework

The EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework, adopted by European Commission adequacy decision on July 10, 2023, replaces the invalidated Privacy Shield. Based on U.S. Executive Order 14086 (signed October 7, 2022), the Framework establishes:

- Proportionality and necessity requirements for U.S. intelligence access to EU data
- A Data Protection Review Court for EU individuals to challenge surveillance
- Enhanced oversight by the Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board

The Framework faces a potential legal challenge (Schrems III) questioning whether the reforms adequately address the CJEU's concerns in Schrems II.

### AI Act and Data Protection

The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) intersects significantly with data protection, requiring data governance measures for training data, prohibiting certain AI practices involving biometric surveillance, and mandating fundamental rights impact assessments for high-risk AI systems.

## Comparative Analysis

### Comprehensive vs. Sectoral Approaches

The most fundamental divergence is between comprehensive (South Korea, Germany/EU) and sectoral (United States) approaches. Comprehensive frameworks provide consistent protection across all sectors and reduce compliance complexity. The U.S. sectoral approach creates gaps (no federal protection for many consumer data categories) and compliance complexity (navigating 15+ state laws).

### Enforcement Intensity

GDPR enforcement has produced substantial fines (over EUR 4 billion cumulative by 2025), with Ireland's DPA imposing major penalties on Meta, TikTok, and other technology companies headquartered there. South Korea's PIPC has ramped up enforcement since gaining independence in 2020. The FTC's enforcement, while impactful in individual cases, lacks the systematic investigation and penalty framework of GDPR.

### Cross-Border Data Flows

The adequacy framework creates a tiered system for international data transfers. South Korea's 2021 adequacy decision enables seamless EU-Korea data flows. The EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework provides a mechanism for transatlantic flows but faces ongoing legal uncertainty. Countries without adequacy must rely on SCCs or BCRs, creating administrative burden.

### Emerging Issues

All jurisdictions face challenges in regulating AI-driven data processing, biometric data, and automated decision-making. South Korea's 2023 PIPA amendments specifically address automated decisions. The EU AI Act provides a complementary framework. The United States has proposed AI-specific executive orders but lacks binding legislation.
